\n1736 (MDCCXXXVI) was a leap year starting on Sunday (dominical letter AG) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Julian calendar, the 1736th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 736th year of the 2nd millennium, the 36th year of the 18th century, and the 7th year of the 1730s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1736 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Sweden: The Civil Code of 1734 is passed.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Stanislaus I of Poland abdicates his throne.
    • \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Francis I, Holy Roman Emperor marries Maria Theresa of Austria, ruler of the Habsburg Empire.
    • \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 The Genbun era begins in Japan. The era of Ky\xc5\x8dh\xc5\x8d Reforms ends.
    • \n
  • March 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Nader Shah, founder of the Afsharid dynasty, is crowned Shah of Iran.
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Porteous Riots erupt in Edinburgh after the execution of smuggler Andrew Wilson, when town guard Captain John Porteous orders his men to fire at the crowd. Porteous is arrested later.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nMay 26: Battle of Ackia.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick, Prince of Wales, marries Augusta of Saxe-Gotha.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ackia: British and Chickasaw Native Americans defeat French troops.
    • \n
  • June 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Leonhard Euler writes to James Stirling describing the Euler\xe2\x80\x93Maclaurin formula, providing a connection between integrals and calculus.
    • \n
  • June 19 \xe2\x80\x93 French Academy of Sciences expedition led by Pierre Louis Maupertuis, with Anders Celsius, begins work on measuring a meridian arc in Me\xc3\xa4nmaa of Finland.[1]
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War, 1735-1739: Russian forces under Peter Lacy storm the Ottoman fortress of Azov.
    • \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 An Edinburgh crowd drags John Porteous out of his cell in Tolbooth Prison and lynches him.
    • \n
  • December 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Ben Franklin builds the first volunteer fire company in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • December 26 - Andrew Michael Ramsay gave an oration in which he related the heritage and internationalism of Freemasonry to that of the Crusades.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Neustrelitz becomes the capital of Mecklenburg-Strelitz.
    • \n
  • Bushehr is founded in Persia.
    • \n
  • The Belgrade fortress is completed.
    • \n
  • George Hamilton, 1st Earl of Orkney, becomes the first Field Marshal of Great Britain.
    • \n
  • A fire in the Russian city of Saint Petersburg burns 2000 houses.
    • \n
  • Fifty-three houses in the English town of Stony Stratford are consumed by fire.
    • \n
  • One of the earliest records of use of a Bathing machine is made at Scarborough in England.
    • \n
  • Charles Marie de La Condamine, with Fran\xc3\xa7ois Fresneau Gataudi\xc3\xa8re, makes the first scientific observations of rubber, in Ecuador.[2]
    • \n
  • Leonhard Euler produces the first published proof of Fermat\'s "little theorem".[3]
    • \n
  • Sir Isaac Newton\'s Method of Fluxions (1671), describing his method of differential calculus, is first published (posthumously) and Thomas Bayes publishes a defense of its logical foundations (anonymously).[4]
    • \n
  • Muhammad ibn Abd al Wahhab writes the Kitab at-tawhidt, marking the beginning of Wahhabism.
    • \n
  • The Haidamakas raid the shtetl of Pavoloch, killing 35.
    • \n
