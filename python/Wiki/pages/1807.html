1807 (MDCCCVII) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1807th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 807th year of the 2nd millennium, the 7th year of the 19th century, and the 8th year of the 1800s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1807 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon attacks Russia.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars and Anglo-Spanish War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Montevideo: The British Army captures Montevideo from the Spanish Empire as part of the British invasions of the R\xc3\xado de la Plata.
    • \n
  • February 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Eylau: Napoleon\'s French Empire begins fighting against Russian and Prussian forces of the Fourth Coalition.
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Eylau: Napoleon fights a hard, but inconclusive battle against the Russians under Bennigsen.
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Coast Survey is established; work begins on August 3, 1816.
    • \n
  • February 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Christopher is elected first President of the State of Haiti, ruling the northern part of the country.
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 In Alabama, former Vice President of the United States Aaron Burr is tried for conspiracy but acquitted.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The U.S. Congress passes an act to "prohibit the importation of slaves into any port or place within the jurisdiction of the United States ... from any foreign kingdom, place, or country" (to take effect 1 January 1808).
    • \n
  • March 25\n
      \n
    • The Slave Trade Act becomes law, abolishing the slave trade in the British Empire. (Slavery is abolished in 1833).
      • \n
    • The Swansea and Mumbles Railway in South Wales, at this time known as the Oystermouth Railway, becomes the first passenger-carrying railway in the world.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 H. W. Olbers discovers the asteroid Vesta.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 4\xe2\x80\x9312 \xe2\x80\x93 Froberg mutiny at Fort Ricasoli on Malta by men of the irregularly-recruited Frobert Regiment, suppressed by the protecting British power.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 French forces capture Danzig after a 6-week siege.
    • \n
  • May 22 \xe2\x80\x93 A grand jury indicts former Vice President of the United States Aaron Burr for treason.
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Selim III, Ottoman Emperor since 1789, is deposed in favour of his nephew Mustafa IV.
    • \n
  • May 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Primitive Methodism originates in an All Day of Prayer at Mow Cop in the north midlands of England.[1]
    • \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Duke of Portland wins the United Kingdom general election.
    • \n
  • June 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Friedland: Napoleon decisively defeats Bennigsen\'s Russian army.
    • \n
  • June 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Chesapeake\xe2\x80\x93Leopard Affair: British Royal Navy warship HMS Leopard attacks and boards the United States Navy frigate USS Chesapeake off Norfolk, Virginia, seeking deserters.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 A disastrous British attack is mounted against Buenos Aires during the second failed invasion of the R\xc3\xado de la Plata.
    • \n
  • July 7\xe2\x80\x93July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Peace of Tilsit is signed between France, Prussia and Russia. Napoleon and Russian Emperor Alexander I ally together against the British. The Prussians are forced to cede more than half their territory, which is formed into the Duchy of Warsaw in their former Polish lands and the Kingdom of Westphalia in western Germany.
    • \n
  • July 13 \xe2\x80\x93 With the death of Henry Benedict Stuart, the last Stuart claimant to the throne of the United Kingdom, the movement of Jacobitism comes to an effective end.
    • \n
  • July 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Nic\xc3\xa9phore Ni\xc3\xa9pce is awarded a patent by Napoleon Bonaparte for the Pyr\xc3\xa9olophore, the world\'s first internal combustion engine, after it successfully powers a boat upstream on the river Sa\xc3\xb4ne in France.
    • \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The North River Steamboat, Robert Fulton\'s first American steamboat, leaves New York City for Albany on the Hudson River, inaugurating the first commercial steamboat service in the world.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Former U.S. Vice President Aaron Burr is acquitted of treason. He had been accused of plotting to annex parts of Louisiana and Mexico to become part of an independent republic.
    • \n
  • September 2\xe2\x80\x937 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Copenhagen: The British Royal Navy bombards Copenhagen with fire bombs and phosphorus rockets to prevent the Dano-Norwegian navy from surrendering to Napoleon; 30% of the city is destroyed and 2,000 citizens are killed.
    • \n
  • September 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Morrison, the first Protestant missionary to China, arrives in Guangzhou.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Serfdom is abolished in Prussia by law.
    • \n
  • October 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Geological Society of London founded.
    • \n
  • November 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Abrantes: The French under Jean-Andoche Junot take the town.
    • \n
  • November 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Portuguese Queen Maria I and the Court embark at Lisbon bound for Brazil. Rio de Janeiro becomes the Portuguese capital.
    • \n
  • December 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Congress passes the Embargo Act.
    • \n
  • December 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Parr, Resident of British Bencoolen, is decapitated by a mob.
    • \n
