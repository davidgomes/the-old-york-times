1645 (MDCXLV) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Julian calendar, the 1645th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 645th year of the 2nd millennium, the 45th year of the 17th century, and the 6th year of the 1640s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1645 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Long Parliament adopts the Directory for Public Worship in England, Wales, Ireland and Scotland, replacing the Book of Common Prayer (1559). Holy Days (other than Sundays) are not to be observed.
    • \n
  • January 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Archbishop of Canterbury William Laud is executed for treason on Tower Hill, London.
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Fairfax is appointed Commander-in-Chief.
    • \n
  • January 29 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Armistice talks open at Uxbridge.
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Inverlochy: The Covenanters are defeated by Montrose.
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: The New Model Army is officially founded.
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Uxbridge armistice talks fail.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Prince Rupert leaves Oxford for Bristol.
    • \n
  • March 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Fearing the spread of the Black Death (plague), Edinburgh Town Council prohibits all gatherings except weddings and funerals.
    • \n
  • April 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The House of Lords passes the Self-denying Ordinance, requiring members of the Parliament of England to resign commissions in the armed services.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Because of the plague, the Edinburgh town council orders that the college graduation ceremony should be brought forward so that students can leave the city (on November 19, teaching resumes in Linlithgow).
    • \n
  • April 23 (St George\'s Day) \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: One hundred and fifty Irish soldiers bound for service with King Charles I of England are captured at sea by Parliamentarians and killed at Pembroke in Wales.
    • \n
  • May 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Auldearn: Scottish Covenanters are defeated by Montrose.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Prince Rupert\'s army sacks Leicester.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Oliver Cromwell is confirmed as the Lieutenant-General of the Cavalry.
    • \n
  • June 14 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Naseby: 12,000 Royalist forces are beaten by 15,000 Parliamentarian soldiers.
    • \n
  • June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: The Royalists lose Carlisle.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Fight at Alford, Aberdeenshire.
    • \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Langport: Cromwell wins in Somerset.
    • \n
  • July 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Qing dynasty regent Dorgon issues an edict ordering all Han Chinese men to shave their forehead and braid the rest of their hair into a queue identical to those of the Manchus.
    • \n
  • July 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Tsar Alexei Mikhailovich of Russia comes to the throne.
    • \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Br\xc3\xb6msebro is signed between Sweden and Denmark\xe2\x80\x93Norway, ending the Torstenson War and ceding Jemtland, Herjedalen, Gotland and \xc3\x96sel (Saaremaa) to Sweden, which also holds the province of Halland for a period of 30 years as a guarantee.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Prince Rupert surrenders Bristol.
    • \n
  • September 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Philiphaugh: Covenanters defeat Montrose at Selkirk.
    • \n
  • September 24 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Rowton Heath: Parliamentarians defeat the Royalist cavalry.
    • \n
  • October 8\xe2\x80\x93October 14 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Third siege of Basing House by Cromwell results in its destruction.
    • \n
  • October 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Jeanne Mance founds the H\xc3\xb4tel-Dieu de Montr\xc3\xa9al, the first hospital in North America.
    • \n
  • October 11 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War: Re-fortification of Bourne Castle in Lincolnshire against a threatened Royalist attack begins.
    • \n
  • November 20 The then Colegio de Santo Tomas, was elevated by Pope Innocent X into the University of Santo Tomas in his brief In Supreminenti. It has the oldest extant University Charter in the Philippines, as well as the whole of Asia.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Bamana forces from S\xc3\xa9gou invade the Mali heartland, destroying the Mali Empire after its 400 years as a unified state.
    • \n
  • The Stolberg-Wernigerode branch of the family of the counts of Stolberg and Wernigerode is founded in Germany.
    • \n
  • The Solar cycle enters the 70-year Maunder Minimum during which sunspots will be rare.[1]
    • \n
  • Wallpaper begins to replace tapestries as a wall decoration.
    • \n
