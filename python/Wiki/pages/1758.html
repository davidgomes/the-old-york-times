\n1758 (MDCCLVIII) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1758th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 758th year of the 2nd millennium, the 58th year of the 18th century, and the 9th year of the 1750s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1758 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 James Abercrombie replaces the Earl of Loudoun as supreme commander in the American colonies. He is replaced himself after failing to take the fort at Ticonderoga.
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Cuddalore: A British fleet under Sir George Pocock engages the French fleet of Anne Antoine, Comte d\'Ach\xc3\xa9 indecisively near Madras.
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 French and Indian War: Mary Campbell is abducted from her home in Pennsylvania by Lenape.
    • \n
  • June 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 French and Indian War: Siege of Louisbourg: James Wolfe\'s attack at Louisbourg, Nova Scotia commences.
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Krefeld: Anglo-Hanoverian forces under Ferdinand of Brunswick defeat the French.
    • \n
  • June 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Domstadtl: Austrian forces under Ernst Gideon von Laudon and Joseph von Siskovits rout an enormous convoy with supplies for the Prussian army, guarded by strong troops of Hans Joachim von Zieten.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 6\n
      \n
    • Pope Clement XIII succeeds Pope Benedict XIV as the 248th pope.
      • \n
    • Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Bernetz Brook: British troops defeat the French.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • July 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War: French and Indian War: French forces hold Fort Carillon against the British at Ticonderoga, New York.
    • \n
  • July 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 French and Indian War: The island battery at Fortress Louisbourg is silenced and all French warships are destroyed or taken.
    • \n
  • August 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Negapatam: Off the coast of India, Admiral Pocock again engages d\'Ach\xc3\xa9\'s French fleet, this time with more success.
    • \n
  • August 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Zorndorf: Frederick defeats the Russian army of Count Wilhelm Fermor near the Oder.
    • \n
  • September 3 \xe2\x80\x93 T\xc3\xa1vora affair: attempted assassination of Joseph I of Portugal.
    • \n
  • September 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 French and Indian War: Battle of Fort Duquesne: A British attack on Fort Duquesne (present day Pittsburgh) is defeated.
    • \n
  • October 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War: Battle of Hochkirch: Frederick loses a hard-fought battle against the Austrians under Marshal Leopold von Daun, who besieges Dresden.
    • \n
  • November 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War: French and Indian War: French forces abandon Fort Duquesne to the British, who then name the area Pittsburgh.
    • \n
  • December 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The ship Duke William sinks in the North Atlantic with the loss of over 360 lives while deporting Acadians from Prince Edward Island to France.
    • \n
  • December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Halley\'s Comet appears for the first time after Halley\'s identification of it.
    • \n
