\n1804 (MDCCCIV) was a leap year starting on Sunday (dominical letter AG) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Friday (dominical letter CB) of the Julian calendar, the 1804th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 804th year of the 2nd millennium, the 4th year of the 19th century, and the 5th year of the 1800s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1804 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Haiti gains independence from France and becomes the first black republic, having the only successful slave revolt ever.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The First Serbian Uprising begins as an intro into Serbian Revolution. By 1817, Principality of Serbia proclaims self-rule from Ottoman Empire, thus becoming the first nation-state in Europe.
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 New Jersey becomes the last northern state to abolish slavery.
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 First Barbary War: Stephen Decatur leads a raid to burn the pirate-held frigate USS Philadelphia at Tripoli to deny her further use by the captors.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\n* February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The USS Philadelphia burns.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • February 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Ohio University is chartered by the Ohio General Assembly.
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Cornishman Richard Trevithick\'s newly built Penydarren steam locomotive operates on the Merthyr Tramroad between Penydarren in Merthyr Tydfil and Abercynon in South Wales, following several trials since February 13, the world\'s first locomotive to work on rails.[1]
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 The 1804 Haiti Massacre, an ethnic cleansing, eradicates the white population on Haiti; it is not stopped until 22 April.
    • \n
  • March 7\n
      \n
    • John Wedgwood founds the Royal Horticultural Society.[2]
      • \n
    • Thomas Charles is instrumental in founding the British and Foreign Bible Society.[3]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Louisiana Purchase: In St. Louis, a formal ceremony is conducted to transfer ownership of Louisiana Territory from France to the United States.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 First performance of Friedrich Schiller\'s play Wilhelm Tell, at Weimar under the direction of Johann Wolfgang von Goethe.
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Napoleonic Code is adopted as French civil law.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Forty merchantmen are wrecked when a convoy led by the HMS Apollo runs aground off Portugal.
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Samuel Taylor Coleridge aboard The Speedwell sails to the Mediterranean
    • \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 High Possil meteorite, the first recorded meteorite to fall in Scotland in modern times, falls at High Possil.
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Addington resigns as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 William Pitt the Younger begins his second term as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • May 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Lewis and Clark Expedition departs from Camp Dubois and begins their historic journey by traveling up the Missouri River.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon Bonaparte is proclaimed Emperor of the French by the French Senate.
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 P\xc3\xa8re Lachaise Cemetery a 118 acres (0.48 km2) cemetery in Paris, France is opened.
    • \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Beethoven\'s Symphony No. 3 in E\xe2\x80\x93flat premiered in Vienna.
    • \n
  • June 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The Twelfth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution is ratified by New Hampshire, and arguably becomes effective (subsequently vetoed by the Governor of New Hampshire).
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Alexander Hamilton is shot during a duel with Aaron Burr and dies the next day.
    • \n
  • July 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Twelfth Amendment to the U.S. Constitution ratified by Tennessee, removing doubt surrounding adoption.
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Lewis and Clark Expedition: The Corps of Discovery, whose purpose is to explore the Louisiana Purchase, suffers its only death when Sergeant Charles Floyd dies, apparently from acute appendicitis.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 German astronomer K. L. Harding discovers the asteroid Juno.
    • \n

\nJuly 11: Burr shot Alexander Hamilton.

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of St. Louis is signed by Quashquame and William Henry Harrison; controversy surrounding the treaty eventually causes the Sauk people to ally with the British during the War of 1812 and is the main cause of the Black Hawk War of 1832.
    • \n
  • November 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Said bin Sultan, Sultan of Muscat and Oman starts to rule.
    • \n
  • November 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The Democratic-Republican-controlled United States Senate begin an impeachment trial against Federalist-partisan Supreme Court of the United States Justice Samuel Chase on charges of political bias (he is acquitted by the United States Senate of all charges on March 1, 1805).
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Coronation of Napoleon I: At the cathedral of Notre Dame de Paris, Napoleon crowns himself as the first Emperor of the French in a thousand years. Witnessing this, Sim\xc3\xb3n Bol\xc3\xadvar dedicates himself to liberating Venezuela from Spanish rule.
    • \n
  • December 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Jefferson defeats Charles C. Pinckney in the United States presidential election.
    • \n
  • December 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Spain declares war on Britain
    • \n
