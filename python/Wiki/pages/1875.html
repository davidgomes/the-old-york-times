1875 (MDCCCLXXV) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Julian calendar, the 1875th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 875th year of the 2nd millennium, the 75th year of the 19th century, and the 6th year of the 1870s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1875 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Midland Railway of England abolishes the Second Class passenger category leaving First Class and Third Class. Other British railway companies follow Midland\'s lead during the rest of the year (Third Class is renamed Second Class in 1956).
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Guangxu becomes 11th Qing dynasty Emperor of China at the age of 4 in succession to his cousin.
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The newly proclaimed King Alfonso XII of Spain (Queen Isabella II\'s son) arrives in Spain to restore the monarchy during the Third Carlist War.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of L\xc3\xa1car (Third Carlist War in Spain) \xe2\x80\x93 Carlist commander Torcuato Mend\xc3\xadri secures a brilliant victory when he surprises and routs a Government force under General Enrique Barg\xc3\xa9s at L\xc3\xa1car, east of Estella, nearly capturing newly crowned King Alfonso XII. The Carlists take several pieces of artillery, more than 2,000 rifles, and 300 prisoners. 800 men of both sides are killed - mostly government troops.
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Jeanne Calment is born in Arles, France. She would go on to become the world\'s oldest verified person to have ever lived, reaching an age of 122 years and 164 days, before passing away on August 4, 1997 of natural causes.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The SS Gothenburg sinks off Australia\'s east coast with the loss of approximately 102 lives, including a number of high-profile civil servants and dignitaries.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The majority of the Yavapai (Wipukyipai) and Tonto Apache (Dil Zh\xc3\xa9\xc3\xa9) tribes are forced by the United States Cavalry under command of Brigadier General George Crook to walk at gunpoint from the Arizona\'s Verde Valley, to the San Carlos Apache Indian Reservation, 180 miles to the southeast. The two tribes are not allowed to return to the Verde Valley until 1900.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Newton Booth, 11th Governor of California, resigns, having been elected Senator. Lieutenant Governor of California Romualdo Pacheco becomes acting Governor. He is later replaced by elected governor William Irwin.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Congress passes the Civil Rights Act, which prohibits racial discrimination in public accommodations and jury duty.
    • \n
  • March 3\n
      \n
    • Bizet\xe2\x80\x99s Carmen is first performed at the Op\xc3\xa9ra-Comique, Paris.
      • \n
    • The first indoor ice hockey game is played at the Victoria Skating Rink in Montreal, Canada.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Roman Catholic Archbishop of New York John McCloskey is named the first cardinal in the United States.
    • \n

\n
    \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Arya Samaj is founded in Mumbai by Swami Dayananda Saraswati.
    • \n

    • \n
  • May 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Saint Petersburg is signed between Japan and Russia.
    • \n
  • May 7 \xe2\x80\x93 German liner SS Schiller wrecks on the rocks off the Isles of Scilly with the loss of 335 lives.
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Aristides wins the first Kentucky Derby.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Metre Convention is signed in Paris.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 The record-setting American clipper Flying Cloud of 1851 is burned for scrap metal.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Two American colleges play each other in arguably the first game of college football:[1] Tufts University and Harvard University at Jarvis Field in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Summer \xe2\x80\x93 Third Carlist War in Spain: Two government armies under General Quesada and Mart\xc3\xadnez Campos start encroaching on Carlist territory. Both they and their Carlist opponent (Mendiri) drive opposing sympathisers from their homes and burn crops in areas they can not hold. Several Carlist generals (Dorregaray, Savalls, and others) are unjustly put on trial for disloyalty. Mendiri is also removed from his command, and is replaced by the Count of Caserta. Despite having 48 infantry battalions, three cavalry regiments, two engineer battalions, and 100 pieces of artillery at his disposal, Caserta is heavily outnumbered by the government forces opposing him.
    • \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 General Postal Union established.
    • \n
  • July 1\xe2\x80\x93July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Trevi\xc3\xb1o (Third Carlist War in Spain) at Trevi\xc3\xb1o \xe2\x80\x93 Advancing on the key city of Vitoria, in Navarre, the Spanish Republican commander General Jenardo de Quesada sends General Tello to attack the Carlist lines just to the southwest at Trevi\xc3\xb1o. The newly appointed Carlist commander General Jos\xc3\xa9 P\xc3\xa9rula is heavily defeated and withdraws and soon afterwards Quesada enters Vitoria in triumph.
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The legendary Mohican Base Ball Club was established in Kennett Square, PA.
    • \n
  • August 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Hibernian F.C. founded by Irishmen in the Cowgate area of Edinburgh, Scotland.[2]
    • \n
  • August 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Captain Matthew Webb becomes the first person to swim the English Channel.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 A murder conviction effectively forces the violent Irish anti-owner coal miners, the "Molly Maguires", to disband.
    • \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Agurdat: An Egyptian invasion of Ethiopia fails when Emperor Yohannes IV defeats an army led by Werner Munzinger.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 English Association football team Birmingham City F.C. is founded as Small Heath Alliance in Birmingham by a group of cricketers from Holy Trinity Church, Bordesley, playing its first match in November.[3]
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 The Ottoman state declares partial bankruptcy and places its finances in the hands of European creditors.
    • \n
  • October 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Chief Lone Horn of the Minneconjou dies at the Cheyenne River, leaving his son Big Foot as the new chief.
    • \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Brigham Young University is founded in Provo, Utah.
    • \n
  • October 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The first performance of the Piano Concerto No. 1 by Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky is given in Boston, Massachusetts with Hans von B\xc3\xbclow as soloist.
    • \n
  • October 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The Theosophical Society is founded in New York by Helena Blavatsky, H. S. Olcott, W. Q. Judge, and others.
    • \n
  • November 9 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars: In Washington, D.C., Indian Inspector E.C. Watkins issues a report stating that hundreds of Sioux and Cheyenne associated with Sitting Bull and Crazy Horse are hostile to the United States (the Battle of the Little Bighorn is fought in Montana the next year).
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Gundat: Ethiopian Emperor Yohannes IV defeats another Egyptian army.
    • \n
  • November 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Times newspaper in London reveals that Isma\'il Pasha has sold Egypt\'s 44% share in the Suez Canal to Britain in a deal secured by Benjamin Disraeli without the prior sanction of the British Parliament.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Notorious New York City politician Boss Tweed escapes from prison and flees to Cuba, then to Spain.
    • \n
  • December 5\xe2\x80\x936 \xe2\x80\x93 German emigrant ship SS Deutschland runs aground in the English Channel resulting in the death of 157 passengers and crew.[4]
    • \n
  • December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Massachusetts Rifle Association, "America\'s Oldest Active Gun Club", is formed.
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 ICRM renamed International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC).
    • \n
  • December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Edinburgh derby in Association football is played: Heart of Midlothian F.C. win 1\xe2\x80\x930 against Hibernian F.C.
    • \n
