\n1848 (MDCCCXLVIII) was a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Julian calendar, the 1848th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 848th year of the 2nd millennium, the 48th year of the 19th century, and the 9th year of the 1840s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1848 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929. It is historically famous for the wave of revolutions, a series of widespread struggles for more liberal governments, which broke out from Brazil to Hungary; although most failed in their immediate aims, they significantly altered the political and philosophical landscape and had major ramifications throughout the rest of the century.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Joseph Jenkins Roberts is sworn in as the first president of the independent African Republic of Liberia.
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The Palermo rising erupts in Sicily, against the Bourbon Kingdom of the Two Sicilies.
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 California Gold Rush: James W. Marshall finds gold at Sutter\'s Mill, in Coloma, California.
    • \n
  • January 31\n
      \n
    • Construction of the Washington Monument begins in Washington, D.C.
      • \n
    • John C. Fr\xc3\xa9mont is court-martialed on grounds of mutiny and disobeying orders. The verdict is set aside by United States President James K. Polk, but Fr\xc3\xa9mont retires to California Territory.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 2\n
      \n
    • Mexican\xe2\x80\x93American War formally ends with signing of the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo, ceding virtually all of what becomes the Southwestern United States to the U.S. The unincorporated California Territory becomes a provisional official possession; it is never organized by the United States Congress as a territory, but directly passes the requirements for statehood in 1850.
      • \n
    • John Henry Newman founds the first Oratory in the English-speaking world when he establishes the Birmingham Oratory at \'Maryvale\', Old Oscott, England.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels publish The Communist Manifesto (Manifest der Kommunistischen Partei) in London.
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Fran\xc3\xa7ois Guizot, Prime Minister of France, resigns. 52 people from the Paris mob are killed by soldiers guarding public buildings.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Louis Philippe I, King of the French, abdicates in favour of his grandson, Prince Philippe, Count of Paris, and flees to England after days of revolution in Paris. The French Second Republic is later proclaimed by Alphonse de Lamartine in the name of the provisional government elected by the Chamber under the pressure of the mob.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Louis-Hippolyte Lafontaine and Robert Baldwin became the first Joint Premiers of the Province of Canada to be democratically elected under a system of responsible government.
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Prince Klemens von Metternich gives up office as State Chancellor and Foreign Minister of the Austrian Empire.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Start of the Hungarian Revolution of 1848. The Hungarian young revolutionary intellectuals led by S\xc3\xa1ndor Pet\xc5\x91fi, M\xc3\xb3r J\xc3\xb3kai, etc., called the M\xc3\xa1rciusi Ifjak (Young men of March) organized peaceful mass demonstrations in Pest, forcing the city\'s Habsburg authorities to accept the 12 points: the claims of the Hungarians for freedom and self-determination within the Habsburg Empire. On the same day Lajos Kossuth and representatives of the Diet of Hungary went to Vienna and forced the emperor and Hungarian king Ferdinand I of Austria to accept Hungarian claims for self-determination within the empire.
    • \n
  • March 18 \xe2\x80\x93 In Berlin barricade fighting between revolutionaries and royalist forces marks the culmination of the German revolutions of 1848\xe2\x80\x9349. Hundreds are killed in the clashes but king Frederick William IV of Prussia is forced to honour the dead and appoint a liberal government.
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Republic of San Marco comes into existence in Venice.
    • \n
  • March 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Founding of the New Zealand city of Dunedin, with the arrival of settlers from Scotland on board the John Wickliffe.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Start of the First Schleswig War (German: Schleswig-Holsteinischer Krieg or Three Years\' War (Danish: Tre\xc3\xa5rskrigen)) a military conflict in southern Denmark and northern Germany rooted in the Schleswig-Holstein Question, contesting the issue of who should control the Duchies of Schleswig and Holstein.
    • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 10\n
      \n
    • A Chartist \'Monster Rally\' is held in Kennington Park London, headed by Feargus O\'Connor. A petition demanding the franchise is presented to the Parliament of the United Kingdom.
      • \n
    • The Illinois and Michigan Canal is completed.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Hungarian national government is formed, under the leadership of Lajos Batthy\xc3\xa1ny. The April Laws, the first democratic revolutionary laws in Hungary are promulgated. These laws are the first modern laws in Hungary, which put an end to the feodal privileges of the nobility and to the serfdom, proclaim the freedom of religion, the freedom of the press, the foundation of the Hungarian National Bank, organises the first democratic election in Hungary based in popular representation, national guard, reunion of Transylvania with Hungary, etc. The Habsburg emperor, and Hungarian king Ferdinand I of Austria, ratifies these laws, which form the base of modern Hungary.
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Pius IX publishes an allocution announcing his refusal to support Piedmont-Sardinia in its war with Austria and dispelling hopes that he might serve as ruler of a pan-Italian republic. The allocution, by which Pius is seen to withdraw his moral support for the Italian unification movement, is a key first step in the soon-to-be crushing reaction against the revolutions of 1848.
    • \n
  • May 15\n
      \n
    • Radicals invade the French Chamber of Deputies.
      • \n
    • 40,000 Romanians meet at Blaj to protest Transylvania becoming a part of Hungary.[1]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The first German National Assembly (Nationalversammlung) opens in Frankfurt, Germany.
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo, ending the Mexican\xe2\x80\x93American War, is ratified by the Mexican government (cf. February 2, above).
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Wisconsin is admitted as the 30th U.S. state.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 The Serbians from Vojvodina start a rebellion against the Hungarian government.
    • \n
  • June 2\xe2\x80\x9312 \xe2\x80\x93 Prague Slavic Congress brings together members of the Pan-Slavism movement.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Austrian army bombards Prague and crushes a working class revolt.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Wallachian Revolution of 1848: The Proclamation of Islaz is made public and a Romanian revolutionary government led by Ion Heliade R\xc4\x83dulescu and Christian Tell is created.
    • \n
  • June 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The French government dissolves the national workshops in Paris, giving the workers the choice of joining the army or going to workshops in the provinces.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Public Health Act establishes Boards of Health across England and Wales, the nation\'s first public health law, giving cities broad authority to build modern sanitary systems.[2]
    • \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian national revolutionary parliament starts to work.
    • \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Women\'s rights \xe2\x80\x93 Seneca Falls Convention: The 2-day Women\'s Rights Convention opens in Seneca Falls, New York and the "Bloomers" are introduced at the feminist convention.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Matale Rebellion against British rule in Sri Lanka.
    • \n
  • July 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Young Irelander Rebellion: a nationalist revolt in County Tipperary against British rule is put down by the Irish Constabulary.[2]
    • \n
  • August 6 \xe2\x80\x93 HMS Daedalus report a sighting of a sea serpent.
    • \n
  • August 14 \xe2\x80\x93 American President James K. Polk annexes the Oregon Country and renames it the Oregon Territory as part of the United States.
    • \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Yucat\xc3\xa1n officially unites with Mexico.
    • \n
  • August 19 \xe2\x80\x93 California Gold Rush: The New York Herald breaks the news to the East Coast of the United States that there is a gold rush in California (although the rush started in January).
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The U.S. barque Ocean Monarch is burnt out off the Great Orme, North Wales, with the loss of 178, chiefly emigrants.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Mathieu Luis becomes the first black member to join the French Parliament as a representative of Guadeloupe.
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The Croatian army of Josip Jela\xc4\x8di\xc4\x87, encouraged in secret by the Habsburg government, crosses the Drava river and attacks Hungary, with the goal of ending the revolution in Hungary.
    • \n
  • September 12 \xe2\x80\x93 One of the successes of the Revolutions of 1848, the Swiss Federal Constitution, patterned on the US Constitution, enters into force, creating a federal republic and one of the first modern democratic states in Europe.
    • \n
  • September 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Vermont railroad worker Phineas Gage survives a 3-foot-plus iron rod being driven through his head.
    • \n
  • September 16 \xe2\x80\x93 William Cranch Bond and William Lassell discover Hyperion, Saturn\'s moon.
    • \n
  • September 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian king and Habsburg emperor Ferdinand V refuses to recognise the Hungarian government led by Lajos Batthy\xc3\xa1ny, thus finally showing its real purpose: putting an end to the Hungarian revolution. The Batthy\xc3\xa1ny Government resigns and the National Defence Committee is formed, which is a temporary crisis government, totally independent from Viena, under the leadership of Lajos Kossuth.
    • \n
  • September 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian revolutionary army, led by J\xc3\xa1nos M\xc3\xb3ga defeats at P\xc3\xa1kozd the Croatian army of Josip Jela\xc4\x8di\xc4\x87, forcing him to retreat towards Vienna.
    • \n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 Publication (anonymously) in London of Elizabeth Gaskell\'s first novel, Mary Barton: A Tale of Manchester Life.
    • \n
  • October 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The National Defence Committee (Orsz\xc3\xa1gos Honv\xc3\xa9delmi Bizottm\xc3\xa1ny) led by Lajos Kossuth becomes the executive power in Hungary after the resignation of the Lajos Batthy\xc3\xa1ny government.
    • \n
  • October 3 \xe2\x80\x93 General Antal Puchner, the commander of the Austrian armies of Transylvania, declares insurrection against Hungary, and, together with the Romanian insurgents led by Avram Iancu, attacks and chases away the Hungarian armed forces occupying Transylvania. During these events (mostly in October\xe2\x80\x93January 1848 \xe2\x80\x93 1849, but also between May\xe2\x80\x93July 1849) 7500-8500 of Hungarian civilians (men, women, and children) were massacred by the Romanian insurgents.[3]
    • \n
  • October 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Romanian bands massacre 640 Hungarian civilians at the town of Zlatna in Transylvania.[4]
    • \n
  • October 28 \xe2\x80\x93 In Catalonia, Spain, the Barcelona\xe2\x80\x93Matar\xc3\xb3 railroad route (the first to be constructed in the Iberian Peninsula) is inaugurated.
    • \n
  • October 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Schwechat: Hungarian forces which crossed the Austrian border in order to unite with the Viennese revolutionaries are defeated by the imperial army led by Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Gr\xc3\xa4tz and Josip Jela\xc4\x8di\xc4\x87.
    • \n
  • October 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Vienna is occupied by the imperial forces led by Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Gr\xc3\xa4tz, who crushes the revolution here.
    • \n
  • November 1 \xe2\x80\x93 In Boston, Massachusetts, the first medical school for women, The Boston Female Medical School (which later merges with Boston University School of Medicine), opens.
    • \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93 A greatly revised Dutch constitution is proclaimed.
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 France ratifies a new constitution. The Second Republic of France is set up, ending the state of temporary government lasting since the Revolution of 1848.
    • \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 United States presidential election, 1848: Whig Zachary Taylor of Louisiana defeats Democrat Lewis Cass of Michigan in the first U.S. presidential election held in every state on the same day.
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Emperor Ferdinand I of Austria abdicates in favor of his nephew, Franz Joseph, who will serve as Emperor of Austria and King of Hungary and Bohemia until his death in 1916.
    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The Austrian imperial army led by Franz Schlik attacks Hungary.
    • \n
  • December 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Prince Louis-Napol\xc3\xa9on Bonaparte is elected first president of the French Second Republic.
    • \n
  • December 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The main Austrian imperial forces, led by Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Gr\xc3\xa4tz, cross the Hungarian border.
    • \n
  • December 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Punta Arenas, the first major settlement in the Strait of Magellan, is founded.
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 President Napoleon III takes his oath of office in front of the French National Assembly.
    • \n
  • December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian forces led by J\xc3\xb3zef Bem enter Kolozsv\xc3\xa1r (Cluj) after defeating the Austrian armies in northern Transylvania.
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Slavery is abolished in R\xc3\xa9union. This day is celebrated every year since 1981.
    • \n
  • December 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The imperial army led by Josip Jela\xc4\x8di\xc4\x87 defeats the Hungarian army led by M\xc3\xb3r Perczel in the Battle of M\xc3\xb3r.
    • \n
