1620 (MDCXX) was a leap year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter ED) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Julian calendar, the 1620th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 620th year of the 2nd millennium, the 20th year of the 17th century, and the 1st year of the 1620s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1620 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Prince Bethlen Gabor signs a peace treaty with Ferdinand II, Holy Roman Emperor.
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The first merry-go-round is seen at a fair (Philippapolis, Turkey).
    • \n
  • June 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The oldest stone church in French North America, Notre-Dame-des-Anges, is begun at Quebec City, Canada.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Under the terms of the Treaty of Ulm, the Protestant Union declares neutrality and ceases to support Frederick V of Bohemia.
    • \n
  • July 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The ship Speedwell departs Delfshaven with the Leiden colonists and Pilgrims.
    • \n
  • August 5 (O.S.) \xe2\x80\x93 The Mayflower and Speedwell depart together from Plymouth, England, but the Speedwell starts to leak again and must stop.
    • \n
  • August 7\n
      \n
    • The mother of Johannes Kepler is arrested for witchcraft.
      • \n
    • Battle of Les Ponts-de-C\xc3\xa9, Poitou: French king Louis XIII defeats his mother Marie de\' Medici.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Mysterious rain of frogs in Weil der Stadt.
    • \n
  • August 14 The Battle of the Yellow Ford in Ulster, Ireland saw the Irish forces of Hugh O\'Neill, Earl of Tyrone ambush and annihilate the English forces of Henry Bagenal.
    • \n
  • September 6 (O.S.) \xe2\x80\x93 The Mayflower departs from Plymouth, England, on its 3rd attempt without the Speedwell, arriving on November 11 (Old Style date) at Cape Cod (named from Concord voyage of 1602).
    • \n
  • September 17\xe2\x80\x93October 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Cecora: The Ottoman Empire defeats Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth\xe2\x80\x93Moldavian troops.
    • \n
  • October 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The Roman Catholic Ras Sela Kristos, half-brother of the Emperor Susenyos crushes a group of rebels in at Mount Amedamit in Gojjam, Ethiopia, who were opposed to Susenyos\' pro-Catholic beliefs.
    • \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Patent is granted to Plymouth Colony.
    • \n
  • November 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of White Mountain: Catholic forces are victorious in only two hours near Prague.
    • \n
  • November 21 (November 11 O.S.) \xe2\x80\x93 The Mayflower arrives inside the tip of Cape Cod at what becomes known as Provincetown Harbor, with the Pilgrims and Planters. 41 Plymouth Colony settlers sign the Mayflower Compact, the first governing document of the colony, onboard the ship.
    • \n
  • November 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Wedding of Gustav II Adolf and Maria Eleonora
    • \n
  • December 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Plymouth Colony: William Bradford and the Mayflower Pilgrims land on what becomes known as Plymouth Rock in Plymouth, Massachusetts.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • A severe frost in England, with the Thames frozen. 13 continuous days of snow in Scotland. On Eskdale Moor only 35 of a flock of 20,000 sheep survive.[1]
    • \n
  • Francis Bacon publishes the Novum Organum (beyond Aristotle\'s Organon) on logical thinking.
    • \n
  • Two officers of the British East India Company attempt to claim the Table Mountain region (in present-day South Africa) for England, but fail.
    • \n
  • Shogun Tokugawa Hidetada restores Osaka Castle. Its current appearance dates from this remodeling.
    • \n
  • The modern violin is developed.
    • \n
  • Witch hunts begin in Scotland.
    • \n
  • Cornelis Drebbel, at the Thames, builds an undersea boat (history of submarines).
    • \n
  • Juan Pablo Bonet, teacher of deaf children in the Spanish court, creates the sign alphabet.
    • \n
  • Thirty Years\' War (1618\xe2\x80\x931648) continues (principally on the territory of today\'s Germany).
    • \n
  • A Short Account of the Destruction of the Indies by Bartolom\xc3\xa9 de las Casas and Origin and progress of the disturbances in the Netherlands by Johannes Gysius was re-published in the Netherlands.[2]
    • \n
