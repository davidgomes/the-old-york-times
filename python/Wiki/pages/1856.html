1856 (MDCCCLVI) was a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Sunday (dominical letter AG) of the Julian calendar, the 1856th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 856th year of the 2nd millennium, the 56th year of the 19th century, and the 7th year of the 1850s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1856 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 Xhosa cattle-killing movement and famine begins in Cape Colony.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Nelson College is founded in Nelson, New Zealand.
    • \n
  • April 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Paris Declaration Respecting Maritime Law abolishes privateering and regulates the relationship between neutral and belligerent and shipping on the high seas.
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Chicago Historical Society Museum is established at 1601 N. Clark Street, Chicago.
    • \n
  • April 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Building workers agitate for the eight-hour day in Melbourne, Australia.
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The iron-hulled paddle steamer RMS Persia concludes a 9-day 16 hour westbound transatlantic crossing at an average 13.11 knots (24.28 km/h), regaining the Blue Riband for the Cunard Line.
    • \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The province of Isabela is created in the Philippines in honor of Queen Isabella II of Spain.
    • \n
  • May 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Queen Victoria of the United Kingdom gives Norfolk Island to the population of the colony at Pitcairn Island, most being descendants of the Mutiny on the Bounty. They first settle on Norfolk Island on June 8.
    • \n
  • May 14 \xe2\x80\x93 A San Francisco Committee of Vigilance is founded in the United States. It lynches two gangsters, arrests most Democratic Party officials and disbands itself on August 18.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 David Livingstone arrives at Quelimane on the Indian Ocean having completed a 2-year transcontinental journey across Africa from Luanda.[3]
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Lawrence, Kansas is captured and burned by pro-slavery forces (the "Sacking of Lawrence").
    • \n
  • May 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Congressman Preston Brooks of South Carolina beats Senator Charles Sumner with a cane in the hall of the United States Senate, for a speech Sumner had made attacking Southerners who sympathized with the pro-slavery violence in Kansas ("Bleeding Kansas"). Sumner is unable to return to duty for 3 years while he recovers; Brooks becomes a hero across the South.
    • \n
  • May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Pottawatomie massacre: A group of followers of radical abolitionist John Brown kill 5 homesteaders in Franklin County, Kansas.
    • \n
  • June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Black Jack: Antislavery forces, led by John Brown, defeat proslavery forces in Bleeding Kansas.
    • \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 500 Mormon handcart pioneers leave Iowa City and head west for Salt Lake City, Utah, carrying all their possessions in two-wheeled handcarts.
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: Shi Dakai arrives at Nanjing.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Natal becomes a Crown colony.[4]
    • \n
  • July 14\xe2\x80\x9315 \xe2\x80\x93 In Spain, General Leopoldo O\'Donnell takes control of the government, bringing an end to the bienio progresista.
    • \n
  • July 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Train Wreck (the worst railroad calamity in the world to date) occurs near Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA.
    • \n
  • July 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Christchurch, New Zealand is chartered as a city.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Last Island hurricane destroys Last Island, Louisiana, leaving 400 dead. The whole island is broken up into several smaller islands by the storm.
    • \n
  • August 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Osawatomie: Proslavery forces defeat antislavery forces in Bleeding Kansas.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Seton Hall University is founded by Archdiocese of Newark Bishop James Roosevelt Bayley, a cousin of U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt and nephew of Saint Elizabeth Ann Seton.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: Wei Changhui and Qin Rigang assassinate Yang Xiuqing.
    • \n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Second Opium War between several western powers and China begins with the Arrow Incident on the Pearl River.
    • \n
  • November 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-Persian War: War is declared between Great Britain and Persia.
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 United States presidential election, 1856: Democrat James Buchanan defeats former President Millard Fillmore, representing a coalition of "Know-Nothings" and Whigs, and John C. Fr\xc3\xa9mont of the fledgling Republican Party, to become the 15th President of the United States.
    • \n
  • November 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: Shi Dakai arrives at the Heavenly Capital once more with 100,000 men and demands that Wei Changhui and Qin Rigang be executed. Shi subsequently becomes head of the government.
    • \n
  • November 17 \xe2\x80\x93 American Old West: On the Sonoita River in present-day southern Arizona, the United States Army establishes Fort Buchanan in order to help control new land acquired in the Gadsden Purchase.
    • \n
  • November 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Niagara University is founded in Niagara Falls, New York.
    • \n
  • November 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Coup of 1856 leads to Luxembourg\'s unilateral adoption of a new, reactionary constitution, as King-Grand Duke William III signs the new constitution without the Chamber of Deputies\' consent.
    • \n
  • December 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Under the County and Borough Police Act, in any county or area of England and Wales where a police force has not already been established, the Justices of the Peace must from this date take steps to create one according to nationally defined standards.[5]
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 National Portrait Gallery, London, established.[6]
    • \n
  • December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Bushehr surrenders to the British.
    • \n
