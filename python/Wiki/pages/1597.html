1597 (MDXCVII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1597th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 597th year of the 2nd millennium, the 97th year of the 16th century, and the 8th year of the 1590s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1597 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Turnhout: Maurice of Nassau defeats a Spanish force under Jean de Rie of Varas in the Netherlands.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 In Nagasaki, Japan, 26 people are martyred. They practiced Catholicism and were taken captive after all forms of Christianity were outlawed the previous year.
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Sir Anthony Shirley, England\'s "best-educated pirate", raids Jamaica.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Amiens is taken by Spanish forces, but retaken by France on September 25.
    • \n
  • March 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho Brahe\'s stipend is stopped.
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho Brahe leaves Ven and moves to Copenhagen (Farverg\xc3\xa5rden).
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Christian Friis and Axel Brahe go to Ven to check complaints, and a commission is established to investigate Tycho Brahe\'s leadership.
    • \n
  • April 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The vicar on Ven is dismissed: he had followed Tycho\'s orders not to perform an exorcism.
    • \n
  • April 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Probable first performance of William Shakespeare\'s The Merry Wives of Windsor.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Johannes Kepler marries Barbara Muhleck.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho Brahe is not allowed to make observations from The Watermill Tower, and he is not allowed to perform chemical experiments at his house in Farvergade.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho writes a recommendation supporting Longomontanus, who is planning to study in Germany.
    • \n
  • June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho leaves Copenhagen and goes to Rostock.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho is removed from his job working at Epiphany Chapel in Roskilde.
    • \n
  • June 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho flees and becomes a servant of the king of Spain.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 The Isle of Dogs (a play now lost) is written by Thomas Nashe and Ben Jonson, and performed at the Swan Theatre.
    • \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho Brahe\'s famous letter to the king Christian IV is sent from Rostock.
    • \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Scottish poet Alexander Montgomerie is declared an outlaw after the collapse of a Catholic plot.
    • \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Beginning of the Siege of Namwon.
    • \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Islands Voyage: Robert Devereux, 2nd Earl of Essex, and Sir Walter Raleigh set sail on an expedition to the Azores.
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Christian IV of Denmark refuses to let Tycho Brahe return to Denmark.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Chilcheollyang: The Japanese fleet defeats the Koreans. It is the only Japanese naval victory in the Imjin War.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho Brahe leaves Rostock, where plague is spreading, and travels to Wandsb\xc3\xa6k.
    • \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 John Gerard, a Jesuit priest, escapes from the Tower of London.
    • \n
  • October 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho starts new observations in Wandsb\xc3\xa6k, where he writes his famous elegy.
    • \n
  • October 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Myeongnyang: The Koreans, commanded by Yi Sunsin, are victorious over the Japanese.
    • \n
  • December 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Johannes Kepler writes a letter to Tycho about his book, Mysterium Cosmographicum.
    • \n
  • December 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Tycho writes his preface to the Emperor Rudolf II in his book, Mecanica.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Jacopo Peri writes Dafne, now recognised as the first opera.
    • \n
  • Bali is discovered by the Dutch explorer Cornelis Houtman.
    • \n
  • Abbas I ends the Uzbek raids on his lands.
    • \n
  • Yaqob succeeds his father Sarsa Dengel as Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • The first edition of Francis Bacon\'s Essays is published.
    • \n
  • Andreas Libavius publishes Alchemia, a pioneering chemistry textbook.[1]
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nAndreas Libavius\'s Alchemia, an early chemistry text, published 1597.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • 12 million pesos of silver cross the Pacific. Although it is unknown just how much silver flowed from the Spanish base of Manila in the Philippines to the Ming Dynasty of China, it is known that the main port for the Mexican silver trade\xe2\x80\x94Acapulco\xe2\x80\x94shipped out 150,000 to 345,000 kg (4 to 9 million taels) of silver annually from this year to 1602.
    • \n
  • A Dutch Expedition commanded by Frederick de Houtman returns to Amsterdam after having successfully reached the Spice Islands. This achievement opens the Spice trade, which had until then been monopolised by the Portuguese, to the Dutch, who in the next years launch several more expeditions to the Indies.
    • \n
  • Tobias Hess corresponds with Simon Studion and agrees with him that the Papacy must fall in 1604.
    • \n
\n
    \n
  • Serb Uprising of 1596\xe2\x80\x9397
