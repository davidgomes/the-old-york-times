1956 (MCMLVI) was a leap year starting on Sunday (dominical letter AG) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1956th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 956th year of the 2nd millennium, the 56th year of the 20th century, and the 7th year of the 1950s decade.

    • \n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 British spies Guy Burgess and Donald Maclean resurface in the Soviet Union after being missing for 5 years.
    • \n
  • February 14\xe2\x80\x9326 \xe2\x80\x93 20th Congress of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Only a little more than four months after the release of the 70mm version of Oklahoma!, the film version of Rodgers and Hammerstein\'s Carousel, starring Gordon MacRae and Shirley Jones, is released in CinemaScope 55. MacRae and Jones had previously starred in Oklahoma! Carousel, intended for showing in 55mm, ends up being shown only in 35mm.

  • February 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Elvis Presley enters the United States music charts for the first time, with "Heartbreak Hotel".
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Norma Jean Mortenson legally changes her name to Marilyn Monroe.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Doris Day records her most famous song: Que Sera, Sera (Whatever Will Be, Will Be). It is from Alfred Hitchcock\'s The Man Who Knew Too Much, in which Day co-stars with James Stewart.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Nikita Khrushchev attacks the veneration of Joseph Stalin as a "cult of personality".
    • \n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The International Air Transport Association finalizes a draft of the radiotelephony spelling alphabet for the International Civil Aviation Organization.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Morocco declares its independence from France.
    • \n
  • March 9\n
      \n
    • The British deport Archbishop Makarios from Cyprus to the Seychelles.
      • \n
    • Soviet Armed Forces suppresses mass demonstrations in the Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic, reacting to Nikita Khrushchev\'s de-Stalinization policy.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 10 - The Fairey Delta 2 broke the World Air Speed Record, raising it to 1,132 mph (1,811 km/h) or Mach 1.73, an increase of some 300 mph (480 km/h) over the previous record, and thus becoming the first aircraft to exceed 1,000 mph (1,600 km/h) in level flight.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 After having opened in London the previous year, Laurence Olivier\'s film, Richard III, adapted from Shakespeare\'s play, has its U.S. premiere in theatres and on NBC Television on the same day. On TV it is not shown in prime time, but as an afternoon matin\xc3\xa9e, in a slightly cut version. It is one of the first such experiments of its kind. Olivier is later nominated for an Oscar for his performance.
    • \n
  • March 12\n
      \n
    • 96 U.S. Congressmen sign the Southern Manifesto, a protest against the 1954 Supreme Court ruling (Brown v. Board of Education) that desegregated public education.
      • \n
    • The Dow Jones Industrial Average closes above 500 for the first time rising 2.40 points, or 0.48%, to 500.24.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Elvis Presley releases his first gold album titled Elvis Presley.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The Broadway musical My Fair Lady opens in New York City.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 At age 48, Dutch boxer Bep van Klaveren contests his last match in Rotterdam.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Tunisia gains independence from France.
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The 28th Academy Awards ceremony is held.
    • \n
  • March 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Pakistan becomes the first Islamic republic, and a national holiday is observed in the country including the former East Pakistan state.

June[edit]

\n
    \n

    • \n
  • June 13\n
      \n
    • International Criminal Police Organization (ICPO).

    \n
    • \n

      \n
    • MP Sydney Silverman\'s bill for the abolition of the death penalty in the UK passes the British House of Commons.

It is also one of two Rodgers and Hammerstein films to be nominated for Best Picture (which it does not win).

      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 30 \xe2\x80\x93 A TWA Lockheed Constellation and United Airlines Douglas DC-7 collide in mid-air over the Grand Canyon in Arizona, killing all 128 people aboard both aircraft in the deadliest civil aviation disaster to date; the accident leads to sweeping changes in the regulation of cross-country flight and air traffic control over the United States.

    • \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x93 A joint resolution of Congress is signed by President Dwight D. Eisenhower, authorizing "In God we trust" as the U.S. national motto.
    • \n
  • July 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Cricket: Jim Laker sets an extraordinary record at Old Trafford in the fourth Test between England and Australia, taking 19 wickets in a first class match (the previous best was 17).
