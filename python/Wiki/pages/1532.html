Year 1532 (MDXXXII) was a leap year starting on Monday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 18 \xe2\x80\x93 English parliament bans payment by English church to Rome
    • \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Quipaipan in Peru: Atahualpa wins the civil war in the Inca Empire, defeating his brother Hu\xc3\xa1scar.
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Francisco Pizarro lands on the northern coast of Peru.
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Sir Thomas More resigns as Lord Chancellor of England.
    • \n
  • June 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Suleiman the Magnificent leads another invasion of Hungary.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Union of Brittany and France: The Duchy of Brittany is absorbed into the Kingdom of France.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Lady Anne Boleyn is created Marquess of Pembroke by her fianc\xc3\xa9, King Henry VIII of England.
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Francisco Pizarro and his men capture Inca Atahualpa at Cajamarca, ambushing and slaughtering a large number of his followers without loss to themselves. He subsequently offers a ransom of approx. $100 million in gold.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Prince is published five years after death of the author Niccol\xc3\xb2 Machiavelli.
    • \n
  • Pantagruel is published by Fran\xc3\xa7ois Rabelais.
    • \n
  • Henry VIII of England grants the Thorne brothers a Royal Charter to found Bristol Grammar School.
    • \n
  • Stamford School founded by William Radcliffe.
    • \n
  • The Paris Parlement has the city\'s beggars arrested \'to force them to work in the sewers, chained together in pairs\'.[1]
    • \n
\n
