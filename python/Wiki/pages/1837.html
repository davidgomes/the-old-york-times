\n1837 (MDCCCXXXVII) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1837th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 837th year of the 2nd millennium, the 37th year of the 19th century, and the 8th year of the 1830s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1837 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Galilee earthquake.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Michigan becomes the 26th state admitted to the United States.
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 Charles Dickens\'s Oliver Twist begins publication in serial form in London.
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Seminoles attack Fort Foster in Florida.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 In Philadelphia, the Institute for Colored Youth (ICY) is founded as the first institution for the higher education of black people in the United States.
    • \n
  • March 4\n
      \n
    • Martin Van Buren succeeds Andrew Jackson as President of the United States.
      • \n
    • The city of Chicago is incorporated.
      • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJune 20: Queen Victoria, Queen of the United Kingdom (1837\xe2\x80\x931901).
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April 12- The conglomerate of Procter & Gamble has its origins when British-born businessmen William Procter and James Gamble begin selling their first manufactured goods (soap and candles) in Cincinnati, Ohio.[1]
    • \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 W. F. Cooke and Charles Wheatstone patented a system of electrical telegraph.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Panic of 1837 begins in New York City.
    • \n
  • June 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Houston, is incorporated by the Republic of Texas.
    • \n
  • June 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The Broad Street Riot occurs in Boston, Massachusetts, fueled by ethnic tensions between the Irish and the Yankees.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 18-year-old Queen Victoria accedes to the throne of the United Kingdom on the death of her uncle William IV without legitimate heirs. She will reign for more than 63 years.[2] Under Salic law, the Kingdom of Hanover passes to William\'s brother, Ernest Augustus, Duke of Cumberland, ending the personal union of Britain and Hanover which has persisted since 1714.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Charles W. King sets sail on the American merchant ship Morrison. In the Morrison incident, he is turned away from Japanese ports with cannon fire.
    • \n
  • July 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Queen Victoria moves from Kensington Palace into Buckingham Palace, the first reigning British monarch to make this, rather than St James\'s Palace, her London home.[3]
    • \n
  • August 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Dutch sack the fortress of Bonjol, ending the Padri War.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Aranzueque: Liberal victory for the forces loyal to Queen Isabel II of Spain, end of the Carlist campaign known as the Expedici\xc3\xb3n Real \xe2\x80\x93 The First Carlist War.
    • \n
  • September 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Samuel Morse files a caveat for a patent for the telegraph.[4]
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 10 - October 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The French army besieges and captures Constantine in French Algeria.
    • \n
  • October 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry David Thoreau makes his first journal entry at the suggestion of Ralph Waldo Emerson.
    • \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 American abolitionist and newspaper editor Elijah Lovejoy is killed by a pro-slavery mob, at his warehouse in Alton, Illinois.
    • \n
  • November 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Mount Holyoke Female Seminary, later Mount Holyoke College, is founded in South Hadley, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • November\xe2\x80\x93December \xe2\x80\x93 In the Canadas, William Lyon Mackenzie leads the Upper Canada Rebellion and Louis-Joseph Papineau leads the Lower Canada Rebellion.
    • \n
  • December 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Fire in the Winter Palace, Saint Petersburg.
    • \n
  • December 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Caroline affair on the Niagara River, the basis for the Caroline test for anticipatory self-defence in international relations.
    • \n
