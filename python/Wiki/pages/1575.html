Year 1575 (MDLXXV) was a common year starting on Saturday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Queen Elizabeth I of England grants a monopoly on producing printed sheet music to Thomas Tallis and William Byrd.[1]
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 William I of Orange founds Leiden University.
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry III of France is crowned at Reims.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry III of France marries Louise de Lorraine-Vaud\xc3\xa9mont.
    • \n
  • June 24 \xe2\x80\x93 William I of Orange marries Charlotte of Bourbon.
    • \n
  • June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Nagashino: Oda Nobunaga defeats Takeda Katsuyori in Japan\'s first \'modern\' battle.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Raid of the Redeswire: Sir John Carmichael defeats Sir John Forster. Last battle between England and Scotland.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Edmund Grindal succeeds Matthew Parker as Archbishop of Canterbury.
    • \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Sidney is appointed Lord Lieutenant of Ireland.
    • \n
  • October 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Dormans: Catholic forces under Henry I, Duke of Guise defeat the Protestants, capturing Philippe de Mornay among others.
    • \n
  • October 22 \xe2\x80\x93 City of "Villa de la Asunsion" (today Aguascalientes (Aguascalientes) is founded in New Spain by permission from Philip II of Spain.
    • \n
  • December 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Stephen B\xc3\xa1thory becomes king of Poland.
    • \n
  • December 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Valdivia earthquake.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Russians occupy Pernau in Western Estonia and the fortress of Weissenstein.
    • \n
  • Portugal founds the city of Luanda, Angola.
    • \n
  • Seat of the Audiencia Real in Chile moves from Concepci\xc3\xb3n to Santiago.
    • \n
  • Abraham Ortelius becomes geographer to Philip II of Spain.
    • \n
  • The bubonic plague decimates Venice.
    • \n
  • Gaspar da Cruz, a Portuguese Dominican friar, writes about his travels to the Ming Dynasty of China, including the Chinese civil service handbook The Bureaucratic System of the Ming Dynasty, and how the Chinese draw lots to determine which days of the year are most auspicious or most ill-fated to travel upon.
    • \n
\n
    \n
  • Captains of vessels flying the Spanish flag are legally required to maintain a logbook.[2]
    • \n
\n
