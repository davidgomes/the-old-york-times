\n1841 (MDCCCXLI) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Julian calendar, the 1841st year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 841st year of the 2nd millennium, the 41st year of the 19th century, and the 2nd year of the 1840s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1841 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles Elliot of the United Kingdom and Qishan of the Qing dynasty agree to the Convention of Chuenpee.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Britain occupies Hong Kong. Later in the year, the first census of the island records a population of about 7,500.[1]
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The active volcano Mount Erebus in Antarctica is discovered and named by James Clark Ross.[2]
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Ross discovers the "Victoria Barrier", later known as the Ross Ice Shelf. On the same voyage, he discovers the Ross Sea, Victoria Land and Mount Terror.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 A fire ruins and destroys two-thirds of the villa (modern-day city) of Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico.
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 First known reference to Groundhog Day in North America, in the diary of a James Morris.
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Act of Union (British North America Act, 1840) proclaimed in Canada.
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The two colonies of The Canadas are merged into the United Province of Canada.
    • \n
  • February 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The first ongoing filibuster in the United States Senate begins and lasts until March 11.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Governor Fenner, carrying emigrants to the United States, sinks off Holyhead (Wales) with the loss of 123 lives.
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 El Salvador proclaims itself an independent republic, bringing an end to the (already de facto defunct) Federal Republic of Central America.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Martin Van Buren is succeeded as President of the United States by William Henry Harrison.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 United States v. The Amistad: The Supreme Court of the United States rules in the case that the Africans who seized control of the ship had been taken into slavery illegally.
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 SS President under the command of the legendary captain Richard Roberts ("I\'d Go to Sea in a Bathtub") founders in rough seas with all passengers and crew lost.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 President William Henry Harrison dies of pneumonia, becoming the first President of the United States to die in office and at one month, the American president with the shortest term served. He is succeeded by Vice President John Tyler, who becomes the tenth President of the United States.
    • \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 President John Tyler is sworn in.
    • \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 Start of the Sino-Sikh War.
    • \n
  • May 3 \xe2\x80\x93 New Zealand becomes a British colony.[3]
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Lt. Charles Wilkes lands at Fort Nisqually in Puget Sound.
    • \n
  • May 22 \xe2\x80\x93 1841 rebellion in Guria: The Georgian province of Guria revolts against the Russian Empire.
    • \n
  • June 6 (Sunday)\n
      \n
    • United Kingdom Census held, the first to record names and approximate ages of every household member and to be administered nationally.
      • \n
    • Marian Hughes becomes the first woman to take religious vows in communion with the Anglican Province of Canterbury since the Reformation, making them privately to E. B. Pusey in Oxford.[4]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Fordham University is founded in The Bronx by the Society of Jesus. Its name at its founding is St. John\'s College.
    • \n
  • June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Ballet Giselle first presented by the Ballet du Th\xc3\xa9\xc3\xa2tre de l\'Acad\xc3\xa9mie Royale de Musique at the Salle Le Peletier in Paris, France.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJuly 18: Coronation of Emperor Pedro II of Brazil
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Scottish missionary David Livingstone arrives at Kuruman in the Northern Cape, his first posting in Africa.
    • \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Cook arranges his first railway excursion, in England.[3]
    • \n
  • July 17 \xe2\x80\x93 First edition of the humorous magazine Punch published in London.[5]
    • \n
  • July 18 (Sunday)\n
      \n
    • Coronation ceremony of Emperor Pedro II of Brazil in Rio de Janeiro.
      • \n
    • The sixth bishop of Calcutta, Daniel Wilson, and Dr. James Taylor, Civil Surgeon at Dhaka, establish the first modern educational institution in the Indian subcontinent, Dhaka College.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • July 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Mercantile Agency (ancestor of Dun & Bradstreet) is founded in New York City by Lewis Tappan.
    • \n
  • August 11 (Wednesday) \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick Douglass speaks in front of the Anti-Slavery Convention in Nantucket, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • August 16 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. President John Tyler vetoes a bill which called for the re-establishment of the Second Bank of the United States. Enraged Whig Party members riot outside the White House in the most violent demonstration on White House grounds in U.S. history.
    • \n
  • August 20\xe2\x80\x93October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Niger expedition of 1841 up the Niger River by paddle steamers under the auspices of the British Society for the Extinction of the Slave Trade and the Civilisation of Africa; largely abortive due to the high incidence of disease among the crews.
    • \n
  • September 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Britain annexes Sarawak from Brunei; James Brooke is appointed rajah.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Queen\'s University is founded in Kingston, Ontario, by Rev. Thomas Liddell, who carries a Royal Charter from Queen Victoria and becomes the school\'s first principal.
    • \n
  • October 30 \xe2\x80\x93 A fire at the Tower of London destroys its Grand Armoury and causes a quarter of a million pounds worth of damage.[6]
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Dallas, Texas is founded by John Neely Bryan.[7]
    • \n
  • November 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Scottish surgeon James Braid first sees a demonstration of animal magnetism by Charles Lafontaine in Manchester, which leads to his study of the phenomenon that he (Braid) eventually calls hypnotism.
    • \n
  • December 23 \xe2\x80\x93 First Anglo-Afghan War: At a meeting with the Afghan general Akbar Khan, the British diplomat Sir William Hay Macnaghten is shot dead at close quarters.
    • \n
