Year 1563 (MDLXIII) was a common year starting on Friday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Sarsa Dengel succeeds his father Menas as Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • February 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Francis, Duke of Guise, is assassinated while besieging Orl\xc3\xa9ans.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Edict of Amboise signed at the Ch\xc3\xa2teau d\'Amboise by Catherine de\' Medici, acting as regent for her son Charles IX of France, having been negotiated between the Huguenot Louis, Prince of Cond\xc3\xa9, and Anne, duc de Montmorency, Constable of France. It accords some toleration to the Huguenots, especially to aristocrats. It officially ends the first phase of the French Wars of Religion and the combined Huguenot and royal armies then march north to besiege the English in Le Havre.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 At Bornholm, the Danish fleet fires on the Swedish navy, leading to a Danish defeat and precipitating the Northern Seven Years\' War.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The English surrender Le Havre to the French after a siege.
    • \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Northern Seven Years\' War: War against the Kingdom of Sweden is declared by Denmark\xe2\x80\x93Norway and the Free City of L\xc3\xbcbeck.
    • \n
  • September 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Northern Seven Years\' War: King Frederick II of Denmark, advancing from Halland, takes \xc3\x84lvsborg fortress from Sweden.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Council of Trent (opened December 13, 1545) officially closes. It reaffirms all major Roman Catholic doctrines, and declares the Deuterocanonical books of the Old Testament to be canonical, along with the rest of the Bible. Chapter 1, Session 24, promulgates the decree Tametsi, stipulating that for a marriage to be valid, consent (the essence of marriage) as expressed in the vows has to be given publicly before witnesses, one of whom has to be the parish priest.
    • \n
