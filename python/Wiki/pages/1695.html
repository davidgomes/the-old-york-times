1695 (MDCXCV) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1695th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 695th year of the 2nd millennium, the 95th year of the 17th century, and the 6th year of the 1690s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1695 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929. It was also a particularly cold and wet year. Contemporary records claim that wine froze in the glasses in the Palace of Versailles.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Mustafa II (1695\xe2\x80\x931703) succeeds Ahmed II as Ottoman Emperor.
    • \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 The Parliament of England decides not to renew the Licensing Order of 1643 requiring press censorship.[1]
    • \n
  • June 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Commission of Enquiry into the Massacre of Glencoe in Scotland in 1692 reports to the Parliament of England, blaming Sir John Dalrymple, Secretary of State over Scotland, and declares that a soldier should refuse to obey a "command against the law of nature".
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Bank of Scotland is founded by an Act of the Parliament of Scotland.
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Wren Building is started in Williamsburg, Virginia (completed in 1700).
    • \n
  • August 13\xe2\x80\x9315 \xe2\x80\x93 Nine Years\' War: Bombardment of Brussels by French troops.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Nine Years\' War: France surrenders Namur in the Spanish Netherlands to forces of the Grand Alliance led by King William III of England following the 2-month Siege of Namur.[2]
    • \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 English pirate Henry Every perpetrates one of the most profitable raids in history with the capture of the Grand Mughal ship Ganj-i-Sawai. In response, Emperor Aurangzeb threatens to put an end to all English trading in India.
    • \n
  • December 31 \xe2\x80\x93 A window tax is imposed in England.[2] Some windows are bricked up to avoid it.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Russia declares war on Turkey.
    • \n
  • English manufacturers call for an embargo on Indian cloth and silk weavers picket the House of Commons of England.
    • \n
  • A \xc2\xa32 fine is imposed for swearing in England.
    • \n
  • After 23 years of construction, Spain completes Castillo de San Marcos to protect St. Augustine, Florida, from foreign threats.
    • \n
  • After many years of construction, the Potala Palace in Lhasa is completed.
    • \n
  • Gold is discovered in Brazil.
    • \n
  • In Amsterdam, the bank Wed. Jean Deutz & Sn. floats the first sovereign bonds on the local market. The scheme is designed to fund a 1.5 million guilder loan to the Holy Roman Emperor. From this date on, European leaders commonly take advantage of the low interest rates available in the Dutch Republic and borrow several hundred millions on the Dutch capital market.[3]
    • \n
