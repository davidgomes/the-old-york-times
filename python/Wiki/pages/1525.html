Year 1525 (MDXXV) was a common year starting on Sunday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Swiss Anabaptist Movement is born when Conrad Grebel, Felix Manz, George Blaurock, and about a dozen others baptize each other in the home of Manz\'s mother on Neustadt-Gasse, Z\xc3\xbcrich, breaking a thousand-year tradition of church-state union.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Pavia: Spanish forces under Charles de Lannoy and the Marquis of Pescara defeat the French army and capture Francis I of France, after his horse is wounded by Cesare Hercolani.
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The last Aztec Emperor, Cuauht\xc3\xa9moc, is killed by Hern\xc3\xa1n Cort\xc3\xa9z.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 In the German town Memmingen the pamphlet The Twelve Articles: The Just and Fundamental Articles of All the Peasantry and Tenants of Spiritual and Temporal Powers by Whom They Think Themselves Oppressed is published , the first human rights related document written in Europe.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Albert of Prussia commits Prussian Homage.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Insurgent peasants led by radical pastor Thomas Muentzer were defeated at the Battle of Frankenhausen, ending the German Peasants\' War in the Holy Roman Empire.
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Martin Luther marries ex-nun Katharina von Bora. The painter Lucas Cranach the Elder is one of the witnesses.
    • \n
  • June 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry VIII of England appoints his illegitimate son Henry Fitzroy Duke of Richmond and Somerset.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Santa Marta, the first city in Colombia, is founded by Spanish conqueror Rodrigo de Bastidas.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • European-brought diseases sweep through the Andes, killing thousands, including the Inca.
    • \n
  • Mixco Viejo, capital of the Pocomam Maya State, falls to the Spanish Conquistadores of Pedro de Alvarado in what is now Guatemala after a 3-month siege.
    • \n
  • The Bubonic Plague spreads in southern France.
    • \n
  • The New Testament is translated into English by William Tyndale.
    • \n
  • The first French ambassador arrives in Istanbul.
    • \n
  • The Chinese Ministry of War under the Ming Dynasty orders ships having more than one mast sailing along the southeast coast to be seized, investigated, and destroyed; this in an effort to curb piracy and limit private commercial trade abroad.
    • \n
  • Over 75,000 peasants are killed in Germany.
    • \n
  • Francis I of France and Suleiman I strike an alliance against Habsburg Empire.
    • \n
  • Age of Samael ends and the Age of Gabriel starts according to Johannes Trithemius.
    • \n
  • The word "spatula" is first used in English.
    • \n
