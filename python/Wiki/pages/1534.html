Year 1534 (MDXXXIV) was a common year starting on Thursday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Parliament of England passes the Act Respecting the Oath to the Succession recognising the marriage of Henry VIII and Anne Boleyn, and their children as the legitimate heirs to the throne.[1]
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 A group of Anabaptists, led by Jan Matthys, seize M\xc3\xbcnster in Westphalia and declare it "The New Jerusalem", begin to exile dissenters and forcibly baptize all others.
    • \n
  • April 5 (Easter Sunday) \xe2\x80\x93 Anabaptist Jan Matthys is killed by the Landsknechte, who lay siege to M\xc3\xbcnster on the day he predicted as The Second Coming of Christ. His follower John of Leiden takes control of the city.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Sir Thomas More confined in the Tower of London
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Jacques Cartier explores Newfoundland while searching for the Northwest Passage.
    • \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Jacques Cartier is the first European to discover the Saint Lawrence River.
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Copenhagen opens its gates to Count Christopher of Oldenburg leading the army of L\xc3\xbcbeck (and the Hanseatic League), nominally in the interests of the deposed King Christian II of Denmark. The surrenders of Copenhagen and, a few days later, of Malm\xc3\xb6 represent the high point of the Count\'s War for the forces of the League. These victories presumably lead the Danish nobility to recognize Christian III as King on July 4.[2][3]
    • \n
  • June 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Jacques Cartier discovers Prince Edward Island, Canada.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Election of Christian III as King of Denmark and Norway in the town of Rye.
    • \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The first known exchange occurs between Europeans and natives of the Gulf of St. Lawrence, in New Brunswick.
    • \n
  • August 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Ignatius of Loyola and six others take the vows that lead to the establishment of the Society of Jesus in Montmartre (Paris).
    • \n
  • August 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Piero de Ponte becomes the 45th Grandmaster of the Knights Hospitaller.
    • \n
  • October 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Paul III succeeds Pope Clement VII as the 220th pope.
    • \n
  • October 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Huguenots post placards all over France attacking the Catholic Mass, provoking a violent sectarian reaction.
    • \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93December 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The English Reformation Parliament passes the Act of Supremacy establishing Henry VIII as supreme head of the Church of England.[1]
    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Over 200 Spanish settlers led by conquistador Sebasti\xc3\xa1n de Belalc\xc3\xa1zar found what is now Quito, Ecuador.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Act for the Submission of the Clergy confirmed by the Parliament of England, requiring churchmen to submit to the king and forbidding the publication of ecclesiastical laws without royal permission.
    • \n
  • Manco Inca Yupanqui is crowned as Sapa Inca in Cusco, Peru by Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro in succession to his brother T\xc3\xbapac Huallpa (d. October 1533).
    • \n
  • Cambridge University Press is given a Royal Charter by Henry VIII of England and becomes the first of the privileged presses.
    • \n
  • Gargantua is published by Fran\xc3\xa7ois Rabelais.
    • \n
  • Martin Luther\'s translation of the complete Christian Bible into German is printed by Hans Lufft in Wittenberg, adding the Old Testament and Apocrypha to Luther\'s 1522 translation of the New Testament and including woodcut illustrations.
    • \n
  • First book printed in Yiddish (in Krak\xc3\xb3w), Mirkevet ha-Mishneh, a Tanakh concordance by rabbi Asher Anchel, translating difficult phrases in biblical Hebrew.[4]
    • \n
