\n1783 (MDCCLXXXIII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1783rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 783rd year of the 2nd millennium, the 83rd year of the 18th century, and the 4th year of the 1780s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1783 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nThe first manned hot-air balloon, designed by the Montgolfier brothers, takes off from the Bois de Boulogne, on November 21, 1783
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: The Kingdom of Great Britain acknowledges the independence of the United States of America. At this time the Spanish government does not grant diplomatic recognition.
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Great Britain formally declares that it will cease hostilities with the United States.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 1783 Calabrian earthquakes: First of a sequence of five earthquakes in Calabria, Italy (February 5\xe2\x80\x937, March 1 & 28), leaving 50,000 dead.
    • \n
  • February 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Continental Army\'s Corps of Engineers is disbanded.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Last celebration of Massacre Day in Boston, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Newburgh Conspiracy: A potential uprising in the Continental Army stationed at Newburgh, New York, is defused when George Washington asks the officers to support the supremacy of the United States Congress.
    • \n
  • April 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Crimean Khanate, which had existed since 1441 and was the last remnant of the Mongol Golden Horde, is annexed by the Russian Empire of Catherine the Great.
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Preliminary articles of peace ending the American Revolutionary War are ratified.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The first United Empire Loyalists, fleeing the new United States, reach Parrtown in Saint John, New Brunswick, Canada.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 A Great Jubilee Day celebrating end of American Revolution held in Trumbull, Connecticut.
    • \n
  • June 4 or June 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The Montgolfier brothers publicly demonstrate their montgolfi\xc3\xa8re hot air balloon at Annonay in France.
    • \n
  • June 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The volcano Laki in Iceland begins an 8-month eruption starting the chain of natural disasters known as the M\xc3\xb3\xc3\xb0uhar\xc3\xb0indin, killing tens of thousands throughout Europe, including up to 33% of Iceland\'s population, and causing widespread famine. It has been described as one of "the greatest environmental catastrophes in European history".[1]
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nThe first manned hydrogen balloon La Charli\xc3\xa8re on its first flight on December 1, 1783, piloted by Prof. Jacques Charles with Nicolas-Louis Robert.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Grants of land in Canada to American loyalists are announced.
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Georgievsk is signed between Imperial Russia and the Kingdom of Kartli-Kakheti making Georgia a protectorate of Russia.
    • \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Mount Asama erupts, causing turmoil in Edo period Japan.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The British East India Company packet ship Antelope (1781) is wrecked off Ulong Island in the Palau (Pelew) group, resulting in the first sustained European contact with those islands.[2]
    • \n
  • August 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The 1783 Great Meteor passes on a thousand-mile track across the North Sea, Great Britain and France, prompting scientific discussion.
    • \n
  • August 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Jacques Charles and Les Fr\xc3\xa8res Robert launch the world\'s first hydrogen-filled balloon, Le Globe, in Paris.
    • \n
  • September 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Peace of Paris: A treaty between the United States and Great Britain is signed in Paris, formally ending the American Revolutionary War; and treaties are signed between Britain, France and Spain at Versailles ending hostilities with the Franco-Spanish Alliance. This also was the beginning of the Old West.
    • \n
  • September 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Dickinson College is chartered in Carlisle, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • October 3 \xe2\x80\x93 First Waterford Crystal glassmaking business begins production in Waterford, Ireland.
    • \n
  • November 2 \xe2\x80\x93 In Rocky Hill, New Jersey, United States General George Washington gives his Farewell Address to the Army.
    • \n
  • November 21 \xe2\x80\x93 In Paris, Jean-Fran\xc3\xa7ois Pil\xc3\xa2tre de Rozier and Fran\xc3\xa7ois Laurent, marquis d\'Arlandes, make the first untethered hot air balloon flight (flight time: 25 minutes, Maximum height: 900 m).
    • \n
  • November 24 \xe2\x80\x93 In Spain, the Cedula of Population is signed, stating that anyone who will swear fealty to Spain and is of the Roman Catholic faith is welcome to populate Trinidad and Tobago.
    • \n
  • November 25 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: The last British troops leave New York City 3 months after the signing of the Treaty of Paris.
    • \n
  • November 27 \xe2\x80\x93 John Michell and the black holes: The english rector, John Michell concludes that some stars might have enough gravity force to prevent light escapes from them, so he called them "dark stars".
    • \n
  • November 29 \xe2\x80\x93 New Jersey earthquake: An earthquake of 5.3 magnitude strikes New Jersey.
    • \n
  • December 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Jacques Charles and Nicolas-Louis Robert make the first manned flight in a hydrogen-filled balloon, La Charli\xc3\xa8re, in Paris.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 At Fraunces Tavern in New York City, U.S. General George Washington formally bids his officers farewell.
    • \n
  • December 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Louis-S\xc3\xa9bastien Lenormand makes the first ever recorded public demonstration of a parachute descent by jumping from the tower of the Montpellier observatory in France using his rigid-framed model which he intends as a form of fire escape.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Loyalists from New York settle Great Abaco in the Bahamas.
    • \n
  • The city of Sevastopol is founded on the Crimean Peninsula of the Russian Empire.
    • \n
  • Princess Yekaterina Vorontsova-Dashkova is elected an honorary member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences, the first female foreign member and its second female member after Eva Ekeblad.
    • \n
  • The Evan Williams (whiskey) distillery is founded in Bardstown, Kentucky.
    • \n
