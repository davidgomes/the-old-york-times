\n1810 (MDCCCX) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1810th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 810th year of the 2nd millennium, the 10th year of the 19th century, and the 1st year of the 1810s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1810 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Australian seal hunter Frederick Hasselborough discovers Campbell Island in the subantarctic.[1]
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The marriage of Napoleon and Jos\xc3\xa9phine is annulled.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Tyrolean rebel leader Andreas Hofer is executed.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: The French Army, under the command of Andr\xc3\xa9 Mass\xc3\xa9na, retreats from Portugal.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon marries Marie-Louise of Austria.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Venezuela achieves home rule: Vicente Empar\xc3\xa1n, Governor of the Captaincy General of Venezuela, is removed by the people of Caracas and a junta is installed.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April through Summer \xe2\x80\x93 Kingdom of Hawaii unified.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Beethoven composes his famous piano piece, F\xc3\xbcr Elise.
    • \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Macon\'s Bill Number 2 becomes law in the United States, intending to motivate Britain and France to stop seizing American vessels during the Napoleonic Wars.
    • \n
  • May 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Lord Byron swims across the Hellespont in Turkey.[2]
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Rev. Henry Duncan opens the world\'s first commercial savings bank in Ruthwell, Scotland.[3]
    • \n
  • May 18\xe2\x80\x93May 25 \xe2\x80\x93 May Revolution: Armed citizens of Buenos Aires expel the Viceroy and establish a provincial government for Argentina (the Primera Junta), the first South American state to proclaim independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Society in Dedham for Apprehending Horse Thieves is founded in Dedham, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 John Jacob Astor forms the Pacific Fur Company.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 Nicolas Appert publishes L\'art de conserver pendant plusieurs ann\xc3\xa9es toutes les substances animales ou v\xc3\xa9g\xc3\xa9tales, the first description of modern food preservation using airtight containers.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon annexes the Kingdom of Holland.
    • \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick Hasselborough discovers Macquarie Island in the subantarctic.[4][5]
    • \n
  • July 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Patria Boba: A junta of seven patriots led by Jos\xc3\xa9 Acevedo y G\xc3\xb3mez assemble in Bogot\xc3\xa1 in the Viceroyalty of New Granada (modern-day Colombia) to declare its independence from the Spanish Empire.
    • \n
  • August 2\xe2\x80\x93 200 citizens are slaughtered in the Royal barracks and the surrounding streets of Quito, Ecuador by royalist troops.
    • \n
  • August 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Santa Cruz de Mompox, in modern-day Colombia, declares independence from the Spanish Empire.
    • \n
  • August 20\xe2\x80\x9327 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Grand Port: The French force the British Royal Navy fleet attempting to blockade a harbour on Isle de France (Mauritius) to surrender.
    • \n
  • August 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Jean Baptiste Bernadotte, Marshal of France, is elected Crown Prince of Sweden by the Swedish Riksdag of the Estates.
    • \n
  • September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Tonquin sets sail from New York Harbor with 33 employees of John Jacob Astor\'s newly created Pacific Fur Company on board. After a 6-month journey around the tip of South America, the ship arrives at the mouth of the Columbia River and Astor\'s men establish the fur-trading town of Astoria.
    • \n
  • September 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Grito de Dolores: Miguel Hidalgo, a Catholic priest from Guanajuato, incites the revolt that becomes the Mexican War of Independence.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Chile forms its First National Junta, which is the country\'s first step towards its independence.
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Republic of West Florida declares independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • September 26 \xe2\x80\x93 A new Act of Succession is adopted by the Riksdag of the Estates and Jean Baptiste Bernadotte becomes heir to the Swedish throne.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 King George III of the United Kingdom is recognized as insane.
    • \n
  • October 12 \xe2\x80\x93 First Oktoberfest: The Bavarian royalty invites the citizens of Munich to join the celebration of the marriage of Crown Prince Ludwig of Bavaria to Princess Therese of Saxe-Hildburghausen.
    • \n
  • October 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States annexes the Republic of West Florida.
    • \n
  • November 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-Swedish War (1810\xe2\x80\x9312): Sweden declares war on the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • November 23 \xe2\x80\x93 English actress Sarah Booth debuts at the Theatre Royal, Covent Garden in London.
    • \n
