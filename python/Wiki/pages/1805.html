1805 (MDCCCV) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1805th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 805th year of the 2nd millennium, the 5th year of the 19th century, and the 6th year of the 1800s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1805 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929. After thirteen years the First French Empire abolished the French Republican Calendar in favour of the Gregorian calendar.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Michigan Territory is created.
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The Harmony Society is officially formed as a Christian community in Harmony, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Justice Samuel Chase is acquitted of impeachment charges by the United States Senate.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Jefferson is sworn in for a second term as President of the United States.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The New Brunswick Legislature passes a bill to advance literacy in the province, which eventually leads to the creation of public education in what is now Canada.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Beethoven\'s Symphony No. 3, Eroica, has its public premiere at the Theater an der Wien in Vienna under his baton.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Derne: United States Marines and Berbers attack the Tripolitan city of Derna (the "Shores of Tripoli").
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Rutger Jan Schimmelpenninck is appointed as Grand Pensionary of the Batavian Republic by Napoleon.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon is crowned King of Italy.
    • \n
  • May 31\xe2\x80\x93June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Diamond Rock: A Franco-Spanish fleet captures the strategic island of Diamond Rock off Martinique in the West Indies from the British occupying force.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Tuscan-born composer Luigi Boccherini is buried in St. Michael\'s Basilica, Madrid, after being found dead on May 28.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The First Barbary War ends between Tripoli and the United States of America.
    • \n
  • June 4\n
      \n
    • The first Trooping the Colour ceremony is held at the Horse Guards Parade in London.
      • \n
    • Detroit burns to the ground; most of the city is destroyed.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Lewis and Clark Expedition in the United States: Scouting ahead of the expedition, Meriwether Lewis and four companions sight the Great Falls of the Missouri River, confirming they are heading in the right direction.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Muhammad Ali Pasha founds his dynasty in Egypt.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 An earthquake killed 5,573 people in Molise and Campania, Italy
    • \n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars: War of the Third Coalition, Ulm Campaign: Battle of Elchingen \xe2\x80\x93 An Austrian corps under Johann von Riesch is defeated by Marshal Ney near Elchingen in Bavaria.
    • \n
  • October 16\xe2\x80\x9319 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Third Coalition, Ulm Campaign: Battle of Ulm \xe2\x80\x93 Austrian General Mack von Leiberich is forced to surrender his entire army to Napoleon after being surrounded.
    • \n
  • October 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars: War of the Third Coalition \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Trafalgar: British naval fleet led by Admiral Horatio Nelson defeats a combined French and Spanish fleet off the coast of Spain; however, Admiral Nelson is fatally shot.
    • \n

\nOctober 21: Battle of Trafalgar, victory for Nelson.

\nDecember 2: Battle of Austerlitz, victory for Napoleon.

\n
\n
    \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Lewis and Clark Expedition arrives at the Pacific Ocean.
    • \n
  • November 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of D\xc3\xbcrenstein: 8,000 French troops attempt to slow the retreat of a vastly superior Russian and Austrian force.
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Sch\xc3\xb6ngrabern: Russian forces under Pyotr Bagration delay the pursuit by French troops under Joachim Murat.
    • \n
  • November 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Beethoven\'s only opera Fidelio in its original form (known retrospectively as Leonore) is premiered at the Theater an der Wien in Vienna, which at this time is under French military occupation.
    • \n
  • November 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Pontcysyllte Aqueduct is opened in Wales; it is 1,007 ft (307 m) long and 126 ft (38 m) tall.
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Austerlitz: French troops under Napoleon decisively defeat a joint Russo-Austrian force.
    • \n
  • December 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Peace of Pressburg is signed between France and Austria.
    • \n
