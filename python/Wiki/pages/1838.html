\n1838 (MDCCCXXXVIII) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1838th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 838th year of the 2nd millennium, the 38th year of the 19th century, and the 9th year of the 1830s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1838 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 10 \xe2\x80\x93 A fire destroys Lloyd\'s Coffee House and the Royal Exchange in London.
    • \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The first known report about the lowest temperature on Earth was made, indicating \xe2\x88\x9260 \xc2\xb0C (\xe2\x88\x9276 \xc2\xb0F) in Yakutsk.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 4\xe2\x80\x9322 \xe2\x80\x93 The paddle steamer SS Sirius makes the transatlantic crossing to New York from Cork, Ireland, in eighteen days, though not using steam continuously.[1]
    • \n
  • April 8\xe2\x80\x9323 \xe2\x80\x93 Isambard Kingdom Brunel\'s paddle steamer SS Great Western (1838) makes the transatlantic crossing to New York from Avonmouth, England, in fifteen days, inaugurating a regular steamship service.[2]
    • \n
  • April 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Nicaragua declares independence from the Federal Republic of Central America.[3]
    • \n
  • May\n
      \n
    • The People\'s Charter is drawn up in the United Kingdom, demanding universal suffrage.
      • \n
    • Lord Durham and his entourage arrive in Upper Canada to investigate the cause of the 1837 rebellion in that province. This leads to Durham submitting the Durham Report to Britain.
      • \n
    • An insurrection breaks out in Tizim\xc3\xadn, beginning the campaign for the independence of Yucat\xc3\xa1n from Mexico.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The people of the Cherokee Nation are forcibly relocated during the Trail of Tears in the United States.
    • \n
  • May 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Braulio Carrillo is sworn in as Head of State of Costa Rica, thus beginning his second term in office.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 28 Indigenous Australians are killed in the Myall Creek massacre.
    • \n
  • June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Coronation of Queen Victoria takes place at Westminster Abbey in London.[4]
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Grace Darling and her father rescue thirteen survivors from the Forfarshire off the Farne Islands.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Supporters of Infante Carlos, Count of Molina, are victorious in the Battle of Maella during the First Carlist War.
    • \n
  • October 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Lilburn Boggs, Governor of Missouri, by Missouri Executive Order 44 declares Mormons to be enemies of the state and encourages the extermination or exile of the religious minority, forcing nearly 10,000 Mormons out of the state.[5]
    • \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Bombay Times and Journal of Commerce is founded (renamed The Times of India in 1861).
    • \n
  • November 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Dissolution of the Federal Republic of Central America: Honduras and Costa Rica follow the example of Nicaragua and secede from the federation.[3]
    • \n
  • December 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Boers win a decisive victory over the Zulus in the Battle of Blood River.
    • \n
  • December\n
      \n
    • First Anglo-Afghan War: British and Presidency armies set out from Punjab in support of Shah Shujah Durrani\'s claim to the throne of Afghanistan.
      • \n
    • Pastry War: Mexico is invaded by French forces.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Proteins are discovered by Gerardus Johannes Mulder[6] and named by J\xc3\xb6ns Jacob Berzelius.[7]
    • \n
  • Friedrich Bessel makes the first accurate measurement of distance to a star.
    • \n
  • The Pitcairn Islands become a Crown colony of the United Kingdom; and women there are the first in the world to be granted and maintain women\'s suffrage.[8]
    • \n
  • Five nuns from the Religious Sisters of Charity in Ireland become the first women of religion to set foot on Australian soil.
    • \n
  • Biblical criticism: Christian Hermann Weisse proposes the two-source hypothesis.
    • \n
  • Duke University is established in North Carolina.
    • \n
  • The 5th century BC bronze Chatsworth Head is acquired by the 6th Duke of Devonshire at Smyrna from H. P. Borrell.
    • \n
  • The Royal Polytechnic Institution University of Westminster is established in Regent Street, London.
    • \n
