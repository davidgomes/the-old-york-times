1869 (MDCCCLXIX) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Julian calendar, the 1869th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 869th year of the 2nd millennium, the 69th year of the 19th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1860s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1869 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Abdur Rahman Khan is defeated at Tinah Khan and exiled from Afghanistan.
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Scotland\'s oldest professional football team, Kilmarnock F.C., is founded.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Elizabeth Cady Stanton is the first woman to testify before the United States Congress.
    • \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The P.E.O. Sisterhood, a philanthropic educational organization for women, is founded at Iowa Wesleyan College in Mount Pleasant, Iowa.
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Republic of Ezo is proclaimed on the northern Japanese island of Ezo (which will be renamed Hokkaid\xc5\x8d on September 20) by remaining adherents to the Tokugawa shogunate.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Prospectors in Moliagul, Victoria, Australia, discover the largest alluvial gold nugget ever found, known as the "Welcome Stranger".
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Ranavalona II, the Merina Queen of Madagascar, is baptized.
    • \n
  • February 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The 2\xc2\xbd-year-old Mahbub Ali Khan begins a 42-year reign as Nizam of Hyderabad.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 In Japan, the daimyo of the Tosa, Hizen, Satsuma and Ch\xc5\x8dsh\xc5\xab Domains are persuaded to \'return their domains\' to the Emperor Meiji, leading to creation of a fully centralized government in the country.[1]
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 North German Confederation issues 10gr and 30gr value stamps, printed on goldbeater\'s skin.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Ulysses S. Grant succeeds Andrew Johnson as the 18th President of the United States of America.
    • \n
  • March 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Dmitri Mendeleev makes a formal presentation of his periodic table to the Russian Chemical Society.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Southern Illinois University Carbondale is founded.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Titokowaru\'s War ends with surrender of the last M\xc4\x81ori troops at large in the South Taranaki District of New Zealand\'s North Island.
    • \n

  • May 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Sainsbury\'s first store, in Drury Lane, London, is opened.[3]
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Boston University is chartered by the Commonwealth of Massachusetts.

  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Cincinnati Red Stockings open the baseball season as the first fully professional team.
    • \n
  • June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Sherwood College is founded in Nainital, India.
    • \n
  • June 15 \xe2\x80\x93 John Wesley Hyatt patents celluloid, in Albany, New York.
    • \n
  • June 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The fortress of Gory\xc5\x8dkaku is turned over to Imperial Japanese forces, bringing an end to the Republic of Ezo, the Battle of Hakodate and the Boshin War.
    • \n
  • June 30-July 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Estonian Song Festival takes place in Tartu.

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Swedish town G\xc3\xa4vle is destroyed in a city fire. 8,000 people became homeless.

    • \n
  • September 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The foundation stone is laid for Neuschwanstein Castle in Bavaria (southern Germany).
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Work on the Wallace Monument is completed in Stirling, Scotland.
    • \n
  • September 12\xe2\x80\x9313 \xe2\x80\x93 The P&O\'s SS Carnatic runs aground and sinks in the Red Sea; 31 drowned.
    • \n
  • September 24 \xe2\x80\x93 "Black Friday": The Fisk\xe2\x80\x93Gould Scandal causes a financial panic in the United States.

\n
    \n
  • October 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Austria-Hungary sends reinforcements to battle the uprising in Krivo\xc5\xa1ije
    • \n

    • \n
  • November 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hudson\'s Bay Company surrenders its claim to Rupert\'s Land in Canada under its letters patent back to the British Crown.[5]
    • \n
  • November 23 \xe2\x80\x93 In Dumbarton, Scotland, the clipper ship Cutty Sark is launched (it is one of the last clippers built, and the only one to survive into the 21st century).[4]

  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Publication of Leo Tolstoy\'s novel War and Peace complete in book form concludes in Russia.

    • \n
  • December 8 \xe2\x80\x93 First Vatican Council opens.

  • December 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The first American chapter of Kappa Sigma is founded at the University of Virginia.

  • December 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Wyoming territorial legislature gives women the right to vote, the first such law in the world.

  • December 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Triple Alliance forces take Asunci\xc3\xb3n in the Paraguayan War.
