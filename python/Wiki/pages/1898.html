1898 (MDCCCXCVIII) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1898th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 898th year of the 2nd millennium, the 98th year of the 19th century, and the 9th year of the 1890s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1898 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 New York City annexes land from surrounding counties, creating the City of Greater New York, the world\'s second largest at this time. The city is geographically divided into five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx and Staten Island.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Novelist \xc3\x89mile Zola\'s open letter to the President of the French Republic on the Dreyfus affair, J\'accuse, is published on the front page of the Paris daily newspaper L\'Aurore, accusing the government of wrongfully imprisoning Alfred Dreyfus and of antisemitism.
    • \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The electric car belonging to Henry Lindfield of Brighton runs away on a hill in Purley, London, England, and hits a tree; thus he becomes the world\'s first fatality from an automobile accident on the public highway.[1][2][3]
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War: The USS Maine explodes and sinks in Havana harbor, Cuba, for reasons never fully established, killing 266 men. This event helps lead the United States to declare war on Spain.
    • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Annie Oakley promotes the service of women in combat situations with the United States military. On this day, she writes a letter to President McKinley "offering the government the services of a company of 50 \'lady sharpshooters\' who would provide their own arms and ammunition should war break out with Spain."[4] In the history of women in the military, there are records of female U.S. Revolutionary and Civil War soldiers who enlisted using male pseudonyms, but Oakley\'s letter represents possibly the earliest political move towards women\'s rights for combat service in the United States military.
    • \n
  • April 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War: The United States Navy begins a blockade of Cuban ports and the USS Nashville captures a Spanish merchant ship.
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War: The United States declares war on Spain; the U.S. Congress announces that a state of war has existed since April 21 (later backdating this one more day to April 20).
    • \n
  • April 25: In Essen German company Rheinisch-Westf\xc3\xa4lisches Elektrizit\xc3\xa4tswerk RWE is founded.[5]
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 An explosion in Santa Cruz, California kills 13 workers at the California Powder Works.[6]
    • \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Manila Bay: Commodore Dewey destroys the Spanish squadron. The first battle of the war, as well as the first battle in the Philippines Campaign.
    • \n
  • May 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Thousands of Chinese scholars and Beijing citizens protest in front of the capital control yuan, asking for reform.
    • \n
  • May 7\xe2\x80\x93May 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Bava-Beccaris massacre: Hundreds of demonstrators are killed when General Fiorenzo Bava-Beccaris orders troops to fire on a rally in Milan, Italy (in 1900, King Umberto I of Italy is killed in an act of vengeance for his praise of the shooting).
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The first games of the Italian Football League are played.
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Bombardment of San Juan, the first major battle of the Puerto Rican Campaign during the Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War.
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The territory of Kwang-Chou-Wan is leased by China to France, according to the Treaty of 12 April 1892, as the Territoire de Kouang-Tch\xc3\xa9ou-Wan, forming part of French Indochina.[7]
    • \n
  • May 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Secondo Pia takes the first photographs of the Shroud of Turin and discovers that the image on Shroud itself appears to be a photographic negative.

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War: Battle of San Juan Hill \xe2\x80\x93 United States troops including Buffalo Soldiers and Theodore Roosevelt\'s Rough Riders take a strategic position close to Santiago de Cuba from the Spanish.
    • \n
  • July 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Joshua Slocum completes a 3-year solo circumnavigation of the world.
    • \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States annexes the Hawaiian Islands.
    • \n
  • July 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War: Battle of Santiago Bay \xe2\x80\x93 Troops under United States General William R. Shafter take the city of Santiago de Cuba from the Spanish.
    • \n
  • July 18 "The Adventures of Louis de Rougemont" first appear in The Wide World Magazine, as its August 1898 issue goes on sale.[9]
    • \n
  • July 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War: The United States invasion of Puerto Rico begins with a landing at Gu\xc3\xa1nica Bay.
    • \n
  • August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish\xe2\x80\x93American War: Hostilities end between American and Spanish forces in Cuba.
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Opening of the Gornergrat railway, connecting Zermatt to the Gornergrat.
    • \n
  • August 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Southern Cross Expedition, the first British venture of the Heroic Age of Antarctic Exploration, sets sail from London.
    • \n
  • August 25 \xe2\x80\x93 700 Greeks and 15 Englishmen are slaughtered by the Turks in Heraklion, Greece, leading to the establishment of the autonomous Cretan State.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Caleb Bradham names his soft drink Pepsi-Cola.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Omdurman: British and Egyptian troops led by Horatio Kitchener defeat Sudanese tribesmen led by Khalifa Abdullah al-Taashi, thus establishing British dominance in the Sudan.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Italian anarchist Luigi Lucheni assassinates Empress Elisabeth of Austria-Hungary in Geneva as an act of propaganda of the deed.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Fashoda Incident: A powerful flotilla of British gunboats arrives at the French-occupied fort of Fashoda on the White Nile, leading to a diplomatic stalemate until French troops are ordered to withdraw on November 3.
    • \n
  • September 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Empress Dowager Cixi of China engineers a coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat, marking the end of the Hundred Days\' Reform; the Guangxu Emperor is arrested.
