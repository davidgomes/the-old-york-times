1958 (MCMLVIII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1958th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 958th year of the 2nd millennium, the 58th year of the 20th century, and the 9th year of the 1950s decade.

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1\n
      \n

  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Anne de Vries releases the fourth and final volume of Journey Through the Night.

\n
    \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Egypt and Syria unite to form the United Arab Republic.
    • \n

  • February 5\n
      \n
    • Gamal Abdel Nasser is nominated as the first president of the United Arab Republic.

      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Manchester United footballers are among the 21 people killed in the Munich air disaster in West Germany, on the return flight from a European Cup game in Yugoslavia. 23 people survive, but four of them, including manager Matt Busby and players Johnny Berry and Duncan Edwards, are in a serious condition. Busby and Berry would pullthrough although Berry would never play again. Edwards died fortnight later.[1]
    • \n

      \n
    • The strongest ever known solar maximum is recorded.[2]
      • \n
    • Marshal Chen Yi succeeds Zhou Enlai as Chinese Minister of Foreign Affairs.

    • Ruth Carol Taylor is the first African American woman hired as a flight attendant. Hired by Mohawk Airlines, her career lasts only six months, due to another discriminatory barrier \xe2\x80\x93 the airline\'s ban on married flight attendants.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hashemite Kingdoms of Iraq and Jordan unite in the Arab Federation with King Faisal II of Iraq as head of state.

  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 A test rocket explodes at Cape Canaveral.
    • \n

    • \n
  • February 23\n
      \n
    • Cuban rebels kidnap five-time world driving champion Juan Manuel Fangio, releasing him 28 hours later.
      • \n
    • Arturo Frondizi is elected president of Argentina.

    \n
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 In Cuba, Fidel Castro\'s Radio Rebelde begins broadcasting from Sierra Maestra.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Bertrand Russell launches the Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament.

  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 One of the worst school bus accidents in U.S. history occurs at Prestonburg, Kentucky; 29 are killed.

  • May 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Actor-singer Paul Robeson, whose passport has been reinstated, sings in a sold-out one-man recital at Carnegie Hall. His Carnegie Hall concerts are later released on records and on CD.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Interviewed in the Chave d\'Ouro caf\xc3\xa9, when asked about his rival Ant\xc3\xb3nio de Oliveira Salazar, Humberto Delgado utters one of the most famous comments in Portuguese political history: "Obviamente, demito-o! (Obviously, I\'ll sack him!)".

    • \n
  • May 13\n
      \n
    • French Algerian protesters seize government offices in Algiers, leading to a military coup.

    \n
    • \n

Gigi is Lerner and Loewe\'s first musical written especially for film, and is deliberately written in a style evoking the team\'s My Fair Lady, which was still playing on Broadway at the time and could not be filmed yet.

    \n
    • \n

    • \n

    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Explorer 1 ceases transmission.
    • \n
  • May 28 - Real Madrid beats A.C.Milan 3-2 at Heysel Stadium, Brussels and wins the 1957-58 European Cup (football).
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The bodies of unidentified United States soldiers killed in action during World War II and the Korean War are buried at the Tomb of the Unknowns in Arlington National Cemetery.
