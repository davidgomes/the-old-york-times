1673 (MDCLXXIII) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Julian calendar, the 1673rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 673rd year of the 2nd millennium, the 73rd year of the 17th century, and the 4th year of the 1670s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1673 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Impostor Mary Carleton is hanged at Newgate Prison in London for multiple thefts and returning from penal transportation.
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Premi\xc3\xa8re of Moli\xc3\xa8re\'s com\xc3\xa9die-ballet The Imaginary Invalid in Paris. During the fourth performance, on February 17, the playwright, playing the title r\xc3\xb4le, collapses on stage, dying soon after.
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Test Act: Roman Catholics and others who refuse to receive the sacrament of the Church of England cannot vote, hold public office, preach, teach, attend the universities or assemble for meetings in England. On June 12, the king\'s Catholic brother, James, Duke of York, is forced to resign the office of Lord High Admiral because of the Act.[1]
    • \n
  • April 27? \xe2\x80\x93 Jean-Baptiste Lully\'s first opera, Cadmus et Hermione, is premi\xc3\xa8red in France.
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 In America, trader Louis Joliet and Jesuit missionary-explorer Jacques Marquette begin exploring the Mississippi River and the Great Lakes.
    • \n
  • June 7 \xe2\x80\x93 First Battle of Schooneveld: In a sea battle of the Third Anglo-Dutch War, fought off the Netherlands coast, the Dutch Republic fleet (commanded by Michiel de Ruyter) defeats the allied Anglo-French fleet commanded by Prince Rupert of the Rhine.
    • \n
  • June 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Second Battle of Schooneveld: The Dutch fleet again defeats the Anglo-French fleet.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 French explorers Father Jacques Marquette and Louis Joliet reach the headwaters of the Mississippi River and descend to Arkansas.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 6 \xe2\x80\x93 French troops conquer Maastricht.
    • \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The Netherlands and Denmark sign a defense treaty.
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Edmund Halley enters Queen\'s College at Oxford, as an undergraduate.
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 In the American colonies, a Dutch battle fleet of 23 ships demands the surrender of New York.
    • \n
  • August 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Dutch forces under Admiral Cornelis Evertsen de Jonge recapture New York from the English (regained by the English in 1674).
    • \n
  • August 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Texel (Kijkduin): The Dutch fleet under Michiel de Ruyter defeats the English and French fleet. This prevents England\'s Blackheath Army from landing in Zeeland
    • \n
  • August 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Leopold I, Spain, Netherlands and the Lutherans form an anti-French covenant.
    • \n
  • September 12 \xe2\x80\x93 William, Prince of Orange occupies Naarden.
    • \n
  • November 9 \xe2\x80\x93 King Charles II of England removes Anthony Ashley Cooper, 1st Earl of Shaftesbury, from his position as Lord Chancellor.
    • \n
  • November 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Polish and Lithuanian military units under the command of soon-to-be-king Jan Sobieski defeat the Turkish army in the Battle of Khotyn. In this battle, rockets of Kazimierz Siemienowicz are successfully used.
    • \n
  • November 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Dutch troops commanded by Raimondo Montecuccoli and William, Prince of Orange conquer Bonn.
    • \n
  • November 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Christopher Wren is knighted.
    • \n
  • November 23 \xe2\x80\x93 James, Duke of York, marries Mary of Modena.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • France begins its expedition against Ceylon.
    • \n
  • Chelsea Physic Garden, the second oldest botanic garden in England, is founded by the Society of Apothecaries for the study of medicinal and other plants.
    • \n
  • The Mitsui family\'s trading and banking house is founded in Japan.
    • \n
  • The stalactic grotto of Antiparos (Aegean Sea) is discovered.
    • \n
  • Archpriest Petrovich Avvakum writes his Zhitie (Life) as the first Russian autobiography.
    • \n
