1982 (MCMLXXXII) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1982nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 982nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 82nd year of the 20th century, and the 3rd year of the 1980s decade.

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 New ITV franchises, Central, TVS and TSW, are launched.
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Commodore 64 8-bit home computer is launched by Commodore International in Las Vegas[1] (released in August); it becomes the all-time best-selling single personal computer model.[2]
    • \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 AT&T Corporation agrees to break up and divest itself of 22 subdivisions.[3]
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Mark Thatcher, son of British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher, disappears in the Sahara during the Dakar Rally; he is rescued January 14.
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 A brutal cold snap sends temperatures to all-time record lows in dozens of cities throughout the Midwestern United States.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Shortly after takeoff, Air Florida Flight 90 crashes into Washington, D.C.\'s 14th Street Bridge and falls into the Potomac River, killing 78. On the same day, a Washington Metro train derails to the north, killing 3 (the system\'s first fatal accident).
    • \n
  • January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Cold Sunday sweeps over the northern United States.
    • \n
  • January 18 \xe2\x80\x93 1982 Thunderbirds Indian Springs Diamond Crash: Four Northrop T-38 aircraft of the United States Air Force Thunderbirds Demonstration Squadron crash at Indian Springs Air Force Auxiliary Field, Nevada, killing all 4 pilots.
    • \n
  • January 26\n
      \n
    • Mauno Koivisto is elected President of Finland.
      • \n
    • Unemployment in the United Kingdom increases by 129,918 to 3,070,621, a post-war record number.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Garret FitzGerald government of the Republic of Ireland is defeated 82\xe2\x80\x9381 on its budget; the 22nd D\xc3\xa1il \xc3\x89ireann is dissolved.
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 United States Army Brigadier General James L. Dozier is rescued by the Italian anti-terrorism Nucleo Operativo Centrale di Sicurezza (NOCS) force after being held captive for 42 days by the Red Brigades.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The first computer virus, the Elk Cloner, written by 15-year old Rich Skrenta, is found.[4] It infects Apple II computers via floppy disk.
    • \n
