1854 (MDCCCLIV) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1854th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 854th year of the 2nd millennium, the 54th year of the 19th century, and the 5th year of the 1850s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1854 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles Dickens commences writing the novel Hard Times.

    • \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The iron clipper RMS Tayleur runs aground off the east coast of Ireland on her maiden voyage out of Liverpool bound for Australia with the loss of at least 300 of around 650 on board.

    • \n
  • February 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The British recognize the independence of the Orange Free State in South Africa; its official independence is declared 6 days later.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Britain sends Russia an ultimatum to withdraw from two Romanian provinces it has conquered, Moldavia and Wallachia.
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Republican Party (United States) is founded in Ripon, Wisconsin.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 The British East India Company annexes Jhansi State in India under the doctrine of lapse.

    • \n

    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 In Venezuela, slavery was abolished

    • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Hard Times begins serialisation in Charles Dickens\' magazine, Household Words.
    • \n
  • April 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States packet ship Powhattan is wrecked off the New Jersey shore with more than 200 victims.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Catholic University of Ireland (forerunner of University College Dublin) is founded.
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: United States diplomatic minister Robert McLane arrives at the Heavenly Capital aboard the American warship USS Susquehanna.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The Kansas\xe2\x80\x93Nebraska Act becomes law (replacing the Missouri Compromise of 1820), creating the Kansas Territory and the Nebraska Territory, west of the State of Missouri and the State of Iowa. The Kansas\xe2\x80\x93Nebraska Act also establishes that these two new Territories will decide either to allow or disallow slavery depending on balloting by their residents. These areas would have been strictly "free territory" under the Missouri Compromise, which allowed slavery in the State of Missouri but disallowed it in any other new state north of latitude 36\xc2\xb0 30\', which forms most of the southern boundary of Missouri. This prohibition of slavery extended all the way from the western boundary of Missouri to the Pacific Ocean.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 The Grand Excursion takes prominent Eastern United States inhabitants from Chicago to Rock Island, Illinois by railroad, then up the Mississippi River to Saint Paul, Minnesota by steamboat.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The first class of the United States Naval Academy graduates at Annapolis, Maryland.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Bomarsund in the \xc3\x85land Islands off the coast of Finland. British Royal Navy seaman\'s mate Charles D. Lucas throws a live Russian artillery shell overboard by hand before it explodes, for which he is awarded the first Victoria Cross in 1857.
    • \n

    • \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Wood\'s despatch is sent by Charles Wood, 1st Viscount Halifax to Lord Dalhousie, Governor General of India, proposing radical improvements to the Indian educational system.[3]

  • August 31\xe2\x80\x93September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 An epidemic of cholera in London kills 10,000. Dr John Snow traces the source of one outbreak (that killed 500) to a single water pump, validating his theory that cholera is water-borne, and forming the starting point for epidemiology.[5]

  • September 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Crimean War: Battle of Alma \xe2\x80\x93 The French\xe2\x80\x93British alliance wins the first major land engagement of the war.

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The watch company founded in 1850 in Roxbury, Massachusetts by Aaron Lufkin Dennison relocates to Waltham to become the Waltham Watch Company, pioneer in the American system of watch manufacturing.
    • \n
  • October 9\xe2\x80\x9311 \xe2\x80\x93 United States diplomats in Europe meet and draft the Ostend Manifesto, setting out a rationale for the U.S. to acquire Cuba from Spain.
    • \n
  • October 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The great fire of Newcastle and Gateshead in England is ignited by a spectacular explosion.
    • \n
  • October 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Age newspaper is founded in Melbourne, Australia.
    • \n
  • October 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Crimean War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Balaclava: The allies gain an overall victory, except for the disastrous cavalry Charge of the Light Brigade, from which only 200 of 700 men survive.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 Florence Nightingale and her team of 38 trained volunteer nurses, having set out on October 21 from England, arrive at Selimiye Barracks in Scutari in the Ottoman Empire to care for British Army troops invalided from the Crimean War.[6]
    • \n
  • November 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Crimean War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Inkerman: The Russians are defeated.
    • \n
  • November 17 \xe2\x80\x93 In Egypt, the Suez Canal company is formed.
    • \n
  • December 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Eureka Stockade Miners\' Rebellion breaks out in Ballarat, Victoria (Australia).
    • \n
  • December 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Pius IX in the apostolic constitution Ineffabilis Deus defines ex Cathedra the dogma of Immaculate Conception, which holds that the Blessed Virgin Mary was conceived without original sin.
