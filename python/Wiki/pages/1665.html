\n1665 (MDCLXV) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1665th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 665th year of the 2nd millennium, the 65th year of the 17th century, and the 6th year of the 1660s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1665 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The Journal des s\xc3\xa7avans begins publication in France, the first scientific journal.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Second Anglo-Dutch War begins.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society begins publication, the first scientific journal in English.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 A new legal code is approved for the Dutch and English towns of New York guaranteeing all Protestants the right to continue their religious observances unhindered.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Bucharest allows Jews to settle in the city in exchange for an annual tax of 16 guilders.
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Margaret Porteous is the first person recorded to die in the Great Plague of London. This last major outbreak of Bubonic plague in the British Isles has possibly been introduced by Dutch prisoners of war. Two-thirds of Londoners leave the city, but over 68,000 die. Plague spreads to Derby.
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Great fire of Newport, Shropshire, England.
    • \n
  • June 12 \xe2\x80\x93 England installs a municipal government in New York City (the former Dutch settlement of New Amsterdam).
    • \n
  • June 13 (June 3 O.S.) \xe2\x80\x93 Second Anglo-Dutch War: English naval victory at the Battle of Lowestoft under James Stuart, Duke of York.
    • \n
  • June 30 \xe2\x80\x93 King Charles II of England issues a second charter for the Province of Carolina, which clarifies and expands the borders of the Lords Proprietors\' tracts.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The first documented case of cyclopia is found in a horse.
    • \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 King Charles II of England leaves London with his entourage, fleeing the Great Plague. He moves his court to Salisbury, then Exeter.
    • \n
  • August 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Second Anglo-Dutch War: Dutch naval victory at the Battle of V\xc3\xa5gen.
    • \n
  • August 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Ye Bare & Ye Cubbe, the first play in English in the American colonies, is performed in Pungoteague, Virginia.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Hooke\'s Micrographia published in London, first applying the term \'cell\' to plant tissue, which he discovered first in cork, then in living organisms, using a microscope.
    • \n
  • September 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles II of Spain becomes King while not yet four years old.
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Moli\xc3\xa8re\'s L\'Amour m\xc3\xa9decin is first presented, before Louis XIV of France at the Palace of Versailles with music by Jean-Baptiste Lully.
    • \n
  • October 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The University of Kiel is founded.
    • \n
  • October 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Louis XIV of France and Jean-Baptiste Colbert founded the Manufacture royale des glaces of Saint Gobain which is the oldest French company of the CAC 40 with 350 years in 2015.
    • \n
  • October 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Mbwila: Portuguese forces defeat and kill King Ant\xc3\xb3nio I of Kongo.
    • \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The London Gazette, the oldest surviving journal, is first published as The Oxford Gazette.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Colonisation of R\xc3\xa9union begins with the French East India Company sending twenty settlers.
    • \n
  • Ferdinando Carlo Gonzaga is invested as Duke of Mantua.
    • \n
  • Joan Blaeu completes publication of his Atlas Maior (Theatrum Orbis Terrarum) in Amsterdam.
    • \n
  • John Bunyan publishes The End of the World, The Resurrection of the Dead and Eternal Judgment and The Holy City or the New Jerusalem.
    • \n
  • The English poet John Milton popularizes the Chinese sailing carriage in a famous poem; this peculiar Chinese invention was first written of in the West by Abraham Ortelius in his atlas of 1584.
    • \n
