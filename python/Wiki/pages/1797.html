1797 (MDCCXCVII) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1797th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 797th year of the 2nd millennium, the 97th year of the 18th century, and the 8th year of the 1790s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1797 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Tripoli (a peace treaty between the United States and Tripoli) is signed at Algiers (see also 1796).
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The parliament of the Cisalpine Republic adopts the Italian green-white-red tricolour as the official flag (this is considered the birth of the flag of Italy).
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Action of 13 January 1797, part of the French Revolutionary Wars: Two British Royal Navy frigates drive the French 74-gun ship of the line Droits de l\'Homme (1794) aground on the coast of Brittany with over 900 deaths.
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Rivoli: French forces under General Napoleon Bonaparte defeat an Austrian army (28,000 men) under Feldzeugmeister J\xc3\xb3zsef Alvinczi near Rivoli (modern-day Italy), ending Austria\'s fourth and final attempt to relieve the fortress city of Mantua.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of the Third Partition of Poland is signed in St. Petersburg by the Russian Empire, Austria and the Kingdom of Prussia.
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Mantua: Field marshal Dagobert von Wurmser capitulates the fortress city to the French. Only 16,000 men of the garrison are capable of marching out as prisoners of war.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Castel Bolognese: An French corps (9,000 men) under General Claude Victor-Perrin defeats the forces from the Papal States at Castel Bolognese near Faenza, Italy.
    • \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 First performance of Gott erhalte Franz den Kaiser, with the music composed in January by Joseph Haydn which also becomes the tune to the German national anthem, Deutschland, Deutschland \xc3\xbcber alles.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Battle of Cape St Vincent, part of the French Revolutionary Wars: The British Royal Navy under Admiral Sir John Jervis defeats a larger Spanish fleet off Cape St. Vincent, Portugal.
    • \n
  • February 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish Governor Jos\xc3\xa9 Mar\xc3\xada Chac\xc3\xb3n peacefully surrenders the colony of Trinidad to a British naval force commanded by Sir Ralph Abercromby.
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Tolentino: Pope Pius VI signs a peace treaty with Revolutionary France. He is forced to deliver works of art, treasures, territory, the Comtat Venaissin and 30 million francs.
    • \n
  • February 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The last invasion of Britain begins: French forces under the command of American Colonel William Tate land near Fishguard in Wales.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 William Tate surrenders to the British at Fishguard.
    • \n
  • February 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Bank of England (national bank of Britain) issues the first one-pound and two-pound notes (discontinued March 11, 1988).
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 John Adams is sworn in as the second President of the United States of America.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Valvasone: The Austrian army led by Archduke Charles fights a rear guard action at the crossing of the Tagliamento River but is defeated by Napoleon Bonaparte at Valvasone.
    • \n
  • April 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Spithead and Nore mutinies in the British Royal Navy.
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of San Juan: Sir Ralph Abercromby unsuccessfully invades San Juan, Puerto Rico in what will be one of the largest British attacks on Spanish territories in the western hemisphere, and one of the worst defeats of the British Royal Navy for years to come.
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Veronese Easter: Citizens of Verona, Italy, began an unsuccessful eight-day rebellion against the French occupying forces.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Armistice of Leoben: On behalf of the French Republic a delegation under Napoleon Bonaparte signs a peace treaty with the Holy Roman Empire at Leoben.[1]
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The first ship of the United States Navy, the frigate USS United States, is commissioned.
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the First Coalition: Napoleon Bonaparte conquers Venice, ending the city and Republic of Venice\'s 1,100 years of independence. The last doge of Venice, Ludovico Manin, steps down.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 William Wilberforce marries Barbara Ann Spooner.
    • \n
  • June 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon Bonaparte creates in a decree the birth of the Cisalpine Republic. He appoints ministers and establishes the first constitution.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Horatio Nelson is wounded at the Battle of Santa Cruz, losing an arm.
    • \n
  • August 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Massacre of Tranent: British troops attack protestors against enforced recruitment into the militia at Tranent in Scotland, killing 12.
    • \n
  • From c. October \xe2\x80\x93 The XYZ Affair inflames tensions between France and the United States.
    • \n
  • October 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Camperdown: the British Royal Navy defeats the fleet of the Batavian Republic off the coast of Holland.[2]
    • \n
  • October 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Campo Formio ends the War of the First Coalition.
    • \n
  • October 21 \xe2\x80\x93 In Boston Harbor, the 44-gun United States Navy frigate USS Constitution is launched to fight Barbary pirates off the coast of Tripoli; the ship will remain in commission in the 21st century.
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Prussian heir apparent, Frederick William, becomes King of Prussia as Fredrick William III.
    • \n
  • December 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon leads a successful French charge against Fort l\'Aiguilette to secure Toulon.
    • \n
\n

Undated[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Foundation of the secret Lautaro Lodge as the Logia de los Caballeros Racionales ("Lodge of Rational Knights"), perhaps in C\xc3\xa1diz; membership will include many leaders of the Spanish American wars of independence such as Francisco de Miranda, Bernardo O\'Higgins and Jos\xc3\xa9 de San Mart\xc3\xadn.
    • \n
