\n1682 (MDCLXXXII) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1682nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 682nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 82nd year of the 17th century, and the 3rd year of the 1680s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1682 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Work begins on construction of the Royal Hospital Chelsea for old soldiers in London.[1]
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Ren\xc3\xa9-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle, exploring rivers in America, reaches the mouth of the Mississippi River.
    • \n
  • April 9 \xe2\x80\x93 At the mouth of the Mississippi River, near modern Venice, Louisiana, Robert de La Salle buries an engraved plate and a cross, claiming the territory as La Louisiane for France.
    • \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Louis XIV of France moves his court to Versailles.
    • \n
  • May 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The reign of Peter the Great officially begins in Russia.
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Moscow Uprising of 1682: A mob takes over the Kremlin and lynches the leading boyars and military commanders.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Iyasus succeeds his father Yohannes I as Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Vesuvius begins a period of volcanic activity lasting for 10 days.
    • \n
  • August 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Following the Bideford witch trial, three women become the last known to be hanged for witchcraft in England, at Exeter.[2]
    • \n
  • September 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Bishop Gore School in Swansea, Wales, is founded.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 A comet is observed, which later becomes known as Comet Halley, after Edmond Halley successfully predicts that it will return in 1758.
    • \n
  • October 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Sultan Mehmed IV departs Istanbul for Adrianople.
    • \n
  • October 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Kara Mustafa departs with the Ottoman army to Adrianople.
    • \n
  • October 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania is founded by William Penn.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Celia Fiennes noblewoman and traveller, begins her journeys across Britain, in a venture that would prove to be her life\'s work. Her aim was to chronicle the towns, cities and great houses of the country. Her travels continued until at least 1712, and would take her to every county in England, though the main body of her journal was not written until the year 1702.
    • \n
  • The Richard Wall House, believed to be the longest continuously-inhabited residence in the USA, is built in Pennsylvania.
    • \n
