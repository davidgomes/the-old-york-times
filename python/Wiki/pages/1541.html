Year 1541 (MDXLI) was a common year starting on Saturday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Pedro de Valdivia founds Santiago del Nuevo Extremo which will become the capital of Chile.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Francis Xavier leaves Lisbon on a mission to the Portuguese East Indies.
    • \n
  • April 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Sahart: The Emperor of Ethiopia defeats an attack by the forces of Imam Ahmad Gragn.
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish explorer Hernando de Soto reaches the Mississippi River, naming it the Rio de Espiritu Santo ("River of the Holy Spirit").
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Jacques Cartier departs from Saint-Malo, France on his third voyage.
    • \n
  • June 18 \xe2\x80\x93 By the Crown of Ireland Act, the Parliament of Ireland declares King Henry VIII of England and his heirs to be Kings of Ireland, replacing the Lordship of Ireland with the Kingdom of Ireland.[1]
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Est\xc3\xaav\xc3\xa3o da Gama departs Massawa, leaving behind 400 matchlock men and 150 slaves under his brother Crist\xc3\xb3v\xc3\xa3o da Gama, with orders to assist the Emperor of Ethiopia to defeat Ahmad ibn Ibrahim al-Ghazi, who has invaded his Empire.
    • \n
  • August 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Janissaries of Suleiman the Magnificent take Buda by ruse, hiding themselves as visitors.
    • \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 An Algerian military campaign by Charles V of Spain (Habsburg) is unsuccessful.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Portuguese defeat the Muslims near Lake Tana.
    • \n
  • Ottoman Sultan Suleiman the Magnificent seals off The Golden Gate in Jerusalem.
    • \n
  • Iceland adopts the Lutheran faith.
    • \n
  • Gerardus Mercator makes his first globe.
    • \n
  • The first official translation of the entire Bible into Swedish is made.
    • \n
  • John Calvin translates his Institutio Christianae religionis into French as L\'Institution chr\xc3\xa9tienne.
    • \n
  • Elia Levita\'s chivalric romance, the Bovo-Bukh, is first printed, the earliest published secular work in Yiddish.
    • \n
