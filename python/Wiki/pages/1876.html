1876 (MDCCCLXXVI) was a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Julian calendar, the 1876th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 876th year of the 2nd millennium, the 76th year of the 19th century, and the 7th year of the 1870s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1876 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1\n
      \n
    • The Reichsbank opens in Berlin.
      • \n
    • The Bass Brewery Red Triangle becomes the world\'s first registered trademark symbol.[1]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The National League of Professional Base Ball Clubs is formed at a meeting in Chicago; it replaced the National Association of Professional Base Ball Players. Morgan Bulkeley of the Hartford Dark Blues is selected as the league\'s first President.
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Montejurra (Third Carlist War in Spain) \xe2\x80\x93 The new commander General Fernando Primo de Rivera marches on the remaining Carlist stronghold at Estella, where he meets a force of about 1,600 men under General Carlos Calder\xc3\xb3n at nearby Montejurra. After a courageous and costly defence Calder\xc3\xb3n is forced to withdraw.
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Third Carlist War \xe2\x80\x93 Government troops under General Primo de Rivera drive through the weak Carlist forces protecting Estella and take the city by storm.
    • \n
  • February 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Johns Hopkins University is founded in Baltimore.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Premiere of first stage production of the verse-play Peer Gynt by Henrik Ibsen with incidental music by Edvard Grieg, in Oslo (then called Christiania), Norway
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Third Carlist War in Spain \xe2\x80\x93 The Carlist forces do not succeed, and the promises are never fulfilled. The Carlist pretender Carlos, Duke of Madrid, goes into exile in France bringing the conflict to an end after four years.
    • \n
  • February/March \xe2\x80\x93 The Harvard Lampoon humor magazine is founded in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • Spring \xe2\x80\x93 Vast numbers of Native Americans in the United States move north to an encampment of the Sioux chief Sitting Bull in the region of the Little Bighorn River, creating the last great gathering of native peoples on the Great Plains.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 American librarian Melvil Dewey first publishes the Dewey Decimal Classification system.[2]
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Alexander Graham Bell is granted a United States patent for an invention he calls the telephone (patent #174,466).
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Alexander Graham Bell makes the first successful telephone call, saying "Mr. Watson, come here, I want to see you.."
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Through constitutional reform taking legal effect, Louis De Geer becomes the first Prime Minister of Sweden.
    • \n

  • May 1\n
      \n
    • Queen Victoria takes the title Empress of India.
      • \n

      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Centennial Exposition begins in Philadelphia.
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Berlin Memorandum: Germany, Russia and Austria-Hungary propose an armistice between Turkey and its insurgents.
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 British Prime Minister Benjamin Disraeli rejects the Berlin Memorandum.
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Nikolaus Otto files his patent for the four-stroke cycle internal combustion engine.[3]
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Wyatt Earp starts work in Dodge City, Kansas, serving under Marshal Larry Deger.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Transcontinental Express arrives in San Francisco via the First Transcontinental Railroad, 83 hours and 39 minutes after having left New York City.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of the Rosebud: 1,500 Sioux and Cheyenne led by Crazy Horse beat back General George Crook\'s forces at Rosebud Creek in Montana Territory.
    • \n
  • June 24 \xe2\x80\x93 First published review of The Adventures of Tom Sawyer by Mark Twain, in a British magazine; the book\'s first edition had appeared earlier in June in England. (The book was published in the U.S. in December 1876.)
    • \n
  • June 25 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of the Little Bighorn: 300 men of the U.S. 7th Cavalry Regiment under Lieutenant Colonel George Armstrong Custer are wiped out by 5,000 Lakota, Cheyenne and Arapaho led by Sitting Bull and Crazy Horse.
    • \n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Texas A&M University opens for classes.
    • \n
  • October 31 \xe2\x80\x93 A catastrophic cyclone strikes the coast of present-day Bangladesh, killing 200,000.
    • \n
  • November 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The British Colony of New Zealand dissolves its nine provinces and replaces them with 63 counties.
    • \n
  • November 2 \xe2\x80\x93 A giant squid, 6.1 meters long, washes ashore at Thimble Tickle Bay in Newfoundland.
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The long-awaited First Symphony of Johannes Brahms is premiered at Karlsruhe under the baton of Otto Dessoff.
    • \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. presidential election, 1876: After long and heated disputes, Rutherford Birchard Hayes is eventually declared the winner over Samuel Jones Tilden. A failed grave robbery of the Lincoln Tomb took place on the same night.
    • \n
  • November 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Centennial Exposition ends in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • November 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Corrupt Tammany Hall leader William Marcy Tweed (better known as Boss Tweed) is delivered to authorities in New York City after being captured in Spain.
    • \n
  • November 25 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Dull Knife Fight: In retaliation for the dramatic American defeat at the Battle of the Little Bighorn, United States Army troops under General Ranald S. Mackenzie sack Chief Dull Knife\'s sleeping Cheyenne village at the headwaters of the Powder River (the soldiers destroy all of the villagers\' winter food and clothing, and then slash their ponies\' throats).
    • \n
  • November 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Porfirio D\xc3\xadaz becomes President of Mexico.
    • \n
  • December 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The Brooklyn Theatre fire kills at least 278, possibly more than 300.
    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The first cremation in the United States takes place in a crematory built by Francis Julius LeMoyne.
    • \n
  • December 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Ashtabula River Railroad bridge disaster occurs, leaving 92 dead.
    • \n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Japan brings a fleet to Incheon, the port of modern-day Seoul. The Japanese force the Korean government to sign the Japan\xe2\x80\x93Korea Treaty of 1876, which opened 3 ports to Japanese trade and forced Korea\'s Joseon dynasty to cease considering itself a tributary of China. On China\'s urging, Korea also signs treaties with the European powers in effort to counterbalance Japan.
    • \n
  • The Northern Chinese Famine of 1876\xe2\x80\x9379, which will claim 30 million lives and become the 5th worst famine in recorded history, begins after the droughts of the previous year.

    • \n
  • In D\xc3\xbcsseldorf German company Henkel is founded.

    • \n
  • The Conchological Society of Great Britain & Ireland is founded.
    • \n
  • Lars Magnus Ericsson and Carl Johan Andersson start a small mechanical workshop in Stockholm, Sweden, dealing with telegraphy equipment, which grows into the worldwide company Ericsson.

    • \n
  • Stockport Lacrosse Club, thought to be the oldest existing lacrosse club in the world, is founded at Cale Green Cricket Club Davenport, Greater Manchester, England, where they will still be playing in the 21st century.
