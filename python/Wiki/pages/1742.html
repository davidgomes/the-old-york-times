\n1742 (MDCCXLII) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1742nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 742nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 42nd year of the 18th century, and the 3rd year of the 1740s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1742 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nApril 13: Handel\'s Messiah.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 The House of Commons of Great Britain votes on the alleged rigging of the Chippenham by-election. It becomes a motion of no confidence which leads to the resignation of Robert Walpole.[1]
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Walpole made Earl of Orford and resigns as First Lord of the Treasury and Chancellor of the Exchequer, effectively ending his period as Prime Minister of Great Britain.[2] On his formally relinquishing office five days later, he will have served 20 years and 314 days as Prime Minister, the longest single term ever, and also longer than the accumulated terms of any other British Prime Minister.
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles VII Albert becomes Holy Roman Emperor.
    • \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 John Carteret, 2nd Lord Carteret becomes Secretary of State for the Northern Department in Great Britain.
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Spencer Compton, Earl of Wilmington, becomes Prime Minister of Great Britain.
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 George Frideric Handel\'s oratorio The Messiah is first performed in Dublin, Ireland.
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick the Great\'s army defeats Austrians in Chotusitz; later Austria cedes Silesia to Prussia.
    • \n
  • May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Austrian Succession: Battle of Sahay
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 War of Jenkins\' Ear: British troops repel those of Spain (under Montiano) in the Battle of Bloody Marsh in the Province of Georgia.
    • \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x93 William Pulteney is created 1st Earl of Bath in Great Britain.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Construction starts on the Foundling Hospital in London.
    • \n
  • November 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Royal Danish Academy of Sciences and Letters is founded.
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The Pennsylvania Journal first appears in print in the United States.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Daniel le Pelley succeeds Nicolas le Pelley as Seigneur of Sark.
    • \n
  • The Kingdom of Prussia captures Jihlava.
    • \n
  • Christian Goldbach formulates Goldbach\'s conjecture.
    • \n
  • Colin Maclaurin publishes his Treatise on Fluxions.
    • \n
  • Anders Celsius proposes the Celsius temperature scale (see 1741).
    • \n
  • James Bradley succeeds Edmond Halley as Astronomer Royal.
    • \n
  • The University of Erlangen is founded.
    • \n
  • The Lopukhina Conspiracy arises at the Russian court.
    • \n
  • Molde, Norway, becomes a city.
    • \n
  • Eisenach, Germany builds its Stadtschloss (city castle).
    • \n
  • In Peru, Juan Santos takes the name Atahualpa II and begins an ill-fated rebellion against the Spanish rule.
    • \n
  • The Afghan tribes unite as a monarchy.
    • \n
  • Henry Fielding publishes Joseph Andrews.
    • \n
  • Charles Jervas\'s English translation of Don Quixote is published posthumously. Through a printer\'s error, the translator\'s name is printed as Charles Jarvis, leading the book to forever be known as the Jarvis translation. It is acclaimed as the most faithful English rendering of the novel made up to that time.
    • \n
  • Spain completes the construction of Fort Matanzas in the Matanzas Inlet, approximately 15 miles (24 km) south of St. Augustine, Florida.
    • \n
  • Rome decrees that Roman ceremonial practice in Latin (not in Chinese) is to be the law for Chinese missions.
    • \n
