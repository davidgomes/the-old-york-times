1852 (MDCCCLII) was a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Julian calendar, the 1852nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 852nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 52nd year of the 19th century, and the 3rd year of the 1850s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1852 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 President Louis-Napol\xc3\xa9on Bonaparte proclaimed a new constitution for the French Second Republic.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Nine men representing various Jewish charitable organizations came together to form what became the Mount Sinai Hospital in New York City.
    • \n
  • January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Kingdom recognized the independence of the Transvaal.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Caseros or Battle of Monte Caseros, Argentina: The Argentine provinces of Entre Rios and Corrientes allied with Brazil and members of Colorado Party of Uruguay, defeated Buenos Aires troops under Juan Manuel de Rosas.
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The first British public toilet for women was opened in Bedford Street, London.
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Ormond Street Hospital for Sick Children, London, admitted its first patient.
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Studebaker Brothers Wagon Company, precursor of the automobile manufacturer, was established.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 HMS Birkenhead sank near Cape Town, British Cape Colony. Only 193 of the 643 on board survived after troops stood firm on the deck so as not to flood the lifeboats containing women and children.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Archibald Montgomerie, 13th Earl of Eglinton was appointed Lord Lieutenant of Ireland.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The first American experimental steam fire engine was tested.[1]
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Phi Mu sorority was founded in Macon Georgia
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Uncle Tom\'s Cabin by Harriet Beecher Stowe was published in book form in Boston.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Second Anglo-Burmese War began.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: Taiping forces began the siege of Guilin.
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: The siege of Guilin was lifted.
    • \n
  • June 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: Taiping forces entered Hunan.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 United States statesman Henry Clay was the first to receive the honor of lying in state in the United States Capitol rotunda.
    • \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick Douglass delivered his famous speech "What to the Slave is the Fourth of July?" in Rochester, New York.
    • \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Clay steamboat disaster in Riverdale, Bronx, with several deaths including Stephen Allen.
    • \n
  • August 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Boat Race between Yale and Harvard, the first American intercollegiate athletic event, was held.
    • \n
  • September 24 \xe2\x80\x93 French engineer Henri Giffard made the first airship trip from Paris to Trappes.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 7 \xe2\x80\x94 After learning that U.S. President Fillmore has sent Commodore Matthew C. Perry, to open trade with Japan, Nicholas I of Russia sent Rear Admiral Yevfimy Putyatin to lead the Pallada on a similar mission. Putyatin arrived on August 21, 1853, one month after Perry.[2]
    • \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x94 After nearly five years imprisonment in France, former Algerian Emir Abdelkader El Djezairi was released by orders of then-president Louis-Napol\xc3\xa9on Bonaparte.[3]
    • \n
  • October 23 \xe2\x80\x94 The conjecture of the four color theorem was first proposed, as student Francis Guthrie of University College London presented the question of proving, mathematically, that no more than four colors are needed to give separate colors to bordering shapes on a map. The theorem was not proven for almost 123 years, until 1976.[4]
    • \n
  • October 31 \xe2\x80\x94 General Joaquin Solares of Guatemala led an invasion of neighboring Honduras, beginning a war that lasted until February 13, 1856.[5]
    • \n
  • November 2 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. presidential election, 1852: Democrat Franklin Pierce of New Hampshire defeated Whig Winfield Scott of Virginia.
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Camillo Benso, Count of Cavour becomes the Piedmontese prime minister.
    • \n
  • November 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The new Palace of Westminster was opened in London.
    • \n
  • November 21\xe2\x80\x9322 \xe2\x80\x93 The New French Empire was confirmed by plebiscite: 7,824,000 for, 253,000 against.
    • \n
  • November 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The first roadside pillar boxes in the British Isles were brought into public use in Saint Helier on Jersey in the Channel Islands at the suggestion of English novelist Anthony Trollope, at this time an official of the British General Post Office.[6]
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 The Western Railroad was chartered to build a railroad from Fayetteville, North Carolina to the coal fields of Egypt, North Carolina.[7]
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon III became Emperor of the French.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The French captured Laghouat.
    • \n
  • December 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: The Taiping army took Hanyang and begins the siege of Wuchang.
    • \n
  • December 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Taiping Rebellion: The Taiping army took Hankou.
    • \n
