\n1717 (MDCCXVII) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1717th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 717th year of the 2nd millennium, the 17th year of the 18th century, and the 8th year of the 1710s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1717 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Count Carl Gyllenborg, the Swedish ambassador to the Kingdom of Great Britain, is arrested in London over a plot to assist the Pretender to the British throne, James Francis Edward Stuart.[1]
    • \n
  • January 4 (December 24, 1716 Old Style) \xe2\x80\x93 Great Britain, France and the Dutch Republic sign the Triple Alliance[1] in an attempt to maintain the Treaty of Utrecht (1713), Britain having signed a preliminary alliance with France on November 28 (November 17) 1716.
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 Following the treaty between France and Britain, James Stuart leaves France and seeks refuge with Pope Clement XI.[1]
    • \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Silent Sejm, in the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, marks the beginning of the Russian Empire\'s increasing influence and control over the Commonwealth.
    • \n
  • February 26\xe2\x80\x93March 6 \xe2\x80\x93 What is now the northeastern United States is paralyzed by a series of blizzards that bury the region.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Dancer John Weaver performs in the first ballet in Britain, shown at the Theatre Royal, Drury Lane, The Loves of Mars and Venus.
    • \n
  • March 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Benjamin Hoadly, Bishop of Bangor, brings the Bangorian Controversy within the Church of England into the open by delivering a sermon to, and supposedly at the request of, King George I of Great Britain on The Nature of the Kingdom of Christ with the text "My kingdom is not of this world" (John 18:36), concluding there is no Biblical justification for church government.[2]
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Whydah Gally, flagship of "Black Sam" Bellamy, is wrecked in a storm off Wellfleet, Massachusetts. The Whydah sinks with a reputed four and a half tons of treasure on board, and all but two of her crew are lost, including Bellamy.
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Spain unites its South American colonies as the Viceroyalty of New Granada.
    • \n
  • June 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Premier Grand Lodge of England, the \'Modern\' and first Free-Masonic Grand Lodge (which merges with the Ancient Grand Lodge of England in 1813 to form the United Grand Lodge of England), is founded in London.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The month-long Siege of Belgrade ends with Prince Eugene of Savoy\'s Austrian troops capturing the city from the Ottoman Empire.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 The first known Druid revival ceremony is held by John Toland at Primrose Hill, in London, at the Autumnal Equinox, to found the Mother Grove, what is later to become the Ancient Order of Druids.
    • \n
  • September 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Guatemala earthquake: A 7.4 magnitude earthquake strikes Antigua Guatemala, destroying much of the city and making authorities consider moving the capital of Guatemala to a different location.
    • \n
  • November\n
      \n
    • Crews on two ships commanded by Benjamin Hornigold and Edward Teach attack and capture the British-built French Guineaman Concorde in the eastern Caribbean. Hornigold soon accepts a British amnesty for all pirates, but Teach rejects it and subsequently becomes known as Blackbeard.
      • \n
    • A rift between George I of Great Britain and his son the Prince of Wales leads to the latter being banished from the royal household.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Blackbeard teams up with Stede Bonnet but later takes his ship and demotes Bonnet to guest. The Queen Anne\'s Revenge and Revenge take several ships as prizes in the Caribbean. Blackbeard eventually adds two more ships to his party and sails north to the North American coast.
    • \n
  • December 24\xe2\x80\x93December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Christmas flood: A disastrous flood hits the North Sea coast between the Netherlands and Denmark; thousands die or lose their houses.
    • \n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • 1717 Omani invasion of Bahrain.
    • \n
  • Fran\xc3\xa7ois-Marie Arouet is sentenced to imprisonment in the Bastille for eleven months because of a satirical verse against the R\xc3\xa9gent of France and his infamous daughter Marie Louise \xc3\x89lisabeth d\'Orl\xc3\xa9ans who at the time was hiding an illegitimate pregnancy and soon to give birth;[3] Arouet will emerge with the pseudonym Voltaire and the completed text of his first play, \xc5\x92dipe.
    • \n
  • Tatar invasions in Transylvania, with many towns devastated, including Cavnic, Sighet and Dej.
    • \n
  • Lady Mary Wortley Montagu, wife of the British ambassador to Istanbul, has her son inoculated.
    • \n
  • Casa de Contrataci\xc3\xb3n ("House of Trade") is set up in C\xc3\xa1diz.
    • \n
  • Maharaja Pamheiba of Manipur is converted to Hinduism by Shantidas Goswami, and decrees it to be the official religion of his state.
    • \n
  • Most recent rupture of New Zealand\'s Alpine Fault with an earthquake estimated to have had a magnitude between 7.8 and 8.1.
    • \n
  • Thomas Fairchild, a nurseryman at Hoxton in the East End of London, becomes the first person to produce a successful scientific plant hybrid, Dianthus Caryophyllus barbatus, known as "Fairchild\'s Mule".[4]
    • \n
