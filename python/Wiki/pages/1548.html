Year 1548 (MDXLVIII) was a leap year starting on Sunday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Uedahara: Firearms are used for the first time on the battlefield in Japan, and Takeda Shingen is defeated by Murakami Yoshikiyo.
    • \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Sigismund II Augustus succeeds his father, Sigismund I the Old, as King of Poland and Grand Duke of Lithuania.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 A marriage treaty is signed between Scotland and France, whereby 5-year-old Mary, Queen of Scots, is betrothed to the future King Francis II of France.
    • \n
  • August 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Mary, Queen of Scots, leaves for France.
    • \n
  • October 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of La Paz, Bolivia, is founded.
    • \n
  • October 31 \xe2\x80\x93 At the first sejm of King Sigismund II Augustus of Poland, deputies demand that the king renounce his wife Barbara Radziwi\xc5\x82\xc5\x82.
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Siam attacks Tavoy beginning the Burmese\xe2\x80\x93Siamese War 1548.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Ming Dynasty government of China issues a decree banning all foreign trade and closes down all seaports along the coast; these Hai jin laws came during the Wokou wars with Japanese pirates, while the Portuguese began regular trade missions to China in 1549, and the ban on maritime trade was fully lifted in 1567.
    • \n
  • John Dee starts to study at Katholieke Universiteit Leuven.
    • \n
\n
