Year 1556 (MDLVI) was a leap year starting on Wednesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles I, having already abdicated as Holy Roman Emperor, resigns the Kingdom of Spain in favour of his son, Philip II, and retires to a monastery.
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Shaanxi earthquake, the deadliest earthquake in history, occurs with its epicenter in Shaanxi province, China; 830,000 people may have been killed.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Battle of Talikota fought between the Deccan Sultanates and the Kingdom of Vijayanagar.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Truce of Vaucelles: Fighting temporarily ends between France and Spain.
    • \n
  • February 14\n
      \n
    • Akbar ascends to the throne of the Mughal Empire at age 13; he will rule until his death in 1605 by which time most of the north and centre of the Indian subcontinent will be under his control.
      • \n
    • Archbishop of Canterbury Thomas Cranmer is declared a heretic.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 22 (approx.) \xe2\x80\x93 Sophia Jagiellon marries Henry V, Duke of Brunswick-L\xc3\xbcneburg.[1]
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 In Oxford, Thomas Cranmer is burned at the stake for treason.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 The Truce of Vaucelles collapses, and war resumes between Henry II of France and Philip II of Spain.
    • \n
  • November 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Second Battle of Panipat: Fifty miles north of Delhi, a Mogul Army defeats Hindu forces of General Hemu, to ensure Akbar the throne of India.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The kings of Spain take control of the Flanders region, including what is now the French d\xc3\xa9partement of Nord.
    • \n
  • The Plantations of Ireland are started in King\'s County (now County Offaly) and Queen\'s County (now County Laois), the earliest attempt at systematic ethnic cleansing in Ireland, by the Roman Catholic ruler Queen Mary I of England.
    • \n
  • Future King John III of Sweden becomes ruler of Finland as Hertig Johan.
    • \n
  • Ivan the Terrible conquers Astrakhan, opening the Volga River to Russian traffic and trade.
    • \n
  • Welser banking families of Augsburg lose colonial control of Venezuela.
    • \n
  • Lorenzo Priuli becomes Doge of Venice.
    • \n
  • The false Martin Guerre appears in the French village of Artigat.
    • \n
  • The first printing press in India is introduced by Jesuits at Saint Paul\'s College, Goa.
    • \n
