Year 1561 (MDLXI) was a common year starting on Wednesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 The Edict of Orleans suspends the persecution of the Huguenots in France.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Kingston Grammar School is founded in England.
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The citizens of Nuremberg see what appears to be an aerial battle followed by the appearance of a large black triangular object and a large crash (with smoke) outside the city. A news notice (an early form of newspaper) is printed on (April 14) describing the event.[1]
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Madrid is declared the capital of Spain by Philip II.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Old St Paul\'s Cathedral in the City of London is badly damaged by fire and the spire is destroyed after being struck by lightning. The spire is not rebuilt.
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Sweden conquers Livonia (modern Estonia).
    • \n
  • June 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Francis Coxe, an English astrologer, is pilloried at Cheapside in London and makes a public confession of his involvement in "sinistral and divelysh artes".[2]
    • \n
  • June 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Erik XIV is crowned King of Sweden.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Killing of the hated encomendero Pedro de Avenda\xc3\xb1o and two other Spaniards triggered the Second Great Rebellion of the Mapuche in the Arauco War.
    • \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Saint Basil\'s Cathedral in Moscow (started in 1534) is finished.
    • \n
  • August \xe2\x80\x93 English merchant Anthony Jenkinson arrives in Moscow on his second expedition to the Grand Duchy of Moscow.
    • \n
  • August 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Mary, Queen of Scots, is denied passage through England after returning from France. She arrives at Leith, Scotland on August 19.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 The Protestant reformer John Knox has a three-day debate in Maybole, Ayrshire, Scotland with Quintin Kennedy, commendator of Crossraguel Abbey, on transubstantiation. The result is inconclusive but Kennedy is fighting a losing battle against the Reformation, which had been confirmed by the Scottish government in 1560.
    • \n
  • October 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Fourth Battle of Kawanakajima: Takeda Shingen defeats Uesugi Kenshin in the climax of their ongoing conflicts.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The first Calvinists settle in England after fleeing Flanders.
    • \n
  • Publication of the Anglo-Genevan metrical psalter including the "Old 100th", the version of the hymn "All People That on Earth Do Dwell" made from Psalm 100, attributed to the probably-Scottish clergyman and biblical translator William Kethe, exiled in Geneva.[3]
    • \n
  • Ruy L\xc3\xb3pez de Segura develops modern techniques of chess playing in Spain.
    • \n
  • First publication (anonymously in London) of William Baldwin\'s Beware the Cat (written early 1553), an early example of extended fiction (and specifically of horror fiction) in English. This edition appears to have been suppressed and no copies survive.[4]
    • \n
  • Between 1561 and 1670 3,229 alleged witches are executed in southwestern Germany, most by burning.
    • \n
