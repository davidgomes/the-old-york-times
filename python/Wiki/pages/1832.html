\n1832 (MDCCCXXXII) was a leap year starting on Sunday (dominical letter AG) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Friday (dominical letter CB) of the Julian calendar, the 1832nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 832nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 32nd year of the 19th century, and the 3rd year of the 1830s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1832 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Florida Legislative Council grants a city charter for Jacksonville, Florida.
    • \n
  • February 12\n
      \n
    • Ecuador annexes the Gal\xc3\xa1pagos Islands.
      • \n
    • A cholera epidemic in London claims at least 3,000 victims. It spreads to France and North America later in the year.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 In Hiram, Ohio, a group of men beat, tar and feather Mormon leader Joseph Smith.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 United States: The Black Hawk War begins.
    • \n
  • May 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of London creates an independent Kingdom of Greece. Otto of Wittelsbach, Prince of Bavaria, is chosen King. Thus begins the history of modern Greece.
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Greece is recognized as a sovereign nation; the Treaty of Constantinople ends the Greek War of Independence in July.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Egyptians, aided by Maronites, seize Acre from the Ottoman Empire after a 7-month siege.
    • \n
  • May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Francois Arban, early French balloonist makes his 1st ascent.[1]
    • \n
  • May 30\n
      \n
    • Germany: Hambacher Fest, a demonstration for civil liberties and national unity, ends with no result.
      • \n
    • Canada: The Rideau Canal in eastern Ontario is opened.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 5\xe2\x80\x936 \xe2\x80\x93 France: June Rebellion, anti-monarchist riots, chiefly by students, in Paris.
    • \n
  • June 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Reform Act becomes law in Britain.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Andr\xc3\xa9-Michel Guerry presents his Essay on moral statistics of France, to the French Academy of Sciences, a significant step in the founding of empirical social science.
    • \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Durham University is founded in the north of England by act of Parliament given royal assent by King William IV.
    • \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Commissioner of Indian Affairs post created within the United States Department of War.
    • \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 United States Survey of the Coast revived (with Department of the Treasury).
    • \n
  • August 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Bad Axe Massacre ends the last major Native American rebellion east of the Mississippi in the U.S.
    • \n
  • August 7 \xe2\x80\x93 William Howley, Archbishop of Canterbury, has his coach attacked by an angry mob on his first official visit to Canterbury because of his opposition to the Reform Act in Britain.
    • \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 China ceases production of iron shuriken.[citation needed]
    • \n
  • August 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Black Hawk (Sauk leader) surrenders to the United States authorities, ending the Black Hawk War.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Belvedere College, Dublin, is founded by the order of the Jesuit Society of Ireland.[citation needed]
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles Carroll, the last surviving signer of the Declaration of Independence dies at his home in Maryland at age 95.
    • \n
  • November 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Wabash College, a small, private, liberal arts college for men, is founded.
    • \n
  • December 3 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. presidential election, 1832: Andrew Jackson is re-elected president.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Antwerp: The last remaining Dutch enforcement, the citadel, is under French attack.
    • \n
  • December 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Konya: The Egyptians defeat the main Ottoman army in central Anatolia.
    • \n
  • December 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Siege of Antwerp ends with the Netherlands losing the city.
    • \n
