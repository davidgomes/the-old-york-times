\n1740 (MDCCXL) was a leap year starting on Friday (dominical letter CB) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Julian calendar, the 1740th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 740th year of the 2nd millennium, the 40th year of the 18th century, and the 1st year of the 1740s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1740 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The North Carolina General Assembly incorporates the town of Newton as Wilmington, North Carolina, named for Spencer Compton, 1st Earl of Wilmington and patron of Royal Governor Gabriel Johnston.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nFrederick II comes to power in Prussia.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April 8 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Austrian Succession: The Royal Navy captures the Spanish ship of the line Princesa off Cape Finisterre and takes her into British service.
    • \n
  • May 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick II comes to power in Prussia upon the death of his father, Frederick William I.
    • \n
  • June 26 \xe2\x80\x93 War of Jenkins\' Ear \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Fort Mose: A Spanish column of 300 regular troops, free black militia and Indian auxiliaries storms Britain\'s strategically crucial position of Fort Mose, Florida.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Pogrom: Jews are expelled from Little Russia.
    • \n
  • August 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The song Rule, Britannia! is first performed at Cliveden, the country home of Frederick, Prince of Wales, in England.[1]
    • \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Benedict XIV succeeds Pope Clement XII as the 247th pope.
    • \n
  • October 9\xe2\x80\x9322 \xe2\x80\x93 Batavia massacre: Troops of the Dutch East India Company massacre 5,000\xe2\x80\x9310,000 Chinese Indonesians in Batavia.[2]
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nOath of Fealty to Maria Theresa
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • October 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Maria Theresa inherits the hereditary dominions of the Habsburg Monarchy (Austria, Bohemia, Hungary and modern-day Belgium) under the terms of the Pragmatic Sanction of 1713. However, her succession to the Holy Roman Empire is contested widely because she is a woman.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 Hertford College, Oxford, is founded for the first time.[3]
    • \n
  • November 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Samuel Richardson\'s popular and influential epistolary novel Pamela; or, Virtue Rewarded is published in London.
    • \n
  • December 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick II of Prussia invades the Habsburg possession of Silesia, starting the War of the Austrian Succession.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • By an act of the Parliament of Great Britain, alien immigrants (including Huguenots and Jews) in the colonies receive British nationality.
    • \n
  • Enfield, North Carolina, is founded.
    • \n
  • Adam Smith enters Balliol College, Oxford.
    • \n
  • George Whitefield founds the Bethesda Orphanage near Savannah, Georgia.
    • \n
  • Spain begins construction on Fort Matanzas in the Matanzas Inlet, approximately 15 miles (24 km) south of St. Augustine, Florida.
    • \n
  • Annual British iron production reaches 17,000 tons.
    • \n
  • The University of Pennsylvania is officially established.
