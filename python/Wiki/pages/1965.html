1965 (MCMLXV) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1965th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 965th year of the 2nd millennium, the 65th year of the 20th century, and the 6th year of the 1960s decade.

January[edit]

\n
Main article: January 1965

    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Mirzapur Cadet College formally opens for academic activities in East Pakistan (now Bangladesh).
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Prime Minister of Northern Ireland and the Taoiseach of the Republic of Ireland meet for the first time in 43 years.
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The unmanned Gemini 2 is launched on a suborbital test of various spacecraft systems.
    • \n
  • January 20\n
      \n

      • \n
    • Indonesian President Sukarno announces the withdrawal of the Indonesian government from the United Nations.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Anti-Hindi agitations break out in India, because of which Hindi does not get "National Language" status and remains one of the 23 official languages of India.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The state funeral of Sir Winston Churchill takes place in London with the largest assembly of statesmen in the world until the 2005 funeral of Pope John Paul II.[1]

Main article: February 1965
\n
    \n

  • February 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Gambia becomes independent from the United Kingdom.
    • \n

    • Suat Hayri \xc3\x9crg\xc3\xbcpl\xc3\xbc forms the new (interim) government of Turkey (29th government).
      • \n

March[edit]

\n
Main article: March 1965
\n
    \n
  • March 2\n
      \n
    • Vietnam War: Operation Rolling Thunder \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Air Force 2d Air Division, United States Navy and Republic of Vietnam Air Force begin a 3\xc2\xbd-year aerial bombardment campaign against North Vietnam.
      • \n
    • The film of The Sound of Music premieres at the Rivoli Theater in New York City.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Bloody Sunday: Some 200 Alabama State Troopers attack 525 civil rights demonstrators in Selma, Alabama as they attempt to march to the state capitol of Montgomery.
    • \n
  • March 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Vietnam War: Some 3,500 United States Marines arrive in Da Nang, South Vietnam, becoming the first American ground combat troops in Vietnam.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The second attempt to march from Selma to Montgomery, Alabama, under the leadership of Martin Luther King, Jr., stops at the bridge that was the site of Bloody Sunday, to hold a prayer service and return to Selma, in obedience to a court restraining order. White supremacists beat up white Unitarian Universalist minister James J. Reeb later that day in Selma.
    • \n
  • March 10\n
      \n
    • An engagement is announced between Princess Margriet of the Netherlands and Pieter van Vollenhoven, who will become the first commoner and the first Dutchman to marry into the Dutch Royal Family.
      • \n
    • Goldie, a London Zoo golden eagle, is recaptured 12 days after her escape.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 White Unitarian Universalist minister James J. Reeb, beaten by White supremacists in Selma, Alabama on March 9 following the second march from Selma, dies in a hospital in Birmingham, Alabama.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 President Lyndon B. Johnson makes his "We Shall Overcome" speech.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Police clash with 600 SNCC marchers in Montgomery, Alabama.
    • \n
  • March 17\n
      \n
    • In Montgomery, Alabama, 1,600 civil rights marchers demonstrate at the Courthouse.
      • \n
    • In response to the events of March 7 and 9 in Selma, Alabama, President Lyndon B. Johnson sends a bill to Congress that forms the basis for the Voting Rights Act of 1965. It is passed by the Senate May 26, the House July 10, and signed into law by President Johnson August 6.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 18\n
      \n
    • Cosmonaut Alexey Leonov, leaving his spacecraft Voskhod 2 for 12 minutes, becomes the first person to walk in space.
      • \n
    • A United States federal judge rules that SCLC has the lawful right to march to Montgomery, Alabama to petition for \'redress of grievances\'.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The wreck of the SS Georgiana, reputed to have been the most powerful Confederate cruiser ever built, is discovered off the Isle of Palms, South Carolina, by teenage diver E. Lee Spence, exactly 102 years after she was sunk with a million dollar cargo, while attempting to run past the Union blockade into Charleston.
    • \n
  • March 20\n
      \n
    • "Poup\xc3\xa9e de cire, poup\xc3\xa9e de son", sung by France Gall (music and lyrics by Serge Gainsbourg) wins the Eurovision Song Contest 1965 for Luxembourg.
      • \n
    • The Indo-Pakistani War of 1965 begins.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 21\n
      \n
    • Ranger program: NASA launches Ranger 9, which is the last in a series of unmanned lunar space probes.
      • \n
    • Martin Luther King, Jr. leads 3,200 civil rights activists in the third march from Selma, Alabama to the capitol in Montgomery.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Nicolae Ceau\xc8\x99escu becomes the first secretary of the Romanian Communist Party.
    • \n
  • March 23\n
      \n
    • Events of March 23, 1965: Large student demonstration in Morocco, joined by discontented masses, meets with violent police and military repression.
      • \n
    • Gemini 3: NASA launches the United States\' first 2-person crew (Gus Grissom, John Young) into Earth orbit.
      • \n
    • The first issue of The Vigilant is published from Khartoum.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Martin Luther King, Jr. and 25,000 civil rights activists successfully end the 4-day march from Selma, Alabama, to the capitol in Montgomery.
    • \n
  • March 30\n
      \n
    • Funeral services are held for Detroit homemaker Viola Liuzzo, who was shot dead by 4 Klansmen as she drove marchers back to Selma at night after the civil rights march.
      • \n
    • The second ODECA charter, signed on 12 December 1962, becomes effective.
      • \n

April[edit]

\n
Main article: April 1965
\n
    \n
  • April 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The world\'s first space nuclear power reactor, SNAP-10A, is launched by the United States from Vandenberg AFB, California. The reactor operates for 43 days and remains in low Earth orbit.
    • \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 At the 37th Academy Awards, My Fair Lady wins 8 Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director. Rex Harrison wins an Oscar for Best Actor. Mary Poppins takes home 5 Oscars. Julie Andrews wins an Academy Award for Best Actress for her performance in the title role. Sherman Brothers receives 2 Oscars including Best Song, "Chim Chim Cher-ee".
    • \n
  • April 6\n
      \n
    • The Intelsat I ("Early Bird") communications satellite is launched. It becomes operational May 2 and is placed in commercial service in June.
      • \n
    • The British Government announces the cancellation of the TSR-2 aircraft project.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 9\n
      \n
    • The West German parliament extends the statute of limitations on Nazi war crimes.
      • \n
    • In Houston, the Harris County Domed Stadium (more commonly known as the Astrodome) opens.
      • \n
    • Charlie Brown and the Peanuts Gang appear on the cover of Time.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The 1965 Palm Sunday tornado outbreak: An estimated 51 tornadoes (47 confirmed) hit in 6 Midwestern states, killing between 256 to 271 people and injuring some 1,500 more.
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 In Cold Blood killers Richard Hickock and Perry Smith, convicted of murdering 4 members of the Herbert Clutter family of Holcomb, Kansas, are executed by hanging at the Kansas State Penitentiary for Men in Lansing, Kansas.
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Students for a Democratic Society march against the Vietnam War draws 25,000 protestors to Washington, D.C.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Consecration of Saint Clement of Ohrid Macedonian Orthodox Cathedral in Toronto, Canada.
    • \n
  • April 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The New York World\'s Fair in Flushing Meadows, New York, reopens.
    • \n
  • April 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Pennine Way officially opens.
    • \n
  • April 24\n
      \n
    • The 1965 Yerevan demonstrations start in Yerevan, demanding recognition of the Armenian Genocide.
      • \n
    • The bodies of Portuguese opposition politician Humberto Delgado and his secretary Arajaryr Moreira de Campos are found in a forest near Villanueva del Fresno, Spain (they were killed February 12).
      • \n
    • In the Dominican Republic, officers and civilians loyal to deposed President Juan Bosch mutiny against the right-wing junta running the country, setting up a provisional government. Forces loyal to the deposed military-imposed government stage a countercoup the next day, and civil war breaks out, although the new government retains its hold on power.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Teenage sniper Michael Clark kills 3 and wounds others shooting at cars from a hilltop along Highway 101 just south of Orcutt, California. Sixteen-year-old Clark kills himself as police rush the hilltop.
    • \n
  • April 28\n
      \n
    • U.S. troops occupy the Dominican Republic.
      • \n
    • Vietnam War: Prime Minister of Australia Robert Menzies announces that the country will substantially increase its number of troops in South Vietnam, supposedly at the request of the Saigon government (it is later revealed that Menzies had asked the leadership in Saigon to send the request at the behest of the Americans).
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Australia announces that it is sending an infantry battalion to support the South Vietnam government.
    • \n

    • \n
  • May 12\n
      \n
    • West Germany and Israel establish diplomatic relations.
      • \n
    • The Italian liner SS Michelangelo enters service.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 A West German court of appeals condemns the behavior of ex-defense minister Franz Josef Strauss during the Spiegel scandal.
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The largest antiwar teach-in to date begins at Berkeley, California, attended by 30,000.
    • \n
  • May 22 \xe2\x80\x93\n
      \n

    • The first skateboarding championship is held.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Muhammad Ali knocks out Sonny Liston in the first round of their championship rematch with the "Phantom Punch" at the Central Maine Civic Center in Lewiston.
    • \n
  • May 27 - Internazionale beats Benfica 1-0 at the San Siro, Milan and wins the 1964-65 European Cup (football).
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 A mining accident in Dhanbad, India kills 274.
    • \n
  • May 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Racing driver Jim Clark wins the Indianapolis 500, and later wins the Formula One world driving championship in the same year.
    • \n
