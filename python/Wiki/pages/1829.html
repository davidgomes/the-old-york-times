1829 (MDCCCXXIX) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1829th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 829th year of the 2nd millennium, the 29th year of the 19th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1820s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1829 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 August Klingemann\'s adaptation of Johann Wolfgang von Goethe\'s Faust premieres in Braunschweig.[1]
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Tarqui took place.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Andrew Jackson succeeds John Quincy Adams as the seventh President of the United States.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 German composer Felix Mendelssohn conducts the first performance of Johann Sebastian Bach\'s St Matthew Passion since the latter\'s death in 1750, in Berlin; the success of this performance sparks a revival of interest in Bach.
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Greece receives autonomy from the Ottoman Empire in the London Protocol, signed by Russia, France and Britain, effectively ending the Greek War of Independence. Greece continues to seek full independence through diplomatic negotiations with the three Great Powers.
    • \n
  • March 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Pius VIII succeeds Pope Leo XII as the 253rd pope.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April\xe2\x80\x93September \xe2\x80\x93 Felix Mendelssohn pays his first visit to Britain. This includes the first London performance of his concert overture to A Midsummer Night\'s Dream and his trip to Fingal\'s Cave.[2]
    • \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Vicente Guerrero becomes the president of Mexico.
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Mexican city of Cuautla, Morelos, is founded.
    • \n
  • April 13 - Passage of the Catholic Relief Act by Parliament of the United Kingdom granting a substantial measure of Catholic emancipation in Britain and Ireland.[3]
    • \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The patent for an instrument called the accordion is applied for by Cyrill Demian (Officially approved on May 23.)
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Joseph Smith claims to receive the Aaronic Priesthood from John the Baptist.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Philadelphia Inquirer is founded as The Pennsylvania Inquirer.
    • \n
  • June 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Swan River Colony (later to become the cities of Perth and Fremantle) is founded in Western Australia. This secures the western \'third\' of the Australian landmass for the British.
    • \n
  • June 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Slave trade: HMS Pickle captures the armed slave ship Voladora off the coast of Cuba.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Oxford University Boat Club wins the first inter-university Boat Race,[3] rowed at Henley-on-Thames.[4]
    • \n
  • June 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Peel establishes the Metropolitan Police Service in London, the first modern police force. The first officers, known by the nickname "bobbies", go on patrol on September 29.[3]
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War (1828\xe2\x80\x9329): Russian Field-Marshal Hans Karl von Diebitsch launches the Transbalkan offensive, which brings the Russian army within 68 km of Istanbul.
    • \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 George Shillibeer begins operating the first bus service in London.[5]
    • \n
  • July 23 \xe2\x80\x93 In the United States, William Burt obtains the first patent for a form of typewriter, the typographer.
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 France: The Prince de Polignac succeeds the Vicomte de Martignac as Prime Minister of France.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 First ascent of Finsteraarhorn, the highest summit of the Bernese Alps.
    • \n
  • August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Mrs. Helen Dance, wife of the captain of the ship Sulphur, cuts down a tree to mark the founding day of the town of Perth in Western Australia.
    • \n
  • September 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War (1828\xe2\x80\x9329): The Treaty of Adrianople gains for Russia some territory at the mouth of the Danube and along the eastern coast of the Black Sea.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 1 \xe2\x80\x93 South African College inaugurated in Cape Town.
    • \n
  • October 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Rail transport: Stephenson\'s Rocket wins the Rainhill Trials.
    • \n
  • November 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The original Welland Canal opens for a trial run with a ceremony at Port Dalhousie.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 India: In the face of fierce opposition, British Lord William Bentinck carries a regulation declaring that all who abet suttee in India are guilty of culpable homicide.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • King\'s College London is founded under the patronage of King George IV and the Prime Minister The Duke of Wellington. It will become the third official university in England.
    • \n
  • The Chalmers University of Technology is founded in Gothenburg.
    • \n
  • The last of the Bounty mutineers dies at Pitcairn Island.
    • \n
