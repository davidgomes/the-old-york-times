\n1704 (MDCCIV) was a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Julian calendar, the 1704th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 704th year of the 2nd millennium, the 4th year of the 18th century, and the 5th year of the 1700s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1704 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929. In the Swedish calendar it was a leap year starting on Friday, one day ahead of the Julian and ten days behind the Gregorian calendar.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nAugust 4: Gibraltar.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • January 25\xe2\x80\x9326 \xe2\x80\x93 Apalachee massacre: English colonists from the Province of Carolina and their native allies stage a series of brutal raids against a largely pacific population of Apalachee in Spanish Florida.
    • \n
  • February 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Raid on Deerfield (Queen Anne\'s War): French Canadians and Native Americans sack Deerfield, Massachusetts, killing over 50 English colonists.
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 In America, Mardi Gras is celebrated with the Masque de la Mobile in the capital of Louisiana (New France), Mobile, Alabama.
    • \n
  • April 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Biskupice between the Hungarians (Kurucs) and Danes.
    • \n
  • April 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The first regular newspaper in the Thirteen Colonies of British North America, The Boston News-Letter, is published.
    • \n
  • May 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Smolenice: Kuruc rebels defeat the Austrian army and its allies.
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Koronc\xc3\xb3: Austrians and their allies from Denmark, Prussia, Croatia, Germany and Vojvodina defeat the Kurucs.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Daniel Defoe documents the Great Storm of 1703 in England with eyewitness testimonies in The Storm.
    • \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Great Northern War \xe2\x80\x93 Charles XII of Sweden forces the election of his ally Stanis\xc5\x82aw Leszczy\xc5\x84ski as King of Poland in place of Augustus II the Strong.
    • \n
  • August 3 (July 23 Old Style) \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Spanish Succession \xe2\x80\x93 Capture of Gibraltar from Spain by English and Dutch forces under Sir George Rooke.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nAugust 13: Battle of Blenheim.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • August 13 (August 2 OS) \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Spanish Succession \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Blenheim: Allied troops under John Churchill, Earl of Marlborough and Prince Eugene of Savoy defeat the Franco-Bavarian army.
    • \n
  • August 24 (August 13 OS) \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Spanish Succession \xe2\x80\x93 French and English fleets clash off M\xc3\xa1laga, causing heavy casualties in both sides but without sinking any ships.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Spanish Succession \xe2\x80\x93 The Twelfth Siege of Gibraltar by French and Spanish troops begins.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Great Northern War: Russian troops under Tsar Peter the Great capture Tartu and Narva.
    • \n
  • The Sultanate of Brunei cedes its north-east territories to the Sultanate of Sulu.
    • \n
  • The lower three counties of the Province of Pennsylvania become the colony of Delaware.
    • \n
  • An earthquake strikes Gondar in Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • A Tale of a Tub, the first major satire by Jonathan Swift (written 1694\xe2\x80\x931697), is published in London, running through three editions this year.
    • \n
  • Isaac Newton publishes his Opticks.
    • \n
  • The Students\' Monument is built in Aiud, Romania.
    • \n
  • Rome decrees that Roman ceremonial practice in Latin (not in Chinese) is to be the law for Chinese missions.
    • \n
  • Thomas Darley purchases the bay Arabian horse Darley Arabian in Aleppo, Syria, and ships him to stud in England where he becomes the most important foundation sire of all modern thoroughbred racing bloodstock.
    • \n
