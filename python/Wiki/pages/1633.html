\n1633 (MDCXXXIII) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1633rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 633rd year of the 2nd millennium, the 33rd year of the 17th century, and the 4th year of the 1630s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1633 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Galileo Galilei arrives in Rome for his trial before the Inquisition.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Samuel de Champlain reclaims his role as commander of New France on behalf of Cardinal Richelieu.
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Galileo Galilei convicted of heresy by the Roman Catholic Church [1]
    • \n
  • June 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles I is crowned King of Scots, at St Giles\' Cathedral, Edinburgh
    • \n
  • June 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Roman Catholic Church forces Galileo Galilei to recant his heliocentric view of the Solar System: Eppur si muove (Italian) (Which, in fact, he did not say).
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Liaoluo Bay: A large Ming dynasty fleet under Zheng Zhilong defeats a Dutch East India Company fleet at the island of Quemoy.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Jews of Pozna\xc5\x84 are granted the privilege of forbidding Christians to enter into their city quarter.
    • \n
  • In Ethiopia, the Emperor Fasilides expels the Jesuit missionaries.
    • \n
  • Shogun Tokugawa Iemitsu of Japan begins issuing the Sakoku Edicts outlawing Christianity, beginning a policy of extreme isolationism until 1853.
    • \n
  • Completion of St Columb\'s Cathedral, Derry, Ireland, the first post-Reformation Anglican cathedral built in the British Isles and the first Protestant cathedral built in Europe.[2]
    • \n
  • Mission San Luis de Apalachee is built in the New World by two Spanish friars.
    • \n
  • A professorship in Arabic studies is founded at Cambridge University.
    • \n
