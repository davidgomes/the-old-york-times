\n1638 (MDCXXXVIII) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Julian calendar, the 1638th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 638th year of the 2nd millennium, the 38th year of the 17th century, and the 9th year of the 1630s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1638 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Scottish National Covenant is signed in Edinburgh.
    • \n
  • March 3 \xe2\x80\x93 A mercenary army under Bernard of Saxe-Weimar fighting for France defeats imperial forces at the Battle of Rheinfelden.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Hamburg is signed by France and Sweden.
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Settlers from Sweden arrive on the ships Kalmar Nyckel and Fogel Grip to establish the settlement of New Sweden in Delaware, beginning the Swedish colonization of the Americas.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Anne Hutchinson is banished from the Massachusetts Bay Colony for heresy and goes to Rhode Island.
    • \n
  • April 3 \xe2\x80\x93 John Wheelwright is banished from Boston and founds Exeter, New Hampshire.
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Shogunate forces defeat the last remnants of the Shimabara Rebellion in the fortress of Hara.
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Construction begins on the Red Fort in Delhi (India) for Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan who is transferring his capital there from Agra.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Signing of the Kandyan Treaty between the Singhala King Rajasimha II and the Dutch to rid Ceylon of the Portuguese.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish troops under Ferdinand of Austria defeat a much larger Dutch force near Antwerp at the Battle of Kallo during the Eighty Years\' War.
    • \n
  • June 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Patriarch Cyril of Constantinople is deposed for high treason and strangled and thrown into the sea by Janissaries on Ottoman Sultan Murad IV\'s command.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • September 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Hartford is signed, ending the Pequot War between British American colonists and the Pequot.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 John Spofford arrives in Boston Harbor on the ship John of London and is one of the first people to establish Rowely, Essex County, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • October 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Thunderstorm breaks out in Widecombe-in-the-Moor, England.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 The General Assembly of the Church of Scotland is summoned to Glasgow by King Charles I of England.
    • \n
  • December 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Mazarin becomes first adviser to French potentate Richelieu on the death of Leclerc du Tremblay.
    • \n
  • December 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The full moon is in total eclipse from 1:12 to 2:47 UT and the solstice occurs later in the day at 16:05 UT.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Scottish Covenanters meet at Muchalls Castle to compose responses to the Bishops of Aberdeen.
    • \n
  • Pedro Teixeira makes the first ascent of the Amazon River, from its mouth to Quito, Ecuador. The same trip had been made in the opposite direction in 1541.
    • \n
  • Dutch merchant Willem Kieft is appointed Director of New Amsterdam by the Dutch West India Company.
    • \n
  • The Netherlands colonizes Mauritius.
    • \n
  • The Dutch settle in Ceylon.
    • \n
  • The Finnish postal service, Suomen Posti, is founded.
    • \n
  • New Haven, the first planned city in America, is founded.
    • \n
  • Sultan Murad IV captures Baghdad.
    • \n
  • Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan and his sons capture the city of Kandahar from the Safavids.
    • \n
  • Shipwrecked English buccaneer Peter Wallace called "Ballis" by the Spanish, settles near and perhaps gives his name to the Belize River, the first known European settlement in Belize.
    • \n
  • The Beijing Gazette makes an official switch in its production process of newspapers, from woodblock printing to movable type printing; private newspapers in Ming dynasty China were first mentioned in 1582.
    • \n
