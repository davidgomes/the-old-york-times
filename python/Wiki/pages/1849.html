\n1849 (MDCCCXLIX) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1849th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 849th year of the 2nd millennium, the 49th year of the 19th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1840s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1849 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 1\n
      \n
    • After 10 days, the insurrection in Brescia is ended by Austrian troops.
      • \n
    • The Hungarian Revolutionary Army under the leadership of Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey starts the victorious Spring Campaign, which leads to the liberation of much of Hungary from the Austrian forces.
      • \n
    • In the battle of Hatvan the Hungarian revolutionary army under the command of Andr\xc3\xa1s G\xc3\xa1sp\xc3\xa1r defeat the Austrians led by general Franz Schlik.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Revolutions of 1848 in the German states end in failure as King Frederick William IV of Prussia refuses to accept the offer of the Frankfurt National Assebly to be crowned as German emperor.
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Hungarians under the generals Gy\xc3\xb6rgy Klapka and J\xc3\xa1nos Damjanich defeat the Austrian-Croatian army led by Franz Schlik and Josip Jela\xc4\x8di\xc4\x87 at the battle of T\xc3\xa1pi\xc3\xb3bicske.
    • \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The main Hungarian forces led by Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey defeat at the Battle of Isaszeg the main imperial forces led by Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Gr\xc3\xa4tz, forcing them to retreat towards West.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarians led by J\xc3\xa1nos Damjanich defeat at V\xc3\xa1c the Austrians led by Christian G\xc3\xb6tz, who dies after the battle due to his injuries.
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Because of his series of defeats suffered from the Hungarian army, Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Gr\xc3\xa4tz is released from the supreme command of the Austrian forces in Hungary and replaced by Ludwig von Welden.
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian revolutionary parliament declares in Debrecen independence from the Habsburg Empire.
    • \n
  • April 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian revolutionary army led by Gy\xc3\xb6rgy Klapka and J\xc3\xa1nos Damjanich defeat at Nagysall\xc3\xb3 the Austrian army led by Lt. Gen. Ludwig von Wohlgemuth.
    • \n
  • April 21\n
      \n
    • Great Famine (Ireland): 96 inmates of the overcrowded Ballinrobe Union Workhouse have died over the course of the preceding week from illness and other famine-related conditions, a record high.
      • \n
    • The Austrian government asks Russian help against the Hungarian revolution. The tsar Nicholas I of Russia, agrees to send troops against Hungary.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Kennedy arrived to America.
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 James Bruce, 8th Earl of Elgin, the Governor General of Canada, signs the Rebellion Losses Bill, outraging Montreal\'s English population and triggering the Montreal Riots.
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Kom\xc3\xa1rom. The Hungarian forces relieve the city and castle with the same name from a long Austrian siege. The Austrian imperial forces and their Croatian, Romanian and Serbian allies are chased out from Hungary or near the borders of the country.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Giuseppe Garibaldi enters Rome to defend it from the French troops of General Charles Oudinot.
    • \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 The Second Carlist War ends in Spain.
    • \n
  • May 2 \xe2\x80\x93 A new independent Hungarian government, led by Bertalan Szemere, is formed. The head of state of Hungary becomes Lajos Kossuth as governor president.
    • \n
  • May 3\n
      \n
    • The May Uprising in Dresden, last of the Revolutions of 1848 in the German states, begins. Richard Wagner is among the participants.
      • \n
    • The Mississippi River levee at Sauv\xc3\xa9\'s Crevasse breaks, flooding much of New Orleans.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The May Uprising in Dresden is suppressed by the Kingdom of Saxony.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Astor Place Riot takes place in Manhattan over a dispute between two Shakespearean actors. Over 20 people are killed.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Troops of the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies take Palermo and crush the republican government of Sicily.
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The St. Louis Fire starts when a steamboat catches fire and nearly burns down the entire city.
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian army led by Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey capture the Castle of Buda, liberating the Hungarian capital city completely. The leader of the defending Austrian forces, general Heinrich Hentzi dies because of his injuries. The Hungarian govern moves back from Debrecen to Budapest.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Julius Jacob von Haynau replaces Ludwig von Welden in the leadership of the Austrian forces in Hungary, because of the failure of the latter to stop the advance of the Hungarian forces.
    • \n
  • June 5\n
      \n
    • Denmark becomes a constitutional monarchy.
      • \n
    • The first Russian troops led by Lieutenant General Fyodor Sergeyevich Panyutin, who come in the aid of the Habsburgs, cross the Hungarian border at Pozsony, in order to crush the Hungarian revolution.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Fort Worth, Texas, is founded.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The main Russian forces led by Ivan Paskevich cross the Hungarian border, and together with the Austrian troops led by Julius Jacob von Haynau start the final attack against the Hungarian revolution. Now the Hungarian revolutionary troops, numbering 173 000 soldiers, which even before the Russian attack were in inferiority regarding their numbers, and the quality of their weapons and war industry, faced a force of 370 000 Austro-Russian forces,[3] and other tens of thousands of Croatian, Serbian and Romanian insurgents, who served the Habsburg imperial interests.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Russian troops under the command of Alexander von L\xc3\xbcders break in Transylvania and together with the Austrian forces start to operate against the Hungarian troops led by J\xc3\xb3zef Bem.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Ruso-Austrian army led by Julius Jacob von Haynau defeats the Hungarians under the command of Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey at Pered.
    • \n
  • June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Austrian army led by Julius Jacob von Haynau defeats the Hungarians led by Ern\xc5\x91 Poeltenberg at Gy\xc5\x91r. The Hungarian army is forced to retreat towards Budapest.

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian army led by Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey repulses the combined attack of the Austrian and Russian troops led by Julius Jacob von Haynau at the Battle of Kom\xc3\xa1rom. During the battle G\xc3\xb6rgey suffers a heavy head injury, which prevents him from taking advantage of this success.
    • \n
  • July 3 \xe2\x80\x93 French troops occupy Rome; the Roman Republic surrenders.
    • \n
  • July 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Fredericia: the Danish Army beats the Prussian army at Fredericia, Jutland, thereby putting an end to the Prussian/Danish War until 1864.
    • \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian army led by Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey is defeated by the Austrians led by Julius Jacob von Haynau at the third Battle of Kom\xc3\xa1rom.
    • \n
  • July 14\n
      \n
    • Because of the imminent Austrian attack, the Hungarian government moves from Budapest to Szeged.
      • \n
    • Hungarian troops led by Richard Guyon, defeat the Croatian-Austrian army led by Josip Jela\xc4\x8di\xc4\x87 at Kishegyes, securing southern Hungary for the revolutionary government.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • July 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Undecided battle at V\xc3\xa1c between Hungarians led by Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey and the Russians led by Ivan Paskevich. The Russians are unsuccessful in destroying the Hungarian army, which retreats towards east.
    • \n
  • July 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The French scientist Hippolyte Fizeau measures the speed of light with an instrument placed on the Earth.
    • \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian government led by Bertalan Szemere promulgates the Nationality Law which gave important rights to the nationalities of Hungary, like the right to use their mother tongue in school, church, army, court and administration. The Romanians are declared nation and not minority in Transylvania. The Jews receive equality thanks to the Emancipation Decree.[4]
    • \n
  • July 31 \xe2\x80\x93 The Russian troops in Transylvania led by Alexander von L\xc3\xbcders crush the Hungarian forces under the lead of J\xc3\xb3zef Bem in the Battle of Segesv\xc3\xa1r. The Hungarian poet and revolutionary S\xc3\xa1ndor Pet\xc5\x91fi is killed in the battle by the Russians.
    • \n
  • August 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The Russian main forces under Ivan Paskevich defeat the Hungarian army under J\xc3\xb3zsef Nagys\xc3\xa1ndor at Debrecen.
    • \n
  • August 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hungarian defenders of Kom\xc3\xa1rom led by Gy\xc3\xb6rgy Klapka destroy the besieging Austrian forces, liberating Gy\xc5\x91r and Sz\xc3\xa9kesfeh\xc3\xa9rv\xc3\xa1r. But this victory comes too late to change the course of the military events in the eastern part of the country, where the Hungarian forces are about to crumble under the heavy Austro-Russian pressure.
    • \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The Austrian forces under the lead of Julius Jacob von Haynau defeat the Hungarian main forces under Henryk Dembi\xc5\x84ski in the Battle of Sz\xc5\x91reg.
    • \n
  • August 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The main Ruso-Austrian forces led by Julius Jacob von Haynau win a decisive victory against the Hungarians led by J\xc3\xb3zef Bem in the Battle of Temesv\xc3\xa1r.
    • \n
  • August 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Lajos Kossuth and the Hungarian Government of Bertalan Szemere resign and give all powers to the hands of Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey. After this Kossuth, the ministries and many military officers live Hungary and ask asylum in Turkey.
    • \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The main Hungarian army under the lead of Arthur G\xc3\xb6rgey capitulate before the Russian troops led by Theodor von R\xc3\xbcdiger at Vil\xc3\xa1gos. The end of the Hungarian revolution.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Venice (the Republic of San Marco) surrenders to Austrian troops after a 4-month siege.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The first segment of the Pennsylvania Railroad, from Lewistown to Harrisburg, Pennsylvania, opens for service.
    • \n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Kom\xc3\xa1rom, the last bastion of the Hungarian revolution surrenders to the Austrian forces.

      \n
    • The 13 Martyrs of Arad are executed after the Hungarian War of Independence, in repression by the Austrian authorities led by Julius Jacob von Haynau. These martyrs were the generals of the Hungarian revolutionary army, who did not flee from Hungary after the suppression of the Hungarian revolution by the Russo-Austrian forces.
      • \n
    • Lajos Batthy\xc3\xa1ny, the first Hungarian prime minister is executed by Austria in Pest.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 Austin College receives a charter in Huntsville, Texas.
    • \n
  • November 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Constitution of California is ratified in a general election.
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 A Russian court sentences Fyodor Dostoyevsky to death for anti-government activities linked to a radical intellectual group, the Petrashevsky Circle. Facing a firing squad on December 23 the group members are reprieved at the last moment and exiled to the katorga prison camps in Siberia.
