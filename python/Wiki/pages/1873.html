1873 (MDCCCLXXIII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Julian calendar, the 1873rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 873rd year of the 2nd millennium, the 73rd year of the 19th century, and the 4th year of the 1870s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1873 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July - The end of the war between the United Kingdom and Ghana\'s King Kofi KariKari, who is involved in the trading of slaves leads to the establishment of the Gold Coast Colony.
    • \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Prince Edward Island joins the Canadian Confederation.
    • \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Alpens (Third Carlist War): Campaigning in Catalonia, a government column under General Jos\xc3\xa9 Cabrinety is ambushed at Alpens, 15 miles east of Berga, by Carlist forces under General Francisco Savalls. After heavy fighting, with Cabrinety killed, virtually the entire column of 800 men is killed or captured.
    • \n
  • July 21 \xe2\x80\x93 At Adair, Iowa, Jesse James and the James\xe2\x80\x93Younger Gang pull off the first successful train robbery in the American Old West (US$3,000 from the Rock Island Express).
    • \n
  • August 4 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars: While protecting a railroad survey party in Montana, the Seventh Cavalry, under Lieutenant Colonel George Armstrong Custer, clashes for the first time with the Sioux, near the Tongue River (only 1 man on each side is killed).
    • \n
  • August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 A peace treaty is signed between Imperial Russia and the Khanate of Khiva, making the khanate a Russian protectorate.
    • \n
  • August 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The Austro-Hungarian North Pole Expedition discovers Franz Josef Land.[1]
    • \n
  • September 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Agreement for establishment of the International Meteorological Organization (IMO).
    • \n
  • September 16 \xe2\x80\x93 German troops leave France upon completion of payment of indemnity for the Franco-Prussian War.
    • \n
  • September 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Ohio Agricultural and Mechanical College, later Ohio State University, opens its doors with 25 students, including 2 women.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The New York stock market crash triggers the Panic of 1873, part of the Long Depression.
    • \n
  • September 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Classes begin at Drury University in Springfield, Missouri, USA.

  • December 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Heineken Brewery is founded in Amsterdam, the Netherlands.
    • \n

    • \n
  • December 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Bocairente (Third Carlist War): Campaigning in Valenica, Spanish Republican General Valeriano Weyler is attacked at Bocairente, northwest of Alcoy, by a greatly superior Carlist force under General Jos\xc3\xa9 Sant\xc3\xa9s. Weyler was initially driven back, losing some of his guns, but in a brilliant counter-attack he turns defeat into victory and Sant\xc3\xa9s is heavily repulsed and forced to withdraw.
    • \n
  • December 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Woman\'s Christian Temperance Union is founded, in Hillsboro, Ohio, USA.
    • \n
  • December 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Bilbao (Third Carlist War; until 2 May 1874): Campaigning in Navarre, Pretender Don Carlos VII and General Joaqu\xc3\xadn El\xc3\xado besiege Bilbao, held by General Ignacio del Castillo and 1,200 men. The Carlist force is ten times this number and includes most of the troops from Navarre, Vizcaya and \xc3\x81lava, although a considerable force is left in Guip\xc3\xbazcoa. Despite defeat at nearby Somorrostro, Republic commander Marshal Francisco Serrano, supported by Generals Manuel de la Concha and Arsenio Mart\xc3\xadnez-Campos, brilliantly breaks the siege and Concha then marches on Estella.
