\n1823 (MDCCCXXIII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Julian calendar, the 1823rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 823rd year of the 2nd millennium, the 23rd year of the 19th century, and the 4th year of the 1820s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1823 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 In Paviland Cave on the Gower Peninsula of Wales, William Buckland inspects the "Red Lady of Paviland", the first identification of a prehistoric (male) human burial.[1]
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Gioachino Rossini\'s Semiramide is first performed.
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 First worldwide carnival parade took place in Cologne, Germany.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Explorer James Weddell\'s expedition to Antarctica reaches latitude 74\xc2\xb015\' S and longitude 34\xc2\xb016\'45" W: the southernmost position any ship had reached before, a record that will hold for more than 80 years.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Agust\xc3\xadn de Iturbide, Emperor of Mexico, abdicates thus ending the short-lived First Mexican Empire.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Eleven-year-old Franz Liszt gives a concert after which he is personally congratulated by Ludwig van Beethoven.
    • \n
  • June 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Raffles Institution established as the Singapore Institution by the founder of Singapore, Sir Stamford Raffles.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The congress of Central America declares absolute independence from Spain, Mexico, and any other foreign nation, including North America, and a republican system of government is established.
    • \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Peel ensures the passage of five Acts of Parliament in the United Kingdom, effectively abolishing the death penalty for over one hundred offences;[2] in particular, the Judgement of Death Act allows judges to commute sentences for capital offences other than murder or treason to imprisonment or transportation.[3]
    • \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Gaols Act passed by Parliament of the United Kingdom, based on the prison reform campaign of Elizabeth Fry.[2]
    • \n
  • July 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The Basilica of Saint Paul Outside the Walls in Rome is almost completely destroyed by fire.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Sim\xc3\xb3n Bol\xc3\xadvar is named President of Peru.
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Joseph Smith claimed in 1838 that on this day he had first come to the place where the golden plates were stored, having been directed there by God through an angel.
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 First Anglo-Burmese War: Burmese attack the British on Shapura, an island close to Chittagong.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Medical journal The Lancet is founded by Thomas Wakley in London.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 According to tradition, William Webb Ellis invents the sport of rugby football at Rugby School in England.[2]
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 James Monroe first introduces the Monroe Doctrine in the State of the Union address, declaring that any European attempts to recolonize the Americas would be considered a hostile act towards the United States.
    • \n
