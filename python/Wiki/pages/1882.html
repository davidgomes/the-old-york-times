1882 (MDCCCLXXXII) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1882nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 882nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 82nd year of the 19th century, and the 3rd year of the 1880s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1882 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 2\n
      \n
    • The Standard Oil Trust is secretly created in the United States to control multiple corporations set up by John D. Rockefeller and his associates.[1]
      • \n
    • Irish-born author Oscar Wilde arrives in the United States for an extended lecture tour; when asked by a customs official if he has anything to declare, he replies "I have nothing to declare but my genius"[2] according to later tradition.[3]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles J. Guiteau is found guilty of the assassination of James A. Garfield (President of the United States), despite an insanity defense raised by his lawyer. He will be hanged on June 30.[4]
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 American showman P. T. Barnum acquires the elephant Jumbo from London Zoo.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Roderick McLean fails in an attempt to assassinate Queen Victoria at Windsor.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 British gunboats enter Monrovia, with Arthur Havelock demanding that Liberia cede disputed territory to the British colony of Sierra Leone of which he is Governor.
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Polygamy is made a felony by the Edmunds Act passed by the United States Congress.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Koch announces the discovery of the bacterium responsible for tuberculosis (Mycobacterium tuberculosis).
    • \n
  • March 28\n
      \n
    • Republican Jules Ferry makes primary education in France free, non-clerical (laique) and obligatory.
      • \n
    • German medical products company Beiersdorf is founded.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Knights of Columbus, a Catholic fraternal service organization, is founded in New Haven, Connecticut.
    • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Old West outlaw Jesse James is shot in the back of the head and killed by Robert Ford.

    • \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Phoenix Park Murders in Ireland: Lord Frederick Cavendish, the newly appointed Chief Secretary for Ireland, and Thomas Henry Burke, his Permanent Undersecretary, are fatally stabbed in Phoenix Park, Dublin, by members of the Irish National Invincibles (militant Irish republicans).

  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Chinese Exclusion Act is the first important law which restricts immigration into the U.S.A.

  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Triple Alliance is formed between Germany, Austria-Hungary and Italy.
    • \n
  • June 6\n

      • \n

    \n
    • \n
  • June 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The \xe2\x80\x98Urabi Revolt breaks out in Egypt against Khedive Tewfik Pasha and European influence in that country.
    • \n
  • June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-French Convention of 1882 signed marking territorial boundaries between Guinea and Sierra Leone.

  • June 30 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. presidential assassin Charles Guiteau is hanged.

  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Zikhron Ya\'akov is founded in northern Israel.
