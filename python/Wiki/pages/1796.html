1796 (MDCCXCVI) was a leap year starting on Friday (dominical letter CB) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Julian calendar, the 1796th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 796th year of the 2nd millennium, the 96th year of the 18th century, and the 7th year of the 1790s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1796 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Dutch (and general) elections are held for the National Assembly of the Batavian Republic (the next Dutch general elections are held in 1888).
    • \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The capital of Upper Canada is moved from Newark to York.
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Qianlong Emperor of China abdicates at age 84 to make way for his son, the Jiaqing Emperor.
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Kingdom of Great Britain is granted control of Ceylon by the Dutch.[1]
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Widow Jos\xc3\xa9phine de Beauharnais marries General Napol\xc3\xa9on Bonaparte.
    • \n
  • March 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon Bonaparte arrives at Nice to take command of the Army of Italy (37,000 men and 60 guns), which is scattered in detachments as far as Genoa.[2]
    • \n
  • March 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Carl Gauss obtains conditions for the constructibility by ruler and compass of regular polygons, and is able to announce that the regular 17-gon is constructible by ruler and compasses.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nMarch 9: Jos\xc3\xa9phine de Beauharnais, wife of Napoleon Bonaparte
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The only night of the supposed Shakespearean play Vortigern and Rowena (actually written by William Henry Ireland) ends in the audience\'s laughter.
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the First Coalition \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Montenotte: Napoleon Bonaparte gains his first victory as an army commander.
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The French proclaim the Republic of Alba on the occupied territories. Two days later, King Victor Amadeus III of Sardinia signs the Armistice of Cherasco, in the headquarters of Napoleon. The fortresses of Coni, Tortoni and Alessandria with all their guns are given up.[3]
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Case of the Lyons Mail: During the night, five highwaymen attack the mail between Paris and Lyon, kill the postmen and steal the funds sent to the armies in Italy.
    • \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon Bonaparte forms an advanced guard (3,500 infantry and 1,500 cavalry) under General Claude Dallemagne. He sends this force along the south bank of the Po River to cross it with boats at Piacenza.[4]
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the First Coalition \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Lodi: General Napoleon Bonaparte defeats the Austrian rearguard in forcing a crossing of the bridge over the Adda River in Italy. The Austrians lose some 2,000 men, 14 guns, and 30 ammunition wagons.\n
      \n
    • Persian Expedition of 1796: Russian troops storm Derbent.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Edward Jenner administers the first smallpox vaccination, in England.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon\'s troops take Milan.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The last mock Garrat Elections are held in Surrey, England.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The French-Republican army divisions of the Army of Italy invade the territories of the Serenissima Repubblica di San Marco.\n
      \n
    • Tennessee is admitted as the 16th U.S. state.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 British explorer Mungo Park becomes the first European to reach the Niger River.[1]
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon Bonaparte seizes the Papal States which become part of the revolutionary Cisalpine Republic. Pope Pius VI signs a armistice at Bologna and is forced to pay contribution (34 million francs).
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Carl Friedrich Gauss discovers that every positive integer is representable as a sum of at most 3 triangular numbers.
    • \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States takes possession of Detroit from Great Britain under the terms of the Jay Treaty.
    • \n
  • July 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Mungo Park reaches S\xc3\xa9gou, the capital of the Bamana Empire.
    • \n
  • July 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Surveyors of the Connecticut Land Company name an area in Ohio "Cleveland" after Gen. Moses Cleaveland, the superintendent of the surveying party.
    • \n
  • July 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Austrian army under Marshal Wurmser advances from the Alps and captures Rivoli and Verona. The French abandoned the east bank of the Mincio River, the outnumbered division (15,000 men) of Massena retreats towards Lake Garda.
    • \n
  • August 4 \xe2\x80\x93 French Revolutionary Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Lonato: The French Army of Italy under Napoleon crushes an Austrian brigade.
    • \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Castiglione: Napoleon Bonaparte defeats the Austrian army (25,000 men) under Wurmser. He is forced to retreat north up the Adige Valley.
    • \n
  • August 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Opening to traffic of the Wearmouth Bridge in England, designed by Thomas Paine in cast iron. Its span of 72 m (237 feet) makes it the world\'s longest single-span vehicular bridge extant at this date.[5][6][7]
    • \n
  • August 19 \xe2\x80\x93 By the Second Treaty of San Ildefonso, Spain and France form an alliance against Great Britain.
    • \n
  • September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 French Revolutionary Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Bassano: French forces (20,000 men) under Andr\xc3\xa9 Mass\xc3\xa9na defeat the Austrians in Veneto. Wurmser retreats towards Vicenza with just 3,500 men of his originally 11,000 left to him.
    • \n
  • September 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Mantua: Napoleon Bonaparte fights a pitched battle at La Favorita on the east side of the Mincio River. The Austrians withdraw into the fortress of Mantua, which is crowded nearly with 30,000 men. Within six weeks, 4,000 die from wounds or sickness.[8]
    • \n
  • September 17 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. President George Washington issues his Farewell Address, which warns against partisan politics and foreign entanglements.
    • \n
  • September 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Empress Catherine the Great signs a agreement with Great Britain, formally joining Russia to the coalition.
    • \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93 John Adams defeats Thomas Jefferson in the U.S. presidential election.
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Tripoli (between the United States and Tripoli) is signed at Tripoli (see also 1797).
    • \n
  • November 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Catherine the Great dies and is succeeded by her son Paul I of Russia. His wife Sophie Marie Dorothea of W\xc3\xbcrttemberg becomes Empress consort.\n
      \n
    • French forces (9,500 men) under Mass\xc3\xa9na attack the Austrian army at Fontaniva. After a desperate assault he is outnumbered and forced to retreat to Verona.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • November 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Caldiero: French forces are defeated by the Austrians at Caldiero and pushed back to Verona. This marked Napoleon\'s first defeat, losing nearly 2,000 men and 2 guns.[9]\n
      \n
    • Groton, New Hampshire is incorporated as a town.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • November 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Arcole: French forces under General Napoleon Bonaparte defeat the Austrians at Arcole. After a bold maneuver he outflank the Austrian army (24,000 men) under Freiherr J\xc3\xb3zsef Alvinczi and cut off its line of retreat. Alvinczi is forced to take up a defensive position behind the Brenta River.[10]
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 The British government begins work on a 40-acre (162,000 m\xc2\xb2) site at Norman Cross for the world\'s first purpose-built prisoner-of-war camp.[11]
    • \n
  • December 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The U.S. Electoral College meets to elect John Adams president of the United States.
    • \n
