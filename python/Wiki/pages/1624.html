\n1624 (MDCXXIV) was a leap year starting on Monday (dominical letter GF) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Julian calendar, the 1624th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 624th year of the 2nd millennium, the 24th year of the 17th century, and the 5th year of the 1620s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1624 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 After 90 years of Ottoman occupation, the Safavid empire recaptures Baghdad.
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Afonso Mendes, appointed by Pope Gregory XV as Prelate of Ethiopia, arrives at Massawa from Goa.
    • \n
  • April 29 - Louis XIII of France appoints Cardinal Richelieu chief minister of the Royal Council.
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 A Dutch fleet captures Bahia, Brazil from the Spanish.
    • \n
  • 24 May - After years of unprofitable operation, Virginia\'s charter is revoked and it becomes a royal colony.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 The first Dutch settlers arrive in New Netherland; they disembark at Governors Island.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Compi\xc3\xa8gne, signed between France and the Netherlands
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July or August \xe2\x80\x93 Portuguese Jesuit priest Ant\xc3\xb3nio de Andrade becomes the first European to enter Tibet.
    • \n
  • August \xe2\x80\x93 The Siege of Breda begins, and will continue for 10 months.
    • \n
  • August 5\xe2\x80\x9314 \xe2\x80\x93 The King\'s Men perform Thomas Middleton\'s satire A Game at Chess at the Globe Theatre, London, until it is suppressed in view of its allusions to the Spanish Match.
    • \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Cardinal Richelieu is appointed by Louis XIII of France to be his first minister following the arrest of his predecessor the previous day.
    • \n
  • Early October \xe2\x80\x93 Action of October 1624: A Tuscan/Papal/Neapolitan galley fleet defeats the Algerians near Sardinia.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Martin Luther\'s German translation of the Bible is publicly burned by order of the Pope.
    • \n
  • The Netherlands establishes a trading colony at Tainan on Taiwan.
    • \n
  • The Virginia Land Company\'s charter is revoked and Virginia becomes a crown colony.
    • \n
  • The city of Oslo in Norway is destroyed by fire for the fourteenth time; King Christian IV of Denmark\xe2\x80\x93Norway decrees its rebuilding on a new site where it will be renamed Christiania.
    • \n
  • Jakob Bartsch records the constellation Camelopardalis around the North Star.
    • \n
  • The Palace of Versailles is first built by Louis XIII, as a hunting lodge.
    • \n
  • The Japanese Shogun expels the Spanish from the land and severs trade with the Philippines.
    • \n
  • Mail service begins in Denmark.
    • \n
  • The University of Saint Francis Xavier is founded in Bolivia.
    • \n
  • Henry Briggs publishes Arithmetica Logarithmica.
    • \n
  • The French Parliament passes a decree forbidding criticism of Aristotle on pain of death.[1]
    • \n
  • Queen Nzinga of Ndongo and Matamba starts to rule.
    • \n
  • The Latymer School and Latymer Upper School in London are founded by the bequest of Edward Latymer.
    • \n
  • The city of Dunfermline is destroyed by fire, but The Abbey, The Palace, the Abbot House and many other buildings survive.
    • \n
  • Frans Hals produces the painting now known as the Laughing Cavalier.[2]
    • \n
