\n1827 (MDCCCXXVII) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1827th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 827th year of the 2nd millennium, the 27th year of the 19th century, and the 8th year of the 1820s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1827 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ituzaingo (Passo do Ros\xc3\xa1rio): A force of the Brazilian Imperial Army meets Argentine\xe2\x80\x93Uruguayan troops in combat.
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Baltimore & Ohio Railroad is incorporated, becoming the first railroad in America offering commercial transportation of both people and freight.
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Brazilian marines sail up the Rio Negro and attack the temporary naval base of Carmen de Patagones, Argentina; they are defeated by the local citizens.
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Shrigley Abduction: Ellen Turner, a wealthy heiress in Cheshire, England, is abducted by Edward Gibbon Wakefield, the future politician in colonial New Zealand.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Freedom\'s Journal, the first African-American owned and published newspaper in the United States, is founded in New York City by John Russwurm.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 7\xe2\x80\x93April 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Monte Santiago: A squadron of the Brazilian Imperial Navy defeats Argentine vessels in a major naval engagement.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 UK: George Canning succeeds Lord Liverpool as British Prime Minister.
    • \n
  • April 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Greek War of Independence \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Phaleron: Ottoman troops defeat the Greek rebels.
    • \n
  • April 26\xe2\x80\x93May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Royal Netherlands Navy\'s British-built paddle steamer Cura\xc3\xa7ao makes the first Transatlantic Crossing by steam, from Hellevoetsluis to Paramaribo.[1]
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Fly Whisk Incident in Ottoman Algeria: Hussein Dey slaps the French consul Pierre Deval on the face, eventually leading to Invasion of Algiers in 1830.
    • \n
  • May 20\xe2\x80\x93July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Zarafa, a giraffe presented by the Ottoman Viceroy of Egypt, Mehmet Ali Pasha, to King Charles X of France, the first to be seen in Europe for over three centuries, walks from Marseilles to Paris.[2]
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Maryland Democratic Party is founded by supporters of Andrew Jackson in Baltimore and hosts its first meeting at the Baltimore Atheneum.
    • \n
  • May 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Romanian inventor Petrache Poenaru receives a French patent for the invention of the first fountain pen with a replaceable ink cartridge.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Greek War of Independence: The Treaty of London between France, Britain, and Russia, demands that the Turks agree to an armistice in Greece.
    • \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Kingdom of Hawaii: The Diocese of Honolulu is founded.
    • \n
  • August 31 \xe2\x80\x93 UK: Frederick John Robinson, 1st Viscount Goderich, becomes Prime Minister of the UK, following the death of George Canning.
    • \n
  • September 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Finland: The Great Fire of Turku destroys 3/4 of the city, with many human casualties.
    • \n
  • September 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Petition for a land grant for 215 acres on the north bank of Rio Grande just across from Paso del Norte (present day Ciudad Ju\xc3\xa1rez) is approved; first residence is built on what is present day El Paso, Texas.
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Joseph Smith claimed in 1838 that on this day he had taken the Golden plates from the place where they were stored, and that he began writing down the Book of Mormon from them the following December.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Persian War, 1826-1828: The Russians under Ivan Paskevich storm Yerevan, ending a millennium of Muslim domination in Eastern Armenia.
    • \n
  • October 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Greek War of Independence \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Navarino: British, French, and Russian naval forces destroy the Turko-Egyptian fleet in Greece. This is the last naval action to be fought under sail alone.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Laos: King Anouvong of Vientiane leads the Laotian Rebellion against Siam and successfully attacks Nakhon Ratchasima (the Siamese later invade Vientiane and nearly destroy the whole city).
    • \n
  • Englishman John Walker invents the first friction match which he names Lucifer.
    • \n
  • Egypt: Cairo University School of Medicine is established as the first African medical school in the Middle East.
    • \n
  • John James Audubon begins publication of the 10-volume The Birds of America in the United Kingdom.
    • \n
