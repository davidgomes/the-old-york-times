\n1790 (MDCCXC) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1790th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 790th year of the 2nd millennium, the 90th year of the 18th century, and the 1st year of the 1790s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1790 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 United States President George Washington gives the first State of the Union address, in New York City.
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 11 minor states of the Austrian Netherlands which took part in the Brabant Revolution at the end of 1789 sign a Treaty of Union creating the United States of Belgium. Prime Minister of Great Britain William Pitt refuses to recognize the new confederation\'s independence.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Mozart\'s opera Cos\xc3\xac fan tutte premiered in Vienna.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The first boat specialized as a rescue lifeboat is tested on the River Tyne in England.
    • \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 In New York City the Supreme Court of the United States convenes for the first time.
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Louis XVI of France declares to the National Assembly that he will maintain the constitutional laws.
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Two Quaker delegates petition the United States Congress for the abolition of slavery.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The first United States Census is authorized; it is held later in the year.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 France is divided into 83 d\xc3\xa9partements, which cut across the former provinces, in an attempt to dislodge regional loyalties based on noble ownership of land.
    • \n
  • March 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The legislature of New York consents to the admission to the Union of a new state, Vermont, formed within the boundaries of New York, contingent upon the successful conclusion of negotiations concerning disputed real-estate claims and the boundary between the two states.
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Jefferson reports to President George Washington in New York as the new United States Secretary of State.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nMay 12: Battle of Reval
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States patent system is established.
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Reval: Gustav III of Sweden sends the battlefleet to eliminate the Russian squadron wintering at Reval (Estonia), but is defeated: 8 Russians, 51 Swedes are killed, 250 captured, and 2 ships are sunk.
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Rhode Island ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the last of the 13 original states to do so.
    • \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Royal assent is given to establishment of the port of Milford Haven in Wales.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Compromise of 1790: Thomas Jefferson, James Madison, and Alexander Hamilton come to an agreement: Madison agrees to not be "strenuous" in opposition for the assumption of state debts by the federal government; Hamilton agrees to support the capital site being above the Potomac.
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The alleged London Monster is arrested in London: he later receives 40 years for 10 assaults.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Louis XVI of France accepts a constitutional monarchy.
    • \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Swedish War \xe2\x80\x93 Second Battle of Svensksund: In a massive Baltic Sea battle of 300 ships, the Swedish navy captures one third of the Russian galley fleet: 304 Swedes are killed, 3,500 Russians killed and 6,000 captured, 51 Russian galleys and other rowing craft are sunk and 22 are taken.
    • \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 French Revolution: The Civil Constitution of the Clergy is passed. This completes the destruction of the monastic orders, legislating out of existence all regular and secular chapters for either sex, abbacies and priorships.
    • \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x93 French Revolution: Citizens of Paris celebrate the unity of the French people and the national reconciliation in the F\xc3\xaate de la F\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration.
    • \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The signing of the Residence Bill establishes a site along the Potomac River as the District of Columbia, the capital district of the United States.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Alexander Hamilton\'s Assumption Bill, giving effect to his First Report on the Public Credit, is passed in the United States Congress, allowing the federal government to assume the consolidated debts of the U.S. states.
    • \n
  • July 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Convention of Reichenbach is signed between Prussia and Austria.
    • \n
  • July 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Inventor Samuel Hopkins becomes the first to be issued a U.S. patent (for an improved method of making potash).
    • \n
  • August 4 \xe2\x80\x93 A newly passed U.S. tariff act creates the system of cutters for revenue enforcement (later named the United States Revenue Cutter Service), the forerunner of the Coast Guard.
    • \n
  • August 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of V\xc3\xa4r\xc3\xa4l\xc3\xa4 ends the Russo-Swedish War.
    • \n
  • September 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Peking opera is born when the Four Great Anhui Troupes introduce Anhui opera to Beijing in honor of the Qianlong Emperor\'s eightieth birthday
    • \n
  • September 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Leopold II, Holy Roman Emperor starts to rule.
    • \n
  • October 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Commissioners appointed by the legislature of New York announce the successful conclusion of negotiations between New York and Vermont concerning disputed real-estate claims and the consent of the legislature to the admission to the Union of the state of Vermont, which was formed within what New York claimed as its territory under an Order in Council that King George III issued on July 20, 1764.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 Holy Roman forces recapture the Austrian Netherlands
    • \n
  • December 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hawkesbury and Nepean Wars begin in New South Wales, Australia as a result of deterioration in relations and increasing colonization.
    • \n
  • December 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War (1787\xe2\x80\x9392): 26,000 Turkish soldiers lose their lives during Alexander Suvorov\'s storm of Izmail.
    • \n
