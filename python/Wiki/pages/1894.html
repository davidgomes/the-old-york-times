1894 (MDCCCXCIV) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1894th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 894th year of the 2nd millennium, the 94th year of the 19th century, and the 5th year of the 1890s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1894 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 4 \xe2\x80\x93 A military alliance is established between the French Third Republic and the Russian Empire.
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 William Kennedy Dickson receives a patent for motion picture film.
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 New England Telephone and Telegraph installs the first battery-operated telephone switchboard in Lexington, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 In Korea, peasant unrest erupts in the Donghak Peasant Revolution, a massive revolt of followers of the Donghak movement. Both China and Japan send military forces, claiming to come to the ruling Joseon dynasty government\'s aid.
    • \n
  • February 12\n
      \n
    • French anarchist \xc3\x89mile Henry sets off a bomb in a Paris caf\xc3\xa9, killing one person and wounding twenty.
      • \n
    • The barque Elisabeth Rickmers of Bremerhaven is wrecked at Haurvig, Denmark, but all crew and passengers are saved.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 At 04:51 GMT, French anarchist Martial Bourdin attempts to destroy the Royal Observatory, Greenwich, London, England, with a bomb, killing himself instead.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Local Government Act (coming into effect December 1894\xe2\x80\x93January 1895) reforms local government in Britain, creating a system of urban and rural districts with elected councils, with elected parish councils in rural areas, and gives women, irrespective of marital status, the right to vote and stand in local (but not national) elections.[1]
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 For the first time, Coca-Cola is sold in bottles.
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 A syzygy of planets occurs as Mercury transits the Sun as seen from Venus, and Mercury and Venus both transit the Sun as seen from Saturn, but no two of the transits are simultaneous.
    • \n
  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Coxey\'s Army (of the unemployed), the first significant protest march in the United States, departs from Massillon, Ohio, for Washington, D.C.
    • \n
