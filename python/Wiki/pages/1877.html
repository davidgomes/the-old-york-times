1877 (MDCCCLXXVII) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1877th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 877th year of the 2nd millennium, the 77th year of the 19th century, and the 8th year of the 1870s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1877 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Queen Victoria is proclaimed Empress of India by the Royal Titles Act 1876, introduced by Benjamin Disraeli, the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom .
    • \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Wolf Mountain: Crazy Horse and his warriors fight their last battle with the United States Cavalry in Montana.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Conference of Constantinople ends with Ottoman Turkey rejecting proposals of internal reform and Balkan provisions.
    • \n
  • January 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Satsuma Rebellion, a revolt of disaffected samurai in Japan against the new imperial government. The Rebellion lasts until September of that year when it is crushed by a professionally led army of draftees.[1]
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 The Nineteenth Century magazine is founded in London.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 In the Compromise of 1877, the U.S. presidential election, 1876 is resolved with the selection of Rutherford B. Hayes as the winner, even though Samuel J. Tilden won the popular vote on November 7, 1876.
    • \n
  • March 4\n
      \n
    • Emile Berliner invents the microphone.
      • \n
    • Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky\'s ballet Swan Lake debuts.
      • \n
    • Rutherford B. Hayes becomes President of the United States, succeeding Ulysses S. Grant as a result of the Compromise of 1877.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 1877 Australia v. England series: The first Test cricket match is held between England and Australia.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 For the only time in history, The Boat Race between the Universities of Cambridge and Oxford is declared a "dead heat" (i.e. a draw).
    • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Kingdom annexes the South African Republic, violating the Sand River Convention of 1852, causing a new Xhosa War.[2]
    • \n
  • April 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War: the Russian Empire declares war on the Ottoman Empire.
    • \n
  • May 5 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars: Sitting Bull leads his band of Lakota into Canada to avoid harassment by the United States Army under Colonel Nelson Miles.
    • \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Realizing that his people are weakened by cold and hunger, Chief Crazy Horse of the Oglala Sioux surrenders to United States troops in Nebraska.
    • \n
  • May 8\xe2\x80\x9311 \xe2\x80\x93 At Gilmore\'s Gardens in New York City, the first Westminster Kennel Club Dog Show is held.
    • \n
  • May 9 (May 10 0:59 UTC) \xe2\x80\x93 Iquique earthquake and tsunami: An earthquake of at least magnitude 8.5 Ms occurs on the west coast of South America, killing 2,541 around the Pacific Rim.
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The 16 May 1877 crisis occurs in France.
    • \n
  • May 21 (May 9 O.S.) \xe2\x80\x93 By a speech in the Parliament of Romania by Mihail Kog\xc4\x83lniceanu, the country declares itself independent from the Ottoman Empire (recognized in 1878 after the end of the Romanian independence war).
    • \n
  • June 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Ossian Flipper becomes the first African American cadet to graduate from the United States Military Academy.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of White Bird Canyon: The Nez Perce defeat the U.S. Cavalry at White Bird Canyon in the Idaho Territory.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Molly Maguires are hanged at Carbon County Prison in Mauch Chunk, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • June 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The eruption of the volcano Cotopaxi in Ecuador causes severe mudflows that wipe out surrounding cities and valleys, killing 1,000.
    • \n
  • June 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The British Mediterranean fleet is sent to Besika Bay.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Conclusion of serial publication of Leo Tolstoy\'s Anna Karenina in The Russian Messenger.[3]
    • \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club begins its first lawn tennis tournament at Wimbledon.
    • \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Great Railroad Strike of 1877: Riots by Baltimore and Ohio Railroad railroad workers in Baltimore lead to a sympathy strike and rioting in Pittsburgh, and a full-scale worker\'s rebellion in St. Louis, briefly establishing a Communist government before U.S. President Rutherford B. Hayes calls in the armed forces.[4]
    • \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War: The first battle in the Siege of Plevna is fought.
    • \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The second battle in the Siege of Plevna is fought.
    • \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War: The Turkish army and its allies destroy the Bulgarian city of Stara Zagora and massacre the inhabitants.
    • \n
  • August 9 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of the Big Hole: Near Big Hole River in Montana, a small band of Nez Perce people who refuse government orders to move to a reservation, clash with the United States Army. The army loses 29 soldiers and the Indians lose 89 warriors in an Army victory.
    • \n
  • August 11 \xe2\x80\x93 American astronomer Asaph Hall discovers Deimos, the outer moon of Mars.
    • \n
  • August 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Asaph Hall discovers Phobos, the inner moon of Mars.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Battle of Lovcha, third battle in the Siege of Plevna, is fought. Russian forces successfully reduce the Ottoman fortress at Lovcha.
    • \n
  • September 5 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars: Oglala Sioux chief Crazy Horse is bayoneted by a United States soldier, after resisting confinement in a guardhouse at Fort Robinson in Nebraska.
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty 7 is concluded between several mainly Blackfoot First Nations tribes and the Canadian Confederation at the Blackfoot Crossing of the Bow River, settling the Blackfoot on Indian reserves in what will become southern Alberta.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Blantyre mining disaster in Scotland kills 207 miners.
    • \n
  • November 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Henrik Ibsen\'s first contemporary realist drama The Pillars of Society is premi\xc3\xa8red at the Odense Teater.[5]
    • \n
  • November 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Edison announces his invention of the phonograph, a machine that can record sound, considered Edison\'s first great invention. Edison demonstrates the device for the first time on November 29.
    • \n
  • November 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The first college lacrosse game is played between New York University and Manhattan College.
    • \n
  • December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The fourth battle of the Russo-Turkish War is fought, concluding the Siege of Plevna.
    • \n
  • December 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Serbia restates its previous declaration of war against Turkey.
    • \n
