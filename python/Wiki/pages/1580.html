Year 1580 (MDLXXX) was a leap year starting on Friday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar, and a leap year starting on Tuesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Proleptic Gregorian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry, King of Portugal dies with no direct heirs, precipitating a Succession crisis.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Michel de Montaigne signs the preface to his most significant work, Essays. They are published later the same year.
    • \n
  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Iberian Union: King Philip II of Spain becomes King of Portugal under the name Philip I, following the death without heirs of King Henry of Portugal, in a personal union of the crowns, thus maintaining Portuguese independence (in Europe and throughout the Portuguese Empire). The Philippine Dynasty rule lasts until 1640.
    • \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Dover Straits earthquake.[1]
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 England signs a commercial treaty with the Ottoman Empire.[2]
    • \n
  • June 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Juan de Garay founds Buenos Aires.
    • \n
  • June 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Book of Concord, a collection of Lutheran confessional documents, is published.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The Ostrog Bible, the first complete printed Bible translation into a Slavic language (Old Church Slavonic), is first printed at Ostroh in the Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth (modern-day Ukraine) by Ivan Fyodorov.
    • \n
  • August 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Alc\xc3\xa2ntara: Spanish armies, led by Fernando \xc3\x81lvarez de Toledo, defending the claim of King Philip II of Spain to the Portuguese throne, defeat the armies of Portuguese claimant Ant\xc3\xb3nio, Prior of Crato.
    • \n
  • September 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Francis Drake returns to Plymouth in England from his voyage of circumnavigation (westabout) on the Golden Hind,[3] the second completed in a continuous voyage and the first under its original commander.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Billy Mitchell volcano on the island of Bougainville undergoes a catastrophic eruption (VEI 6).
    • \n
  • The first session of the Jewish Vaad (Council of Four Lands) is held in Lublin, Poland; 70 delegates of Jewish local qahals meet to discuss taxation and other issues important to Jewish communities.
    • \n
  • Old City of Zamo\xc5\x9b\xc4\x87 established in Poland by Jan Zamoyski.
    • \n
  • Jesuit missionaries arrive at the court of Akbar, Ruler of the Mughal Empire.[4]
    • \n
