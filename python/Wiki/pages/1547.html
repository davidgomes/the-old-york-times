Year 1547 (MDXLVII) was a common year starting on Saturday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 the first Lithuanian-language book, Simple Words of Catechism is published in K\xc3\xb6nigsberg.
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Grand Duke Ivan IV of Muscovy becomes the first Tsar of Russia. This replaces the 264-year-old Grand Duchy of Moscow with the Tsardom of Russia.
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Edward VI succeeds his father Henry VIII as King of England.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Edward VI of England is crowned at Westminster Abbey.
    • \n
  • March 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry II succeeds his father Francis I as King of France.
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Catherine Parr, widow of King Henry VIII of England, secretly marries Thomas Seymour, 1st Baron Seymour of Sudeley.
    • \n
  • April 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of M\xc3\xbchlberg: Emperor Charles V defeats the Lutheran forces of the Schmalkaldic League.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Duchy of Brittany unites with the Kingdom of France.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Pinkie Cleugh: An English army under the Duke of Somerset, Lord Protector of England, defeats a Scottish army under James Hamilton, 2nd Earl of Arran, the Regent. The English seize Edinburgh.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The first book in the Lithuanian language is printed (The Simple Words of Catechism by Martynas Ma\xc5\xbevydas).
    • \n
  • Huguenots increasingly immigrate to the English county of Kent, especially Canterbury.
    • \n
  • The Chambre Ardente is established in Paris for trying heretics.
    • \n
  • Work on construction of the Ch\xc3\xa2teau de Chambord in the Loire Valley for Francis I of France ceases.
    • \n
  • John Dee visits the Low Countries to study navigation with Gemma Frisius.
    • \n
  • Edward VI of England outlaws execution by boiling.
    • \n
