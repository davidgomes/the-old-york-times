Year 1504 (MDIV) was a leap year starting on Monday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 French troops of King Louis XII surrender Gaeta to the Spanish under Gonzalo Fern\xc3\xa1ndez de C\xc3\xb3rdoba.
    • \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 After the death of Sten Sture the Elder on December 14 the year before, Svante Nilsson is elected new Regent of Sweden.
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Lyon: France cedes Naples to Ferdinand II of Aragon who becomes King of Naples as Ferdinand III.
    • \n
  • February 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Christopher Columbus uses his knowledge of a lunar eclipse this night to convince Jamaican tribesmen to provide him with supplies.
    • \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 English guilds subject to state control.
    • \n
  • April 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor, routes troops to Bavaria.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Michelangelo\'s sculpture of David is erected in Florence.
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 A settled engagement is arranged between Karel of Luxembourg and Claudia the Beaujeu.
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Blois: Philip I of Castile, Maximilian I and Louis XII agree terms.
    • \n
  • October 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Isabella I of Castile signs her testament.
    • \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Christopher Columbus returns to Spain from his fourth voyage, during which he and his younger son, Ferdinand, explored the coast of Central America from Belize to Panama.
    • \n
  • November 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Isabella I of Castile, Catholic Queen of Castile and Aragon, dies, ending her patronage for Columbus. The Crown of Castile passes to her daughter Joanna.
    • \n
  • about December 8: Islamic scholar Ahmad ibn Abi Jum\'ah issued the Oran fatwa for Muslims in Spain. (1 Rajab 910 AH in Hijri calendar, Gregorian date is approximate)
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Babur besieges and captures Kabul.
    • \n
  • Islamization of the Sudan region: A Funj leader, Amara Dunqas, founds the Sultanate of Sennar.
    • \n
  • Sheikh Ahmad, final leader of the Great Horde, is last heard of as a Lithuanian prisoner at Vilnius.
    • \n
  • Juan de la Cosa begins his first independent voyage, to the Isthmus of Panama.
    • \n
  • In Florence, Leonardo da Vinci and Niccol\xc3\xb2 Machiavelli become involved in a scheme to divert the Arno river, cutting the water supply to Pisa to force its surrender: Colombino, the project foreman, fails to follow da Vinci\xe2\x80\x99s design, and the project is a major failure.
    • \n
  • Venetian ambassadors suggest to Turkey the construction of a Suez Canal.
    • \n
  • Aldus Manutius publishes his edition of Demosthenes in Venice.
    • \n
  • Matthias Gr\xc3\xbcnewald paints a Crucifixion.
    • \n
  • The Signoria of Florence commissions both Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo to paint the walls of the Grand Council Chamber in the Palazzo Vecchio.
    • \n
  • Raphael paints The Marriage of the Virgin, which exemplifies some major principles of High Renaissance art.
    • \n
