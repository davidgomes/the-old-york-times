\n1820 (MDCCCXX) was a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Julian calendar, the 1820th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 820th year of the 2nd millennium, the 20th year of the 19th century, and the 1st year of the 1820s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1820 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Trienio Liberal in Spain: A constitutionalist military insurrection at C\xc3\xa1diz leads to the summoning of the Spanish Parliament (March 7).
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Indiana University Bloomington is founded as the Indiana State Seminary (renamed Indiana College, 1846).
    • \n
  • January 28 (NS) \xe2\x80\x93 History of Antarctica: An Imperial Russian Navy expedition led by Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen and Mikhail Petrovich Lazarev sights the Antarctic ice sheet.[1]
    • \n
  • January 29 \xe2\x80\x93 George IV of the United Kingdom ascends the throne on the death at Windsor Castle of his father George III (after 59 years on the throne), ending the period known as the British Regency. There will be a gap of 21 years before the title Prince of Wales is next used.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 History of Antarctica: Irish Royal Navy captain Edward Bransfield lands on the mainland of Antarctica.[1]
    • \n
  • February 6\n
      \n
    • Capture of Valdivia: Lord Cochrane occupies Valdivia in the name of the Republic of Chile.
      • \n
    • 86 free African American colonists sail from New York City to Freetown, Sierra Leone.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Minh M\xe1\xba\xa1ng starts to rule in Vietnam.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 A revolt begins in Santa Mar\xc3\xada Chiquimula, Totonicap\xc3\xa1n department of Guatemala.
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 A plot to murder the Cabinet of the United Kingdom, the Cato Street Conspiracy, is exposed in England; the principals are the last to suffer decapitation following their hanging for treason outside Newgate Prison in London on May 1.
    • \n
  • March 3 and March 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Slavery in the United States: The Missouri Compromise becomes law, allowing admission of Missouri and Maine, slave and free states respectively, as U.S. states.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 King Ferdinand VII of Spain accepts the new constitution, beginning the Trienio Liberal.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Maine is admitted as the 23rd U.S. state.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 Hans Christian \xc3\x98rsted discovers the relationship between electricity and magnetism.
    • \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 A Proclamation, signed "By order of the Committee of Organisation for forming a Provisional Government", begins the "Radical War" in Scotland.
    • \n
  • April 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The statue of the Venus de Milo (Aphrodite of Milos, c.150 BC-125 BC) is discovered on the Greek island of Milos by a peasant named Yorgos Kentrotas.
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Alexander Ypsilantis is declared leader of Filiki Eteria, a secret organization to overthrow Ottoman rule over Greece.
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 King William I of W\xc3\xbcrttemberg marries his cousin, Pauline Therese, in Stuttgart.
    • \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The last hanging, drawing and quartering in Britain is meted out to the Cato Street conspirators for treason (only hanged and beheaded).
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 HMS Beagle (the ship that will later take young Charles Darwin on his scientific voyage) is launched at Woolwich Dockyard.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 John Stuart Mill sets out on his formative boyhood trip to France.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 A revolt under Guglielmo Pepe forces Ferdinand I of the Two Sicilies to sign a constitution modeled on the Spanish Constitution of 1812.
    • \n
  • July 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Saint Cronan\'s Boys\' National School opens in Bray, Co. Wicklow, Ireland under the title Bray Male School. It is the oldest school in Bray and its notable pupils will include President of Ireland Cearbhall \xc3\x93 D\xc3\xa1laigh.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Opening of Union Chain Bridge across the River Tweed between England and Scotland, a wrought iron suspension bridge designed by Captain Samuel Brown. Its span of 449 ft (137 m) is the world\'s longest for a vehicular bridge at this time.[2]
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 A Constitutionalist insurrection breaks out at Oporto, Portugal.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The Daoguang Emperor succeeds the throne of the Qing Dynasty in China.
    • \n
  • September 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Revolution breaks out in Lisbon against John VI of Portugal.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 9 \xe2\x80\x93 History of Ecuador: Guayaquil declares independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • October 25\xe2\x80\x93November 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Congress of Troppau (Opava) is convened between the rulers of Russia, Austria and Prussia.
    • \n
  • November 20 \xe2\x80\x93 After the sinking of the American whaleship Essex of Nantucket by a sperm whale in the southern Pacific Ocean the survivors are left afloat in three small whaleboats. They eventually resort, by common consent, to cannibalism to allow some to survive.
    • \n
  • December 3 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. presidential election: James Monroe is re-elected, virtually unopposed.
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The town of Tuscumbia, Alabama, is incorporated.
    • \n
