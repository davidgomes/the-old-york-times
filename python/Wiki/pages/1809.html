\n1809 (MDCCCIX) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1809th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 809th year of the 2nd millennium, the 9th year of the 19th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1800s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1809 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n
\n
    \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of the Dardanelles between the United Kingdom and the Ottoman Empire is concluded.
    • \n
  • January 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: French Marshal Jean Lannes begins the Siege of Zaragoza.
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: Battle of Corunna in Galicia (Spain): The British (under General Sir John Moore, who is killed) resist an attempt by the French (under Marshal Soult) to prevent them embarking.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Illinois Territory is created.
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Franz I of Austria declares war on France.
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Fulton patents the steamboat in the United States.[1][2]
    • \n
  • February 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Miami University (Ohio) is established on the township of land required to be set aside for it under the conditions of the Miami Purchase in 1794.
    • \n
  • February 20\n
      \n
    • A decision by the Supreme Court of the United States states that the power of the federal government is greater than any individual state.
      • \n
    • The Siege of Zaragoza grinds to a halt as Jose Palafox surrenders. Over 60,000 have been killed on both sides in 41 days of street fighting.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Valls: Spanish forces are defeated in Catalonia by Marshal Laurent de Gouvion Saint-Cyr.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Action of 27 February 1809: Captain Bernard Dubourdieu captures HMS Proserpine
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Embargo Act of 1807 is repealed in the United States; the Nonintercourse Act replaces it.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 First inauguration of James Madison - James Madison succeeds Thomas Jefferson as the President of the United States.
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 A military coup ousts Gustav IV Adolf of Sweden; he is confined in Gripsholm Castle.
    • \n
  • March 29\n
      \n
    • At the Diet of Porvoo, Finland\'s four Estates pledge allegiance to Alexander I of Russia, commencing the secession of the future Grand Principality of Finland from Sweden. The Emperor in return, promises to retain and uphold former laws and privileges as well as the dominant Lutheran religion. His pledge is later interpreted by the Finns as a confirmation of constitutional laws, which had, effectively, established Finland as a separate state in real union with the Russia.
      • \n
    • King Gustav IV Adolf of Sweden abdicates after the Coup of 1809 and is later exiled.
      • \n
    • Peninsular War\n
        \n
      • First Battle of Porto: 18,000 Portuguese soldiers are drowned in a rout after defeat by the French under Marshal Soult.
        • \n
      • Battle of Medell\xc3\xadn at Extremadura: massive Spanish casualties in a rout by French cavalry under Marshal Victor.
        • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Tiroleans rise under the command of Andreas Hofer against French and Bavarian occupation.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars: The War of the Fifth Coalition begins when forces of the Austrian Empire invade Bavaria.
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Abensberg, Bavaria: Napoleon defeats Austria.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The 2,000 Guineas Stakes horse race is first run in England.[3]
    • \n
  • April 19 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Fifth Coalition:\n
      \n
    • Battle of Raszyn: The armies of the Austrian Empire are defeated by the Duchy of Warsaw.
      • \n
    • Battle of Teugen-Hausen: The armies of the Austrian Empire are defeated by the French and their Bavarian allies.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Eckm\xc3\xbchl: French troops under Napoleon I and Marshal Davout defeat the Austrians under Archduke Charles.
    • \n
  • May 5\n
      \n
    • Mary Kies is the first American woman to be awarded a patent (for a technique of weaving straw hats with silk and thread).[4]
      • \n
    • The Swiss canton of Aargau denies Jews citizenship.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Gustav IV Adolf is officially deposed from the Swedish throne by the Riksdag of the Estates.
    • \n
  • May 10\xe2\x80\x9311 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: Battle of Grij\xc3\xb3: the Anglo-Portuguese Army commanded by Sir Arthur Wellesley defeats the French army commanded by Marshal Soult in Portugal.
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: Second Battle of Porto: the Anglo-Portuguese Army commanded by Wellesley drives the French army commanded by Marshal Soult out of Porto and forces them to retreat from the country.
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon I of France orders the annexation of the Papal States to the French Empire. When he announces that the Pope\'s secular power has ended, the Pope excommunicates him.
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Aspern-Essling: Austrian troops under Archduke Karl beat the French under Napoleon in a hard-fought battle.
    • \n
  • May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Dartmoor Prison opens in England to house French prisoners of war.[5]
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Sweden promulgates a new Instrument of Government, which restores political power to the Riksdag of the Estates after authoritarian rule since 1772. On the same day, Duke Charles (uncle of the deposed king Gustav IV Adolf) is elected King under the name Charles XIII.
    • \n
  • June 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Shoja Shah of Afghanistan signs a treaty with the British. Only weeks later, he is succeeded by Mahmud Shah.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 5\xe2\x80\x93July 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Wagram: Napoleon defeats the Austrians.
    • \n
  • July 6 \xe2\x80\x93 French troops arrest Pope Pius VII and take him to Liguria.
    • \n
  • July 8\xe2\x80\x939 \xe2\x80\x93 Finnish War: The Swedish archipelago fleet defeats the Russians in the naval battle of Porkala
    • \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 French Marshal Marmont engages in the inconclusive Battle of Znaim against the Austrians.
    • \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of La Paz (current Bolivia) declares its independence from the Spanish Crown and forms the Junta Tuitiva, the first independent government in Spanish America, led by Pedro Domingo Murillo.
    • \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsula War: Battle of Talavera: Sir Arthur Wellesley\'s British, Portuguese and Spanish army defeats a French force.[3]
    • \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Walcheren Campaign: A British invasion army lands on Walcheren.
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Seventy disciples of Vilna Gaon arrive in Palestine.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Ecuador declares independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • August 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Almonacid de Toledo: A poorly led Spanish army is defeated by King Joseph Bonaparte\'s French army.
    • \n
  • September 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Peace of Hamina is signed between Russia and Sweden in the Finnish War. The territory to become the Grand Principality of Finland is ceded to Russia by the Treaty of Fredrikshamn.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 A new theatre to hold the Royal Opera House opens in London to replace the first, burnt down in a fire in 1808. The price increases lead to the Old Price Riots which last for 64 days.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Prince Klemens von Metternich becomes foreign minister of the Austrian Empire.
    • \n
  • October 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Along the Natchez Trace in Tennessee, explorer Meriwether Lewis dies under mysterious circumstances at an inn called Grinder\'s Stand.
    • \n
  • October 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Sch\xc3\xb6nbrunn cedes the Illyrian Provinces to France.
    • \n
  • November 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Berners Street hoax: Theodore Hook manages to attract dozens of people to 54 Berners Street in London.
    • \n
  • November 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Oca\xc3\xb1a: A Spanish army is ridden down and 4000 are killed and wounded by French forces.
    • \n
  • November 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Gustav Koerner: The revolutionary, journalist, lawyer, politician, and statesman of Illinois and Germany and Colonel of the U.S. Army was born in Frankfurt
    • \n
  • November 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Benjamin Bathurst, a British diplomat, mysteriously disappears (possibly murdered) in Perleberg, west of Berlin.
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Boyd massacre: Whangaroa M\xc4\x81ori people kill and eat 66 crew and passengers of the brigantine Boyd in New Zealand.[6]
    • \n
  • December 26 \xe2\x80\x93 A British invasion force leaves Vlissingen.
    • \n
  • December 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Wearing masks at balls is forbidden in Boston, Massachusetts.
    • \n
