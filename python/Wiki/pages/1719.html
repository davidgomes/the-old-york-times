\n1719 (MDCCXIX) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1719th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 719th year of the 2nd millennium, the 19th year of the 18th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1710s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1719 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 23\n
      \n
    • The Principality of Liechtenstein is created within the Holy Roman Empire.
      • \n
    • The Riksdag of the Estates recognizes Ulrika Eleonora\'s claim to the Swedish throne, after she has agreed to sign a new Swedish constitution. Thus, she is recognized as Queen regnant of Sweden.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Treaty of Stockholm is signed.
    • \n
  • February 29 \xe2\x80\x93 King christian the 6th of Denmark appoints Niels Jakobsen as the leader of the first Danish Curling team.
    • \n
  • April 19 \xe2\x80\x93 In Louisiana (New France), Bienville\'s brother Serigny arrives on a French man-of-war, bringing news that war was declared between France and Spain (from December 1718).
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Daniel Defoe publishes Robinson Crusoe.
    • \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 French army under James FitzJames, 1st Duke of Berwick invades the Basque provinces of Spain.
    • \n
  • May 14 \xe2\x80\x93 In Louisiana (New France), at war with Spain, Bienville, from Mobile, captures Pensacola, but Pensacola is later recaptured by the Spanish, and again re-taken by Bienville.[1]
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of \xc3\x96sel Island: A Russian naval force defeats the Swedish fleet.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Glen Shiel: British forces defeat the Jacobites and their Spanish allies.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Austrians are defeated in the Battle of Francavilla.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • December 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Andrew Bradford publishes the American Weekly Mercury, Pennsylvania\'s first newspaper.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Prussia conducts Europe\'s first systematic census.
    • \n
  • Miners in Falun, Sweden find the apparently petrified body of Fet-Mats Israelsson in an unused part of the copper mine.
    • \n
  • Raine\'s Foundation School, Bethnal Green, opens in Wapping (opened by Henry Raine).
    • \n
  • Thomas Elfe, born in 1719 was a successful colonial period furniture craftsman in Charleston, South Carolina.
    • \n
