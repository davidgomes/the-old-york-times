\n1715 (MDCCXV) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1715th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 715th year of the 2nd millennium, the 15th year of the 18th century, and the 6th year of the 1710s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1715 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Tuscarora War: The Tuscarora and their allies sign a peace treaty with the Province of Carolina and agree to move to a reservation near Lake Mattamuskeet, effectively ending the Tuscarora War. Large numbers of Tuscarora subsequently move to New York.
    • \n
  • March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry St John, 1st Viscount Bolingbroke, flees from Great Britain to France. His part in secret negotiations with France leading to the Treaty of Utrecht has cast suspicion on him in the eyes of the Whig government of Britain. He becomes secretary of state to the Pretender, James Edward Stuart.[1]
    • \n
  • May 3 \xe2\x80\x93 A total solar eclipse is seen across southern England, Sweden and Finland (the last total eclipse visible in London for almost 900 years).
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Ottoman\xe2\x80\x93Venetian War (1714\xe2\x80\x9318): The fall of Nauplion, the capital of the Venetian "Kingdom of the Morea", seals the fate of the Peloponnese peninsula, which is soon completely retaken by the Ottomans.
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 1715 Treasure Fleet: A Spanish treasure fleet of 10 ships under General Don Juan Ubilla leaves Havana, Cuba for Spain. Seven days later, nine of them sink in a storm off the coast of Florida (some centuries later, treasure salvage is found from these wrecks).
    • \n
  • August 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Opening of Old Dock, Liverpool, England, the world\'s first enclosed commercial wet dock (Thomas Steers, engineer).[2][3]
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 The first major Jacobite rising in Scotland against the rule of King George I of Great Britain breaks out. The Earl of Mar raises the standard of James Edward Stuart and marches on Edinburgh. James, the son of the deposed King James VII, arrives from France.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 King Louis XIV of France dies after a reign of 72 years, leaving his throne to his great-grandson Louis XV, who will reign for 58 years. Regent for the new, 5-year-old monarch is Philippe d\'Orl\xc3\xa9ans, nephew of Louis XIV.
    • \n
  • November 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Battle of Sheriffmuir during the Jacobite rising in Scotland. Although the action is inconclusive, the forces of the Kingdom of Great Britain led by John Campbell, 2nd Duke of Argyll, halt the Jacobite advance.
    • \n
  • November 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Preston: Government forces defeat the Jacobite incursion at the conclusion of a five-day siege and action.
    • \n
  • November 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Third Barrier Treaty signed by Britain, the Holy Roman Empire and the Dutch Republic.[4]
    • \n
  • November 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Application of Nueva Planta decrees in Majorca and the other Balearic Islands formerly under the Crown of Aragon bringing them under the laws of the Crown of Castile.
    • \n
  • December 22 \xe2\x80\x93 James Edward Stuart rejoins Jacobite rebels in Scotland[1] but fails to rouse his army.
    • \n
  • December 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Swedish troops occupy Norway.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Yamasee War: the Province of Carolina goes to war with the Yamasee Native Americans.
    • \n
  • Karlsruhe Palace is built, resulting in the town of Karlsruhe growing up around it.
    • \n
  • The ancient right to evaluate royal decrees publicly before they are given the force of law by the high court of Paris (the Parlement) is restored.
    • \n
