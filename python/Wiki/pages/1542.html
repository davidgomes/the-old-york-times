Year 1542 (MDXLII) was a common year starting on Sunday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ba\xc3\xa7ente: The Portuguese under Crist\xc3\xb3v\xc3\xa3o da Gama capture a Moslem-occupied hillfort in northern Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Catherine Howard, the fifth wife of Henry VIII of England, is executed for adultery.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Guadalajara, Mexico, is founded by the Spaniards after 3 previous attempts failed, due to aggressive opposition from local tribes.
    • \n
  • March 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Antoine Escalin des Eymars, the French ambassador, returns from Constantinople with promises of Ottoman aid in a war against Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor.
    • \n
  • April 4\xe2\x80\x9316 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Jarte in Ethiopia: The Portuguese under Crist\xc3\xb3v\xc3\xa3o da Gama encounter the army of Imam Ahmad Gragn and inflict upon him two successive defeats.
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Prome Kingdom, in modern-day central Burma, is conquered by the Taungoo Dynasty.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor declares war on King Francis I of France. This time King Henry VIII of England is allied to the Emperor, while James V of Scotland and Sultan Suleiman I are allied to the French.
    • \n
  • August \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of the Hill of the Jews: During the rainy season, Crist\xc3\xb3v\xc3\xa3o da Gama captures a strategic position and many badly-needed horses.
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Haddon Rig: Scotland defeats England.
    • \n
  • August 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Citizens of Hildesheim in the Holy Roman Empire profess themselves to the Lutheran teachings. As a pledge owner, the city provides for the carrying out of the Protestant Reformation in the city and Peine. Priests from the localities of Clauen, Hohenhameln, So\xc3\x9fmar, Schmedenstedt, Lengede and Rosenthal resume their offices in the interest of the Reformation.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Wofla in Ethiopia: Reinforced with at least 600 arquebusiers and cavalry, Imam Ahmad Gragn attacks the Portuguese camp. The Portuguese are scattered; Crist\xc3\xb3v\xc3\xa3o da Gama is captured and executed.
    • \n
  • September 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Earliest recorded Preston Guild Court in Lancashire, England, in the modern sequence, which lasts unbroken until 1922.
    • \n
  • September 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Portuguese explorer Juan Rodr\xc3\xadguez Cabrillo lands in what is now San Diego Bay and names it "San Miguel"; it will later become the city of San Diego.
    • \n
  • October 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Cabrillo becomes the first European to set foot on Santa Catalina Island, California.
    • \n
  • November 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Solway Moss: An English army invades Scotland and defeats a Scottish army.
    • \n
  • December 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Mary, Queen of Scots, becomes queen regnant at the age of only one week on the death of her father, James V of Scotland.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The first contact of Japan with the West occurs when a Portuguese ship, blown off its course to China, lands Fern\xc3\xa3o Mendes Pinto, Diogo Zeimoto and Cristov\xc3\xa3o Borralho in Japan.
    • \n
  • Pope Paul III establishes the Holy Office with jurisdiction over the Roman Inquisition.
    • \n
  • Bartolom\xc3\xa9 de las Casas completes A Short Account of the Destruction of the Indies, which would be published in 1552.[1]
    • \n
