2009 (MMIX) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar, the 2009th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 9th year of the 3rd millennium, the 9th year of the 21st century, and the 10th and last year of the 2000s decade.

\n

  • International Year of Natural Fibres[3]
    • \n
  • International Year of Reconciliation
    • \n

    • Austria, Japan, Mexico, Turkey, and Uganda assume their seats on the United Nations Security Council.
      • \n
    • Asunci\xc3\xb3n, the capital of Paraguay, becomes the American Capital of Culture and Vilnius and Linz become the European Capitals of Culture.
      • \n
    • Slovakia adopts the euro as its national currency, replacing the Slovak koruna.[4]
      • \n

    • \n

    • \n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 1\n
      \n
    • Patriarch Kirill of Moscow is enthroned as the Patriarch of the Russian Orthodox Church.[19]
      • \n
    • J\xc3\xb3hanna Sigur\xc3\xb0ard\xc3\xb3ttir is appointed as the new Prime Minister of Iceland, becoming the world\'s first openly lesbian head of government.[20]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The deadliest bushfires in Australian history begin; they kill 173, injure 500 more, and leave 7,500 homeless. The fires come after Melbourne records the highest-ever temperature (46.4 \xc2\xb0C, 115 \xc2\xb0F) of any capital city in Australia. The majority of the fires are ignited by either fallen or clashing power lines or deliberately lit.
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Taliban releases a video of Polish geologist Piotr Sta\xc5\x84czak, whom they had abducted a few months earlier, being beheaded. It is the first killing of a Western hostage in Pakistan since American journalist Daniel Pearl was murdered in 2002.[21]
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 A Russian and an American satellite collide over Siberia, creating a large amount of space debris.[22]
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Morgan Tsvangirai is sworn in as the new Prime Minister of Zimbabwe following the power-sharing deal with President Robert Mugabe signed in September 2008.[23]
    • \n
  • February 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The JEM rebels in Darfur, Sudan sign a pact with the Sudanese government, planning a ceasefire within the next three months.[24]
    • \n
  • February 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Former Serbian president Milan Milutinovi\xc4\x87 is acquitted by the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia regarding war crimes during the Kosovo War in 1998/1999.[25]
    • \n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The President of Guinea-Bissau, Jo\xc3\xa3o Bernardo Vieira, is assassinated during an armed attack on his residence in Bissau.[26]
    • \n
  • March 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Gunmen attack a bus carrying Sri Lankan cricketers in Lahore, Pakistan, killing eight people and injuring several others.[27]
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The International Criminal Court (ICC) issues an arrest warrant for Sudanese President Omar al-Bashir for war crimes and crimes against humanity in Darfur. Al-Bashir is the first sitting head of state to be indicted by the ICC since its establishment in 2002.[28]
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA\'s Kepler Mission, a space photometer that will search for extrasolar planets in the Milky Way galaxy, is launched from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station, Florida, USA.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The President of Madagascar, Marc Ravalomanana, is overthrown in a coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat, following a month of rallies in Antananarivo. The military appoints opposition leader Andry Rajoelina as the new president.[29]

April[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nTrain commuters in Mexico City wearing surgical masks due to the outbreak of swine flu
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Albania and Croatia are admitted to the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO).
    • \n
  • April 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The second G-20 summit, involving state leaders rather than the usual finance ministers, meets in London. Its main focus is an ongoing global financial crisis.
    • \n
  • April 3\xe2\x80\x93April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The 21st NATO Summit is held, 60 years after the founding of the organization. Former Danish Prime Minister Anders Fogh Rasmussen is appointed as the new Secretary General of NATO.
    • \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 North Korea launches a rocket from its Tonghae Satellite Launching Ground, which it says is carrying the Kwangmy\xc5\x8fngs\xc5\x8fng-2 satellite, prompting an emergency meeting of \xe2\x80\x93 but no official reaction from \xe2\x80\x93 the United Nations Security Council.[30]
    • \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 L\'Aquila earthquake: A 6.3 magnitude earthquake strikes near L\'Aquila, Italy, killing nearly 300 and injuring more than 1,500.[31]
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Former Peruvian President Alberto Fujimori is sentenced to 25 years in prison for ordering killings and kidnappings by security forces.
    • \n
  • April 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Maersk Alabama hijacking: Cargo ship MV Maersk Alabama is captured by Somali pirates, the first successful pirate seizure of a ship registered under the American flag since the 1820s.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 A political crisis begins in Fiji when President Josefa Iloilo suspends the nation\'s Constitution, dismisses all judges and constitutional appointees and assumes all governance in the country after the Court of Appeal rules that the government of Prime Minister Frank Bainimarama is illegal.[32]
    • \n
  • April 11\xe2\x80\x9312 \xe2\x80\x93 The Fourth East Asia Summit is postponed after Thai Prime Minister Abhisit Vejjajiva declares a state of emergency in Bangkok and surrounding areas.[33][34]
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Maersk Alabama hijacking: The United States Navy rescues Captain Richard Phillips, killing three pirates and capturing a fourth.
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty-four heads of state and government meet in Port of Spain, Trinidad for the 5th Summit of the Americas.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Roxana Saberi, an Iranian-American journalist, is sentenced to eight years in prison for espionage by an Iranian court.[35] She is released the following month, after an appeals court reduces and suspends her sentence.
    • \n
  • April 21 \xe2\x80\x93 UNESCO launches The World Digital Library.[36]
    • \n
  • April 24 \xe2\x80\x93 2009 swine flu outbreak: The World Health Organization expresses concern at the spread of influenza from Mexico and the United States to other countries.[37][38][39] International cases and resulting deaths are confirmed.
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Amidst Russia\'s effort to improve relations with NATO and with the West in general, NATO expels two Russian diplomats from NATO headquarters in Brussels over a spy scandal in Estonia. Russia\'s Foreign Ministry criticises the expulsions.[40]
    • \n

May[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 STS-125: A crew of seven astronauts is launched aboard the Space Shuttle Atlantis, on a thirteen-day mission to refurbish the Hubble Space Telescope. The Space Shuttle is landed successfully on May 24, at Edwards Air Force Base, California.
    • \n
  • May 18\n
      \n
    • The third C40 Cities Climate Leadership Group meets in Seoul.
      • \n
    • Following more than a quarter-century of fighting, the Sri Lankan Civil War ends with the total military defeat of the LTTE.[41][42]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Sri Lanka announces victory in its 27-year war against the terrorist organisation Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Former President of South Korea Roh Moo-hyun, under investigation for alleged bribery during his presidential term, commits suicide.[43]
    • \n
  • May 25 \xe2\x80\x93 North Korea announces that it has conducted a second successful nuclear test in North Hamgyong Province. The United Nations Security Council condemns the reported test.[44]
    • \n

June[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n

\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Air France Flight 447, en route from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, to Paris, crashes into the Atlantic Ocean, killing all 228 on board.
    • \n
  • June 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The General Assembly of the Organization of American States votes to lift the 1962 suspension of Cuba from the organization.
    • \n
  • June 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The outbreak of the H1N1 influenza strain, commonly referred to as "swine flu", is deemed a global pandemic,[45] becoming the first condition since the Hong Kong flu of 1967\xe2\x80\x931968 to receive this designation.
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Following the apparent reelection of Iranian president Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, supporters of defeated candidate Mir-Hossein Mousavi accuse the government of fraud, and launch a series of sustained protests; the first such protests in Iran since 1979.[46]
    • \n
  • June 18 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA launches the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter/LCROSS probes to the Moon, the first American lunar mission since Lunar Prospector in 1998.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The death of Neda Agha-Soltan, an Iranian student shot during a protest, is captured on what soon becomes a viral video that helps to turn Neda into an international symbol of the civil unrest following the presidential election.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 As a step toward total independence from the Kingdom of Denmark, Greenland assumes control over its law enforcement, judicial affairs, and natural resources. Greenlandic becomes the official language.[47]
    • \n
  • June 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The death of American entertainer Michael Jackson triggers an outpouring of worldwide grief. Online, reactions to the event cripple several major websites and services, as the abundance of people accessing the web addresses pushes internet traffic to potentially unprecedented and historic levels.[48][49][50][51]
    • \n
  • June 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Supreme Court of Honduras orders the arrest and exile of President Manuel Zelaya, claiming he was violating the nation\'s constitution by holding a referendum to stay in power.[52] The ouster is condemned by the United Nations, the Organization of American States,[53] and multiple nations around the world.
    • \n
  • June 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Yemenia Flight 626 crashes off the coast of Moroni, Comoros, killing all but one of the 153 passengers and crew.[54]
    • \n

July[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 The Sears Tower was officially renamed the Willis Tower after being bought by London-based Willis Group.
    • \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Organization of American States suspends Honduras due to the country\'s recent political crisis after its refusal to reinstate President Zelaya.[55][56]
    • \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Over 150 are killed when a few thousand ethnic Uyghurs target local Han Chinese during major rioting in \xc3\x9cr\xc3\xbcmqi, Xinjiang.
    • \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 A public memorial service is held for Michael Jackson. It is regarded as one of the most prominent funerals of all time since the death of Elvis Presley in 1977.[57][58][59]
    • \n
  • July 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Caspian Airlines Flight 7908 crashes near Qazvin, Iran, killing all 168 on board.
    • \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Iceland\'s national parliament, the Althingi, votes to pursue joining the EU.[60]
    • \n
  • July 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The longest total solar eclipse of the 21st century, lasting up to 6 minutes and 38.8 seconds, occurs over parts of Asia and the Pacific Ocean.
    • \n

August[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Bolivia becomes the first South American country to declare the right of indigenous people to govern themselves.[61]
    • \n
  • August 4 \xe2\x80\x93 North Korean leader Kim Jong-il pardons two American journalists, who had been arrested and imprisoned for illegal entry earlier in the year, after former U.S. President Bill Clinton meets with Kim in North Korea.[62]
    • \n
  • August 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Typhoon Morakot hits Taiwan, killing 500 and stranding more than 1,000 via the worst flooding on the island in half a century.[63]
    • \n
  • August 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Kingdom imposes direct rule on the Turks and Caicos Islands after an inquiry that found evidence of government corruption.[64]
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Abdelbaset al-Megrahi, imprisoned for the 1988 bombing of Pan Am Flight 103, is released by the Scottish government on compassionate grounds as he has terminal prostate cancer. He returns to his native Libya.[65]
    • \n
