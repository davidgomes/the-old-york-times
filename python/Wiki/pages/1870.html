1870 (MDCCCLXX) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1870th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 870th year of the 2nd millennium, the 70th year of the 19th century, and the 1st year of the 1870s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1870 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Ems Dispatch serves as a reason for a war between Prussia and France.
    • \n
  • July 15\n
      \n
    • Reconstruction Era: Georgia becomes the last former Confederate state to be readmitted to the Union.
      • \n
    • The British government admits the former Hudson\'s Bay Company territory of Rupert\'s Land and the North-Western Territory to the Dominion of Canada.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • July 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Pastor aeternus: Pope Pius IX declares papal infallibility in matters of faith and morals.
    • \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Franco-Prussian War: France declares war on Prussia.
    • \n
  • August 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Official opening of the Tower Subway beneath the River Thames in London, the world\'s first underground passenger "tube" railway.[1] Although this lasts as a railway operation only until November, it demonstrates the technologically successful first use of the cylindrical wrought iron tunnelling shield devised by Peter W. Barlow and James Henry Greathead[2] and of a permanent tunnel lining of cast iron segments.[3]
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Republic of Ploie\xc8\x99ti, an uprising against Domnitor Carol of Romania, fails.
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Red River Rebellion ends with the arrival of the Wolseley Expedition and the fleeing of Louis Riel.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Franco-Prussian War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Sedan: Prussian forces defeat the French armies and take emperor Napoleon III and 100,000 of his soldiers prisoner at Sedan.
    • \n
  • September 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Emperor Napoleon III of France is deposed and the Third Republic is declared. Empress Eug\xc3\xa9nie flees to England with her children.
    • \n
  • September 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Louisa Ann Swain of Laramie, Wyoming, becomes the first woman in the United States to cast a vote legally since 1807.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Old Faithful Geyser is observed and named by Henry D. Washburn during the Washburn\xe2\x80\x93Langford\xe2\x80\x93Doane Expedition to Yellowstone.
    • \n
  • September 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Paris (1870\xe2\x80\x9371) in the Franco-Prussian War begins.
    • \n
  • September 20 \xe2\x80\x93 With Bersaglieri soldiers entering Rome at Porta Pia, the unification of Italy is completed, ending the last remnant of the Papal States and the Papal temporal power.
    • \n
