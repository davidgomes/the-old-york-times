1945 (MCMXLV) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1945th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 945th year of the 2nd millennium, the 45th year of the 20th century, and the 6th year of the 1940s decade.

January[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJanuary 27: The Soviet Red Army liberates Auschwitz.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Allied advance from Paris to the Rhine continues; the United States Army crosses the Siegfried Line.
    • \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Germany begins Operation Bodenplatte, an attempt by the Luftwaffe to cripple Allied air forces in the Low Countries.
      • \n
    • The Chenogne massacre in which German prisoners were allegedly killed by American forces near the village of Chenogne, Belgium.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Australia recognizes the Polish Committee of National Liberation as the government of Poland.
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: British General Bernard Montgomery holds a press conference at Zonhoven describing his supporting role at the Battle of the Bulge.
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Soviet Union begins the Vistula\xe2\x80\x93Oder Offensive in Eastern Europe against the German Army.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Soviet Union begins the East Prussian Offensive to eliminate German forces in East Prussia.
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Adolf Hitler takes residence in the F\xc3\xbchrerbunker in Berlin.
    • \n
  • January 17\n
      \n
    • WWII: The Soviet Union occupies Warsaw, Poland.
      • \n
    • The Holocaust: A Soviet patrol arrests Raoul Wallenberg in Hungary.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Holocaust: The SS begins evacuation of Auschwitz concentration camp. Nearly 60,000 prisoners, mostly Jews, are forced to march to other locations in Germany; as many as 15,000 die. The 7,000 too sick to move are left without supplies being distributed.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Franklin D. Roosevelt is inaugurated to a fourth term as President of the United States, the only President ever to exceed two terms.
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Hungary agrees to an armistice with the Allies.
      • \n
    • German Grand Admiral Karl D\xc3\xb6nitz orders the start of Operation Hannibal, the mass evacuation by sea of German troops and civilians from the Courland Pocket, East Prussia and the Polish Corridor.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: AP war correspondent Joseph Morton, nine OSS men, and four SOE agents are executed by the Germans at Mauthausen concentration camp under Hitler\'s Commando Order of 1942 which stipulates the immediate execution of all captured Allied commandos or saboteurs without trial, even those in proper uniforms. Morton is the only Allied correspondent to be executed by the Axis during the war.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Infantry action at Holtzwihr, France, for which Audie Murphy is awarded the Medal of Honor.[1]
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Holocaust: The Soviet Red Army liberates the Auschwitz and Birkenau concentration camps.
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Supplies begin to reach China over the newly reopened Burma Road.
    • \n
  • January 30 - WWII:\n
      \n
    • MV Wilhelm Gustloff, with over 10,000 mainly civilian Germans from Gotenhafen (Gdynia) is sunk in Gda\xc5\x84sk Bay by three torpedoes from the Soviet submarine S-13 in the Baltic Sea; up to 9,400 are thought to have died \xe2\x80\x93 the greatest loss of life in a single ship sinking in war action in history.
      • \n
    • Raid at Cabanatuan: 121 American soldiers and 800 Filipino guerrillas free 813 American prisoners of war from the Japanese-held camp in the city of Cabanatuan in the Philippines.
      • \n
    • Adolf Hitler makes his last public speech to be delivered personally, on broadcast radio, expressing the belief that Germany will triumph.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Eddie Slovik is executed by firing squad near Sainte-Marie-aux-Mines for desertion, the only U.S. soldier since the American Civil War ever executed for this offense.
    • \n

  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • American and Filipino ground forces land on Corregidor Island in the Philippines.

    • Combined American and Filipino forces recapture the Bataan Peninsula.
      • \n
    • Venezuela declares war on Germany.

    \n
    • \n

    • \n

      • \n
    • Uruguay declares war on Germany and Japan.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Battle of Iwo Jima: A group of United States Marines reach the top of Mount Suribachi on the island and are photographed raising the American flag. The photo, Raising the Flag on Iwo Jima (taken by Joe Rosenthal), later wins a Pulitzer Prize.
      • \n
    • The 11th Airborne Division, with Filipino guerrillas, freed the captives of the Los Ba\xc3\xb1os internment camp.
      • \n
    • The capital of the Philippines, Manila, is liberated by combined American and Filipino ground troops.
      • \n
    • American and Filipino troops enter Intramuros, Manila.
      • \n
    • The German garrison in Pozna\xc5\x84 capitulates to Red Army and Polish troops.
      • \n
    • Bombing of Pforzheim: Heaviest of a series of bombing raids on Pforzheim in Germany by Allied aircraft is carried out by the British Royal Air Force. As many as 17,600 people, or 31.4% of the town\'s population, are killed in the raid and about 83% of the town\'s buildings destroyed, two-thirds of its complete area and between 80 and 100% of the inner city.
      • \n
    • Turkey joins the war on the allies side.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Egyptian Premier Ahmad Mahir Pasha is killed in Parliament after reading a decree.
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 In Bucharest, a violent demonstration takes place, during which the bol\xc5\x9fevic group opens fire on the army and protesters. In response, Andrei Y. Vishinsky, USSR vice commissioner of foreign affairs and president of the Allied Control Commission for Romania, travels to Bucharest to compel Nicolae R\xc4\x83descu to resign as premier.
    • \n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 President Franklin D. Roosevelt gives what will be his last address to a joint session of the United States Congress, reporting on the Yalta Conference.
    • \n
  • March 2\n
      \n
    • Former U.S. Vice-President Henry A. Wallace starts his term of office as U.S. Secretary of Commerce, serving under President Franklin D. Roosevelt.

      \n

      \n

    \n
    • \n

    • \n
  • March 8\n
      \n
    • Josip Broz Tito forms a government in Yugoslavia.
      • \n
    • The Nazi authorities kill 117 Dutch men in reprisal for the attempted murder of Hanns Albin Rauter.
      • \n
    • Operation Sunrise: Waffen-SS General Karl Wolff meets with Allen Welsh Dulles of the United States Office of Strategic Services at Lucerne in neutral Switzerland to negotiate surrender of the Axis forces in Italy to the Allies.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 9\xe2\x80\x9310 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Bombing of Tokyo: "Operation Meetinghouse" \xe2\x80\x93 USAAF B-29 bombers flying from the Mariana Islands attack Tokyo, Japan, with incendiary bombs, killing 100,000 citizens in the firebombing.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The film Les Enfants du Paradis premi\xc3\xa8res in Paris.
    • \n
  • March 11\n
      \n
    • The Empire of Japan establishes the Empire of Vietnam, a puppet state which will last only until August 23, with B\xe1\xba\xa3o \xc4\x90\xe1\xba\xa1i as its ruler.
      • \n
    • Sammarinese general election gives San Marino the world\'s first democratically elected communist government, which will hold power to 1957.[7][8]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Swinem\xc3\xbcnde is destroyed by the USAAF killing an estimated 8,000 to 23,000 civilians, mostly refugees saved by Operation Hannibal.
    • \n
  • March 15\xe2\x80\x9331 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Soviet Red Army carries out the Upper Silesian Offensive.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The 17th Academy Awards ceremony is held, broadcast via radio for the first time. Best Picture goes to Going My Way.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Battle of Iwo Jima unofficially ends, with pockets of guerrilla resistance persisting until the official conclusion of the battle.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Kobe, Japan is fire-bombed by 331 B-29 bombers, killing over 8,000 people.
    • \n
  • March 18 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: 1,250 American bombers attack Berlin.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Adolf Hitler orders that all industries, military installations, machine shops, transportation facilities and communications facilities in Germany be destroyed.
      • \n
    • Off the coast of Japan, bombers hit the aircraft carrier USS Franklin, killing about 800 of her crewmen and crippling the ship.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • British troops liberate Mandalay, Burma.
      • \n
    • Bulgarian and Soviet troops successfully defend the north bank of the Drava River as the Battle of the Transdanubian Hills concludes.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 22\n
      \n
    • Arab League is formed with the adoption of a charter in Cairo, Egypt.
      • \n
    • Hildesheim Cathedral in Germany is destroyed in an air raid.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 24\n
      \n
    • WWII \xe2\x80\x93 Operation Varsity: Two airborne divisions capture bridges across the Rhine River to aid the Allied advance.

    • The cartoon character Sylvester the cat debuts in Life with Feathers.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 26 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Battle of Iwo Jima officially ends, with the destruction of the remaining areas of Japanese resistance.

  • March 29\n

      • \n
    • The "Clash of Titans": George Mikan and Bob Kurland duel at Madison Square Garden in New York as Oklahoma State University defeats DePaul 52\xe2\x80\x9344 in basketball.
