\n1842 (MDCCCXLII) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1842nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 842nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 42nd year of the 19th century, and the 3rd year of the 1840s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1842 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 Michael Alexander takes office as first appointee to the Anglican-German Bishopric in Jerusalem.
    • \n
  • January 6\xe2\x80\x9313 \xe2\x80\x93 First Anglo-Afghan War: Massacre of Elphinstone\'s army (Battle of Gandamak): British East India Company troops destroyed by Afghan forces on the road from Kabul to Jalalabad, Afghanistan, by Akbar Khan, son of Dost Mohammad Khan.
    • \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Delft University of Technology established by William II of the Netherlands as a \'Royal Academy for the education of civilian engineers\'.[1]
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Antarctic explorer James Clark Ross, charting the eastern side of James Ross Island, reaches a Farthest South of 78\xc2\xb009\'30"S.[2]
    • \n
  • February 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Debre Tabor: Ras Ali Alula, Regent of the Emperor of Ethiopia, defeats warlord Wube Haile Maryam of Semien.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Commonwealth v. Hunt: the Massachusetts Supreme Court makes strikes and unions legal in the United States.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Gaylad, ridden by Tom Olliver, wins the Grand National at Aintree Racecourse.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Mexican troops led by Rafael Vasquez invade Texas, briefly occupy San Antonio, and then head back to the Rio Grande. This is the first such invasion since the Texas Revolution.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Giuseppe Verdi\'s third opera Nabucco premieres at La Scala in Milan; its success establishes Verdi as one of Italy\'s foremost operatic composers.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Relief Society, a philanthropic and educational women\'s organization of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church) is founded.
    • \n
  • March 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Anesthesia is used for the first time in an operation; Dr. Crawford Long performs the operation using ether.
    • \n
  • March 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Middleton Junction and Oldham Branch Railway line opened up to Werneth in North West England.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 First Anglo-Afghan War: British victory at the Battle of Jellalabad.
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Versailles rail accident: A train traveling between Versailles and Paris in France derails due to a broken locomotive axle and catches fire, killing at least 55 passengers in the locked carriages.
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Income Tax Act establishes the first peacetime income tax in the United Kingdom; 7 pence in the pound, for incomes over 150 pounds.[3]
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Dorr Rebellion: Militiamen supporting Thomas Wilson Dorr attack the arsenal in Providence, Rhode Island, but are repulsed.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 In South Africa, hunter Dick King rides into a British military base in Grahamstown to warn that the Boers have besieged Durban (he had left 11 days earlier). The British army dispatches a relief force.
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Queen Victoria becomes the first reigning British monarch to travel by train, on the Great Western Railway between Slough and London Paddington station.[4]
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Anselmo de Andrade, Portuguese economist and politician, is born in Vila Real de Santo Ant\xc3\xb3nio.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 James Nasmyth patents the steam hammer in the United Kingdom.[5]
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 8 \xe2\x80\x93 A total solar eclipse occurs in Asia.
    • \n
  • July 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Tri-Kap fraternity is founded at Dartmouth College. It is the oldest local fraternity in the United States.
    • \n
  • August 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Armed Occupation Act is signed, providing for the armed occupation and settlement of the unsettled part of the Peninsula of East Florida.
    • \n
  • August 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Webster\xe2\x80\x93Ashburton Treaty is signed, establishing the United States\xe2\x80\x93Canada border east of the Rocky Mountains.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Mines Act 1842 becomes law, prohibiting underground work for all women and boys under 10 years old in England.
    • \n
  • August 14 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars: United States general William J. Worth declares the Second Seminole War to be over.
    • \n
  • August 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Nanking, an unequal treaty between Britain and Qing dynasty China, ends the First Opium War and establishes Hong Kong as a British colony until 1997.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Chushul ends the Sino-Sikh War.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Josef Groll brews the first pilsner beer in the city of Pilsen, Bohemia (now the Czech Republic).
    • \n
  • October 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Iberian Peninsula is struck by a category 2 hurricane.
    • \n
  • November 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The University of Notre Dame in South Bend, Indiana (United States) is established by Father Edward Sorin of the Roman Catholic Congregation of Holy Cross.
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Citadel, The Military College of South Carolina, is established.
    • \n
