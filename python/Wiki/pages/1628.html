1628 (MDCXXVIII) was a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Julian calendar, the 1628th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 628th year of the 2nd millennium, the 28th year of the 17th century, and the 9th year of the 1620s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1628 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Shah Jahan crowned as ruler of the Mughal Empire in Agra.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Writs issued in February by King Charles I required every county in England (not just seaport towns) to pay ship tax by this date.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Oliver Cromwell makes first appearance in the English Parliament as Member for Huntingdon.
    • \n
  • May\xe2\x80\x93August 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: As a result of its refusal to accept the Capitulation of Franzburg, Stralsund is besieged by Wallenstein\'s imperial army.
    • \n
  • June 7 \xe2\x80\x93 King Charles I reconvenes the English Parliament and accepts the Petition of Right as a concession to gain his subsidies.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: With the help of Danish and Swedish reinforcements, Stralsund is able to resist Wallenstein\'s siege until the landing of a Danish army, led by Christian IV of Denmark, forces Wallenstein to raise the siege and move his army to confront the new threat.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Swedish 64 gun sailing ship Vasa sinks on her maiden voyage in the Stockholm harbor.
    • \n
  • August 23 \xe2\x80\x93 George Villiers, the first Duke of Buckingham, is assassinated by John Felton.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: Wallenstein defeats Christian IV of Denmark\'s army in the Battle of Wolgast.
    • \n
  • September 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Puritans settle Salem, which will later become part of Massachusetts Bay Colony.
    • \n
  • September 7\xe2\x80\x93September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Eighty Years\' War: Dutch admiral Piet Hein captures 16 ships of the Spanish treasure fleet in the Battle in the Bay of Matanzas. The immense booty taken brings in over 11 million guilders, part of which is used to fund the entire army of the Dutch Republic for 8 months long.
    • \n
  • October 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Abaza Mehmet Pasha surrenders to Ottoman forces, ending the Abaza rebellion.
    • \n
  • October 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Siege of La Rochelle ends with the surrender of the Huguenots.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • William Harvey publishes Exercitatio Anatomica de Motu Cordis et Sanguinis in Animalibus in Frankfurt, containing his findings about blood circulation.
    • \n
  • Publication of Sir Edward Coke\'s Institutes of the Lawes of England begins with A Commentary upon Littleton. This will remain an influential legal text on both sides of the Atlantic for three centuries.
    • \n
  • The Collegiate School, today the oldest educational institution in North America, is established.
    • \n
  • The War of the Mantuan Succession breaks out over Mantua and Montferrat. The war is fought between the Duke of Savoy, who is supported by Spain, and the Duke of Nevers, who is supported by France.
    • \n
\n
    \n
  • The first black slaves arrive in the Dutch Manhattan.
    • \n
