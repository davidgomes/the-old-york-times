1964 (MCMLXIV) was a leap year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter ED) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1964th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 964th year of the 2nd millennium, the 64th year of the 20th century, and the 5th year of the 1960s decade.

January[edit]

\n
Main article: January 1964
\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 The Federation of Rhodesia and Nyasaland is dissolved.
    • \n
  • January 5\n
      \n
    • U.S. Senator Barry Goldwater announces that he will seek the Republican nomination for President.
      • \n
    • In the first meeting between leaders of the Roman Catholic and Orthodox churches since the 15th century, Pope Paul VI and Patriarch Athenagoras I of Constantinople meet in Jerusalem.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 A British firm, the Leyland Motor Corp., announces the sale of 450 buses to the Cuban government, challenging the United States blockade of Cuba.
    • \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 In his first State of the Union Address, U.S. President Lyndon Johnson declares a "War on Poverty".
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Martyrs\' Day: Armed clashes between United States troops and Panamanian civilians in the Panama Canal Zone precipitate a major international crisis, resulting in the deaths of 21 Panamanians and 4 U.S. soldiers.
    • \n
  • January 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Introducing... The Beatles is released by Chicago\'s Vee-Jay Records to get the jump on Capitol Records\' release of Meet the Beatles!, scheduled for January 20. The two record companies fight over Vee-Jay\'s release of this album in court.
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 United States Surgeon General Luther Terry reports that smoking may be hazardous to one\'s health (the first such statement from the U.S. government).
    • \n
  • January 12\n
      \n
    • Zanzibar Revolution: The predominantly Arab government of Zanzibar is overthrown by African nationalist rebels; a United States Navy destroyer evacuates 61 U.S. citizens.
      • \n
    • Routine U.S. naval patrols of the South China Sea begin.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 In Manchester, New Hampshire, 14-year-old Pamela Mason is murdered. Edward Coolidge is tried and convicted of the crime, but the conviction is set aside by the landmark Fourth Amendment case "Coolidge vs. New Hampshire (1971)."
    • \n
  • January 16\n
      \n
    • Musical Hello, Dolly! opens in New York\'s St. James Theatre.
      • \n
    • John Glenn, the first American to orbit the Earth, resigns from NASA.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 John Glenn announces that he will seek the Democratic nomination for U.S. Senator from Ohio.
    • \n
  • January 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Plans to build the New York City World Trade Center are announced.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Meet the Beatles!, the first Beatles album from Capitol Records in the United States, is released ten days after Chicago\'s Vee-Jay Records releases Introducing... The Beatles. The two record companies battle it out in court for months, eventually coming to a conclusion.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Kenneth Kaunda is inaugurated as the first Prime Minister of Northern Rhodesia.
    • \n
  • January 23\n
      \n
    • Pope Paul VI institutes the World Day of Prayer for Vocations. During this celebration the Pope reminds the universal Church that still today salvation comes to everyone. It continues to be celebrated every Fourth Sunday of Easter also known as Good Shepherd Sunday.
      • \n
    • Thirteen years after its proposal and nearly 2 years after its passage by the United States Senate, the 24th Amendment to the United States Constitution, prohibiting the use of poll taxes in national elections, is ratified.
      • \n
    • Arthur Miller\'s After the Fall opens Off-Broadway. A semi-autobiographical work, it arouses controversy over his portrayal of late ex-wife Marilyn Monroe.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 27\n
      \n
    • France and the People\'s Republic of China announce their decision to establish diplomatic relations.
      • \n
    • U.S. Senator Margaret Chase Smith, 66, announces her candidacy for the Republican presidential nomination.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 A U.S. Air Force jet training plane that strays into East Germany is shot down by Soviet fighters near Erfurt; all 3 crew men are killed.
    • \n
  • January 29\xe2\x80\x93February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The 1964 Winter Olympics are held in Innsbruck, Austria.
    • \n
  • January 29\n
      \n
    • The Soviet Union launches 2 scientific satellites, Elektron I and II, from a single rocket.
      • \n
    • Ranger 6 is launched by NASA, on a mission to carry television cameras and crash-land on the Moon.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 General Nguy\xe1\xbb\x85n Kh\xc3\xa1nh leads a bloodless military coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat, replacing D\xc6\xb0\xc6\xa1ng V\xc4\x83n Minh as Prime Minister of South Vietnam.
    • \n

February[edit]

\n
Main article: February 1964
\n
    \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Beatles vault to the #1 spot on the U.S. singles charts for the first time, with "I Want to Hold Your Hand", starting the British Invasion in America.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Protesting against alleged de facto school racial segregation, Black and Puerto Rican groups in New York City boycott public schools.
    • \n
  • February 4\n
      \n
    • The Government of the United States authorizes the Twenty-fourth Amendment to the United States Constitution, outlawing the poll tax.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 India backs out of its promise to hold a plebiscite in the disputed territory of Kashmir. In 1948, India had taken the issue of Kashmir to the United Nations Security Council and offered to hold a plebiscite in the held Kashmir under UN supervision.
    • \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Cuba cuts off the normal water supply to the United States Guantanamo Bay Naval Base, in reprisal for the U.S. seizure 4 days earlier of 4 Cuban fishing boats off the coast of Florida.
    • \n
  • February 7\n
      \n
    • A Jackson, Mississippi jury, trying Byron De La Beckwith for the murder of Medgar Evers in June 1963, reports that it cannot reach a verdict, resulting in a mistrial.
      • \n
    • The Beatles arrive from England at New York City\'s JFK International Airport, receiving a tumultuous reception from a throng of screaming fans, marking the first occurrence of "Beatlemania" in the United States.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Beatles appear on The Ed Sullivan Show, marking their first live performance on American television. Seen by an estimated 73 million viewers, the appearance becomes the catalyst for the mid-1960s "British Invasion" of American popular music.
    • \n
  • February 11\n
      \n
    • Greeks and Turks begin fighting in Limassol, Cyprus.
      • \n
    • The Republic of China (Taiwan) severs diplomatic relations with France because of French recognition of the People\'s Republic of China.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 17\n
      \n
    • Wesberry v. Sanders (376 US 1 1964): The Supreme Court of the United States rules that congressional districts have to be approximately equal in population.
      • \n
    • Gabonese president L\xc3\xa9on M\'ba is toppled by a military coup and his archrival, Jean-Hilaire Aubame, is installed in his place. However, French intervention restores M\'ba\'s government the next day.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Chrysler\'s second generation hemi racing engine debuts at the Daytona 500. The 426 hemi-powered Plymouth of Richard Petty (#43) wins. Hemi-powered Plymouths finish 1-2-3.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Cassius Clay (later Muhammad Ali) beats Sonny Liston in Miami Beach, Florida, and is crowned the heavyweight champion of the world.
    • \n
  • February 26 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. politician John Glenn slips on a bathroom rug in his Columbus, Ohio apartment and hits his head on the bathtub, injuring his left inner ear, and prompting him (later that week) to withdraw from the race for the Democratic Party Senate nomination.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The government of Italy asks for help to keep the Leaning Tower of Pisa from toppling over.
    • \n
  • February 29 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. President Lyndon B. Johnson announces that the United States has developed a jet airplane (the A-11), capable of sustained flight at more than 2,000 miles per hour (3,200 km/h) and of altitudes of more than 70,000 feet (21,000 m).
    • \n

March[edit]

\n
Main article: March 1964
\n
    \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Teamsters President Jimmy Hoffa is convicted by a federal jury of tampering with a federal jury in 1962.
    • \n
  • March 6\n
      \n
    • Constantine II becomes King of Greece, upon the death of his father King Paul.
      • \n
    • Malcolm X, suspended from the Nation of Islam, says in New York City that he is forming a black nationalist party.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 9\n
      \n
    • New York Times Co. v Sullivan (376 US 254 1964): The United States Supreme Court rules that under the First Amendment, speech criticizing political figures cannot be censored.
      • \n
    • The first Ford Mustang rolls off the assembly line at Ford Motor Company.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 10\n
      \n
    • Soviet military forces shoot down an unarmed reconnaissance bomber that had strayed into East Germany; the 3 U.S. flyers parachute to safety.
      • \n
    • Henry Cabot Lodge Jr., Ambassador to South Vietnam, wins the New Hampshire Republican primary.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Malcolm X leaves the Nation of Islam.
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The New York Times misreports that 38 neighbors of Kitty Genovese, 28, fail to respond to her cries, as she is being stabbed to death in Queens, New York City, prompting investigation into the bystander effect.
    • \n
  • March 14 \xe2\x80\x93 A Dallas, Texas jury finds Jack Ruby guilty of killing John F. Kennedy assassin Lee Harvey Oswald.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Richard Burton and Elizabeth Taylor marry (for the first time) in Montreal.
    • \n
  • March 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Approximately 50 Moroccan students broke into the embassy of Morocco in the Soviet Union and staged an all\xe2\x80\x90day sit-in protesting against sentencing of 11 people to death for the alleged assassination attempt of King Hassan II of Morocco.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The American Geraldine Jerrie Mock is the first woman to fly solo around the world from March 19 to April 17.
    • \n
  • March 20\xe2\x80\x93June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The first United Nations Conference on Trade and Development takes place.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The precursor of the European Space Agency, ESRO (European Space Research Organization) is established per an agreement signed on June 14, 1962.
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Non ho l\'et\xc3\xa0 by Gigliola Cinquetti (music by Nicola Salerno, text by Mario Panzeri) wins the Eurovision Song Contest 1964 for Italy.
    • \n
  • March 26 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. Defense Secretary Robert McNamara delivers an address that reiterates American determination to give South Vietnam increased military and economic aid, in its war against the Communist insurgency.
    • \n
  • March 27 (Good Friday) \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Alaskan earthquake, the second most powerful known (and the most powerful earthquake in the United States) at a magnitude of 9.2, strikes Southcentral Alaska, killing 125 people and inflicting massive damage to the city of Anchorage.
    • \n
  • March 28\n
      \n
    • King Saud of Saudi Arabia abdicates the throne.
      • \n
    • Radio Caroline becomes the United Kingdom\'s first "Pirate" radio station, broadcasting from a ship anchored just outside UK territorial waters on the east coast.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Merv Griffin\'s game show Jeopardy! debuts on NBC; Art Fleming is its first host.
    • \n
  • March 31 \xe2\x80\x93 The military overthrows Brazilian President Jo\xc3\xa3o Goulart in a coup, starting 21 years of dictatorship in Brazil. It ends in 1985.
    • \n

April[edit]

\n
Main article: April 1964
\n
    \n
  • April 1 Deployed military rule in Brazil ended the then government democratically elected president Jo\xc3\xa3o Goulart.
    • \n
  • April 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Mrs. Malcolm Peabody, 72, mother of Massachusetts Governor Endicott Peabody, is released on $450 bond after spending 2 days in a St. Augustine, Florida jail, for participating in an anti-segregation demonstration there.
    • \n
  • April 4\n
      \n
    • The Beatles hold the top 5 positions in the Billboard Top 40 singles in America, an unprecedented achievement. The top songs in America as listed on April 4, in order, are: Can\'t Buy Me Love, Twist and Shout, She Loves You, I Want to Hold Your Hand, and Please Please Me.
      • \n
    • Three high school friends in Hoboken, N.J., open the first Blimpie on Washington Street.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nApril 8: Gemini 1 launched.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Jigme Palden Dorji, premier of the Himalayan kingdom of Bhutan, is shot dead by an unidentified assassin in Puncholing, near the Indian border.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 IBM announces the System/360.
    • \n
  • April 8\n
      \n
    • Four of 5 railroad operating unions strike against the Illinois Central Railroad without warning, bringing to a head a 5-year dispute over railroad work rules.
      • \n
    • Gemini 1 is launched, the first unmanned test of the 2-man spacecraft.
      • \n
    • From Russia with Love premiers in U.S. movie theaters.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Nations Security Council adopts by a 9\xe2\x80\x930 vote a resolution deploring a British air attack on a fort in Yemen 12 days earlier, in which 25 persons were reported killed.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Demolition of the Polo Grounds sports stadium commences in New York City.
    • \n
  • April 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The Brazilian Congress elects Field Marshal Humberto de Alencar Castelo Branco as President of Brazil.
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 In Detroit, Malcolm X delivers a speech entitled "The Ballot or the Bullet"
    • \n
  • April 13\n
      \n
    • The 36th Academy Awards ceremony is held.
      • \n
    • Sidney Poitier is the first African-American to win an Academy Award in the category Best Actor in a Leading Role in Lilies of the Field.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 A Delta rocket\'s third-stage motor ignites prematurely in an assembly room at Cape Canaveral, killing 3.
    • \n
  • April 16\n
      \n
    • The Rolling Stones release their debut album, The Rolling Stones.
      • \n
    • Sentences totaling 307 years are passed on 12 men who stole \xc2\xa32.6m in used bank notes, after holding up the night mail train traveling from Glasgow to London in August 1963 \xe2\x80\x93 a heist that became known as the Great Train Robbery.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 17\n
      \n
    • In the United States, the Ford Mustang is officially unveiled to the public.
      • \n
    • Shea Stadium opens in Flushing, New York.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 19 \xe2\x80\x93 In Laos, the coalition government of Prince Souvanna Phouma is deposed by a right-wing military group, led by Brig. Gen. Kouprasith Abhay. Not supported by the U.S., the coup is ultimately unsuccessful, and Souvanna Phouma is reinstated, remaining Prime Minister until 1975.
    • \n
  • April 20\n
      \n
