2003 (MMIII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar, the 2003rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 3rd year of the 3rd millennium, the 3rd year of the 21st century, and the 4th year of the 2000s decade.

\n

2003 was designated the:

\n
    \n
  • International Year of fresh water.
    • \n
  • European Disability Year.

Events[edit]

\n

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Luiz In\xc3\xa1cio Lula da Silva is elected President of Brazil. He is the first left-wing president of Brazil since Jo\xc3\xa3o Goulart in 1961.
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Police in London arrest 7 suspects in connection with the 2002 Wood Green ricin plot.
    • \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 US Airways Express Flight 5481 crashes at Charlotte Douglas International Airport in Charlotte, North Carolina, killing all 21 people aboard.
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 STS-107: Space Shuttle Columbia is launched on its last flight.
    • \n
  • January 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Canberra Bushfires in Canberra, Australia, kill 4 people.
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The last signal is received from NASA\'s Pioneer 10 spacecraft, some 7.5 billion miles from Earth.
    • \n
  • January 29 \xe2\x80\x93 2003 Phnom Penh riots: In Phnom Penh, Cambodia, the Thai embassy is burned and commercial properties of Thai businesses are vandalized.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Belgium legally recognizes same-sex marriage.
    • \n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 1\n
      \n
    • At the conclusion of the STS-107 mission, the Space Shuttle Columbia disintegrates during reentry over Texas, killing all 7 astronauts on board.
      • \n
    • In Northern Ireland, Protestant Ulster Defence Association Belfast leader John Gregg is killed by a loyalist faction.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Iraq disarmament crisis: U.S. Secretary of State Colin Powell addresses the UN Security Council on Iraq.
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The War in Darfur begins.
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Global protests against the Iraq war: More than 10 million people protest in over 600 cities worldwide, the largest to take place before a war occurs.
    • \n
  • February 18 \xe2\x80\x93 An arsonist destroys a train in Daegu, South Korea, killing more than 190.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Station nightclub fire in West Warwick, Rhode Island, claims the lives of 100 people.
    • \n
  • February 26 \xe2\x80\x93 An American businessman is admitted to the Vietnam France Hospital in Hanoi, Vietnam, with the first identified case of SARS. WHO doctor Carlo Urbani reports the unusual, highly contagious disease to WHO. Both the businessman and doctor later die of the disease.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Former Bosnian Serb leader Biljana Plav\xc5\xa1i\xc4\x87 is sentenced by the U.N. tribunal in The Hague, Netherlands, to 11 years in prison.

\n
