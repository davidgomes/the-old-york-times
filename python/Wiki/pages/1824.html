1824 (MDCCCXXIV) was a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Julian calendar, the 1824th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 824th year of the 2nd millennium, the 24th year of the 19th century, and the 5th year of the 1820s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1824 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 After much controversy, Michael Faraday is finally elected as a member of the Royal Society with only one vote against him.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Ashanti crush British forces in the Gold Coast, killing the British governor Sir Charles MacCarthy (see also Wars between Britain and Ashanti in Ghana and Ashanti Confederacy).
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 First issue of the radical quarterly founded by Jeremy Bentham, The Westminster Review, is published in London.
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Sim\xc3\xb3n Bol\xc3\xadvar is proclaimed dictator of Peru.
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Chumash Revolt of 1824 begins against the Spanish presence in California.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 First Anglo-Burmese War begins.
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Florida State Capitol moves from St. Augustine, Florida to Tallahassee
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States War Department creates the Bureau of Indian Affairs.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1824 is signed.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May\xe2\x80\x93July \xe2\x80\x93 King Kamehameha II of Hawaii and his Queen Consort Kam\xc4\x81malu make a state visit to London, where they both die of smallpox.
    • \n
  • May 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Premi\xc3\xa8re of Beethoven\'s Symphony No. 9 (the "Choral") at the Theater am K\xc3\xa4rntnertor in Vienna. The deaf composer has to be turned around on the stage to witness the enthusiastic audience reaction.
    • \n
  • May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The British take Rangoon, Burma in the First Anglo-Burmese War.
    • \n
  • June 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Royal Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals is established in Great Britain.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Lafayette visits the United States, departing in September 7, 1825
    • \n
  • September 13 \xe2\x80\x93 With his crew and 29 convicts aboard the Amity, John Oxley arrives at and founds the Moreton Bay Penal Settlement at what is now Redcliffe in Queensland, Australia, after leaving Sydney.
    • \n
  • September 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles X succeeds his brother Louis XVIII as King of France.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 4 \xe2\x80\x93 First Constitution of Mexico enacted, declaring the country to be a federal republic.
    • \n
  • October 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Edinburgh Town Council founds the Edinburgh Municipal Fire Brigade, the first fire brigade in Britain, under the leadership of James Braidwood.
    • \n
  • October 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Joseph Aspdin patents Portland cement.
    • \n
  • November 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, the first technological university in the English-speaking world, is founded in Troy, New York.
    • \n
  • November 19 [O.S. November 7] \xe2\x80\x93 In the worst flood to date in Saint Petersburg, water rises 421 cm above normal and 200 lose their lives.
    • \n
  • November 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The first sod is turned in Ontario, for the first of four Welland Canals (the canal opens for a trial run exactly 5 years later to the day).
    • \n
  • December 3 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. presidential election, 1824: None of the four candidates for U.S. President gain a majority of the electoral votes, so the election is thrown into the U.S. House of Representatives.
    • \n
  • December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ayacucho: Peruvian forces defeat the Spanish.
    • \n
  • December 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Chief Pushmataha of the Choctaw Nation dies in Washington.
    • \n
  • December 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The First American Fraternity, Chi Phi (\xce\xa7\xce\xa6), is founded at Princeton University.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nLafayette, portrait by Joseph-D\xc3\xa9sir\xc3\xa9 Court
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • December 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Bathurst War comes to an end with the defeat of the Wiradjuri.
    • \n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Egyptians capture Crete.
    • \n
  • The Montparnasse Cemetery is established in Paris, France.
    • \n
  • The Dutch sign the Masang Agreement, temporarily ending hostilities in the Padri War.
    • \n
  • The name Australia, recommended by Matthew Flinders in 1804, is finally adopted as the official name of the country once known as New Holland.
    • \n
  • The Panoramagram is developed, creating the first volumetric display.
    • \n
  • The Fort Vancouver trading post is established on the lower Columbia River by the Hudson\'s Bay Company.
    • \n
  • The Colorado potato beetle is discovered by Thomas Say.
    • \n
