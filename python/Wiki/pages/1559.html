Year 1559 (MDLIX) was a common year starting on Sunday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 King Philip II of Spain marries his third wife, 14-year-old Elisabeth of Valois.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Elizabeth I of England is crowned in Westminster Abbey.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Queen Elizabeth I of England establishes the Church of England, with the Act of Uniformity 1558 and the Act of Supremacy 1558. The Oath of Supremacy is reinstated.
    • \n
  • March 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Emperor Gelawdewos of Ethiopia, defending his lands against the invasion of Nur ibn Mujahid, Sultan of Harar, is killed in battle. His brother Menas succeeds him as king.
    • \n
  • April 2\xe2\x80\x93April 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Peace of Cateau Cambr\xc3\xa9sis: France makes peace with England and Spain, ending the Italian War of 1551\xe2\x80\x9359. France gives up most of its gains in Italy (including Savoy), keeping only Saluzzo, but keeps the three Lorraine bishoprics of Metz, Toul, and Verdun, and the formerly English town of Calais.
    • \n
  • May 2 \xe2\x80\x93 John Knox returns from exile to Scotland to become the leader of the beginning Scottish Reformation.
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 At Basel, the body of Dutch Anabaptist leader David Joris is exhumed and burned, following his posthumous conviction of heresy.
    • \n
  • June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 A royal edict in France makes heresy punishable by death.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Francis II becomes King of France following the death of his father, Henry II, in a jousting accident.
    • \n
  • August 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Led by Don Trist\xc3\xa1n de Luna y Arellano, a Spanish missionary colony of 1,500 men, on 13 ships, arrives from Vera Cruz at Pensacola Bay, founding the oldest European settlement in the mainland U.S. (St. Augustine is founded in 1565.)
    • \n
  • September 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Just weeks after arrival at Pensacola, the Spanish missionary colony is decimated by a hurricane that kills hundreds, sinks five ships, with a galleon, and grounds a caravel; the 1,000 survivors divide to relocate/resupply the settlement, but suffer famine & attacks, and abandon the effort in 1561.
    • \n
  • September 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The 15-year-old King Francis II of France is crowned at Reims. The crown is too heavy for him and has to be held in place by his nobles.[1]
    • \n
  • December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Pius IV succeeds Pope Paul IV as the 224th pope.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Oda Nobunaga wins control of his native province of Owari.
    • \n
  • Margaret of Parma becomes Governor of the Netherlands in place of her brother, King Philip II of Spain.
    • \n
  • Jean Nicot, French ambassador to Portugal 1559\xe2\x80\x9361, describes the medicinal properties of tobacco which he introduces in the form of snuff to the French court.[2]
    • \n
  • Pope Paul IV promulgates the Pauline Index, an early version of the Index Librorum Prohibitorum.
    • \n
  • Between 1559 and 1736 almost 1,000 people accused of being witches are executed in England.
    • \n
