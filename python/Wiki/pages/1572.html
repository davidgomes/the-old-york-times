Year 1572 (MDLXXII) was a leap year starting on Tuesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Howard, 4th Duke of Norfolk, is tried for treason for his part in the Ridolfi plot to restore Catholicism in England. He is executed on June 2.[1]
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 Harrow School is founded, with a royal charter from Queen Elizabeth I of England.[2]
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Elizabeth I of England issues a proclamation which revokes all commissions on account of the frauds which they had fostered.
    • \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Capture of Brielle: The Sea Beggars, Netherlandish Calvinist rebels, capture the port city of Brielle. This leads to a wave of uprisings in Holland and Zealand against Spanish Habsburg rule, leaving most of those provinces (with the exception of Amsterdam), under rebel control.
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Gregory XIII succeeds Pope Pius V as the 226th pope.
    • \n
  • June 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Sea Beggars capture the city of Gorkum. Several Roman Catholic priests are imprisoned.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Sea Beggars hang nineteen previously imprisoned Roman Catholic priests, the \'Martyrs of Gorkum\' at Brielle.
    • \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Humphrey Gilbert leads 1500 volunteers from England on an expedition to assist the Sea Beggars.[1]
    • \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Wanli Emperor of China ascends the throne at the age of nine; he will rule for 48 years.
    • \n
  • July 29 \xe2\x80\x93 August 2 \xe2\x80\x93 A large Crimean Tatar\xe2\x80\x93Ottoman army which invaded Russia is routed in the Battle of Molodi.
    • \n
  • August 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Huguenot King Henry III of Navarre marries Margaret of Valois, sister of King Charles and daughter of Catherine de\' Medici, in a supposed attempt to reconcile Protestants and Catholics in France.
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 St. Bartholomew\'s Day massacre: Catholics in Paris murder thousands of Protestants, including Gaspard de Coligny and Petrus Ramus, at the order of King Charles IX with Catherine de Medici\'s connivance. Henry of Navarre and the Prince of Cond\xc3\xa9 barely escape the same fate. This brings about the Fourth War of Religion in France.
    • \n
  • October 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Relief of Goes (Eighty Years\' War): Soldiers of the Spanish Tercios wade across the estuary of the Scheldt to relieve the siege of Goes in the Spanish Netherlands.
    • \n
  • November 9\n
      \n
    • Siege of Sancerre: Catholic forces of the king lay siege to Sancerre, a Huguenot stronghold in central France. The fortified city holds out for nearly eight months without bombard artillery. This is one of the last times that slings are used in European warfare.
      • \n
    • Supernova SN 1572 is first observed in the constellation Cassiopeia by Cornelius Gemma. Tycho Brahe, who notes it two days later, will use it to challenge the prevailing view that stars do not change.[3] The supernova remnant remains visible through 1574.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Haarlem is begun by the Duke of Alva, Spanish commander in the Netherlands.
    • \n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Neo-Inca State in Vilcabamba, Peru, the last independent remnant of the Inca Empire, is conquered by Spain.
    • \n
  • Girolamo Mercuriale from Forl\xc3\xac (Italy) writes the work De morbis cutaneis ("On the diseases of the skin"), the first scientific tract on dermatology.
    • \n
  • Imaginary numbers defined by Rafael Bombelli.
    • \n
  • Portugal\'s national epic Os Lus\xc3\xadadas by Lu\xc3\xads de Cam\xc3\xb5es is first published.[4]
    • \n
  • Georg Braun begins publication of his urban atlas Civitates orbis terrarum in Cologne.
    • \n
