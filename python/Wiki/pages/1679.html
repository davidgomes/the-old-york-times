\n1679 (MDCLXXIX) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Julian calendar, the 1679th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 679th year of the 2nd millennium, the 79th year of the 17th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1670s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1679 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 King Charles II of England dissolves the "Cavalier Parliament" after nearly 18 years.[1]
    • \n
  • March 6\xe2\x80\x93May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 In England, the "Habeas Corpus Parliament" (or "First Exclusion Parliament") meets.[1] It is dissolved on July 12 while in recess by Royal prerogative to prevent it passing a Bill excluding the king\'s brother, the Catholic James, Duke of York, from the succession to the English throne as part of the Exclusion Crisis.
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Parliament of England passes the Habeas Corpus Act "for the better securing the liberty of the subject".[1]
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Drumclog: Scottish Covenanters defeat a small government force.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Armenia earthquake: A tremor with an estimated surface wave magnitude of 6.4 takes place in the Yerevan region of the Persian Empire.
    • \n
  • June 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Bothwell Bridge in Scotland: Royal forces led by James Scott, 1st Duke of Monmouth and John Graham of Claverhouse subdue the Scottish Covenanters.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The brigantine Le Griffon, commissioned by Ren\xc3\xa9-Robert Cavelier, Sieur de La Salle, is towed to the southern end of the Niagara River, to become the first ship to sail the upper Great Lakes.
    • \n
  • November 27 \xe2\x80\x93 A fire in Boston, Massachusetts, burns all of the warehouses, 80 houses, and all of the ships in the dockyards.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Tibet\xe2\x80\x93Ladakh\xe2\x80\x93Mughal War (1679\xe2\x80\x9384) starts with the Tibetan invasion of Ladakh.
    • \n
  • European explorers discover Niagara Falls.
    • \n
  • The city of Duluth, Minnesota, is founded. (citation needed)
    • \n
  • The Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb reimposes jizya.
    • \n
  • Malpas Tunnel on the Canal du Midi in H\xc3\xa9rault, France, Europe\'s first navigable canal tunnel, is excavated by Pierre-Paul Riquet (165 m, concrete lined).[2]
    • \n
