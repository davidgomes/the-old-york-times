\n1798 (MDCCXCVIII) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1798th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 798th year of the 2nd millennium, the 98th year of the 18th century, and the 9th year of the 1790s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1798 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 A coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat is staged in the Netherlands (Batavian Republic). Unitarian Democrat Pieter Vreede makes an end to the power of the parliament (with a conservative-moderate majority).
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The papacy is removed from power by French General Louis-Alexandre Berthier.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 French troops enter Bern.
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 French forces invade the Papal States and establish the Roman Republic.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Mississippi Territory is organized by the United States from territory ceded by Georgia and South Carolina; later it is twice expanded to include disputed territory claimed by both the U.S. and Spain (which acquired territory in trade with Great Britain).
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Helvetic Republic, a French client republic, proclaimed following collapse of the Old Swiss Confederacy on French invasion.
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 France annexes Geneva.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Irish republicans and nationalists, known as the Society of United Irishmen, launch a rebellion against British rule in expectation of greater support from France which only sent 1100 men. The United Irishmen are unique amongst Irish nationalist movements in that they unify Catholics and Protestants around republican ideals. The rebellion rages sporadically but is defeated by the British by October.
    • \n
  • June 12\n
      \n
    • The French take Malta.
      • \n
    • A moderate coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat in the Netherlands (Batavian Republic) deposes Pieter Vreede.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Mission San Luis Rey de Francia is founded.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon\'s troops land in Egypt.
    • \n
  • July 7\n
      \n
    • Quasi-War: The United States Congress rescinds treaties with France, sparking the war.
      • \n
    • In the action of USS Delaware vs La Croyable, the newly-formed United States Navy makes its first capture.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Marine Corps is re-established.
    • \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Shubra Khit between French and Mamelukes, during Napoleon\'s march from Alexandria to take Cairo.
    • \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Alien and Sedition Acts become United States law, making it a federal crime to write, publish, or utter false or malicious statements about the United States government.
    • \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Relief of Sick and Disabled Seamen Act is signed into law, creating the Marine Hospital Service, the forerunner to the current United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps.
    • \n
  • July 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of the Pyramids: Napoleon defeats Ottoman forces near the Pyramids.
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon occupies Cairo.
    • \n
  • July 31 \xe2\x80\x93 A second round of elections are held in the Netherlands (Batavian Republic); no general elections this time.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nAugust 1: Battle of the Nile.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • August 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of the Nile (near Abu Qir): Lord Nelson defeats the French navy under Admiral Brueys; Nelson himself is wounded in the head.
    • \n
  • August 22 \xe2\x80\x93 French troops land at Kilcummin in County Mayo to assist the Irish rebellion.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Charles Brockden Brown publishes the first significant American novel, the Gothic fiction Wieland: or, The Transformation; an American Tale.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of St. George\'s Caye: Off the coast of British Honduras (now Belize), a group of British nationals and African slaves defeat a force sent from Mexico to drive them out.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Lyrical Ballads is published anonymously by Samuel Taylor Coleridge and William Wordsworth, inaugurating the English Romantic movement in literature.
    • \n
  • October 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Tory Island: A British Royal Navy squadron under Sir John Borlase Warren prevents French Republican ships commanded by Jean-Baptiste-Fran\xc3\xa7ois Bompart landing reinforcements for the Society of United Irishmen on the Donegal coast; Irish leader Wolfe Tone is captured and later dies of wounds.
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Beginning of the Russo-Ottoman siege of Corfu.
    • \n
  • November 8 \xe2\x80\x93 British whaler John Fearn becomes the first European to land on Nauru.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The first (anonymous) publication occurs of An Essay on the Principle of Population by Thomas Malthus.
    • \n
  • Aarau becomes the temporary capital of the Helvetic Republic.
    • \n
  • Alois Senefelder invents lithography.
    • \n
  • Eli Whitney contracts with the U.S. federal government for 10,000 rifles, which he produces with interchangeable parts.
    • \n
  • The first census in Brazil counts 2 million blacks in a total population of 3.25 million.
    • \n
  • The Ayrshire (Earl of Carrick\'s Own) Yeomanry, a British Army Yeomanry Cavalry Regiment, formed by The Earl of Cassillis at Culzean Castle, Ayrshire in 1794, is adopted onto the British Army List.
    • \n
  • Jenner publishes his work on smallpox vaccination
    • \n
