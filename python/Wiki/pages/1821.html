\n1821 (MDCCCXXI) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1821st year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 821st year of the 2nd millennium, the 21st year of the 19th century, and the 2nd year of the 1820s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1821 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Peter I Island in the Antarctic first sighted by Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen.
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Alexander Island, the largest in Antarctica, is first discovered by Fabian Gottlieb von Bellingshausen.
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Columbian College of the District of Columbia is chartered by President Monroe.
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 James Monroe is sworn in for his second term as President of the United States.
    • \n
  • March 25 (O.S.)/April 6 (N.S.) \xe2\x80\x93 Metropolitan bishop Germanos of Patras raises the revolutionary flag of Greece at the Monastery of Agia Lavra, symbolically marking the beginning of the Greek War of Independence against the Ottoman Empire; later celebrated as Greece\'s traditional Independence Day.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Ecumenical Patriarch Gregory V of Constantinople is blamed by the Ottoman government for being unable to suppress Greek independence and is hanged outside the main gate of the Patriarchal Cathedral immediately after the celebration of Easter.
    • \n
  • May 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Emperor Napoleon dies in exile on Saint Helena of stomach cancer.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Establishment of the Peloponnesian Senate by the Greek rebels.
    • \n
  • June 14 \xe2\x80\x93 King Badi VII of Sennar surrenders his throne and realm to Ismail Pasha, general of the Ottoman Empire, ending the existence of that Sudanese kingdom.
    • \n
  • June 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Filiki Eteria are decisively defeated by the Ottomans at Dr\xc4\x83g\xc4\x83\xc8\x99ani (in Wallachia).
    • \n
  • June 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Carabobo: Sim\xc3\xb3n Bol\xc3\xadvar wins Venezuela\'s independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • June 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The New Hampton School is founded in the United States state of New Hampshire.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States takes possession of its newly bought territory of Florida from Spain.
    • \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 George IV is crowned king of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland.
    • \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Peru declares independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Missouri is admitted as the 24th U.S. state (see History of Missouri).
    • \n
  • August 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Navarino massacre: Greek rebels massacre 3,000 inhabitants of the city of Navarino.
    • \n
  • August 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Jarvis Island is discovered by the crew of the Eliza Frances.
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of C\xc3\xb3rdoba signed in C\xc3\xb3rdoba, Veracruz, Mexico, ratifying the Plan of Iguala of February 24 for Mexico\'s independence from Spain and, although not recognised by the latter, effectively concludes the Mexican War of Independence.
    • \n
  • September 4\n
      \n
    • Chilean general Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Carrera is executed by an Argentinian military tribunal in the city of Mendoza.
      • \n
    • The Ukase of 1821: Russia proclaims territorial sovereignty over Northwestern North America, modern-day Alaska.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Republic of Gran Colombia (a federation covering much of present-day Venezuela, Colombia, Panama, and Ecuador) is established, with Sim\xc3\xb3n Bol\xc3\xadvar as the founding President and Francisco de Paula Santander as vice president.
    • \n
  • September 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Guatemala, El Salvador, Honduras, Nicaragua and Costa Rica gain independence from Spain by the Act of Independence of Central America. On October 29, the newly independent First Mexican Empire proposes that Guatemala should merge with it.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Amherst College is founded in Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Greek War of Independence: Siege of Tripolitsa ends when Greek rebels capture the city of Tripoli, Greece.
    • \n
  • September 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Army of the Three Guarantees enters Mexico City and the Declaration of Independence of the Mexican Empire from Spain is proclaimed, following the Mexican War of Independence.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 American Old West: The Santa Fe Trail is first used by William Becknell.
    • \n
  • November 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Panama declares independence from Spain, joining Gran Colombia.
    • \n
  • December 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Dominican Republic declares independence from Spain (see History of the Dominican Republic). It will be invaded by Haiti in 1822.
    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The South Orkney Islands are discovered by George Powell and Nathaniel Palmer.[1][2]
    • \n
  • December 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The world\'s first geographical society, the Soci\xc3\xa9t\xc3\xa9 de g\xc3\xa9ographie, is established in Paris.
    • \n
  • December 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Eyjafjallaj\xc3\xb6kull volcano in Iceland begins to erupt.
    • \n
\n
