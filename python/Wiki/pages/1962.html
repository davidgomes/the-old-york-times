1962 (MCMLXII) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1962nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 962nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 62nd year of the 20th century, and the 3rd year of the 1960s decade.

January[edit]

\n
Main article: January 1962
\n
    \n
  • January 1\n
      \n
    • Western Samoa becomes independent from New Zealand.
      • \n
    • The United States Navy SEALs, elite special forces, are activated. SEAL Team One is commissioned in the Pacific Fleet and SEAL Team Two in the Atlantic Fleet.
      • \n

      • \n
    • NBC introduces the "Laramie peacock" before a midnight showing of the series Laramie in the United States.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 2 \xe2\x80\x93 NAACP Executive Secretary Roy Wilkins praises U.S. President John F. Kennedy\'s "personal role" in advancing civil rights.
    • \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope John XXIII excommunicates Fidel Castro.
    • \n
  • January 4 \xe2\x80\x93 New York City introduces a subway train that operates without a crew on board.
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The first album on which The Beatles play, My Bonnie, credited to "Tony Sheridan and the Beat Brothers" (recorded last June in Hamburg), is released by Polydor in the U.K.[1][2]
    • \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Harmelen train disaster: 93 die in the worst Dutch rail disaster.
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Cuba and the Soviet Union sign a trade pact.
    • \n
  • January 10 \xe2\x80\x93 An avalanche on Nevado Huascar\xc3\xa1n in Peru causes 4,000 deaths.
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The Indonesian Army confirms that it has begun operations in West Irian.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Albania allies itself with the People\'s Republic of China.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Portugal abandons the U.N. General Assembly due to the debate over Angola.
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 A military coup occurs in the Dominican Republic.
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 A counter-coup occurs in the Dominican Republic; the old government returns except for the new president Rafael Filiberto Bonnelly.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Organization of American States suspends Cuba\'s membership. The suspension is lifted in 2009.
    • \n
  • January 24\n
      \n
    • The East German government readopts conscription.
      • \n
    • The Organisation de l\'arm\xc3\xa9e secr\xc3\xa8te (OAS) bombs the French Foreign Ministry.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n

    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Soviet government changed all place names honoring Molotov, Kaganovich and Georgy Malenkov.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Two of the high-wire "Flying Wallendas" are killed, when their famous seven-person pyramid collapses during a performance in Detroit.
    • \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 Stena Line is established as a ferry operator by Sten A. Olsson in Gothenburg, Sweden.

    • \n
  • April 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The Century 21 Exposition World\'s Fair opens in Seattle.

  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Ranger 4 spacecraft crashes into the Moon.
