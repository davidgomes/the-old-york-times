\n1843 (MDCCCXLIII) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1843rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 843rd year of the 2nd millennium, the 43rd year of the 19th century, and the 4th year of the 1840s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1843 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January\n
      \n
    • Serial publication of Charles Dickens\' novel Martin Chuzzlewit begins in London. In the July chapters, he lands his hero in the United States.
      • \n
    • Publication of Edgar Allan Poe\'s Gothic short story "The Tell-Tale Heart" in a Philadelphia magazine.
      • \n
    • The Quaker magazine The Friend is first published in London.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Publication in China of Illustrated Treatise on the Maritime Kingdoms (\xe6\xb5\xb7\xe5\x9c\x8b\xe5\x9c\x96\xe5\xbf\x97, H\xc7\x8eigu\xc3\xb3 T\xc3\xbazh\xc3\xac) compiled by Wei Yuan and others, the first significant Chinese work on the West.[1][2]
    • \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Antarctic explorer James Clark Ross discovers Snow Hill Island.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Hon\xc3\xb3rio Hermeto Carneiro Le\xc3\xa3o, Marquis of Paran\xc3\xa1, becomes de facto first prime minister of the Empire of Brazil.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Uruguayan Civil War: Argentina supports Oribe of Uruguay and begins a siege of Montevideo.
    • \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The Virginia Minstrels perform the first minstrel show, at the Bowery Amphitheatre in New York City.
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Giuseppe Verdi\'s opera I Lombardi alla prima crociata premieres at La Scala in Milan.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The event that inspired the Beatles song "Being for the Benefit of Mr. Kite!" is held in England.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Lord George Paulet occupies the Kingdom of Hawaii in the name of Great Britain in the Paulet Affair (1843).
    • \n
  • March 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Danish government re-establishes the Althing in Iceland as an advisory body, by royal decree.
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Catawba County, North Carolina, is created and its first court held in Mathias Barringer Jr.\'s house.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Victoria, British Columbia, is founded by the Hudson\'s Bay Company as a trading post and fort.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Petr\xc3\xb3polis is founded by the government of Brazil.[3]
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The world does not end, contrary to the first prediction by American preacher William Miller.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Hyderabad: The Bombay Army led by Major General Sir Charles Napier defeats the Talpur Emirs, securing Sindh as a Province of British India.
    • \n
  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Marc Isambard Brunel\'s Thames Tunnel, the first tunnel under the River Thames and the world\'s first bored underwater tunnel, is opened in London.[4]
    • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 Eta Carinae is temporarily the second-brightest star in the night sky.
    • \n
  • 7th April \xe2\x80\x93 The Indian Slavery Act, 1843 removes legal support for slavery within the territories of the East India Company
    • \n
  • May 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Natal is proclaimed a British colony.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Disruption in Edinburgh of the Free Church of Scotland from the Church of Scotland.
    • \n
  • May 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The first major wagon train headed for the American Northwest sets out with one thousand pioneers from Elm Grove, Missouri on the Oregon Trail.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Chile takes possession of the Strait of Magellan.
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 In Barbados, Samuel Jackman Prescod is the first non-white person elected to the House of Assembly.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Wairau Affray in the South Island of New Zealand: An armed posse of British settlers sent to arrest M\xc4\x81ori chief Te Rauparaha clash with members of his Ng\xc4\x81ti Toa tribe, resulting in 26 deaths
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Edgar Allan Poe\'s short story "The Gold-Bug" begins serialization in American newspapers.

    • \n
  • August 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Tivoli Gardens, one of the oldest still intact amusement parks in the world, opens in Copenhagen, Denmark.

  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Ada Lovelace translates and expands Menabrea\'s notes on Charles Babbage\'s Analytical Engine, including an algorithm for calculating a sequence of Bernoulli numbers, regarded as the world\'s first computer program.[6][7][8]

      • \n

    • \n

    • \n
  • December 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The first total solar eclipse of Saros 139 occurs over southern Asia.

  • December \xe2\x80\x93 The world\'s first Christmas cards, commissioned by Sir Henry Cole in London from the artist John Callcott Horsley, are sent.[12]
