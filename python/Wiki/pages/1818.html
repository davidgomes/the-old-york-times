1818 (MDCCCXVIII) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1818th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 818th year of the 2nd millennium, the 18th year of the 19th century, and the 9th year of the 1810s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1818 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1\n
      \n
    • Battle of Koregaon: Troops of the British East India Company score a decisive victory over the Maratha Empire.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 3 (21:52 UTC) \xe2\x80\x93 Venus occults Jupiter. It is the last occultation of one planet by another before November 22, 2065.
    • \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Mandeswar brings an end to the Third Anglo-Maratha War, ending the dominance of Marathas, and enhancing the power of the British East India Company, which controls territory occupied by 180 million Indians.
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Percy Bysshe Shelley\'s "Ozymandias" is published pseudonymously in London.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Upon his death, King Charles XIII of Sweden (Charles II of Norway) is succeeded on both thrones by his adoptive son Charles XIV/III John, starting the Royal House of Bernadotte.
    • \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Chile proclaims its independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Mary Shelley\'s Frankenstein is published anonymously in London.
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 First Seminole War: General Andrew Jackson and his American army invade Florida.
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Easter Day in Western Christianity falls on its earliest possible date. In Western Christianity, it will not occur on this date again until 2285.
    • \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 John Ross sets sail in search of the Northwest Passage.
    • \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 First Seminole War: Battle of Miccosukee, Florida: General Andrew Jackson defeats chief Kinhagee.
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Congress adopts the flag of the United States as having thirteen red and white stripes and one star for each state (twenty) with additional stars to be added whenever a new state is added to the Union.
    • \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Maip\xc3\xba in the Chilean War of Independence: Patriot rebels led by Jos\xc3\xa9 de San Mart\xc3\xadn decisively defeat the Spanish Royalists.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Brooks Brothers, the oldest men\'s clothier in the United States, opens its first store on the northeast corner of Catherine and Cherry Streets in New York City, where the later South Street Seaport stands.
    • \n
  • April 14\xe2\x80\x93August 9 \xe2\x80\x93 United States Survey of the Coast operations suspended.
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles XIV of Sweden\xe2\x80\x93Norway is crowned king of Sweden.
    • \n
  • The Westmorland Gazette is first published at Kendal in the Lake District of England; in July, Thomas De Quincey will begin a 16-month term as editor.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Separate Topographical Bureau of the United States Department of War established.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Sir Stamford Raffles sets out to visit Lord Hastings, the Governor-General of India, to gain his approval to establish a trading station at the southern tip of the Malay Peninsula (modern-day Singapore).
    • \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Carl III of Sweden\xe2\x80\x93Norway is crowned king of Norway, in Trondheim.
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Border markers are formally installed for the European territory of Moresnet.
    • \n
  • October 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Claudine Th\xc3\xa9venet (known as \'Mary of St. Ignatius\') founds the Roman Catholic order Religieuses de J\xc3\xa9sus-Marie ("Religious of Jesus And Mary") in Lyon, France.
    • \n
  • October 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty between the U.S. and the United Kingdom establishes the boundary between the U.S. and British North America as the 49th parallel from the Lake of the Woods to the Rocky Mountains, also creating the Northwest Angle.
    • \n
  • November 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-Chinese College is founded by Robert Morrison in Malacca (later renamed Ying Wa College).
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Saint Louis Academy, which later becomes Saint Louis University, is founded by Reverend Louis William Valentine Dubourg.
    • \n
  • December 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Illinois is admitted as the 21st U.S. state.
    • \n
  • December 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Christmas carol "Silent Night" (Stille Nacht) with words by the priest Josef Mohr set to music by organist Franz Xaver Gruber is first performed at St. Nikolaus parish church in Oberndorf bei Salzburg in Austria.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Afghans defeat a Persian invasion at the Battle of Kafir Qala.
    • \n
  • The Old Vic theatre founded (as the Royal Coburg Hall) in London.
    • \n
  • The first edition of the Farmers\' Almanac is published in the United States.
    • \n
  • First Serbian dictionary, made by Vuk Karad\xc5\xbei\xc4\x87.
    • \n
  • Besses o\' th\' Barn brass band is formed in Whitefield, near Manchester, by this date.
