2006 (MMVI) was a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Gregorian calendar, the 2006th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 6th year of the 3rd millennium, the 6th year of the 21st century, and the 7th year of the 2000s decade.

Events[edit]

\n

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Russia cuts the shipment of natural gas to Ukraine over a price dispute.[2]
    • \n
  • January 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Ariel Sharon, Prime Minister of Israel, suffers a severe stroke and cerebral hemorrhage.
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 A hotel in Mecca, Saudi Arabia, collapses, killing 76 pilgrims visiting to perform Hajj.[3]
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 A stampede during the Stoning of the Devil ritual on the last day at the Hajj in Mina, Saudi Arabia, kills 362 pilgrims.[4][5]
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA\'s Stardust mission successfully ends, the first to return dust from a comet.[6]
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Ellen Johnson Sirleaf assumes office as President of Liberia, the first female elected head of state in Africa.
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA launches the first space mission to Pluto as a rocket hurls the New Horizons spacecraft on a 9-year journey.[7]
    • \n
  • January 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Benedict XVI issues his first encycylical, Deus caritas est.[8]
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Celebrations are held in Salzburg and around the world, for the 250th anniversary of the birth of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart.[9]
    • \n
\n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Egyptian passenger ferry, MS al-Salam Boccaccio 98 carrying more than 1,400 people, sinks in the Red Sea off the Saudi coast, with only 388 rescued.[10]
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Wowowee stampede at the PhilSports Arena in Pasig City, Philippines, kills 74 people and leaves 600 injured.[11]
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 February 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The 2006 Winter Olympics are held in Turin, Italy.
    • \n
  • February 17 \xe2\x80\x93 A massive mudslide occurs in Southern Leyte, Philippines; the official death toll is set at 1,126.[12]
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Pasta de Conchos mine disaster: Sixty-five miners die after becoming trapped underground, following an explosion in Nueva Rosita, Mexico.[13]
    • \n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The final contact attempt with Pioneer 10 receives no response.[14]
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA\'s Cassini\xe2\x80\x93Huygens spacecraft discovers geysers of a liquid substance shooting from Saturn\'s moon Enceladus, signaling a possible presence of water.[15]
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA\'s Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter enters orbit around Mars.[16][17]
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Nations General Assembly votes overwhelmingly to establish the United Nations Human Rights Council.
    • \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Jack Dorsey, Noah Glass, Biz Stone, and Evan Williams found the popular social-networking service Twitter. They officially launch the site later in the year.
    • \n
  • March 28 \xe2\x80\x93 A scramjet jet engine, HyShot III, designed to fly at 7 times the speed of sound, is successfully tested at Woomera, South Australia.[18][19]
    • \n

    \n

    • \n

    • \n
  • April 11\n
      \n
    • The European Space Agency\'s Venus Express spaceprobe enters Venus\' orbit.[22]

      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Iran announces a deal with Russia, involving a joint uranium enrichment firm on Russian soil;[25] 9 days later Iran announces that it will not move all activity to Russia, thus leading to a de facto termination of the deal.

May[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 The Human Genome Project publishes the last chromosome sequence, in Nature.
    • \n
  • May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 East Timor\'s Foreign Minister Jos\xc3\xa9 Ramos-Horta officially requests military assistance from the governments of Australia, New Zealand, Malaysia, and Portugal.[26]
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The 6.4 Mw Yogyakarta earthquake shakes central Java with an MSK intensity of IX (Destructive), leaving more than 5,700 dead and 37,000 injured.
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Sidoarjo mud flow: The world\'s biggest mud volcano is created by the blowout of a natural gas well being drilled in the subdistrict of Porong, Sidoarjo in East Java, Indonesia. This results in displacement of more than 11,000 persons from 8 villages, as well as damage to road and power infrastructures. Several (Twenty-five) factories are also abandoned.[27]
