\n1759 (MDCCLIX) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1759th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 759th year of the 2nd millennium, the 59th year of the 18th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1750s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1759 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929. In Great Britain, this year was known as the Annus Mirabilis because of British victories in the Seven Years\' War.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJanuary 15: British Museum.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 George Washington marries Martha Dandridge Custis.
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 In Philadelphia, the first American life insurance company is incorporated.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 T\xc3\xa1vora affair: The T\xc3\xa1vora family is executed following accusations of the attempted regicide of Joseph I of Portugal.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The British Museum opens at Montagu House in London (after 6 years of development).
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Bergen: A French army defeats Ferdinand, Duke of Brunswick.
    • \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Josiah Wedgwood founds the Wedgwood pottery company in England.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nAugust 12: Battle of Kunersdorf.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • Thursday July 19: The Great Stockholm Fire 1759 broke out at S\xc3\xb6dermalm.
    • \n
  • July 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War (French and Indian War): In Canada, British forces capture Fort Niagara from French, who subsequently abandon Fort Rouill\xc3\xa9.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War (French and Indian War): At the southern end of Lake Champlain, British forces capture Fort Carillon from French, and rename it Fort Ticonderoga.
    • \n
  • July 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War (French and Indian War): British troops under Jeffrey Amherst take Fort Ticonderoga.
    • \n
  • August 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Minden: Anglo\xe2\x80\x93Hanoverian forces under Ferdinand of Brunswick defeat the French army of the Duc de Broglie, but due to the disobedience of the English cavalry commander Lord George Sackville, the French are able to withdraw unmolested.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Ferdinand VI of Spain dies and is succeeded by his half\xe2\x80\x93brother Charles III. Charles resigns the thrones of Naples and Sicily to his third son, Ferdinand IV.
    • \n
  • August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Kunersdorf: Frederick the Great is rebuffed in bloody assaults on the combined Austro\xe2\x80\x93Russian army of Pyotr Saltykov and Ernst von Laudon. This is one of Frederick\'s greatest defeats.
    • \n
  • August 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Lagos: The British fleet of Edward Boscawen defeats a French force under Commodore Jean-Fran\xc3\xa7ois de La Clue-Sabran off the Portuguese coast.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nSept. 13: Battle \xe2\x80\x93 Plains of Abraham.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Pondicherry: An inconclusive naval battle is fought off the coast of India between the French Admiral d\'Ach\xc3\xa9 and the British under George Pocock. The French forces are badly damaged and sail home, never to return.
    • \n
  • September 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War (French and Indian War): Quebec falls to British forces following General Wolfe\'s victory in the Battle of the Plains of Abraham just outside the city. Both the French Commander (the Marquis de Montcalm) and the British General James Wolfe are fatally wounded.
    • \n
  • September 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Carrington Bowles publishes A Journey Through Europe, a board game designed by John Jefferys, the earliest board game whose designer\'s name is known.
    • \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Smeaton\'s Tower, John Smeaton\xe2\x80\x99s Eddystone Lighthouse off the coast of South West England, is first illuminated.[1]
    • \n
  • October 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Near East earthquakes of 1759 \xe2\x80\x93 The first event in an earthquake doublet occurs to the north of the Sea of Galilee with a surface wave magnitude of 6.6 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII\xe2\x80\x93IX (Severe\xe2\x80\x93Violent). About 2,000 were killed in Safed.
    • \n
  • November 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Quiberon Bay: The British fleet of Sir Edward Hawke defeats a French fleet under Marshal de Conflans near the coast of Brittany. This is the decisive naval engagement of the Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 after this, the French are no longer able to field a significant fleet.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nNovember 20: Battle of Quiberon Bay
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • November 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Maxen: The Austrian army of Marshal von Daun cuts off and forces the surrender of a Prussian force under Friedrich von Finck.
    • \n
  • November 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Near East earthquakes of 1759 \xe2\x80\x93 The second and stronger event in an earthquake doublet occurs to the east of Beirut with a surface wave magnitude of 7.4 and a maximum Mercalli intensity of IX (Violent), destroying all the villages in the Beqaa Valley.
    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The Germantown Union School (now called "Germantown Academy"), America\'s oldest nonsectarian day school, is founded.
    • \n
  • December 31 \xe2\x80\x93 The Guinness Brewery is leased by Arthur Guinness in St. James\'s Gate, Dublin, Ireland, for the brewing of Guinness.
    • \n

\nHarrison\'s marine chronometer
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • The town of Egedesminde (modern Aasiaat) is founded in Greenland.
    • \n
  • English clockmaker John Harrison produces his "No. 1 sea watch" ("H4"), the first successful marine chronometer.[2]
    • \n
  • Kew Gardens established in England by Augusta of Saxe-Coburg, the mother of George III.[3]
    • \n
  • Churton Town, the Orange County, North Carolina county seat laid out in 1754, is renamed Childsburgh in honor of North Carolina attorney general Thomas Child. It is later renamed Hillsborough in 1766.
    • \n
  • Fire destroys 250 houses in Stockholm.
    • \n
  • Madame du Coudray publishes Abr\xc3\xa9g\xc3\xa9 de l\'art des accouchements ("The Art of Obstetrics") and the French government authorizes her to carry her instruction "throughout the realm" and promises financial support.
