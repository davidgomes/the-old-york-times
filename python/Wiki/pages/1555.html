Year 1555 (MDLV) was a common year starting on Tuesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Fall of the Ava Kingdom in Upper Burma.
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The Diet of Augsburg begins.
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 John Rogers, burned at the stake in London, becomes the first Protestant martyr under Mary I of England.
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Oshea Sanders becomes the second Marian Protestant martyr in England, being led barefoot to his execution by burning at the stake.
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Rowland Taylor, Rector of Hadleigh, Suffolk, and John Hooper, deposed Bishop of Gloucester, are burned at the stake in England.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Marcellus II succeeds Julius III as the 222nd pope. He will reign for 22 days.
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 After 18 months of siege, the Republic of Siena surrenders to the Florentine\xe2\x80\x93Imperial army.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Paul IV succeeds Marcellus II as the 223rd pope.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Amasya between the Ottoman Empire and Safavid Persia concludes the Ottoman\xe2\x80\x93Safavid War (1532\xe2\x80\x931555).
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Paul IV creates the first Jewish ghetto in Rome.
    • \n
  • September 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Peace of Augsburg is signed between Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor, and the Lutheran Schmalkaldic League establishing the principle Cuius regio, eius religio, that is, rulers within the Empire can choose the religion of their realm.
    • \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Two of the Oxford Martyrs, Hugh Latimer and Nicholas Ridley, are burned at the stake in England.
    • \n
  • October 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles V abdicates as Holy Roman Emperor and is succeeded by his brother Ferdinand.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Russia breaks a 60-year-old truce with Sweden by attacking Finland.
    • \n
  • Humayun resumes rule of the Mughal Empire.
    • \n
  • Bairam Khan defeats Hindu forces at Panipat.
    • \n
  • Collapse of the Adal Sultanate in the Horn of Africa.
    • \n
  • The Muscovy Company is chartered in England to trade with Muscovy and Richard Chancellor negotiates with the Tsar.
    • \n
  • English captain John Lok returns from Guinea with 5 Africans to train as interpreters for future trading voyages.
    • \n
  • Richard Eden publishes The Decades of the Newe Worlde or West India, a translation into English of parts of Pietro Martire d\'Anghiera\'s De orbe novo decades, Gonzalo Oviedo\'s Natural hystoria de las Indias and others[1] including the first recorded use in English of the country name \'China\'.
    • \n
  • "Negro" \xe2\x80\x93 the Spanish term for "black person" \xe2\x80\x93 is coined.
    • \n
  • Gresham\'s School is founded by Sir John Gresham at Holt, Norfolk in England.
    • \n
  • William Annyas becomes the Mayor of Youghal in Ireland, the first Jew to hold such a position in Ireland.[2]
    • \n
  • John Dee is charged, but cleared, of treason in England.
    • \n
  • Orlande de Lassus\' first book of madrigals is published, in Antwerp.
    • \n
  • Lorenzo de\' Medici orders a violin from Andrea Amati of Cremona.
    • \n
