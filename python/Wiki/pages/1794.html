\n1794 (MDCCXCIV) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1794th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 794th year of the 2nd millennium, the 94th year of the 18th century, and the 5th year of the 1790s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1794 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The French First Republic abolishes slavery.
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The first session of the United States Senate is open to the public.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Canonsburg Academy (now Washington & Jefferson College) is chartered by the General Assembly.[1]
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 General Antoni Madali\xc5\x84ski, a commander of the National Cavalry in the Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth, disobeys an order from the ruling Russian Empire and Kingdom of Prussia imposing demobilization, advancing his troops from Ostro\xc5\x82\xc4\x99ka to Krak\xc3\xb3w.
    • \n
  • March 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Eli Whitney is granted a patent for the cotton gin.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Tadeusz Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko makes his proclamation starting the Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko Uprising against the Russian Empire and Kingdom of Prussia in the Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth and Prussian Partition.
    • \n
  • March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Government authorizes the building of the first six United States Navy vessels (in 1797 the first three frigates, United States, Constellation (1797) and Constitution go into service), not to be confused with October 13, 1775, which is observed as the Navy\'s Birthday.
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Rac\xc5\x82awice: Polish supporters of the Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko Uprising defeat forces of the Russian Empire.
    • \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 French Revolution: Georges Danton is executed.
    • \n
  • April 17\xe2\x80\x9319 \xe2\x80\x93 Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko Uprising \xe2\x80\x93 Warsaw Uprising: The Polish people overthrow the Russian garrison in Warsaw.
    • \n
  • April 29\xe2\x80\x93May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Boulou: The French defeat the Spanish and Portuguese forces.
    • \n
  • May 7 \xe2\x80\x93 French Revolution: Robespierre establishes the Cult of the Supreme Being as the new state religion of the French First Republic.
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 French Revolution: Chemist Antoine Lavoisier is executed by guillotine.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Tourcoing: French troops defeat British forces.
    • \n
  • May 28\xe2\x80\x93June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Glorious First of June (Battle of Ushant): British and French ships battle to a draw.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 British troops capture Port-au-Prince in Haiti.
    • \n
  • June 17\n
      \n
    • The Anglo-Corsican Kingdom is established.
      • \n
    • Battle of Mykonos: The British Royal Navy captures French frigate Sibylle.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Fleurus: French forces defeat the Austrians and their allies, leading to permanent loss of the Austrian Netherlands and destruction of the Dutch Republic. French use of an observation balloon marks the first participation of an aircraft in battle.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Horatio Nelson loses the sight in his right eye in a British military operation at Calvi in Corsica.
    • \n
  • July 13\xe2\x80\x93September 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko Uprising \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Warsaw: The Polish people resist a siege by armies of the Russian Empire and Kingdom of Prussia.
    • \n
  • July 27 (9 Thermidor) \xe2\x80\x93 Thermidorian Reaction: Robespierre is arrested on the orders of the French National Convention; he is executed the next day, ending the French Revolution\'s Reign of Terror.
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Fallen Timbers in Northwestern Ohio.
    • \n
  • August 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Stonyhurst College is finally established as a Roman Catholic school in Lancashire, England, having had several European locations.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The University of Tennessee is established at Knoxville.
    • \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 A Federal army quells the Whiskey Rebellion in the United States.
    • \n
  • October 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Maciejowice: Forces of the Russian Empire defeat Polish supporters of the Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko Uprising; Tadeusz Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko is wounded and captured.
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Praga: Russian General Alexander Suvorov storms Warsaw in the war against the Polish Ko\xc5\x9bciuszko Uprising and captures Praga, one of its suburbs, killing many civilians.
    • \n
  • November 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The first recorded meeting of the Franklin Literary Society is held at Canonsburg Academy (modern-day Washington & Jefferson College).[2]
    • \n
  • November 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States and Great Britain conclude the Jay Treaty, the basis for ten years of peaceful trade between the two nations.
    • \n
