1609 (MDCIX) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1609th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 609th year of the 2nd millennium, the 9th year of the 17th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1600s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1609 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 The Basque witch trials begin.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 One of the world\'s first newspapers, Avisa Relation oder Zeitung, begins publication in Wolfenb\xc3\xbcttel (Holy Roman Empire).
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Bank of Amsterdam is established.
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The last day of Keich\xc5\x8d \xe6\x85\xb6\xe9\x95\xb7 13 (according to the Japanese lunar calendar).
    • \n
  • c. March \xe2\x80\x93 Hugo Grotius publishes Mare Liberum, his legal text on freedom of the seas, in Leiden.
    • \n
  • April 4\n
      \n
    • King Philip III of Spain signs an edict to expel of all moriscos from his country (see September 11).
      • \n
    • English explorer Henry Hudson in the service of the Dutch East India Company sets out from Amsterdam in the Halve Maen.[1]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Invasion of Ryukyu in Japan: Soldiers of the Shimazu clan capture the castle on Ryukyu Island, beginning to make the Ryukyu Kingdom a vassal of Satsuma han.
    • \n
  • April 9 \xe2\x80\x93 By the Treaty of Antwerp the Netherlands and Spain agree the Twelve Years\' Truce (1609\xe2\x80\x931621) in the Eighty Years\' War, allowing the Dutch East India Company to trade within the Spanish Empire.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 London publisher Thomas Thorpe issues Shake-speares Sonnets, with a dedication to "Mr. W.H.", and the poem A Lover\'s Complaint appended; it is uncertain whether this publication has Shakespeare\'s authority.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Second Charter of Virginia is officially ratified; it is intended to replace the council with a Governor who has absolute control in the colony.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • c. Early July \xe2\x80\x93 Samuel de Champlain claims the Lake Champlain area of Vermont for the Kingdom of France.
    • \n
  • July 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Bohemia is granted freedom of religion (Letter of Majesty).
    • \n
  • July 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The German Catholic League is formed to counteract the Protestant Union.
    • \n
  • July 23 \xe2\x80\x93 A hurricane at sea separates the 9 London Company\'s ships (600 more settlers) en route to relieve the Jamestown settlement, one ship sinks, and the Sea Venture is driven ashore at Bermuda on July 25, thus effectively first settling the colony.
    • \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x93 At what is now Crown Point, New York, Samuel de Champlain participates in a battle between the Huron and Iroquois, shooting and killing two Iroquois chiefs; this helps set the tone for French\xe2\x80\x93Iroquois relations for the next 100 years.
    • \n
  • August 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Galileo Galilei demonstrates his first telescope to Venetian officials.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Hudson is the first European to see Delaware Bay.[1][2]
    • \n
  • August \xe2\x80\x93 Seven ships arrive at the colony of Jamestown, Virginia, with 200\xe2\x80\x93300 men, women, and children, reporting the Sea Venture wrecked near Bermuda.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Hudson enters New York Bay aboard the Halve Maen.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown: Capt. George Percy replaces Captain John Smith as president of the Council, and Smith returns to England.
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Valencia expels all the Moriscos (see April 4).
    • \n
  • September 11\xe2\x80\x9312 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Hudson in the Halve Maen sails into Upper New York Bay[3] and begins a journey up the Hudson River.[4]
    • \n
  • October 12 \xe2\x80\x93 A version of the rhyme "Three Blind Mice" is published in London.[5] The editor, and possible author of the verse, is the teenage Thomas Ravenscroft.[6]
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Dutch East India Company imports tea to Europe.
    • \n
  • The Dutch East India Company establishes a trading post in Hirado, Japan.
    • \n
  • Warsaw becomes the capital of Poland.
    • \n
  • The municipality of Buenavista in Marinduque, Philippines is founded.
    • \n
  • The Statutes of Iona are passed, marking the end of the bloody feuds between the clans in the Scottish Highlands.
    • \n
  • The Douay\xe2\x80\x93Rheims Bible Old Testament translation from the Vulgate into English vol. 1 is published in Reims.
    • \n
  • English-born Sister Mary Ward founds the Sisters of Loreto at Saint-Omer, at this time in the Spanish Netherlands.
    • \n
  • Johannes Kepler publishes his first two laws of planetary motion in Astronomia nova.
    • \n
  • Cornelis Drebbel invents the thermostat.
    • \n
