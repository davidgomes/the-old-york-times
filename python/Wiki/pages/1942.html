1942 (MCMXLII) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1942nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 942nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 42nd year of the 20th century, and the 3rd year of the 1940s decade.

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Declaration by United Nations signed by China, UK, USA, and USSR in which they agree "not to make any separate peace with the Axis powers".
      • \n
    • United States and Philippines troops fight the Battle of Bataan against Japanese forces.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 2 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Japanese warplanes bomb Manila, Philippines
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Operation Typhoon, the German attempt to take Moscow, ends in failure.
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The siege of the Bataan Peninsula begins.
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Dutch East Indies campaign: Japan declares war on the Netherlands and invades the Dutch East Indies.
      • \n
    • Malayan Campaign: The Japanese capture Kuala Lumpur, the capital of the Federated Malay States.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 13\n
      \n
    • Sikorsky R-4 first flies, in the United States; it will become the first mass-produced helicopter.
      • \n
    • Heinkel test pilot Helmut Schenk becomes the first person to escape from a stricken aircraft with an ejection seat.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 American film actress Carole Lombard and her mother are among all 22 aboard TWA Flight 3 killed when the Douglas DC-3 plane crashes into Potosi Mountain near Las Vegas while she is returning from a tour to promote the sale of war bonds.
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Japanese forces invade Burma.
      • \n
    • Establishment of United States VIII Bomber Command, later to become the Eighth Air Force, in Savannah, Georgia.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Holocaust: Nazis at the Wannsee Conference in Berlin decide that the "Final Solution (Endl\xc3\xb6sung) to the Jewish problem" is relocation, and later extermination.
    • \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Erwin Rommel launches his new offensive in Cyrenaica.
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Battle of Rabaul begins.
    • \n
  • January 25 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Thailand declares war on the United States and United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The first American forces arrive in Europe, landing in Northern Ireland.
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Malayan Campaign: The last organized Allied forces leave British Malaya, ending the 54-day campaign, and the Johor\xe2\x80\x93Singapore Causeway is severed.
    • \n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 C. S. Lewis\'s The Screwtape Letters first published in book format in England.
    • \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Command staff of the Eighth Air Force reaches England.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Rommel suspends his offensive in Cyrenaica.
    • \n
  • February 7 \xe2\x80\x93 United States Maritime Commission fleet operations transferred to the War Shipping Administration (lasting until September 1, 1946).
    • \n
  • February 8\n
      \n
    • Ant\xc3\xb3nio \xc3\x93scar Carmona is elected president of Portugal.
      • \n
    • WWII: Top United States military leaders hold their first formal meeting to discuss American military strategy in the war.
      • \n
    • Daylight saving time goes into effect in the United States.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The ocean liner SS Normandie catches fire while being converted into the troopship USS Lafayette (AP-53) for WWII at pier 88 in New York City: she capsizes early the following morning.
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Operation Cerberus: A flotilla of Kriegsmarine ships dash from Brest through the English Channel to northern ports; the British fail to sink any of them.
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Singapore surrenders to Japanese forces.
    • \n
  • February 18 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: More than 200 American sailors die in Newfoundland when the USS Truxtun runs aground near Chambers Cove and the USS Pollux runs aground at Lawn Point.
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • Japanese warplanes bomb Darwin, Australia.
      • \n
    • A returning Japanese fighter plane crashes on Melville Island (Australia) and its pilot, Hajime Toyoshima, becomes the first Japanese captured on Australian soil when indigenous resident Matthias Ulungura takes him prisoner.
      • \n
    • United States President Franklin D. Roosevelt signs Executive Order 9066 allowing the United States military to define areas as exclusionary zones. These zones affect the Japanese on the West Coast, and Germans and Italians primarily on the East Coast.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 19-23 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Battle of Sittang Bridge \xe2\x80\x93 British forces retreat to the Sittaung River.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Lieutenant Edward O\'Hare becomes America\'s first U.S. Navy WWII flying ace.
    • \n
  • February 22 \xe2\x80\x93 General George Marshall transmits a direct order to General MacArthur in President Roosevelt\'s name, ordering MacArthur himself to turn over command of the Philippines to a subordinate and report to Australia to assume command of the large American force being built up there. The orders are worded to allow MacArthur to choose the exact moment of his departure; for various reasons, he will not leave until March 12 (Eastern Date).
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Japanese submarine I-17 fires 17 high-explosive shells toward an oil refinery near Santa Barbara, California, causing little damage.
    • \n
  • February 24\n
      \n
    • Struma disaster: MV Struma, carrying Jewish refugees from Axis-allied Romania to British-administered Palestine, is torpedoed and sunk by the Soviet submarine Shch-213, killing about 791 men, women and children, with only one survivor.
      • \n
    • Propaganda: The Voice of America begins broadcasting.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Los Angeles: Over 1,400 AA shells are fired at an unidentified, slow-moving object in the skies over Los Angeles. The appearance of the object triggers an immediate wartime blackout over most of Southern California, with thousands of air raid wardens being deployed throughout the city. In total there are 6 deaths. Despite the several-hour barrage no planes are downed.
    • \n
  • February 26\n
      \n
    • The worst coal dust explosion to date, in Honkeiko, China, claims 1,549 lives.
      • \n
    • The 14th Academy Awards ceremony is held in Los Angeles; How Green Was My Valley wins Best Picture.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Battle of the Java Sea: An allied (ABDA) task force of 14 vessels under Dutch command, trying to stem a Japanese invasion of the Dutch East Indies, is defeated by a 19 vessel Japanese task force in the Java Sea; 2.300 sailors die, including the commander, admiral Karel Doorman; Japanese attain naval hegemony in East-Asia
    • \n
\n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Construction begins on the Badger Army Ammunition Plant, the largest in the United States during WWII.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Executive order 9082 (February 28, 1942) comes into effect reorganizing the United States Army into three major commands: Army Ground Forces, Army Air Forces, and Services of Supply, later redesignated Army Service Forces, with Henry H. Arnold as Commanding General of the United States Army Air Forces.
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: U.S. General Douglas MacArthur, his family, and key members of his staff are evacuated by PT boat, under cover of darkness, from Corregidor in the Philippines. Command of U.S. forces in the Philippines passes to Major General Jonathan M. Wainwright.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: New Zealand and Australia declare war on Thailand.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Holocaust: the Nazi German Be\xc5\x82\xc5\xbcec extermination camp opens in occupied Poland about 1 km south of the railroad station at Be\xc5\x82\xc5\xbcec in the Lublin district of the General Government. At least 434,508 people are killed here up to December 1942.
    • \n
  • March 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Franklin D. Roosevelt, President of the United States, signs Executive Order 9102, creating the War Relocation Authority (WRA), which becomes responsible for the internment of Americans of Japanese and, to a lesser extent, German and Italian descent, many of them legal citizens.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: After being forced to flee the Philippines, U.S. General Douglas MacArthur announces (in Terowie, South Australia), "I came through and I shall return."[1]
    • \n
  • March 23 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Germans burn down the Ukrainian village of Yelino (Koriukivka Raion), killing 296 civilians.[2]
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The evacuation of Polish nationals from the Soviet Union begins. It is conducted in two phases: until 5 April; and between 10 and 30 August 1942, by sea from Krasnovodsk to Pahlavi (Anzali), and (to a lesser extent) overland from Ashkabad to Mashhad. In all, 115,000 people are evacuated, 37,000 of them civilians, 18,000 children (7% of the number of Polish citizens originally exiled to the Soviet Union).[3]
    • \n
  • March 28 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: St Nazaire Raid (Operation Chariot) \xe2\x80\x93 British Commandos raid Saint-Nazaire on the coast of Western France to put its dockyard facilities out of action.
    • \n

April[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nSpring 1942: the Nazi German extermination camp Treblinka II opens in occupied Poland near the village of Treblinka
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 The Holocaust: the Nazi German extermination camp Sobib\xc3\xb3r opens in occupied Poland on the outskirts of the town of Sobib\xc3\xb3r. Between April 1942 and October 1943, at least 160,000 people are killed here.
    • \n
  • Spring \xe2\x80\x93 The Holocaust: the Nazi German extermination camp Treblinka II opens in occupied Poland near the village of Treblinka. Between July 23, 1942 and October 1943, around 850,000 people are killed here,[4] more than 800,000 of whom are Jews.[5]
    • \n
  • April 3 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Japanese forces begin an all-out assault on the United States and Filipino troops on the Bataan Peninsula.
    • \n
  • April 5 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Easter Sunday Raid \xe2\x80\x93 Aircraft of the Japanese Navy attack Colombo in Ceylon (Sri Lanka). Royal Navy cruisers HMS Cornwall and HMS Dorsetshire are sunk southwest of the island.
    • \n
  • April 9 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • The Bataan Peninsula falls and the Bataan Death March begins.
      • \n
    • The Japanese Navy launches an air raid on Trincomalee in Ceylon (Sri Lanka); the Royal Navy aircraft carrier HMS Hermes (95) and Royal Australian Navy destroyer HMAS Vampire are sunk off the country\'s East Coast.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Federal Communications Commission\'s minimum programming time required of TV stations is cut from 15 hours to 4 hours a week during the war.
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The submarine HMS Upholder is sunk.
    • \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The German submarine U-85 is sunk by USS Roper.
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Award of the George Cross to Malta: King George VI awards the George Cross to the island of Malta to mark the Siege of Malta, saying, "To honour her brave people I award the George Cross to the Island Fortress of Malta, to bear witness to a heroism and a devotion that will long be famous in history" (from January 1 to July 24, there is only one 24-hour period during which no bombs fall on this tiny island).
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Henri Giraud the French commander captured in 1940, escapes from K\xc3\xb6nigstein Fortress.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Tokyo, Japan, is attacked by the Doolittle Raid, a small force of B-25 Mitchell bomber aircraft commanded by then-Lieutenant Colonel James "Jimmy" Doolittle.
    • \n
  • April 23\n
      \n
    • WWII: Exeter becomes the first historic English city bombed as part of the Baedeker Blitz in retaliation for the British bombing of L\xc3\xbcbeck.
      • \n
    • Exeter-born William Temple enthroned as Archbishop of Canterbury.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Princess Elizabeth registers for war service in the U.K.
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Reichstag meets for the last time, dissolving itself and proclaiming Adolf Hitler the "Supreme Judge of the German People", granting him the power of life and death over every German citizen.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: A national plebiscite is held in Canada on the issue of conscription.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Jewish Star of David is required wearing for all Jews in the Netherlands and Belgium; Jews in other Nazi-controlled countries have already been wearing it.
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: An explosion at a chemical factory in Tessenderlo, Belgium leaves 200 dead and 1,000 injured.
    • \n
\n

May[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 Operation Pluto: The plan to construct oil pipelines under the English Channel between England and France is tested in the River Medway.
    • \n
  • May 5 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII \xe2\x80\x93 Operation Ironclad: British forces invade the French colony of Madagascar.
    • \n
  • May 7 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: On Corregidor, the last American and Filipino forces in the Philippines surrender to the Japanese under command of 2Lt. Robert L. Obourn (92nd Coastal Artillery Corps Battalion, G Battery) from Fort Mills.[6]
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Battle of the Kerch Peninsula: The German 11th Army begins Operation Trappenjagd (Bustard Hunt) and destroys the bridgehead of the three Soviet Armies (44th, 47th, and 51st) defending the Kerch Peninsula, in the eastern part of the Crimea.[7]
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Battle of the Coral Sea (first battle in naval history where 2 enemy fleets fight without seeing each other\'s fleets) ends in an Allied victory.
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Battle of the Kerch Peninsula: German and Romanian forces launches Unternehmen Trappenjagd (Operation Busted Hunt) aiming at defeating the Soviet Crimean Front defending the Kerch Peninsula. The battle ends in Axis victory. Part of the Eastern Front.
    • \n
  • May 8/May 9 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: At night, gunners of the Ceylon Garrison Artillery on Horsburgh Island in the Cocos Islands mutiny. The mutiny is crushed and three executed (the only British Commonwealth soldiers to be executed for mutiny during the Second World War).
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII \xe2\x80\x93 Second Battle of Kharkov: In the eastern Ukraine, the Soviet Army initiates a major offensive. During the battle the Soviets capture the city of Kharkov from the German Army, only to be encircled and destroyed.
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Japanese minelayer Okinoshima is sunk by the American submarine USS S-42.
    • \n
  • May 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Aaron Copland\'s Lincoln Portrait is performed for the first time by the Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: In the United States, a bill creating the Women\'s Auxiliary Army Corps (WAAC) is signed into law.
    • \n
