\n1779 (MDCCLXXIX) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1779th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 779th year of the 2nd millennium, the 79th year of the 18th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1770s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1779 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 English troops surrender to the Marathas in Wadgaon, India, and are forced to return all territories acquired since 1773.
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Ching-Thang Khomba is crowned King of Manipur.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Claudius Smith is hanged at Goshen, Orange County, New York for supposed acts of terrorism upon the people of the surrounding communities.
    • \n
  • January 29 \xe2\x80\x93 After a second petition for partition from its residents, the North Carolina General Assembly abolishes Bute County, North Carolina (established 1764) by dividing it and naming the northern portion Warren County (for Revolutionary War hero Joseph Warren) and the southern portion Franklin County (for Benjamin Franklin). The General Assembly also establishes Warrenton (also named for Joseph Warren) to be the county seat of Warren County and Louisburg (named for Louis XVI of France) to be the county seat of Franklin County.
    • \n

\nBenedict Arnold
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Captain James Cook dies on the Sandwich Islands on his third and last voyage.
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Aynal\xc4\xb1kavak between Ottoman Turkey and Russian Empire about Crimean Khanate.
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Bavarian Succession: Russian and French mediators at the Congress of Teschen negotiate an end to the war. In the agreement Austria receives a part of the Bavarian territory (the Innviertel) and relinquishes the rest.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Benedict Arnold is court-martialed for malfeasance in his treatment of government property.
    • \n
  • June 16 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: In support of the U.S., Spain declares war on England.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: United States forces led by General Anthony Wayne capture Stony Point, New York from British troops.
    • \n
  • July 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Tekle Giyorgis I begins the first of his five reigns as Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • July 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Minisink: The Goshen Militia is destroyed by Joseph Brant\'s forces.
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: American forces led by Commodore Dudley Saltonstall launch the Penobscot Expedition in what is now Castine, Maine, resulting in the worst naval defeat in U.S. history until Pearl Harbor.
    • \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Siege of Gibraltar (fourteenth and last military siege) starts. This is an action by French and Spanish forces to wrest control of Gibraltar from the established British garrison. The garrison, led by George Augustus Eliott, later 1st Baron Heathfield of Gibraltar, survives all attacks and a blockade of supplies.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nThe Defeat of the Floating Batteries at Gibraltar, September 1782, by John Singleton Copley
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Baton Rouge: Spanish troops under Bernardo de G\xc3\xa1lvez capture the city from the British.
    • \n
  • September 14 - September 15 - Little Beard\'s Town is burnt by the Sullivan Expedition
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolution \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Flamborough Head: The American ship Bonhomme Richard, commanded by John Paul Jones, engages the British ship HMS Serapis. The Bonhomme Richard sinks, but the Americans board the Serapis and other vessels, and are victorious.
    • \n
  • October 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Fort Wilson Riot against James Wilson and others in Philadelphia takes place.
    • \n
  • November 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The North Carolina General Assembly carves a new county from Dobbs County, North Carolina and names it Wayne County in honor of United States General Anthony Wayne.
    • \n
  • December 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Alexandre, Vicomte de Beauharnais marries Jos\xc3\xa9phine Tascher.
    • \n
  • December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Fort Nashborough, later to become Nashville, Tennessee, is founded by James Robertson.
    • \n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Industrial Revolution in England:\n
      \n
    • The Iron Bridge is erected across the River Severn in Shropshire, the world\'s first bridge built entirely of cast iron.[1] It will open to traffic on January 1, 1781.[2]
      • \n
    • The spinning mule is perfected by the Lancashire inventor Samuel Crompton.[2]
      • \n
    • Boulton and Watt\'s Smethwick Engine, now the oldest working engine in the world, is brought into service (May).
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • The city of Tampere, Finland, is founded.
    • \n
  • Joint Spanish-Portuguese survey of Amazonia begins to determine the boundary between the colonial possessions in South America. Continues until 1795.
    • \n
