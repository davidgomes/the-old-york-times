\n1635 (MDCXXXV) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1635th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 635th year of the 2nd millennium, the 35th year of the 17th century, and the 6th year of the 1630s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1635 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Acad\xc3\xa9mie fran\xc3\xa7aise in Paris is formally constituted as the national academy for the preservation of the French language.[1]
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Maronite warlord Fakhr-al-Din II is executed in Constantinople.
    • \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 France declares war on Spain.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 The Peace of Prague is signed, which ends the German civil war aspect of the conflict.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 31 \xe2\x80\x93 The Royal Mail service is made available to the public by Charles I of England.
    • \n
  • August 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Colonial Hurricane strikes Narragansett Bay as a possible Category 3 hurricane, killing over 46 people.
    • \n
  • September 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Sztumska Wie\xc5\x9b is signed between Sweden and the Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth.
    • \n
  • October 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Rhode Island founder Roger Williams is banished from Massachusetts Bay Colony as a religious dissident, after speaking out against punishments for religious offenses and giving away Native American land.
    • \n
  • November 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Parr, dead at the alleged age of 152, is buried in Westminster Abbey.
    • \n
  • November 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Dutch pacification campaign on Formosa against Taiwanese aborigines begins.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Guadeloupe and Martinique are colonized by France.
    • \n
  • Dominica is claimed by France.
    • \n
  • The Ottomans are expelled from Yemen.
    • \n
  • In the Mughal Empire, Shah Jahan\'s Pearl Mosque at Lahore Fort is completed.
    • \n
  • Nagyszombat University (predecessor of Budapest University) is established.
    • \n
  • Boston Latin School, the oldest school in the United States of America, is founded in Boston, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • Japan forbids merchants to travel abroad under penalty of death.
    • \n
  • A Japanese imperial memorandum decrees: "Hereafter entry by the Portuguese galeota is forbidden. If they insist on coming, the ships must be destroyed and anyone aboard those ships must be beheaded."
    • \n
  • Willem and Joan Blaeu publish the first edition of their Atlas Novus in Amsterdam.
    • \n
