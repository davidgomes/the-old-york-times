\n1777 (MDCCLXXVII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1777th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 777th year of the 2nd millennium, the 77th year of the 18th century, and the 8th year of the 1770s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1777 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 2 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of the Assunpink Creek: American general George Washington\'s army defeats the British under Lieutenant General Charles Cornwallis in a second battle at Trenton, New Jersey.
    • \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Princeton: American general George Washington\'s army again defeats the British.
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Mission Santa Clara de As\xc3\xads is founded in what is now Santa Clara, California.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Vermont declares its independence from New York, becoming the Vermont Republic, an independent country, a status it retains until it joins the United States as the 14th state in 1791.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Third voyage of James Cook: English explorer Captain Cook discovers Mangaia and Atiu in the Cook Islands.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 King Joseph I of Portugal dies & is succeeded by his brother & son in law Peter III of Portugal
    • \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Friedrich Maximilian Klinger\'s play Sturm und Drang is premiered by the Seyler Theatre Company in Leipzig, giving its name to the whole Sturm und Drang movement in German literature.
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Battle of Bound Brook: A British and Hessian force led by Charles Cornwallis surprises a Continental Army outpost in New Jersey commanded by Major General Benjamin Lincoln.
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 First performance of Richard Brinsley Sheridan\'s comedy of manners The School for Scandal at the Theatre Royal, Drury Lane in London.[1]
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Lachlan McIntosh and Button Gwinnett shoot each other during a duel near Savannah, Georgia. Gwinnett, a signer of the United States Declaration of Independence, dies 3 days later.
    • \n
  • June 13 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolution: The Marquis de Lafayette lands near Georgetown, South Carolina, to help the Continental Congress train its army.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJune 14: US Flag (had various star patterns)
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • June 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Stars and Stripes is adopted by the Continental Congress as the flag of the United States.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Constitution of Vermont (1777) is signed, officially abolishing slavery.
    • \n
  • July 6 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Fort Ticonderoga: After a bombardment by British artillery under General John Burgoyne, American forces retreat from Fort Ticonderoga, New York.
    • \n
  • August 6 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Oriskany: Loyalists gain a tactical victory over Patriots; Iroquois fight on both sides.
    • \n
  • August 16 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Bennington: British and Brunswicker forces are decisively defeated by American troops.
    • \n
  • September 3 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Cooch\'s Bridge: British and Hessian forces defeat American militia in a minor skirmish in New Castle County, Delaware.
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Brandywine: The British gain a major victory in Chester County, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • September 19 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 First Battle of Saratoga: Battle of Freeman\'s Farm: Patriot forces withstand a British attack at Saratoga, New York
    • \n
  • September 26 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 British troops occupy Philadelphia; members of the Continental Congress flee to Lancaster, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • October 4 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Germantown: Troops under George Washington are repelled by British troops under Sir William Howe.
    • \n
  • October 6 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Forts Clinton and Montgomery: British troops capture Fort Clinton and Fort Montgomery (Hudson River) and are able to dismantle the Hudson River Chain.
    • \n
  • October 7 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Second Battle of Saratoga: Battle of Bemis Heights: British General John Burgoyne is defeated by American troops.
    • \n
  • October 17 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Saratoga: British General John Burgoyne surrenders to the American troops.
    • \n
  • November 15 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolution: After 16 months of debate, the Continental Congress approves the Articles of Confederation in the temporary American capital at York, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • November 17 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolution: The Articles of Confederation are submitted to the states for ratification.
    • \n
  • November 29 \xe2\x80\x93 San Jose, California is founded. It is the first pueblo in Spanish Alta California.
    • \n
  • December 19 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: George Washington\'s Continental Army goes into winter quarters at Valley Forge, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • December 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Third voyage of James Cook: English explorer Captain Cook discovers Kiritimati (Christmas Island).
    • \n
  • December 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Maximilian III Joseph, Elector of Bavaria dies & is succeeded by his distant cousin Charles Theodore, Elector of Bavaria.
    • \n
