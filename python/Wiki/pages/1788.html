\n1788 (MDCCLXXXVIII) was a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Julian calendar, the 1788th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 788th year of the 2nd millennium, the 88th year of the 18th century, and the 9th year of the 1780s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1788 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The first edition of The Times, previously The Daily Universal Register, is published in London.
    • \n
  • January 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Georgia ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the fourth U.S. state under the new government.
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Connecticut ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the fifth U.S. state.
    • \n
  • January 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The leading ship (armed tender HMS Supply) in Captain Arthur Phillip\'s First Fleet arrives at Botany Bay to colonise Australia.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 the Continental Congress, effectively a caretaker government, elects Cyrus Griffin as its last president.
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The La Perouse expedition in the Astrolabe and Boussole arrives off Botany Bay just as Captain Arthur Phillip is attempting to move his colony from there to Sydney Cove in Port Jackson.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Australia Day: Eleven ships of the First Fleet from Botany Bay, led by Captain Arthur Phillip, land at Sydney Cove (which will become Sydney), Australia, where he determines to establish the British prison colony of New South Wales, the first permanent European settlement on the continent.
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Benedict Stuart becomes the new Stuart claimant to the throne of Great Britain as King Henry IX and the figurehead of Jacobitism.
    • \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Isaac Briggs and William Longstreet patent a steamboat.
    • \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Massachusetts ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the sixth U.S. state.
    • \n
  • February 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Sydney was named and founded by the British Colony of New South Wales.
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Austria enters the Russo-Turkish War (1787\xe2\x80\x9392) and attacks Moldavia.
    • \n
  • February 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The uninhabited Lord Howe Island is discovered by the brig HMS Supply, commanded by Lieutenant Ball, who is on his way from Botany Bay to Norfolk Island with convicts to start a penal settlement there. They arrive at Norfolk Island on March 6.
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The La Perouse expedition leaves Sydney Cove for New Caledonia, never to be seen again.
    • \n
  • March 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Edinburgh Evening Courant carries a notice of \xc2\xa3200 reward for the capture of William Brodie, a town councilor doubling as a burglar.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nMarch 21: Fire in New Orleans requires rebuilding Jackson Square area.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • March 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Great New Orleans Fire kills 25% of the population and destroys 856 buildings, including St. Louis Cathedral and The Cabildo, leaving most of the town in ruins.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 American pioneers establish the town of Marietta (in modern-day Ohio), the first permanent American settlement outside the original Thirteen Colonies.
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 America\'s first recorded riot, the \'Doctors\' Mob\', begins. Residents of Manhattan are angry about grave robbers stealing bodies for doctors to dissect. The rioting is suppressed on the 15th.
    • \n
  • April 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Maryland ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the seventh U.S. state.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Royal Dramatic Theatre (Kungliga Dramatiska Teatern), Sweden\'s national drama company, is founded.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The Australian frontier wars begin.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 South Carolina ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the eighth U.S. state.
    • \n
  • June 7 \xe2\x80\x93 France: Day of the Tiles, which some consider the beginning of the French Revolution.
    • \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The African Association, an exploration group dedicated to plotting the Niger River and finding Timbuktu, is founded in England.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 English captains Thomas Gilbert and John Marshall, returning from Botany Bay, become the first Europeans to encounter the Gilbert Islands in the Pacific Ocean.[1] They also chart islands in "Lord Mulgrove\'s range", later known as the Marshall Islands.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 New Hampshire ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the ninth U.S. state, enabling the Constitution to go into effect. (The latter happens on March 4, 1789, when the first Congress elected under the new Constitution assembles.)
    • \n
  • June 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Virginia Ratifying Convention ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the tenth U.S. state under the new government.
    • \n
  • June 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, in Vienna, completes his antepenultimate symphony, now called the Symphony No. 39 in E-flat.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 13 \xe2\x80\x93 A hailstorm sweeps across France and the Dutch Republic with hailstones \'as big as quart bottles\' that take \'three days to melt\'; immense damage is done.[2]
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Governor General Lord Dorchester, by proclamation issued from the Chateau St. Louis in Quebec City, divides the British Province of Quebec into five Districts, namely: Gasp\xc3\xa9, Nassau, Lunenburg, Mecklenburg, and Hesse.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 New York ratifies the United States Constitution and becomes the eleventh U.S. state.
    • \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, in Vienna, completes his penultimate symphony, now called the Symphony No. 40 in G Minor.
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 King Louis XVI of France agrees to convene the Estates-General meeting in May 1789, the first time since 1614.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, in Vienna, completes his final symphony, now called the Symphony No. 41 in C Major, and nicknamed (after his death) The Jupiter.
    • \n
  • August 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The trial of Deacon William Brodie for burglary begins in Edinburgh, Scotland; he is sentenced to death by hanging.
    • \n
  • September 24 \xe2\x80\x93 \'Theater War\' begins when the army of Denmark\xe2\x80\x93Norway invades Sweden.
    • \n
  • October 1 \xe2\x80\x93 William Brodie is hanged at the Tolbooth in Edinburgh.
    • \n
  • Late October \xe2\x80\x93 King George III of the United Kingdom becomes deranged; the Regency Crisis of 1788 starts.
    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Russo-Turkish War (1787\xe2\x80\x9392): The Ottoman fortress of \xc3\x96zi falls to the Russians after a prolonged siege and a murderous storm with a temperature of -23 degrees C.
    • \n
  • December 14 \xe2\x80\x93 King Charles III of Spain dies and is succeeded by his son Charles IV.
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Burns writes his version of the Scots poem Auld Lang Syne.[3]
    • \n
