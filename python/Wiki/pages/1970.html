1970 (MCMLXX) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1970th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 970th year of the 2nd millennium, the 70th year of the 20th century, and the 1st year of the 1970s decade.

May[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Demonstrations against the trial of the New Haven Nine, Bobby Seale, and Ericka Huggins draw 12,000.   • May 6\n
      \n

      • \n

  • May 11\n
      \n
    • Henry Marrow is killed in an alleged hate crime in Oxford, North Carolina.

    • Lubbock tornado: An F5 tornado hits downtown Lubbock, Texas, the first to hit a downtown district of a major city since Topeka, Kansas in 1966; 28 are killed.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The 1976 Winter Olympics are awarded to Denver, Colorado but it is later rejected in 1972.
    • \n
  • May 14\n
      \n
    • Ulrike Meinhof helps Andreas Baader escape and create the Red Army Faction which exists until 1998.

    • In the second day of violent demonstrations at Jackson State University in Jackson, Mississippi, state law enforcement officers fire into the demonstrators, killing 2 and injuring 12.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Thor Heyerdahl sets sail from Morocco on the papyrus boat Ra II, to sail the Atlantic Ocean.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 A fire occurs in the Britannia Bridge over the Menai Strait near Bangor, Caernarfonshire, Wales, contributing to its partial destruction and amounting to approximately \xc2\xa31,000,000 worth of fire damage.

  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Soviet Tupolev Tu-144 becomes the first commercial transport to exceed Mach 2.
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 A British expedition climbs the south face of Annapurna I.
    • \n
  • May 31\n
      \n
    • The 7.9 Mw Ancash earthquake shakes Peru with a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe) and a landslide buries the town of Yungay, Peru. Between 66,794\xe2\x80\x9370,000 were killed and 50,000 were injured.
      • \n
    • The 1970 FIFA World Cup is inaugurated in Mexico.
