Year 1529 (MDXXIX) was a common year starting on Friday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 7\xe2\x80\x93March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Shimbra Kure: Imam Ahmad ibn Ibrahim al-Ghazi, with 200 men armed with matchlocks, defeats the army of Lebna Dengel, Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • April 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Flensburg Disputation, a debate, attended by Stadtholder Christian of Schleswig-Holstein (later King Christian III of Denmark) between Lutherans (led by Hermann Fast) and the more radical Anabaptists (led by Melchior Hoffman). Johannes Bugenhagen, a close associate of Martin Luther, presides. The Disputation marks the rejection of radical ideas by the Danish Reformation.[1]
    • \n
  • April 19 \xe2\x80\x93 At the Diet of Speyer, a group of rulers (German: F\xc3\xbcrst) and independent cities (German: Reichsstadt) protest the reinstatement of the Edict of Worms, beginning the Protestant movement.
    • \n
  • April 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Zaragoza divides the eastern hemisphere between Spanish and Portuguese empires, stipulating that the dividing line should lie 297.5 leagues or 17\xc2\xb0 east of the Moluccas.
    • \n
  • May\xe2\x80\x93July \xe2\x80\x93 Cardinal Thomas Wolsey, Archbishop of York, presides over a legatine court at Blackfriars, London, to rule on the legality of King Henry VIII of England\'s marriage to Catherine of Aragon.[2]
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Turkish army under Suleiman I leaves Constantinople to invade Hungary once again.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the League of Cognac: Battle of Landriano: French forces in northern Italy are decisively defeated by Spain.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The only Continental outbreak of English sweating sickness reaches L\xc3\xbcbeck, spreading from there into Schleswig-Holstein in the next few months.[3]
    • \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor, and Francis I of France sign the Treaty of Cambrai, or "Ladies\' Peace" in the War of the League of Cognac: Francis abandons his claims in Italy, but is allowed to retain the Duchy of Burgundy. Henry VIII of England accedes on August 27.[4]
    • \n
  • September 8\n
      \n
    • Buda is recaptured by the invading forces of the Ottoman Empire.
      • \n
    • The city of Maracaibo, Venezuela is founded by Ambrosius Ehinger.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Vienna: Vienna is besieged by the Ottoman forces of Suleiman the Magnificent.
    • \n
  • October 15 \xe2\x80\x93 With the season growing late, Suleiman abandons the Siege of Vienna, a turning point in the Ottoman wars in Europe.
    • \n
  • October 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Cardinal Wolsey falls from power in England due to his failure to prevent Habsburg expansion in Europe and obtain an annulment of Henry VIII\'s marriage. Thomas More succeeds him as Lord Chancellor.[4]
    • \n
  • November 4\xe2\x80\x93December 17 \xe2\x80\x93 First sitting of the English Reformation Parliament.[4]
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Aylesbury is granted the county town of Buckinghamshire in England by King Henry VIII.
    • \n
  • Stephen B\xc3\xa1thory becomes governor of Transylvania.
    • \n
  • Boromrajathira IV succeeds Rama Thibodi II as king of Ayutthaya.
    • \n
  • Fluorite is first described by Georg Agricola.
    • \n
  • Giorgio Vasari visits Rome.
    • \n
  • Pietro Bembo becomes historiographer of Venice.
    • \n
  • Heinrich Bullinger becomes pastor of Bremgarten, Switzerland.
    • \n
  • Paracelsus visits Nuremberg.
    • \n
  • Paracelsus uses the name Paracelsus for the first time.
    • \n
  • Occultist Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa publishes Declamatio de nobilitate et praecellentia foeminei sexus ("Declamation on the Nobility and Preeminence of the Female Sex"), a book pronouncing the theological and moral superiority of women.
    • \n
  • 25 March \xe2\x80\x94 Blood libel against the Jewish community of Bosen (formerly in Hungary, today in Slovakia), on the first day of Passover. Three Jews are accused and killed, while the boy is discovered alive, kidnapped for the benefit of the scheme.
    • \n
