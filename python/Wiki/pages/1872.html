1872 (MDCCCLXXII) was a leap year starting on Monday (dominical letter GF) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Julian calendar, the 1872nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 872nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 72nd year of the 19th century, and the 3rd year of the 1870s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1872 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Yohannes IV is crowned Emperor of Ethiopia in Axum, the first ruler crowned in that city in over 500 years.
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The government of the United Kingdom buys a number of forts on the Gold Coast from the Netherlands.[1]
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Rex, the most famous parade on Mardi Gras, parades for the first time in New Orleans for Grand Duke Alexei Mikhailovich of Russia.
    • \n
  • February 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Execution of the Filipino priests Jos\xc3\xa9 Burgos, Mariano G\xc3\xb3mez and Jacinto Zamora, collectively known as Gomburza, in Bagumbayan Fields, Manila, Philippines by the authorities of New Spain on charges of subversion arising from the 1872 Cavite mutiny.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Metropolitan Museum of Art opens in New York City.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 In the United States, Yellowstone National Park (once dubbed "Colter\'s Hell" after John Colter, of the Lewis and Clark Expedition) is established as the world\'s first national park.
    • \n
  • March 5\n
      \n
    • George Westinghouse receives a United States patent for the "failsafe" automatic railway air brake.
      • \n
    • The Tichborne case is decided in London against the claimant Arthur Orton (who, as a result, is convicted of perjury in 1874).
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Work begins erecting the Seven Sisters Colliery in South Wales, located on one of the richest coal sources in Britain.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 1872 FA Cup Final: In the first ever final of the FA Cup, the world\'s oldest football competition, Wanderers F.C. defeat Royal Engineers A.F.C. 1\xe2\x80\x930 at The Oval in Kennington, London.[2]
    • \n
  • March 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The 7.4\xe2\x80\x937.9 Mw Lone Pine earthquake shakes eastern California with a maximum Mercalli intensity of X (Extreme). Twenty-seven people were killed and fifty-six were injured.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Third Carlist War begins in northern Spain. Don Carlos, Duke of Madrid, the Carlist pretender (Carlos VII) appoints General Rada commander-in-chief in Spain and calls for a general rising.
    • \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 The magazine Popular Science is first published in the United States.
    • \n
  • May 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Third Carlist War in Spain: The Carlist Army is defeated at the Battle of Oroquieta in Navarre. 1,000 government troops (Moriones) easily defeat the much larger number of Carlists at Oroquieta. 50 Carlists are killed and Moriones take 700 prisoners but Don Carlos escapes.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Victoria Woodhull becomes the first woman nominated for President of the United States, although she is a year too young to qualify and does not appear on the ballot.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The New Zealand Wars end after 17 years with the conclusion of Te Kooti\'s War; Maori spiritual leader Te Kooti Arikirangi Te Turuk crosses the Waikato River and enters the territory of the M\xc4\x81ori King, T\xc4\x81whiao, where he is granted asylum.
    • \n
  • May 22\n
      \n
    • Reconstruction: U.S. President Ulysses S. Grant signs the Amnesty Act of 1872 into law, restoring full civil rights to all but about 500 Confederate sympathizers.
      • \n
    • Georges Bizet\'s comic opera Djamileh is premi\xc3\xa8red at the Op\xc3\xa9ra-Comique in Paris, France.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 Rangers F.C. play their first ever game on the public pitches of Glasgow Green in Scotland.
    • \n
  • June 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Trade unions are legalised in Canada.[3]
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Society of Jesus is pronounced illegal in the German Empire.
    • \n
  • August 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Australian Overland Telegraph Line is completed, providing a telegraphic link between Australia and the rest of the world for the first time.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Thomas Hardy anonymously publishes his novel Under the Greenwood Tree.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 A group of Icaiche Maya under Marcos Canul attack Orange Walk Town in British Honduras; the British send troops against them.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Upon the death of Charles XV of Sweden and Norway, he is succeeded as King of both countries by his brother Oscar II.
    • \n
  • September 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Shriners Temple (called Mecca) is established in New York City.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 1\n
      \n
    • The Virginia Agricultural and Mechanical College begins its first academic session (the university is later renamed Virginia Tech).
      • \n
    • First case reports in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, of the Great Epizootic of 1872 (equine influenza, or the "horse flu") which will substantially disrupt life in North America by mid-December.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • November 5\n
      \n
    • U.S. presidential election, 1872: Ulysses S. Grant defeats Horace Greeley.
      • \n
    • Women\'s suffrage: In defiance of the law, American suffragist Susan B. Anthony votes for the first time (on November 18 she is served an arrest warrant and in the subsequent trial is fined $100 which she never pays).
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Mary Celeste sets sail from New York; bound for Genoa, Italy.
    • \n
  • November 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Great Boston Fire of 1872: In Boston, Massachusetts, a large fire begins to burn on Lincoln Street (the 2-day disaster destroys about 65 acres (0.26 km2) of the city, 776 buildings, much of the financial district and causes US$60 million in damage).
    • \n
  • November 11 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. government geologist Clarence King reveals the diamond hoax in Wyoming.
    • \n
  • November 13 (07:35) (probable date) \xe2\x80\x93 Claude Monet begins painting Impression, Sunrise (Impression, soleil levant, the painting that will give a name to Impressionism) as viewed from his hotel room at Le Havre in France.[4]
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The London Metropolitan Police go on strike.
    • \n
  • November 27 \xe2\x80\x93 A meteor shower display of Andromedids is seen over France.
    • \n
  • November 29\n
      \n
    • American Indian Wars: The Modoc War begins with the Battle of Lost River.
      • \n
    • Horace Greeley, President Ulysses S. Grant\'s opponent in the this year\'s U.S. presidential election, dies. His electoral votes are divided among several candidates.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • November 30 \xe2\x80\x93 First international Association football match to be recognised (retrospectively) by FIFA as "official" takes place at Hamilton Crescent, Scotland; the result is Scotland 0-0 England.[5] Earlier international football matches had already taken place in 1870, in 1871 and again in 1872 at the Oval, London.
    • \n
  • December 3 \xe2\x80\x93 George Smith presents the first translation of the Epic of Gilgamesh to a meeting of the Society of Biblical Archaeology in London.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The now-crewless American ship Mary Celeste is found (still seaworthy) by the British brig Dei Gratia in the Atlantic.
    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Springwell Pit disaster at Dawley in England: eight coal miners fall to their death when a winding chain snaps.
    • \n
  • December 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Challenger expedition: HMS Challenger (1858) sails from Portsmouth in England on the 4-year scientific expedition that lays the foundation for the science of oceanography.
    • \n
