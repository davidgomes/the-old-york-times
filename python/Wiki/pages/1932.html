1932 (MCMXXXII) was a leap year starting on Friday (dominical letter CB) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1932nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 932nd year of the 2nd millennium, the 32nd year of the 20th century, and the 3rd year of the 1930s decade.

January[edit]

\n
Main article: January 1932
\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Post Office Department issues a set of 12 stamps commemorating the 200th anniversary of George Washington\'s birth.
    • \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The British arrest and intern Mohandas Gandhi and Vallabhbhai Patel.
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Stimson Doctrine is proclaimed, in response to the Japanese invasion of Manchuria.
    • \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 In Great Britain the Archbishop of Canterbury forbids Anglican church remarriage of divorced persons.
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Sakuradamon Incident, Korean nationalist Lee Bong-chang fails in his effort to assassinate Hirohito Emperor of Japan. The Kuomintang\'s official newspaper runs an editorial expressing regret that the attempt failed, which is used by the Japanese as a pretext to attack Shanghai later in the month.
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Hattie W. Caraway becomes the first woman elected to the United States Senate.
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Maurice Ravel\'s Concerto in G debuts with piano soloist Marguerite Long and Ravel conducting the Lamoureux Orchestra.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 About 6 million are unemployed in Germany.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The 1932 Salvadoran peasant uprising begins, it is suppressed by the government of Maximiliano Hern\xc3\xa1ndez Mart\xc3\xadnez
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Marshal Pietro Badoglio declares the end of Libyan resistance.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The British submarine M2 sinks with all 60 hands.
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Conflict between Japan and China in the Battle of Shanghai.
    • \n
  • January 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The minority government of Karl Buresch in Austria ends the governmental crisis.
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Japanese warships arrive in Nanking.

The principal issue at the conference is the demand made by Germany for gleichberechtigung ("equality of status" i.e. abolishing Part V of the Treaty of Versailles, which had disarmed Germany) and the French demand for s\xc3\xa9curit\xc3\xa9 ("security" i.e. maintaining Part V).

    • \n
  • February 4\n
      \n

      • \n
    • Japan occupies Harbin, China.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Junnosuke Inoue, prominent Japanese businessman, banker and former governor of the Bank of Japan is assassinated by right-wing extremist group the League of Blood in the League of Blood Incident.

May[edit]

\n
Main article: May 1932
\n
    \n
  • May 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Comedian Jack Benny\'s radio show airs for the first time.
    • \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Paul Gorguloff shoots French president Paul Doumer in Paris; Doumer dies the next day.
    • \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The politically powerful General Kurt von Schleicher meets secretly with Adolf Hitler.[2] General Schleicher tells Hitler that he is scheming to bring down the Br\xc3\xbcning government and asks for Nazi support of the new "presidential government" Schleicher is planning to form.[2] Schleicher and Hitler negotiated a "gentlemen\'s agreement" where in exchange for lifting the ban on the SA and SS and having the Reichstag dissolved for early elections that summer, the Nazis will support Schleicher\'s new chancellor.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93\n
      \n
    • Albert Lebrun becomes the new president of France.
      • \n
    • Violent scenes in the Reichstag as Hermann G\xc3\xb6ring and other Nazi MRDs attack the Defense Minister General Wilhelm Groener for his lack of belief in a supposed Social Democratic putsch.[2] After the debate, General Schleicher tells Groener that he has lost the confidence of the Army and must resign at once.[2]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93\n
      \n
    • Ten weeks after his abduction, the infant son of Charles Lindbergh is found dead just a few miles from the Lindbergh home.
      • \n
    • General Wilhelm Groener resigns as Defense Minister.[2] Schleicher takes control of the Defense Ministry.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Premier of New South Wales, Jack Lang, is dismissed by the State Governor, Sir Philip Game.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Japanese troops leave Shanghai. Back in Japan, the May 15 Incident as an attempted military coup is known occurs. The Japanese prime minister Tsuyoshi Inukai is assassinated by naval officers.
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Massive riots between Hindus and Muslims in Bombay leave thousands dead and injured.
    • \n
  • May 20\xe2\x80\x93May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Amelia Earhart flies from the United States to County Londonderry, Northern Ireland in 14 hours 54 minutes.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Federaci\xc3\xb3n Obrera de la Industria de la Carne initiates a major strike in the Argentinian meat-packing industry.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Judgement in Donoghue v Stevenson handed down in the House of Lords, creating the neighbour principle in English law.
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The first of approximately 15,000 World War I veterans arrive in Washington, D.C. demanding the immediate payment of their military bonus, becoming known as the Bonus Army.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 German chancellor Heinrich Br\xc3\xbcning is dismissed by President von Hindenburg. President Hindenburg asks Franz von Papen to form a new government, known as the "Government of the President\'s Friends", which is openly dedicated to the destruction of democracy and the Weimar Republic. The downfall of Br\xc3\xbcning is largely the work of Schleicher, who been scheming against him since the beginning of May.[1] Schleicher takes the position of Defense Minister in his friend Papen\'s government.
    • \n

    • \n
  • July 12\n
      \n

    • \n

The Papen government sends out the Reichswehr under General Gerd von Rundstedt to depose the elected SPD government in Prussia under Otto Braun.[4] The coup gives Papen control of Prussia, the most powerful Land in Germany, and is a major blow to German democracy.[5]

    • \n
