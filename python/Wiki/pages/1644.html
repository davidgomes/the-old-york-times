1644 (MDCXLIV) was a leap year starting on Friday (dominical letter CB) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Monday (dominical letter GF) of the Julian calendar, the 1644th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 644th year of the 2nd millennium, the 44th year of the 17th century, and the 5th year of the 1640s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1644 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Royalist Oxford Parliament is first assembled by King Charles I of England.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 First English Civil War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Nantwich: The Parliamentarians defeat the Royalists, allowing them to end the 6-week Siege of Nantwich in Cheshire, England.
    • \n
  • February\xe2\x80\x93August \xe2\x80\x93 Explorer Abel Tasman\'s second expedition for the Dutch East India Company maps the north coast of Australia.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ochmat\xc3\xb3w: Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth forces under hetman Stanis\xc5\x82aw Koniecpolski secure a substantial victory over the horde of Crimean Tatars under Tugay Bey.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 In England, Roger Williams is granted an official charter for his Rhode Island Colony, allowing the establishment of a general assembly.
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 A popular Chinese rebellion led by Li Zicheng sacks Beijing, prompting Chongzhen, the last emperor of the Ming dynasty, to commit suicide.
    • \n
  • May 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Ming general Wu Sangui forms an alliance with the invading Manchus and opens the gates of the Great Wall of China at Shanhaiguan pass, letting the Manchus through towards the capital Beijing.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Montijo: The Kingdom of Portugal is victorious over Habsburg Spain in the first major action between the two nations during the Portuguese Restoration War.
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Shanhai Pass: The Manchu Qing dynasty and Wu Sangui gain a decisive victory over Li Zicheng\'s Shun dynasty.
    • \n
  • June 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Li Zicheng claims himself as the emperor of China.
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The invading Qing army, with the help of Ming general Wu Sangui, captures Beijing, China. This marks the beginning of Manchu rule over China proper.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 2 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Marston Moor: The Parliamentarians crush the Royalists, ending Charles I\'s hold on the north of England.
    • \n
  • September 1 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Tippermuir: Montrose defeats Lord Elcho\'s Covenanters, reviving the Royalist cause in Scotland.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 English Civil War \xe2\x80\x93 Second Battle of Lostwithiel: Charles I and the Royalists gain their last major victory.
    • \n
  • September 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Innocent X succeeds Pope Urban VIII as the 236th pope.
    • \n
  • October 1 \xe2\x80\x93 the Jews of Mogilev, Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth, were attacked during Tashlikh.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 The Castle of Elvas in Portugal resists a 9-day siege by the Spanish during the Portuguese Restoration War.
    • \n
  • November 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Shunzhi Emperor, the third emperor of the Qing dynasty, is enthroned in Beijing after the collapse of the Ming dynasty as the first Qing emperor to rule over China.
    • \n
  • November 23\n
      \n
    • Battle of J\xc3\xbcterbog: Sweden\'s forces defeat those of the Holy Roman Empire.
      • \n
    • Areopagitica by John Milton is published in England.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 As Christina comes of age, she is made ruling queen of Sweden.
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Plague breaks out in Edinburgh.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The opera Ormindo is first performed in Venice: music by Francesco Cavalli and libretto by Giovanni Faustini.
    • \n
  • Sigismund\'s Column is erected in Warsaw to commemorate King Sigismund III Vasa, who moved the capital of Poland from Krak\xc3\xb3w to Warsaw in 1596.
    • \n
  • Philosopher Ren\xc3\xa9 Descartes publishes Principia Philosophiae (Principles of Philosophy).
    • \n
  • During the English Civil War, Prince Rupert and his men take Liverpool Castle, which is later reclaimed by Sir John Moore.
    • \n
  • Opchanacanough leads the Powhatan Indians in an unsuccessful uprising against the English at Jamestown. This is the last such Indian rebellion of the region.
    • \n
  • A Spanish officer is murdered in St. Dominic\'s Church, Macau during mass by colonists loyal to Portugal during the Portuguese Restoration War.
    • \n
\n
    \n
  • The West India Company displayed greater interest in profit than in colonization
    • \n
