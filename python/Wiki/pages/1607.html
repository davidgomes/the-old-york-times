1607 (MDCVII) was a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1607th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 607th year of the 2nd millennium, the 7th year of the 17th century, and the 8th year of the 1600s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1607 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Bank of Genoa fails after the announcement of national bankruptcy in Spain.
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 San Agustin Church, Manila, is officially completed; by the 21st century it will be the oldest church in the Philippines
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 A massive wave sweeps along the Bristol Channel, possibly a tsunami, killing 2,000 people.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Premi\xc3\xa8re of Claudio Monteverdi\'s L\'Orfeo, the earliest fully developed opera in the modern-day repertoire, at the Ducal Palace of Mantua.
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Susenyos defeats the combined armies of Yaqob and Abuna Petros II at the Battle of Gol in Gojjam, which makes him Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Gibraltar: A Dutch fleet destroys a Spanish fleet anchored in the Bay of Gibraltar.
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 English colonists make landfall at Cape Henry, Virginia, later moving up the James River.
    • \n
  • May 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown, Virginia, is established as the first permanent English settlement in North America (the modern-day United States), beginning the American frontier.
    • \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown: Christopher Newport, George Percy, Gabriel Archer, and others travel 6 days exploring along the James River up to the falls and Powhatan\'s village.
    • \n
  • May 26\n
      \n
    • Jamestown: The president directs the fort to be strengthened and armed against the many attacks of the natives: "Hereupon the President was contented the Fort should be pallisadoed, the ordinance mounted, his men armed and exercised, for many were the assaults and Ambuscadoes of the Savages ..." [John Smith, Proceedings (Barbour 1964)]
      • \n
    • 200 armed Indians attack the Jamestown settlement, killing 2 and wounding 10.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown: The Fort is pallisadoed: "we laboured, pallozadoing our fort" [Gabriel Archer (Arber)].
    • \n
  • June 5 \xe2\x80\x93 John Hall marries Susanna, daughter of William Shakespeare.
    • \n
  • June 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Newton rebellion: The Tresham landowners family kills 40\xe2\x80\x9350 peasants during protests against the enclosure of common land in Newton, Northamptonshire, England, at the culmination of the Midland Revolt.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown: Captain John Smith is released from arrest and sworn in as a member of the colony Council.
    • \n
  • June 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown: The triangular fort is completed and armed: "The fifteenth of June we had built and finished our Fort, which was triangle wise, having three Bulwarkes, at every corner, like a halfe Moone, and foure or five pieces of Artillerie mounted in them. We had made our selves sufficiently strong for these Savages. We had also sowne most of our Corne on two Mountaines." [George Percy (Tyler 1952:19)]
    • \n
  • June 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Christopher Newport sails back to England.
    • \n
  • June 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown: The colony bears extreme toil in strengthening the fort [from John Smith, Proceedings (Barbour 1964:210)].
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The ship Gift of God of the Plymouth Company arrives at the mouth of the modern-day Kennebec River in Maine. English colonists establish Fort St. George, also known as the Popham Colony. The settlement lasts little more than a year, before residents return to England in the first oceangoing ship built in the New World, a 30-ton pinnace called The Virginia.
    • \n
  • September 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Hamlet is performed aboard the East India Company ship Red Dragon, under the command of Capt. William Keeling, anchored off the coast of Sierra Leone, the first known performance of a Shakespeare play outside England in English, and the first by amateurs.
    • \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Jamestown President Edward Maria Wingfield is deposed and John Ratcliffe elected.
    • \n
  • September 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Flight of the Earls: Hugh O\'Neill, 2nd Earl of Tyrone, and Rudhraighe \xc3\x93 Domhnail, 1st Earl of Tyrconnell, flee Ireland for Spain with ninety followers to avoid capture by the English crown, never to return.
    • \n
  • December (early) \xe2\x80\x93 Captain John Smith of the Jamestown Colony is captured by Opchancanough and then sent to Chief Powhatan for execution; Pocahontas rescues him.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Spain is effectively bankrupt.
    • \n
  • The rule of Andorra passes jointly to the king of France and the Bishop of Urgell.
    • \n
  • Yaqob is defeated in battle and deposed by his cousin Susenyos, who then becomes Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • In the Midland Revolt against Enclosures in England, the term Levellers is first used.
    • \n
  • Missionary Juan Fonte establishes the first Jesuit mission among the Tarahumara, in the Sierra Madre Mountains of Northwest Mexico.
    • \n
