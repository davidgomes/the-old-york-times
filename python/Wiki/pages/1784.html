\n1784 (MDCCLXXXIV) was a leap year starting on Thursday (dominical letter DC) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Monday (dominical letter GF) of the Julian calendar, the 1784th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 784th year of the 2nd millennium, the 84th year of the 18th century, and the 5th year of the 1780s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1784 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The Turks agree to Russia\'s annexation of the Crimea in the Treaty of Constantinople.[citation needed]
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Congress of the United States ratifies the Treaty of Paris with Great Britain to end the American Revolutionary War, with the signature of President of Congress Thomas Mifflin.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Henry Cavendish\'s paper to the Royal Society of London, Experiments on Air, reveals the composition of water.[1]
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 John Wesley ordains ministers for the Methodist Church in the United States.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 A treaty is signed in Paris between the Kingdom of Great Britain and the Dutch Republic formally ending the Fourth Anglo-Dutch War.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Elizabeth Thible is the first woman to ride in a hot air balloon, at Lyon, France.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Cardinal de Rohan is called before the court to account for his actions in the Queen\'s Necklace Affair.
    • \n
  • August 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Britain creates the colony of New Brunswick.
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Russia establishes a colony at Kodiak, Alaska.
    • \n
  • October 31 Revolt of Horea, Clo\xc8\x99ca and Cri\xc8\x99an in Transylvania
    • \n
  • November 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Roman Catholic Apostolic Prefecture of the United States is established.
    • \n
  • November 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The phenomenon of black holes is first posited in a paper by John Michell in the Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London.[2]
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Immanuel Kant\'s essay "Answering the Question: What Is Enlightenment?" is published.
    • \n
  • December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The Methodist Episcopal Church, USA is officially formed at the "Christmas Conference" led by Thomas Coke and Francis Asbury.
    • \n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Emperor Josef II suspends the Hungarian Constitution because of a revolution in Transylvania.
    • \n
  • King Carlos III of the Spanish Empire authorizes land grants in Alta California.
    • \n
  • Princess Yekaterina Vorontsova-Dashkova is named first president of the newly created Russian Academy.
    • \n
  • The North Carolina General Assembly incorporates the town of Morgansborough, named for Daniel Morgan. The town is designated as the county seat for Burke County, North Carolina and is subsequently renamed "Morgantown" and later shortened to become Morganton.
    • \n
  • The North Carolina General Assembly changes the name of Kingston, North Carolina, originally named for King George III of Great Britain, to Kinston.
    • \n
  • The Japanese famine continues as 300,000 die of starvation.
    • \n
  • A huge locust swarm hits South Africa.
    • \n
  • Benjamin Franklin tries in vain to persuade the French to alter their clocks in winter to take advantage of the daylight.
    • \n
  • Benjamin Franklin invents bifocal spectacles.
    • \n
  • Antoine Lavoisier pioneers quantitative chemistry.
    • \n
  • Cholesterol is isolated.
    • \n
  • Carl Friedrich Gauss pioneers the field of summation with the formula summing 1:n as (n(n+1))/2, at the age of 7.
    • \n
  • Madame du Coudray, pioneer of modern midwifery, retires.
    • \n
  • The India Act requires that the governor general be chosen from outside the British East India Company and it makes company directors subject to parliamentary supervision.
    • \n
  • Britain receives its first bales of imported American cotton.
    • \n
