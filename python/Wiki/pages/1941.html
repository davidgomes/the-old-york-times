1941 (MCMXLI) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1941st year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 941st year of the 2nd millennium, the 41st year of the 20th century, and the 2nd year of the 1940s decade.

Events[edit]

\n

Below, the events of World War II have the "WWII" acronym.

\n

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January\xe2\x80\x93August \xe2\x80\x93 10,072 men, women and children with mental and physical disabilities are asphyxiated with carbon monoxide in a gas chamber at Hadamar Euthanasia Centre in Nazi Germany in the first phase of mass killings under the Action T4 program here.
    • \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Thailand Prime Minister Plaek Phibunsongkhram decrees January 1 as the official start of the Thai solar calendar new year (thus the previous year that began April 1 had only 9 months).
    • \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 A decree (Normalschrifterlass) promulgated in Nazi Germany by Martin Bormann on behalf of Adolf Hitler requires replacement of blackletter typefaces by Antiqua.[1]
    • \n
  • January 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The short subject Elmer\'s Pet Rabbit is released, marking the second appearance of Bugs Bunny, and also the first to have his name on a title card.
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: At the Battle of Bardia in Libya, Australian and British troops defeat Italian forces, the first battle of the war in which an Australian Army formation takes part.
    • \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The keel of the USS Missouri is laid at the New York Navy Yard in Brooklyn.
    • \n
  • January 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Lend-Lease Act is introduced into the United States Congress.
    • \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The British Royal Navy light cruiser HMS Southampton (83) is sunk off Malta.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 All persons born in Puerto Rico since this day are declared U.S. citizens by birth, through U.S. federal law.[2]
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Commerce raiding German auxiliary cruiser Pinguin captures the Norwegian whaling fleet near Bouvet Island, effectively ending Southern Ocean whaling for the duration of the war.[3]
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 John Vincent Atanasoff and Clifford Berry describe the workings of the Atanasoff\xe2\x80\x93Berry computer in print.
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: British troops attack Italian-held Eritrea.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Chief Justice Charles Evans Hughes swears in U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt for his third term.
    • \n
  • January 22\n
      \n
    • WWII: Battle of Tobruk: Australian and British forces capture Tobruk from the Italians.
      • \n
    • In Sweden, Victor Hasselblad registers the Hasselblad camera company.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Aviator Charles Lindbergh testifies before the U.S. Congress and recommends that the United States negotiate a neutrality pact with Adolf Hitler.
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Joseph Grew, the U.S. ambassador to Japan, reports to Washington a rumor overheard at a diplomatic reception concerning a planned surprise attack on Pearl Harbor, Hawaii.
    • \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Australians capture Derna, Libya, from the Italians.
    • \n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Nazis forcibly restore Pierre Laval to office in occupied Vichy France.[citation needed]
    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The United Service Organization (USO) is created to entertain American troops.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Air Training Corps: The Air Training Corps is formed in the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • February 5\xe2\x80\x93April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Battle of Keren \xe2\x80\x93 British and Free French Forces fight hard to capture the strategic town of Keren in Italian Eritrea.
    • \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Fall of Benghazi to the Western Desert Force. Lieutenant-General Erwin Rommel is appointed commander of Afrika Korps.
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The U.S. House of Representatives passes the Lend-Lease Act.[4]
    • \n
  • February 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Winston Churchill, in a worldwide broadcast, tells the United States to show its support by sending arms to the British: "Give us the tools, and we will finish the job."
    • \n
  • February 12\n
      \n
    • WWII: Erwin Rommel arrives in Tripoli.
      • \n
    • Reserve Constable Albert Alexander, a patient at the Radcliffe Infirmary in Oxford, becomes the first person treated with penicillin intravenously, by Howard Florey\'s team. He reacts positively but there is insufficient supply of the drug to reverse his terminal infection. A successful treatment is achieved during May.[5]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Aircraft from HMS Formidable attack Massawa in Eritrea.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Admiral Kichisabur\xc5\x8d Nomura begins his duties as Japanese Ambassador to the United States.
    • \n
  • February 19\xe2\x80\x9322 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Three Nights\' Blitz over Swansea, South Wales: Over these 3 nights of intensive bombing, which lasted a total of 13 hours and 48 minutes, Swansea\'s town centre is almost completely obliterated by the 896 high explosive bombs employed by the Luftwaffe; 397 casualties and 230 deaths reported.
    • \n
  • February 22 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: HMS Shropshire bombards Barawa, on the coast between Kismayo and Mogadishu.
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Glenn T. Seaborg isolates and discovers plutonium.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • The occupied Netherlands starts the first popular uprising in Europe against the Axis powers, the "February strike" against German deportation of Jews in Amsterdam and surroundings.
      • \n
    • British submarine HMS Upright attacks an Italian convoy sinking the cruiser Armando Diaz.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The New Zealand Division cruiser HMS Leander (1931) sinks Italian armed merchant raider Ramb I off the Maldives.

    • \n

  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Richard C. Hottelet is arrested by the Gestapo on "suspicion of espionage", but eventually released in July as part of a prisoner exchange with the U.S.

    \n
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Washington state\'s Grand Coulee Dam begins to generate electricity.

  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Rommel launches his first offensive in Cyrenaica.

  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Kingdom of Yugoslavia joins the Axis powers in Vienna.
    • \n
  • March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n

    • An anti-Axis coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat in Yugoslavia forces Prince Paul into exile; 17-year-old King Peter II assumes power.
      • \n
    • Japanese spy Takeo Yoshikawa arrives in Honolulu to study the Pacific Fleet at Pearl Harbor in preparation for a future attack.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 30 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • All German, Italian and Danish ships anchored in United States waters are taken into "protective custody".
      • \n

April[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 The Valley of Geysers is discovered on the Kamchatka Peninsula of Russia by Tatyana Ustinova.
    • \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Axis forces capture Benghazi.
    • \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Germany invades Yugoslavia and Greece.
    • \n
  • April 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The U.S. acquires full military defense rights in Greenland.
    • \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII:\n
      \n
    • The U.S. destroyer USS Niblack, while picking up survivors from a sunken Dutch freighter, drops depth charges on a German U-boat (the first "shot in anger" fired by America against Germany).[citation needed]
      • \n
    • The Independent State of Croatia, a puppet state of the Axis powers, is established with Usta\xc5\xa1e leader Ante Paveli\xc4\x87 as head (Poglavnik) of the government.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: German troops enter Belgrade.
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The Soviet Union and Japan sign a neutrality pact.[7]
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Axis forces reach Halfaya Pass on the Libyan-Egyptian frontier.
    • \n
  • April 17 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Yugoslav Royal Army capitulates.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Prime Minister of Greece Alexandros Koryzis commits suicide as German troops approach Athens.
    • \n
  • April 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Bertolt Brecht\'s anti-war play Mother Courage and Her Children (German: Mutter Courage und ihre Kinder) receives its first theatrical production at the Schauspielhaus Z\xc3\xbcrich.
    • \n
  • April 21 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Greece capitulates. Commonwealth troops and some elements of the Greek Army withdraw to Crete.
    • \n
  • April 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The America First Committee holds its first mass rally in New York City, with Charles Lindbergh as keynote speaker.
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Franklin D. Roosevelt, at his regular press conference, criticizes Charles Lindbergh by comparing him to the Copperheads of the Civil War period. In response, Lindbergh resigns his commission in the U.S. Army Air Corps Reserve on April 28.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: German troops enter Athens.
    • \n

    • \n
  • May 10\n
      \n
    • WWII: The British House of Commons is damaged by the Luftwaffe in an air raid.
      • \n
    • Rudolf Hess parachutes into Scotland, claiming to be on a peace mission.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 11/May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Usta\xc5\xa1e massacre 260\xe2\x80\x93373 Serb men in a Catholic church in Glina, Croatia where the men had assembled to be received into the Catholic faith in exchange for their lives.

  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Konrad Zuse presents the Z3, the world\'s first working programmable, fully automatic computer, in Berlin.
    • \n
  • May 15\n
      \n
    • The first British jet aircraft, the Gloster E.28/39, is flown.
      • \n
    • Joe DiMaggio\'s 56-game hitting streak begins as the New York Yankees\' center fielder goes one for four against Chicago White Sox Pitcher Eddie Smith.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Viet Minh is formed in at P\xc3\xa1c B\xc3\xb3 in Vietnam to overthrow French rule of the nation as an alliance between the Indochina Communist party, led by Ho Chi Minh, and the Nationalist party. It will become the Viet Cong during the Vietnam War.
    • \n
  • May 20 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: The Battle of Crete begins as Germany launches an airborne invasion of Crete.
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 German submarine U-69 (1940) sinks the U.S.-flagged SS Robin Moor off the west African coast, having allowed the passengers and crew to disembark.
    • \n
  • May 24\n
      \n
    • WWII: In the North Atlantic, German battleship Bismarck sinks battlecruiser HMS Hood, killing all but 3 crewmen from a total of 1,418 aboard the pride of the Royal Navy.
      • \n
    • The British submarine HMS Upholder torpedoes and sinks the Italian ocean liner SS Conte Rosso.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: In the North Atlantic, Fairey Swordfish aircraft from the carrier HMS Ark Royal cripple the steering of German battleship Bismarck in an aerial torpedo attack.
    • \n
  • May 27\n
      \n
    • WWII: Franklin D. Roosevelt, President of the United States, proclaims an "unlimited national emergency."[9]
      • \n
    • WWII: German battleship Bismarck is sunk in the North Atlantic, killing 2,300. It is eventually found in 1989.
      • \n
    • The Swiss Socialist Federation is banned.[10]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 WWII: Manolis Glezos and Apostolos Santas tear down the Nazi swastika on the Acropolis in Athens, and replace it with the Greek flag.
    • \n
  • May 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-Iraqi War: British troops complete the re-occupation of the Kingdom of Iraq, returning Prince \'Abd al-Ilah to power as regent for Faisal II.
