\n1619 (MDCXIX) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Julian calendar, the 1619th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 619th year of the 2nd millennium, the 19th year of the 17th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1610s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1619 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 James I of England\'s Banqueting House, Whitehall in London is destroyed by fire. Inigo Jones is commissioned to design a replacement.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Sarhu: Manchu leader Nurhaci is victorious over the Ming forces.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Matthias, Holy Roman Emperor dies, leaving the Holy Roman Empire without an official leader to deal with the Bohemian Revolt.
    • \n
  • May 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Synod of Dort has its final meeting.
    • \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Dutch statesman Johan van Oldenbarnevelt is executed in The Hague after having been convicted of treason.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Jan Pieterszoon Coen, Governor General of the Dutch East Indies, conquers Jayakarta and renames it Batavia.
    • \n
  • June 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Sablat: Protestant forces are defeated.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Dulwich College founded by Edward Alleyn, in Dulwich, London.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x93 In Jamestown, Virginia, the first representative assembly in the Americas, the House of Burgesses, convenes for the first time.
    • \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of V\xc4\x9bstonice: Bohemian forces defeat the Austrians.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Angoul\xc3\xaame ends the civil war in France.
    • \n
  • August 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Frederick V, Elector Palatine is elected the King of Bohemia.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Ferdinand II is elected emperor of the Holy Roman Empire.
    • \n
  • October 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Munich is signed by Ferdinand II and Maximilian I, Elector of Bavaria.
    • \n
  • November 16 \xe2\x80\x93 William Parker School, Hastings is founded by the will of Rev. William Parker
    • \n
  • November 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Humenn\xc3\xa9: Polish troops assist the Holy Roman Emperor by defeating a Transylvanian force, forcing Gabor Bethlen to raise his siege of Vienna.
    • \n
  • December 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty-eight colonists from Berkeley Parish in England disembark in Virginia and give thanks to God (considered by some to be the first Thanksgiving in the Americas).
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Jahangir grants a British mission important commercial concessions at Surat on the west coast of India.
    • \n
  • England establishes its first outpost in India.
    • \n
  • The Polish\xe2\x80\x93Lithuanian Commonwealth reaches the height of its territorial extent.
    • \n
  • Sal\xc3\xa9 Rovers declare the port of Sal\xc3\xa9 on the Barbary Coast to be the Republic of Sal\xc3\xa9, independent of the Sultan of Morocco, with the Dutch-born corsair Jan Janszoon as president.
    • \n
  • The Danish\xe2\x80\x93Dutch whaling settlement of Smeerenburg is founded in Svalbard.
    • \n
  • An expedition in Sri Lanka led by Phillippe de Oliveira deposes and executes the last Jaffna king Cankili II, putting an end to the Jaffna kingdom.
    • \n
  • A Spanish expedition sails around Tierra del Fuego, mapping the coast and discovering the Diego Ram\xc3\xadrez Islands.
