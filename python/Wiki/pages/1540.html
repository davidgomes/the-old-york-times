Year 1540 (MDXL) was a leap year starting on Thursday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 Dunstable Priory falls prey to Henry VIII of England\'s Dissolution of the Monasteries.
    • \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 King Henry VIII of England marries Anne of Cleves, his fourth Queen consort.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor, enters Ghent without resistance and executes rebels.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Waltham Abbey is the final priory to fall prey to the Dissolution of the Monasteries.
    • \n
  • May 17 - Sher Shah Suri defeats and deposes Mughal Emperor Humayan at the Battle of Kannauj, establishing the Sur Empire.
    • \n
  • May 22 - Pope Paul III summons the Council of Trent.[dubious \xe2\x80\x93 discuss] Its purpose is to achieve the reformation of the church, the definition of dogma and the reunion of Christendom.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Coronado captures Hawikuh, at this time known as part of C\xc3\xadbola, but fails to find the legendary gold.
    • \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 King Henry VIII of England divorces Anne of Cleves, his fourth Queen consort.
    • \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 One of the most important political figures of the reign of Henry VIII of England, Thomas Cromwell, is executed on order from the king on charges of treason. Henry marries his fifth wife, Catherine Howard, on the same day.
    • \n
  • Early September \xe2\x80\x93 Gibraltar is sacked by the fleet of Barbary pirate Ali Hamet, a Sardinian renegade in the service of the Ottoman Empire, and many of the leading citizens taken as captives to Pe\xc3\xb1\xc3\xb3n de V\xc3\xa9lez de la Gomera in Morocco.[1] This leads to construction of the defensive Charles V Wall, at this time known as the Muralla de San Benito.
    • \n
  • September 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Gelawdewos succeeds his father Lebna Dengel as Emperor of Ethiopia.
    • \n
  • September 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Society of Jesus (Jesuits) is approved by Pope Paul III, in his bull Regimini militantis Ecclesiae.
    • \n
  • October 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Albor\xc3\xa1n: A Habsburg Spanish fleet under the command of Bernardino de Mendoza destroys an Ottoman fleet commanded by Ali Hamet off Albor\xc3\xa1n Island in the Mediterranean.
    • \n
  • October 18 \xe2\x80\x93 An expedition led by Spanish conquistador Hernando de Soto destroys the fortified village of Mabila in modern-day Alabama, killing paramount chief Tuskaloosa.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Antwerp is besieged for three days by Maarten van Rossum.
    • \n
  • Georg Joachim Rheticus publishes De libris revolutionum Copernici narratio prima in Danzig, an abstract of Copernicus\' as yet unpublished De revolutionibus orbium coelestium and the first printed publication of Copernican heliocentrism.
    • \n
  • Europe is hit by a heat wave and drought lasting for about 7 months. Rivers such as the Rhine and Seine dry up and many people die from dysentery and other illnesses caused by lack of safe drinking water.[2] However, this year\'s vintage from W\xc3\xbcrzburger Stein and other vineyards is particularly notable.[3]
    • \n
  • Martin Luther expels the theologian Caspar Schwenckfeld from Silesia.
    • \n
  • Paracelsus visits Villach.
    • \n
  • approximate date \xe2\x80\x93 The musket is introduced into Japan from Europe.
    • \n
