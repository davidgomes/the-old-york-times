1808 (MDCCCVIII) was a leap year starting on Friday (dominical letter CB) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter ED) of the Julian calendar, the 1808th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 808th year of the 2nd millennium, the 8th year of the 19th century, and the 9th year of the 1800s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1808 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The importation of slaves into the United States is banned by the Act Prohibiting Importation of Slaves; this is also the earliest day under the United States Constitution that an amendment can be made restricting slavery.
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The organizational meeting leading to the creation of the Wernerian Natural History Society, a former Scottish learned society, is held in Edinburgh.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Bragan\xc3\xa7a Portuguese Royal Family arrives in Brazil, fleeing from the French army.
    • \n
  • 26 January \xe2\x80\x93 Rum Rebellion: On the 20th anniversary of the foundation of the colony of New South Wales, disgruntled military officers of the New South Wales Corps (the Rum Corps) overthrow and imprison Governor William Bligh and seize control of the colony.
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 Russia issues an ultimatum to Sweden to join Napoleon\'s Continental System against Great Britain and Ireland.
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 French troops occupy the Papal States (Vatican).
    • \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The ship Topaz (from Boston April 5, 1807 hunting seals) rediscovers the Pitcairn Islands; only one HMS Bounty mutineer is still alive, Alexander Smith (John Adams).
    • \n
  • February 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Anthracite coal is first burned as residential heating fuel by Jesse Fell in Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania.
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Finnish War: Russian troops cross the border into Finland without a declaration of war.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Russian troops occupy Helsinki and threaten Sveaborg.
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The inaugural meeting of the Wernerian Natural History Society, a former Scottish learned society, is held in Edinburgh.
    • \n
  • March 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Brazil: With the arrival of the Portuguese royal family in Brazil (1808), the colony became the seat of Portuguese Empire.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Russian troops occupy Tampere.
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Upon the death of Christian VII, Frederick VI becomes king of Denmark. The next day (March 14), Denmark declares war on Sweden.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles IV of Spain abdicates in favor of his son, Ferdinand VII.
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Russian troops occupy Turku.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • A volcano erupts from an unknown location in the western Pacific. This causes a localized drop in marine air temperatures during this year, and a world-wide drop in marine air temperature for the following decade.[1]
    • \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 John Jacob Astor incorporates the American Fur Company.
    • \n
  • April 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Troops under colonel Carl von D\xc3\xb6beln clash with Russian troops in Pyh\xc3\xa4joki, Finland.
    • \n
  • May 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: The people of Madrid rise up against the French troops.
    • \n
  • May 3\n
      \n
    • Finnish War: The fortress of Sveaborg is lost by Sweden to Russia.
      • \n
    • Peninsular War: The Madrid rebels who rose on May 2 are executed near Pr\xc3\xadncipe P\xc3\xado Hill (Francisco Goya paints the fight and the execution in 1814).
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Finnish War: A landing of Swedish troops at Ala-Lemu, near Turku, fails.
    • \n
  • June 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Finnish War: A second landing of Swedish troops at Ala-Lemu fails.
    • \n
  • June 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Finnish War: The Swedish archipelago fleet defeats the Russians in the naval battle of Turku.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Troops under Colonel Adlercreutz force the Russians to withdraw in Lapua.
    • \n
  • July 22 \xe2\x80\x93 French General Dupont surrenders to Spanish irregular forces following the Battle of Bail\xc3\xa9n.
    • \n
  • August 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Troops under Carl von D\xc3\xb6beln defeat a Russian attack in Kauhajoki.
    • \n
  • August 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Vimeiro: British troops under Duke of Wellington defeat the French under General Jean-Andoche Junot.
    • \n
  • September 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Congress of Erfurt between the emperors Napoleon I of France and Alexander I of Russia starts.
    • \n
  • September 29 \xe2\x80\x93 A truce is declared between Swedish and Russian troops in Finland; it ends on October 19.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Mahmud II (1808\xe2\x80\x931839) succeeds Mustafa IV (1807\xe2\x80\x931808) as sultan of the Ottoman Empire.
    • \n
  • November 19 \xe2\x80\x93 A new truce ends fighting in Finland.
    • \n
  • November 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Tudela: French Marshal Lannes defeats a Spanish army.
    • \n
  • November\n
      \n
    • Swedish troops evacuate Finland. Tsar Alexander I of Russia proclaims Finland a part of Russia.
      • \n
    • James Madison defeats Charles C. Pinckney in the U.S. presidential election.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • December \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon joins his army in Spain.
    • \n
  • December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 At 20:34 UTC Mercury occults Saturn (there are no observation records).
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The original Covent Garden Theatre in London is destroyed by a fire, along with most of the scenery, costumes and scripts.
    • \n
  • December 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Beethoven concert of 22 December 1808: Ludwig van Beethoven conducts and plays piano in a marathon benefit concert at the Theater an der Wien in Vienna consisting entirely of first public performances of works by him including the Symphony No. 5, Symphony No. 6, Piano Concerto No. 4 and Choral Fantasy.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

    \n
  • British siege of Mauritius begins.
    • \n
  • The Academy of Fine Arts, Munich is founded.

  • Goethe\'s Faust: The First Part of the Tragedy is published in full.
    • \n
