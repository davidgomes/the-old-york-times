Year 1562 (MDLXII) was a common year starting on Thursday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Shane O\'Neill of Tyrone pleads his cause at the Palace of Whitehall in London before Queen Elizabeth I of England, who recognises his status. He returns to Ireland on May 26 and resumes his rebellious activities by November.[1]
    • \n
  • January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Huguenots are recognized under the Edict of Saint-Germain.
    • \n
  • January 18 \xe2\x80\x93 First performance of Thomas Norton and Thomas Sackville\'s play Gorboduc before Queen Elizabeth I of England. It is the first known English tragedy and the first English language play to employ blank verse.[2]
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Over 80(?) Huguenots are massacred by the ultra-Catholic Francis, Duke of Guise in Wassy-sur-Blaise, marking the start of the First War of Religion in France. Protestant forces led by Louis I de Bourbon, Prince de Cond\xc3\xa9 and Gaspard de Coligny quickly seize control of Orl\xc3\xa9ans, Rouen, and other cities throughout France.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 English merchant Anthony Jenkinson has an audience with Ivan the Terrible in Moscow before continuing his second expedition through the Grand Duchy of Moscow to Qazvin, capital of the Safavid dynasty in Persia.
    • \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Jean Ribault, French navigator, lands in Florida and later establishes a Huguenot colony at Charlesfort on Parris Island, off the coast of South Carolina.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Fray Diego de Landa, acting Bishop of Yucatan, burns the sacred books of the Maya.
    • \n
  • September 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Hampton Court between Queen Elizabeth I of England and Huguenot leader Louis, Prince of Cond\xc3\xa9.[3]
    • \n
  • September 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Maximilian, son of the Emperor Ferdinand I, succeeds as King of Bohemia.
    • \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 John Hawkins initiates the English trans-Atlantic slave trade, shipping slaves from Sierra Leone on the Guinea Coast to Hispaniola in the Caribbean.[4] As a result, the Spanish prohibit English ships from trading at their new world ports.
    • \n
  • October 4 \xe2\x80\x93 English forces under Ambrose Dudley, 3rd Earl of Warwick, land at Le Havre to aid the Huguenots.[3]
    • \n
  • October 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Rouen is captured by Royalist forces under Antoine de Bourbon, King of Navarre, who is mortally wounded.
    • \n
  • November 5 \xe2\x80\x93 In Scotland, the rebellion of George Gordon, Earl of Huntly is crushed by James Stewart, Earl of Moray, at the Battle of Corrichie.
    • \n
  • November 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Maximilian of Bohemia is elected King of the Romans.
    • \n
  • December 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Dreux: Huguenot forces under Cond\xc3\xa9 and Coligny, and Catholic forces under the ageing Anne, duc de Montmorency, and Francis, Duke of Guise, fight a bloody battle, narrowly won by the Catholic side. The official leaders of both armies (Cond\xc3\xa9 and Montmorency) are captured in the battle.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Mughal Emperor Akbar conquers Malwa and its last Sultan, Baz Bahadur, flees.
    • \n
  • The Church of England approves the Thirty-Nine Articles of Religion, defining its doctrinal stance.[4]
    • \n
  • Dudley Grammar School is established and Gresham\'s School is granted a royal charter in England.
    • \n
  • Fausto Sozzini publishes Brevis explicatio in primum Johannis caput, originating Socinianism.
    • \n
  • Giacomo Barozzi da Vignola publishes Regola delli cinque ordini d\'architettura (Rules of the Five Orders of Architecture);[5] it will become in succeeding centuries the most published book in architectural history.[6]
    • \n
