2015 (MMXV) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar, the 2015th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 15th year of the 3rd millennium, the 15th year of the 21st century, and the 6th year of the 2010s decade.

Events[edit]

\n

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1\n
      \n
    • The Eurasian Economic Union comes into effect, creating a political and economic union between Russia, Belarus, Armenia, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.[2]
      • \n
    • Lithuania officially adopts the euro as its currency, replacing the litas, and becomes the nineteenth Eurozone country.[3]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 3\xe2\x80\x937 \xe2\x80\x93 A series of massacres in Baga, Nigeria and surrounding villages by Boko Haram kills more than 2,000 people.[4][5][6][7]
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The Swiss National Bank abandons the cap on the franc\'s value relative to the euro, causing turmoil in international financial markets.[8][9]
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 After Houthi forces seize the presidential palace, Yemeni President Abd Rabbuh Mansur Hadi resigns after months of unrest.[10]
    • \n
\n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 12\n
      \n
    • Leaders from Russia, Ukraine, Germany and France reach an agreement on the conflict in eastern Ukraine that includes a ceasefire and withdrawal of heavy weapons. However, several days later, the Ukrainian government and pro-Russian rebels claim that, within its first day, the ceasefire was broken 139 times, as both sides failed to withdraw their heavy weapons and fighting had continued.[11][12]
      • \n
    • The United Nations Security Council adopts Resolution 2199 to combat terrorism.[13]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Egyptian military begins conducting airstrikes against a branch of the Islamic militant group ISIL in Libya in retaliation for the group\'s beheading of over a dozen Egyptian Christians.[14]
    • \n
\n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 5\xe2\x80\x938 \xe2\x80\x93 The ancient city sites of Nimrud, Hatra and Dur-Sharrukin in Iraq are demolished by the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant.[15][16][17]
    • \n
  • March 6 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA\'s Dawn probe enters orbit around Ceres, becoming the first spacecraft to visit a dwarf planet.[18][19]
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant becomes allies with fellow jihadist group Boko Haram, effectively annexing the group.[20]
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 An Airbus A320-211 operated by Germanwings crashes in the French Alps, killing all 150 on board.[21]
    • \n
  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 A Saudi Arabia-led coalition of Arab countries starts a military intervention in Yemen in order to uphold the Yemeni government in its fight against the Houthis\' southern offensive.[22][23]
    • \n
\n

April[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 2 \xe2\x80\x93 148 people are killed, the majority students, in a mass shooting at the Garissa University College in Kenya, perpetrated by the militant terrorist organization Al-Shabaab.[24]
    • \n
  • April 25 \xe2\x80\x93 A magnitude 7.8 earthquake strikes Nepal and causes 8,857 deaths[25][26] in Nepal, 130 in India,[27] 27 in China[28] and 4 in Bangladesh[29] with a total of 9,018 deaths.
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The World Health Organization (WHO) declares that rubella has been eradicated from the Americas.[30]
    • \n
\n

May[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May 1\xe2\x80\x93October 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Expo 2015 is held in Milan, Italy.[31]
    • \n
  • May 11\xe2\x80\x9312 \xe2\x80\x93 Version O of Les Femmes d\'Alger by Pablo Picasso sells for US$179.3 million at Christie\'s auction in New York, while the sculpture L\'Homme au doigt by Alberto Giacometti sells for US$141.3 million, setting a new world record for a painting and for a sculpture, respectively.[32][33]
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 A second major earthquake in Nepal, measuring 7.3 on the moment magnitude scale, results in 153 deaths in Nepal,[34] 62 in India,[35] 1 in China[36] and 2 in Bangladesh[36] with a total of 218 deaths.
    • \n
  • May 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Ireland votes to legalize same-sex marriage, becoming the first country to legalize same-sex marriage by popular vote.[37]
    • \n
\n

June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 FIFA President Sepp Blatter announces his intention to resign amidst an FBI-led corruption investigation, and calls for an extraordinary congress to elect a new president as soon as possible.[38]
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The governments of India and Bangladesh officially ratify their 1974 agreement to exchange enclaves along their border.
    • \n
  • June 7\xe2\x80\x938 \xe2\x80\x93 The 41st G7 summit is held in Schloss Elmau, Bavaria.
    • \n
  • June 25\xe2\x80\x9326 \xe2\x80\x93 ISIL claim responsibility for three attacks around the world during the Ramadan:\n
      \n
    • Koban\xc3\xae massacre: ISIL fighters detonate three car bombs, enter Koban\xc3\xae, Syria, and open fire at civilians, killing more than 220.
      • \n
    • Sousse attacks: 22-year-old Seifeddine Rezgui opens fire at a tourist resort at Port El Kantaoui, Tunisia, killing 40 people.
      • \n
    • Kuwait mosque bombing: A suicide bomber attacks the Shia Mosque Imam Ja\'far as-Sadiq at Kuwait City, Kuwait, killing 27 people and injuring 227 others.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 30\n
      \n
    • Cuba becomes the first country in the world to eradicate mother-to-child transmission of HIV and syphilis.[39]
      • \n
    • A Lockheed C-130 Hercules operated by the Indonesian Air Force crashed into a crowded residential neighborhood in Medan shortly after take-off from Soewondo Air Force Base, killing 143 people including 22 others on the ground, marking the second-deadliest air disaster to ever occur in Medan and the deadliest crash in Indonesian Air Force peacetime history.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
\n

  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Greek government-debt crisis: Greece becomes the first advanced economy to miss a payment to the International Monetary Fund in the 71-year history of the IMF.[40]
    • \n
  • July 14\n

    • NASA\'s New Horizons spacecraft performs a close flyby of Pluto, becoming the first spacecraft in history to visit the distant world.[41]

    \n

  • July 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Cuba and the United States reestablish full diplomatic relations, ending a 54-year stretch of hostility between the nations.[43]
    • \n
  • July 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Turkey begins a series of airstrikes against PKK and ISIL targets after the 2015 Suru\xc3\xa7 bombing.
    • \n

August[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Debris found on R\xc3\xa9union Island is confirmed to be that of Malaysian Airlines Flight 370, missing since March 2014.[44]
    • \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 A bombing takes place inside the Erawan Shrine at the Ratchaprasong intersection in Pathum Wan District, Bangkok, Thailand, killing 20 people and injuring 125.[45]
    • \n
\n

September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • September 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Scientists announce the discovery of Homo naledi, a previously unknown species of early human in South Africa.[46]
    • \n
  • September 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Gravitational waves are detected for the first time, by LIGO. This is not announced until February 11 2016.[47]
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Automaker Volkswagen is alleged to have been involved in worldwide rigging of diesel emissions tests, affecting an estimated 11 million vehicles globally.[48][49]
    • \n
  • September 24 \xe2\x80\x93 A stampede during the Hajj pilgrimage in Mecca, Saudi Arabia, kills at least 2,200 people and injures more than 900 others, with more than 650 missing.
    • \n
  • September 28 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA announces that liquid water has been found on Mars.[50]
    • \n
  • September 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Russia begins air strikes against ISIL and anti-government forces in Syria in support of the Syrian government.
    • \n
\n

October[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 3 \xe2\x80\x93 A United States airstrike on a M\xc3\xa9decins Sans Fronti\xc3\xa8res (Doctors Without Borders) hospital in Afghanistan accidentally kills an estimated 20 people.[51]
    • \n
  • October 10 \xe2\x80\x93 A suicide bomb kills at least 100 people at a peace rally in Ankara, Turkey, and injures more than 400 others.
    • \n
  • October 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Hurricane Patricia becomes the most intense hurricane ever recorded in the Western Hemisphere, with winds of 200 mph and a pressure of 879 mbar.[52]
    • \n
  • October 26 \xe2\x80\x93 A magnitude 7.5 earthquake strikes the Hindu Kush region and causes 398 deaths,[53] with 279 in Pakistan, 115 in Afghanistan and 4 in India.
    • \n
  • October 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Flight KGL9268, an Airbus A321 airliner en route to Saint Petersburg from Sharm el-Sheikh, crashes near Al-Hasana in Sinai, killing all 217 passengers and 7 crew members on board.[54]
    • \n
\n

November[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Chinese and Taiwanese presidents, Xi Jinping and Ma Ying-jeou, formally meet for the first time.[55]
    • \n
  • November 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Several suicide bombings occur in Beirut, Lebanon, killing 43 and injuring 239. The Islamic State in Iraq and the Levant claim responsibility.[56]
    • \n
  • November 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Multiple terrorist attacks claimed by Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) in Paris, France, resulting in 130 fatalities.[57]
    • \n
  • November 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Syrian Civil War: Turkey shoots down a Russian fighter jet in the first case of a NATO member destroying a Russian aircraft since the 1950s.[58]
    • \n
  • November 30 \xe2\x80\x93 The 2015 United Nations Climate Change Conference (COP 21) is held in Paris, attended by leaders from 147 nations.[59]
    • \n
\n

December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • December 12 \xe2\x80\x93 A global climate change pact is agreed at the COP 21 summit, committing all countries to reduce carbon emissions for the first time.[60]
    • \n
  • December 22 \xe2\x80\x93 SpaceX lands a Falcon 9 rocket, the first reusable rocket to successfully enter orbital space and return.[61]
