\n1649 (MDCXLIX) was a common year starting on Friday (dominical letter C) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Monday (dominical letter G) of the Julian calendar, the 1649th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 649th year of the 2nd millennium, the 49th year of the 17th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1640s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1649 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles I of England goes on trial for treason and other "high crimes".
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 King Charles I of England, Scotland and Ireland is found guilty of high treason in a public session. He is beheaded three days later, outside the Banqueting Hall in the Palace of Whitehall, London.
    • \n
  • January 30\n
      \n
    • Following the execution of King Charles I, the Commonwealth of England, a republican form of government, replaces the monarchy as the form of government of England and later of Scotland and Ireland. Members of the Long Parliament serve as government.
      • \n
    • Charles, Prince of Wales declares himself King Charles II of England, Scotland and Ireland. At the time, none of the three kingdoms recognize him as ruler.[citation needed]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 In Edinburgh, Scotland claimant King Charles II of England is declared King in his absence. Scotland is the first of the three Kingdoms to recognize his claim to the throne.
    • \n
  • March 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The rebel Frondeurs and the French government sign the Peace of Rueil.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The House of Commons of England passes an act abolishing the House of Lords, declaring that it is "useless and dangerous to the people of England".[1]
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Blake is promoted to become a "General at Sea" of the English fleet.[2]
    • \n
  • May 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Banbury mutiny in England ends \xe2\x80\x93 leaders of the Leveller mutineers in the New Model Army are hanged.
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 An act declaring England to be a Commonwealth is passed by the Rump Parliament.
    • \n
  • May 22\xe2\x80\x93October \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Blake blockades Prince Rupert\'s fleet in Kinsale, Ireland.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August \xe2\x80\x93 The Diggers abandon their last major colony at St. George\'s Hill, Weybridge, England.
    • \n
  • August 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Dubhaltach Mac Fhirbhisigh completes Book VIII of Leabhar na nGenealach, in Galway, within days of an outbreak of the plague.
    • \n
  • August 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Oliver Cromwell lands in Dublin to begin the Cromwellian conquest of Ireland.
    • \n
  • September 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The Italian city of Castro is completely destroyed by the forces of Pope Innocent X, ending the Wars of Castro.
    • \n
  • September 3\xe2\x80\x9311 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Drogheda in Ireland: New Model Army massacre the Irish Catholic Confederation garrison.
    • \n
  • October 2\xe2\x80\x9311 \xe2\x80\x93 Sack of Wexford in Ireland: New Model Army massacre the Irish Catholic Confederation garrison.
    • \n
