Year 1565 (MDLXV) was a common year starting on Monday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 In Russia, it is the beginning of the Oprichnina under Ivan the Terrible.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Battle of Talikota, a watershed battle fought between the Vijayanagara Empire and the Deccan sultanates, ends the last Hindu kingdom in South India.
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Spanish Conquistador Miguel L\xc3\xb3pez de Legazpi lands with his troops on the shores of Cebu Island in the Philippines.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, is founded as S\xc3\xa3o Sebasti\xc3\xa3o do Rio de Janeiro by the Portuguese.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Cebu City is established as San Miguel by L\xc3\xb3pez de Legazpi, becoming the first Spanish settlement in the Philippines.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Ottoman troops land on the island of Malta, beginning the Siege of Malta.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Matsunaga Hisahide assassinates the 13th Ashikaga shogun, Ashikaga Yoshiteru.
    • \n
\n


\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Mary, Queen of Scots, widowed, marries Henry Stuart, Lord Darnley.
    • \n
  • August 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Sark granted as a Fief by Elizabeth I to Hellier de Carteret Seigneur of Saint Ouen
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 St. Augustine, Florida (named after St. Augustine), is established. It is the oldest remaining European settlement in the United States.
    • \n
  • September 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Spanish fleet of Pedro Men\xc3\xa9ndez de Avil\xc3\xa9s lands on modern-day Florida to oust the French under Jean Ribault. He later destroys the French colony of Fort Caroline.
    • \n
  • September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Pedro Men\xc3\xa9ndez de Avil\xc3\xa9s settles in St. Augustine, Florida.
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The Knights of Malta lift the Siege of Malta after four months.
    • \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 The first Martello tower, the Tour de Mortella, designed by Giovan Giacomo Paleari Fratino (el Fratin), is completed as part of the Genovese defence system at Mortella (Myrtle) Point in Upper Corsica.[1]
    • \n
  • October 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Ships belonging to the Matsura clan of Japan fail to capture the Portuguese trading carrack in the Battle of Fukuda Bay, the first recorded naval battle between Japan and the West.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The pencil is first documented by Conrad Gesner; it is becoming common in England.
    • \n
  • John Beddoes School is founded at Presteigne in Wales.
    • \n
  • Huntingdon Grammar School is established.
