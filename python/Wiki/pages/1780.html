\n1780 (MDCCLXXX) was a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter ED) of the Julian calendar, the 1780th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 780th year of the 2nd millennium, the 80th year of the 18th century, and the 1st year of the 1780s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1780 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Cape St. Vincent: British Admiral Sir George Rodney defeats a Spanish fleet.
    • \n
  • February 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Omicron Delta Omega co-ed fraternity is founded by Benjamin Franklin.
    • \n
  • March 11\n
      \n
    • The First League of Armed Neutrality is formed between Denmark, Sweden, and Russia (February 28 O.S.)
      • \n
    • General Lafayette embarks on French frigate Hermione at Rochefort, arriving in Boston on April 28 carrying the news that he has secured French men and ships to reinforce the American side in the American Revolutionary War.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The British Gazette and Sunday Monitor, the first Sunday newspaper in Britain, begins publication.
    • \n
  • April 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The University of M\xc3\xbcnster in M\xc3\xbcnster, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany is founded.
    • \n
  • May 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Epsom Derby horse race is run on Epsom Downs, Surrey, England.[1] The victor is Diomed.[2]
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Charleston, South Carolina is taken by British forces.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nMay 29: Waxhaw Massacre in America.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • May 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Cumberland Compact signed by American settlers in the Cumberland Valley of Tennessee.
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 New England\'s Dark Day: An unaccountable darkness spreads over New England, regarded by some observers as a fulfillment of Bible prophecy.
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Loyalist forces under Col. Banastre Tarleton kill surrendering American soldiers in the Waxhaw Massacre.
    • \n
  • June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 An Anti-Catholic mob led by Lord George Gordon marches on Parliament of Great Britain leading to the outbreak of the Gordon Riots in London.[2]
    • \n
  • June 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Gordon Riots in London are ended by the intervention of troops. About 285 people are shot dead, with another 200 wounded and around 450 arrested.
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Battle of Springfield \xe2\x80\x93 The Continental Army defeats the British in New Jersey.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 French soldiers arrive in Newport, Rhode Island to reinforce colonists in the American Revolutionary War.[3]
    • \n
  • August 16 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Camden: British troops inflict heavy losses on a Patriot army at Camden, South Carolina.
    • \n
  • August 9 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: Spanish admiral Luis de C\xc3\xb3rdova y C\xc3\xb3rdova captures a British convoy totalling 55 vessels amongst Indiamen, frigates and other cargo ships off Cape St. Vincent.[4][5]
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Louis XVI of France abolishes the use of torture in extracting confessions.
    • \n
  • September 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Benedict Arnold gives detailed plans of West Point to Major John Andr\xc3\xa9. Three days later, Andr\xc3\xa9 is captured with papers revealing that Arnold was planning to surrender West Point to the British.
    • \n
  • September 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Benedict Arnold flees to British-held New York.
    • \n
  • October 2 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War: In Tappan, New York, British spy John Andr\xc3\xa9 is hanged by American forces.
    • \n
  • October 7 \xe2\x80\x93 American Revolutionary War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Kings Mountain: Patriot militia forces annihilate Loyalists under British Major Patrick Ferguson at Kings Mountain in South Carolina.
    • \n
  • October 10\xe2\x80\x93October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Hurricane flattens the islands of Barbados, Martinique and Sint Eustatius: 22,000 dead.
    • \n
  • November 29 \xe2\x80\x93 Maria Theresa of Austria dies and her Habsburg dominions pass to her ambitious son, Joseph II, who has already been Holy Roman Emperor since 1765.
    • \n
  • December 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Emperor K\xc5\x8dkaku accedes to the throne of Japan.
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Start of the Fourth Anglo-Dutch War.[6]
    • \n
