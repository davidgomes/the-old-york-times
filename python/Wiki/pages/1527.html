Year 1527 (MDXXVII) was a common year starting on Tuesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Felix Manz, co-founder of the Swiss Anabaptists, is drowned in the Limmat in Z\xc3\xbcrich by the Z\xc3\xbcrich Reformed state church.
    • \n
  • March 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Khanwa: This and two other major Moghul victories lead to their domination of northern India.
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 Paracelsus is appointed as town physician of Basle.
    • \n
  • April 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Treaty of Westminster (1527), an alliance during the War of the League of Cognac.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nSack of Rome
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • May 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Sack of Rome: Spanish and German troops led by the Duke of Bourbon sack Rome, forcing Pope Clement VII to make peace with Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor, marking the end of the High Renaissance. The Pope grows a beard in mourning.
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 In Florence, the Piagnon, a group devoted to the memory of Girolamo Savonarola, drive out the Medici for a second time, re-establishing the Republic of Florence until 1530.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Narv\xc3\xa1ez expedition to conquer Florida sets sail from Spain.
    • \n
  • June 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Protestant Reformation begins in Sweden. The Riksdag of the Estates in V\xc3\xa4ster\xc3\xa5s adopts Lutheranism as the state religion in place of Roman Catholicism.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Niccol\xc3\xb2 Machiavelli, Italian historian, politician, diplomat, philosopher and humanist dies in Florence, Italy.
    • \n
  • June 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Jakarta, current capital of Indonesia, was founded as Jayakarta.
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Paracelsus burns books of Avicenna.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The first known letter is sent from North America by John Rut while at St. John\'s, Newfoundland.
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Sixty Anabaptists meet at the Martyrs\' Synod in Augsburg.
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Diet of Odense (Denmark). King Frederick I declares religious tolerance for Lutherans, permits marriage of priests and forbids seeking papal pallium (approval) for royal appointments of Church officials.[1][2]
    • \n
  • September 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Tarcal: Ferdinand, future Holy Roman Emperor, defeats John Z\xc3\xa1polya and takes over most of Hungary. John appeals to the Turks for help.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Spanish conquest of Guatemala highlands is completed; the first Guatemala City (Ciudad Vieja) is founded.
    • \n
  • Spanish Conquistador Francisco de Montejo invades Yucat\xc3\xa1n.
    • \n
  • Members of the University of Wittenberg flee to Jena in fear of the bubonic plague.
    • \n
  • Bishop Vesey\'s Grammar School at Sutton Coldfield in the West Midlands of England is founded by Bishop John Vesey.
    • \n
  • Sir George Monoux College is founded as a grammar school at Walthamstow, England, by Sir George Monoux, draper and Lord Mayor of London.
    • \n
  • The Ming Dynasty government of China greatly reduces the quotas for taking grain, severely diminishing the state\'s capacity to relieve famines through a previously successful granary system.
    • \n
