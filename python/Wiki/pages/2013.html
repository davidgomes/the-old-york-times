2013 (MMXIII) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar, the 2013th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 13th year of the 3rd millennium, the 13th year of the 21st century, and the 4th year of the 2010s decade.

Events[edit]

\n

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The French military begins a five-month intervention into the Northern Mali conflict, targeting the militant Islamist Ansar Dine group.[2][3]
    • \n
  • January 16\xe2\x80\x9320 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty-nine international workers and one security guard die in a hostage crisis at a natural gas facility near In Am\xc3\xa9nas, Algeria.[4][5][6][7]
    • \n
\n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 North Korea conducts its third underground nuclear test, prompting widespread condemnation and tightened economic sanctions from the international community.[8][9]
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 A meteor explodes over the Russian city of Chelyabinsk, injuring 1,491 people and damaging over 4,300 buildings. It is the most powerful meteor to strike Earth\'s atmosphere in over a century.[10] The incident, along with a coincidental flyby of a larger asteroid, prompts international concern regarding the vulnerability of the planet to meteor strikes.[11][12]
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 American scientists use a 3D printer to create a living lab-grown ear from collagen and animal ear cell cultures. In the future, it is hoped, similar ears could be grown to order as transplants for human patients suffering from ear trauma or amputation.[13]
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Benedict XVI resigns as pope, becoming the first to do so since Gregory XII in 1415, and the first to do so voluntarily since Celestine V in 1294.[14]
    • \n
\n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Cardinal Jorge Mario Bergoglio of Argentina is elected the 266th pope, whereupon he takes the name Francis[15][16][17] and becomes the first Jesuit pope, the first pope from the Americas, and the first pope from the Southern Hemisphere.[18]
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 2012\xe2\x80\x932013 Central African Republic conflict: Central African Republic President Fran\xc3\xa7ois Boziz\xc3\xa9 flees to the Democratic Republic of the Congo, after rebel forces capture the nation\'s capital, Bangui.[19][20][21]
    • \n
  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 The European Union agrees to a \xe2\x82\xac10 billion economic bailout for Cyprus. The bailout loan will be equally split between the European Financial Stabilisation Mechanism, the European Financial Stability Facility, and the International Monetary Fund. The deal precipitates a banking crisis in the island nation.[22][23]
    • \n
  • March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Canada becomes the first country to withdraw from the United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification.[24]
    • \n
\n

April[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nApril 24: Savar building collapse.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • April 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Nations General Assembly adopts the Arms Trade Treaty to regulate the international trade of conventional weapons.[25]
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Two Chechen Islamist brothers explode two bombs at the Boston Marathon in Boston, Massachusetts, in the United States, killing 3 and injuring 264 others.[26][27]
    • \n
  • April 24 \xe2\x80\x93 An eight-storey commercial building collapses in Savar Upazila near the Bangladeshi capital of Dhaka, leaving 1,129 dead[28] and 2,500 injured.[29][30][31] The accident is the deadliest non-terrorist structural collapse in modern times[32] and the third-worst industrial disaster in history.[32][not in citation given]
    • \n
\n

May[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May 15 \xe2\x80\x93 In a study published in the scientific journal Nature, researchers from Oregon Health & Science University in the United States describe the first creation of human embryonic stem cells by cloning.[33]
    • \n
\n

June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 American Edward Snowden discloses operations engaged in by a U.S. government mass surveillance program to news publications and flees the country, later being granted temporary asylum in Russia.[34][35][36]
    • \n
\n

July[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Croatia becomes the 28th member of the European Union.[37]
    • \n
  • July 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Amid mass protests across Egypt, President Mohamed Morsi is deposed in a military coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat, leading to widespread violence.[38][39]
    • \n
\n

August[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Following the military coup in Egypt, two anti-coup camps are raided by the security forces leaving 2,600 dead.[40] The raids were described by Human Rights Watch as "one of the world\xe2\x80\x99s largest killings of demonstrators in a single day in recent history\xe2\x80\x9d.[41]
    • \n
  • August 21 \xe2\x80\x93 1,429 are killed in the Ghouta chemical attack during the Syrian Civil War.[42]
    • \n
\n

September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • September 21 \xe2\x80\x93 al-Shabaab Islamic militants attack the Westgate shopping mall in Nairobi, killing at least 62 civilians and wounding over 170.[43]
    • \n
\n

October[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Delegates from some 140 countries and territories sign the Minamata Treaty, a UNEP treaty designed to protect human health and the environment from emissions and releases of mercury and mercury compounds.[44]
    • \n
  • October 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Saudi Arabia rejects a seat on the United Nations Security Council, making it the first country to reject a seat on the Security Council. Jordan takes the seat on December 6.
    • \n
\n

November[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nNovember 8: Typhoon Haiyan.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • November 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Mangalyaan (aka Mars Orbiter Mission) is launched by India from its launchpad in Sriharikota.[45]
    • \n
  • November 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Typhoon Haiyan ("Yolanda"), one of the strongest tropical cyclones on record, hits the Philippines and Vietnam, causing devastation with at least 6,241 dead.[46]
    • \n
  • November 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Three Studies of Lucian Freud, a series of portraits of Lucian Freud by the British painter Francis Bacon, sells for US$142.4 million in a New York City auction, setting a new world record for an auctioned work of art.[47][48]
    • \n
  • November 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Euromaidan pro-EU demonstrations begin in Ukraine after President Viktor Yanukovych rejects an economic association agreement between the European Union and Ukraine in favor of closer ties to Russia.[49]
    • \n
  • November 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Iran agrees to limit their nuclear development program in exchange for sanctions relief.[50][51]
    • \n
