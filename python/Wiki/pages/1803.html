1803 (MDCCCIII) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1803rd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 803rd year of the 2nd millennium, the 3rd year of the 19th century, and the 4th year of the 1800s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1803 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The first edition of Alexandre Balthazar Laurent Grimod de La Reyni\xc3\xa8re\'s Almanach des gourmands, the first guide to restaurant cooking, is published in Paris.
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 William Symington demonstrates his Charlotte Dundas, the "first practical steamboat", in Scotland.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJanuary 5: steamboat Charlotte Dundas.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • January 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Monroe and Livingston sail for Paris to discuss, and possibly buy, New Orleans; they end up completing the Louisiana Purchase.
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Act of Mediation issued by Napoleon Bonaparte establishes the Swiss Confederation to replace the Helvetic Republic. Under the terms of the act, Graub\xc3\xbcnden, St. Gallen, Thurgau, the Ticino and Vaud become Swiss cantons.
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Edward Despard and six others are hanged and beheaded for plotting to assassinate King George III of the United Kingdom and to destroy the Bank of England.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Marbury v. Madison: The Supreme Court of the United States establishes the principle of judicial review.
    • \n
  • February 25 \xe2\x80\x93 A major redistribution of territorial sovereignty within the Holy Roman Empire is enacted via an act known as the Reichsdeputationshauptschluss.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Ohio is admitted as the 17th U.S. state, retroactive from August 7, 1953.
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Aargau becomes a Swiss canton.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March\xe2\x80\x93April \xe2\x80\x93 The franc germinal is introduced in France.
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The L\'Aigle meteorite falls in Normandy, causing a shift in scientific opinion on the origin of meteorites.
    • \n
  • April 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Louisiana Purchase is made by the United States from France.
    • \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 The First Consul of France Citizen Bonaparte begins making preparations to invade England.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Kingdom resumes war on France after France refuses to withdraw from Dutch territory which begins the Napoleonic Wars.
    • \n
  • May 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Master Malati, a Coptic Christian leader, is beheaded by a Muslim mob in Cairo, Egypt.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Louisiana Purchase is announced to the American people.
    • \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 The convention of Artlenburg leads to the French occupation of Hanover (which had been ruled by the British king).
    • \n
  • July 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Robert Emmet\'s uprising in Ireland begins.
    • \n
  • July 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The wagonway between Wandsworth and Croydon is opened, being the first public railway line in England.
    • \n
  • August 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The British begin the Second Anglo-Maratha War against the Scindia of Gwalior.
    • \n
  • September 3 \xe2\x80\x93 William Wordsworth writes "Upon Westminster Bridge."
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Delhi, during the Second Anglo-Maratha War, between British troops under General Lake, and Marathas of Scindia\'s army under General Louis Bourquin.
    • \n
  • September 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Irish rebel Robert Emmet is executed.
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Assaye (India): British-led troops defeat Maratha forces.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Senate ratifies the Louisiana Purchase Treaty, doubling the size of the United States.
    • \n
  • November 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Verti\xc3\xa8res: The Haitian army led by Jean-Jacques Dessalines defeats the army of Napoleon.
    • \n
  • November 30 \xe2\x80\x93 At the Cabildo in New Orleans, Spanish representatives Governor Manuel de Salcedo and the Marqu\xc3\xa9s de Casa Calvo officially transfer Louisiana (New Spain) to French representative Prefect Pierre Cl\xc3\xa9ment de Laussat. Barely three weeks later, in the same location on December 20, France transfers the same land to the United States as the Louisiana Purchase, with William C. C. Claiborne and James Wilkinson officiating for the U.S.
    • \n
\n

Undated[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Thomas Bruce, 7th Earl of Elgin begins removal of the Elgin Marbles from the Parthenon in Athens where they are at risk of destruction during the Ottoman occupation of Greece.
    • \n
  • Odisha, India is occupied by the British under the British East India Company after the Second Anglo-Maratha War.
    • \n
  • Major breakthroughs in chemistry occur, with the identification of the elements rhodium, palladium, osmium, iridium, and cerium.
    • \n
