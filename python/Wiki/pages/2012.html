2012 (MMXII) was a leap year starting on Sunday (dominical letter AG) of the Gregorian calendar, the 2012th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 12th year of the 3rd millennium, the 12th year of the 21st century, and the 3rd year of the 2010s decade.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Iran\xe2\x80\x93European Union relations: The European Union adopts an embargo against Iran in protest of that nation\'s continued effort to enrich uranium.[3]
    • \n
\n

February[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The Diamond Jubilee of Queen Elizabeth II marks the 60th anniversary of her accession to the thrones of the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand, and the 60th anniversary of her becoming Head of the Commonwealth.[4][5]
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Iran suspends oil exports to Britain and France, following sanctions put in place by the European Union and the United States in January.[6]
    • \n
  • February 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Greek government-debt crisis: Eurozone finance ministers reach an agreement on a second, \xe2\x82\xac130-billion Greek bailout.[7]
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Arab Spring: As a result of continuing protests, Yemeni President Ali Abdullah Saleh is succeeded by his Vice President Abd Rabbuh Mansur Hadi.[8]
    • \n
\n

March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 A series of explosions are reported at a munitions dump in Brazzaville, the capital of the Republic of the Congo, with at least 250 people dead.[9][10]
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 After 244 years since its first publication, the Encyclop\xc3\xa6dia Britannica discontinues its print edition.[11]
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The President of Mali, Amadou Toumani Tour\xc3\xa9, is ousted in a coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat after mutinous soldiers attack government offices.[12]
    • \n
\n

April[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 The National Movement for the Liberation of Azawad unilaterally declares the independence of Azawad from Mali.[13]
    • \n
  • April 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Mutinous soldiers in Guinea-Bissau stage a coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat and take control of the capital city, Bissau. They arrest interim President Raimundo Pereira and leading presidential candidate Carlos Gomes J\xc3\xbanior in the midst of a presidential election campaign.[14]
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Kwangmy\xc5\x8fngs\xc5\x8fng-3, a North Korean Earth observation satellite, explodes shortly after launch. The United States and other countries had called the impending launch a violation of United Nations Security Council demands.[15] The launch was planned to mark the centenary of the birth of Kim Il-sung, the founder of the republic.[15]
    • \n
  • April 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Former Liberian President Charles Taylor is found guilty on 11 counts of aiding and abetting war crimes and crimes against humanity during the Sierra Leone Civil War.[16]
    • \n
\n

May[edit]

\n
    \n
  • May 2 \xe2\x80\x93 A pastel version of The Scream, by the Norwegian painter Edvard Munch, sells for US$120 million in a New York City auction, setting a new world record for an auctioned work of art.[17][18]
    • \n
  • May 12 \xe2\x80\x93 August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The 2012 World Expo takes place in Yeosu, South Korea.[19]
    • \n
  • May 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Tokyo Skytree, the tallest self-supporting tower in the world at 634 metres high, is opened to the public.[20]
    • \n
\n

June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • June 5\xe2\x80\x936 \xe2\x80\x93 The century\'s second and last solar transit of Venus occurs. The next pair are predicted to occur in 2117 and 2125.[21]
    • \n
  • June 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Shenzhou 9, a Chinese spacecraft carrying three Chinese astronauts, including the first-ever female, docks manually with orbiting module Tiangong-1, making them the third country, after the United States and Russia, to successfully perform the mission.[22]
    • \n
  • June 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Lonesome George, the last known individual of the Pinta Island tortoise subspecies, dies in Gal\xc3\xa1pagos National Park, thus making the subspecies extinct.[23]
    • \n
\n

July[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 CERN announces the discovery of a new particle with properties consistent with the Higgs boson after experiments at the Large Hadron Collider.[24][25][26][27][28]
    • \n
  • July 27 \xe2\x80\x93 August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The 2012 Summer Olympics are held in London, England, United Kingdom.[29]
    • \n
  • July 30\xe2\x80\x9331 \xe2\x80\x93 In the worst power outage in world history, the 2012 India blackouts leave 620 million people without power.[30][31][32]
    • \n
\n

August[edit]

\n
    \n
  • August 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Curiosity, the Mars Science Laboratory mission\'s rover, successfully lands on Mars.[33]
    • \n
  • August 31\n
      \n
    • Researchers successfully perform the first implantation of an early prototype bionic eye with 24 electrodes.[34]
      • \n
    • Armenia severs diplomatic relations with Hungary, following the extradition to Azerbaijan and subsequent pardoning of Ramil Safarov, who was convicted of killing an Armenian soldier in Hungary in 2004. The move is also met with fierce criticism from other countries.[35]
      • \n
    \n

September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Canada officially cuts diplomatic ties with Iran by closing its embassy in Tehran, and orders the expulsion of Iranian diplomats from Ottawa, over support for Syria, nuclear plans and human rights abuses.[36]
    • \n
  • September 11\xe2\x80\x9327 \xe2\x80\x93 A series of terrorist attacks are directed against United States diplomatic missions worldwide, as well as diplomatic missions of Germany, Switzerland and the United Kingdom. In the US, opinions are divided over whether the attacks are a reaction to a YouTube trailer for the film Innocence of Muslims. In Libya, among the dead is US ambassador J. Christopher Stevens.[37][38][39][40][41]
    • \n
\n

October[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Austrian skydiver Felix Baumgartner becomes the first person to break the sound barrier without any machine assistance during a record space dive out of the Red Bull Stratos helium-filled balloon from 24 miles (39 kilometers) over Roswell, New Mexico in the United States.[42][43][44]
    • \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven paintings worth $25 million are stolen from the Kunsthal in Rotterdam, the Netherlands.[45][46][47][48]
    • \n
  • October 24\xe2\x80\x9330 \xe2\x80\x93 Hurricane Sandy kills at least 209 people in the Caribbean, Bahamas, United States and Canada. Considerable storm surge damage causes major disruption to the eastern seaboard of the United States.[49][50][51]
    • \n
\n

November[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November 14\xe2\x80\x9321 \xe2\x80\x93 Israel launches Operation Pillar of Defense against the Palestinian-governed Gaza Strip, killing Hamas military chief Ahmed Jabari. In the following week 140 Palestinians and five Israelis are killed in an ensuing cycle of violence. A ceasefire between Israel and Hamas is announced by Egyptian Foreign Minister Mohamed Kamel Amr and US Secretary of State Hillary Clinton after the week-long escalation in hostilities in Southern Israel and the Gaza Strip.[52][53][54][55][56]
    • \n
  • November 25 \xe2\x80\x93 December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Typhoon Bopha, known as "Pablo" in the Philippines, kills at least 1,067 with around 838 people still missing. The typhoon causes considerable damage in the island of Mindanao.[57][58][59]
    • \n
  • November 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The UN General Assembly approves a motion granting Palestine non-member observer state status.[60]
    • \n
\n

December[edit]

    \n
  • December 8 \xe2\x80\x93 In Qatar, the UN Climate Change Conference agrees to extend the Kyoto Protocol until 2020.[61]
