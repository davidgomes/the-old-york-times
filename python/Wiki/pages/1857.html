1857 (MDCCCLVII) was a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Julian calendar, the 1857th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 857th year of the 2nd millennium, the 57th year of the 19th century, and the 8th year of the 1850s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1857 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The biggest Estonian newspaper Postimees is established by Johann Voldemar Jannsen.
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The partly French-owned London General Omnibus Company begins operating.[1]
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The 7.9 Mw Fort Tejon earthquake shakes Central and Southern California with a maximum Mercalli intensity of IX (Violent).
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The University of Calcutta is established in Calcutta as the first multidisciplinary modern university in South Asia. The University of Bombay is also established in Bombay, British India, this year.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The National Deaf Mute College (later renamed Gallaudet University) is established in Washington, D.C., becoming the first school for the advanced education of the deaf.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Federal Constitution of the United Mexican States promulgated.[2]
    • \n
  • March 3\n
      \n
    • France and the United Kingdom formally declare war on China in the Second Opium War.
      • \n
    • The largest slave auction in United States history, dubbed as \'The Weeping Time\'. Over a 2-day period, Pierce M. Butler sells 436 men, women, children, and infants, all of whom are kept in stalls meant for horses at a racetrack in Savannah, Georgia, for weeks beforehand.[3]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 4 \xe2\x80\x93 James Buchanan succeeds Franklin Pierce as President of the United States.
    • \n
  • March 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Dred Scott v. Sandford: The Supreme Court of the United States rules that Blacks are not citizens and slaves can not sue for freedom, driving the country further towards the American Civil War (the ruling is not overturned until the 14th Amendment in 1868).
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Elizabeth Blackwell opens a hospital, the New York Infirmary for Indigent Women and Children.
    • \n
  • March 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Elisha Otis\' first elevator is installed (at 488 Broadway, New York City).
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 End of the Anglo-Persian War.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Spirits\' Book (Le Livre des Esprits), one of the Five Fundamental Works of Spiritism, is published by French educator Allan Kardec.
    • \n
  • May 5\xe2\x80\x93October 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The Art Treasures of Great Britain exhibition is held in Manchester, one of the largest such displays of all time.[4]
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Indian Rebellion of 1857: The 3rd Light Cavalry of the British East India Company\'s army rebels against its British officers, thus beginning the rebellion.
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Indian Rebellion of 1857: Indian combatants capture Delhi from the East India Company.
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Sophia of Nassau marries the future King Oscar II of Sweden\xe2\x80\x93Norway.
    • \n
  • June 26 \xe2\x80\x93 At a ceremony in London, Queen Victoria awards the first sixty-six Victoria Crosses to British troops, for actions during the Crimean War.[1]
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 1\xe2\x80\x93November 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Indian Rebellion of 1857: Siege of Lucknow.
    • \n
  • July 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Indian Rebellion of 1857: The second massacre at Kanpur takes place.
    • \n
  • July 18\n
      \n
    • The Utah Expedition leaves Fort Leavenworth, effectively beginning the Utah War.
      • \n
    • Last use of prison hulks in the United Kingdom.[5]
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Dunbar wrecked near the entrance to Sydney Harbour, Australia, with the loss of 121 lives.
    • \n
  • August 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Matrimonial Causes Act makes divorce without parliamentary approval legally possible in the United Kingdom.[6]
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Panic of 1857 begins: Speculation in U.S. railroad shares and collapse on August 24 of the New York City branch of the Ohio Life Insurance and Trust Company following widespread embezzlement trigger a financial crisis which will extend to Europe.
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Mountain Meadows massacre in Utah.
    • \n
  • September 12 \xe2\x80\x93 The SS Central America sinks off the coast of North Carolina, killing 425 people.
    • \n
  • September 20 \xe2\x80\x93 In India, British forces recapture Delhi,[6] compelling the surrender of Bahadur Shah II, the last Mughal emperor.
    • \n
