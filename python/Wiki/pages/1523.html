Year 1523 (MDXXIII) was a common year starting on Thursday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April \xe2\x80\x93 Martin Luther helps Katharina von Bora, his eventual wife, escape from the Nimbschen convent by placing her and 12 other nuns in herring barrels.
    • \n
  • May \xe2\x80\x93 The Ningbo Incident: Two rival trade delegations from Japan feuded in the Chinese city of Ningbo which resulted in the pillage and plunder of the city.
    • \n
  • June 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Gustav Vasa is elected king of Sweden, finally establishing its full independence from Denmark, marking the end of the Kalmar Union.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Johann Esch and Heinrich Voes became the first Flemish Lutheran martyrs, burned at the stake by Roman Catholic authorities in Brussels.
    • \n
  • November 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Clement VII succeeds Pope Adrian VI as the 219th pope.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Ming Dynasty Chinese navy captures two Western ships with Portuguese breech\xe2\x80\x93loading culverins aboard, which the Chinese call a fo\xe2\x80\x93lang\xe2\x80\x93ji (Frankish culverin). According to the Ming Shi, these cannons are soon presented to the Jiajing Emperor by Wang Hong, and their design is copied in 1529. [1]
    • \n
  • Martin Luther\'s translation of the Pentateuch into German (Das allte Testament Deutsch) is published.
    • \n
  • Wijerd Jelckama, a Frisian warlord and military commander is executed in Leeuwarden. His death ends the successful Frisian rebellion fought by the Arumer Black Heap.
    • \n
\n
