1760 (MDCCLX) was a leap year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter FE) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Julian calendar, the 1760th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 760th year of the 2nd millennium, the 60th year of the 18th century, and the 1st year of the 1760s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1760 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Afghans defeat Marathas in Battle of Barari Ghat.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJune 4: Evangeline commemorates the Expulsion of the Acadians.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 At the Battle of Wandiwash in India, British general Sir Eyre Coote is victorious over the French under the Marquis de Bussy-Castelnau.[1]
    • \n
  • January 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Benning Wentworth creates the New Hampshire Grant of Pownal, Vermont.
    • \n
  • February 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The British Royal Navy ship HMS Royal Katherine runs aground off Bolt Head in England with the loss of 699 lives.
    • \n
  • February 21\xe2\x80\x9326 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 At the Battle of Carrickfergus in the north of Ireland, a force of French troops under the command of privateer Fran\xc3\xa7ois Thurot captures and holds the town and castle of Carrickfergus before retiring; the force is defeated (and Thurot killed) in a naval action in the Irish Sea on February 28.[2]
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 French and Indian War & Anglo-Cherokee War: Cherokee natives attack a North Carolina militia stationed at Fort Dobbs in the western part of the province. The attack is repelled by the militia under the command of General Hugh Waddell.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Fire of Boston, Massachusetts, destroys 349 buildings.
    • \n
  • May\xe2\x80\x93July \xe2\x80\x93 \'Tacky\'s War\', a slave rebellion, occurs in Jamaica.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Expulsion of the Acadians: New England planters arrive to claim land in Nova Scotia taken from the Acadians.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nWedding Supper at the wedding of Princess Isabella of Parma and Joseph II, Holy Roman Emperor, by Martin van Meytens
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • July 3 \xe2\x80\x93 A lightning strike causes a major fire at Portsmouth Royal Dockyard in England.[3][4]
    • \n
  • July 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 French and Indian War: Battle of Restigouche: British defeat French forces in the last naval battle in New France.
    • \n
  • July 19 \xe2\x80\x93 A formal request is made to the Spanish government as to allow the founding of the later city of Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico.
    • \n
  • July 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Warburg: The Anglo-Hanoverian army of Ferdinand of Brunswick storms Warburg, with a heroic role being played by the English commander Lord Granby.
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
\n
\nAugust 15: Frederick the Great.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • August 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The church (later cathedral) of Our Lady of Candlemas of Mayag\xc3\xbcez (Puerto Rico) is founded, establishing the basis for the founding of the city.
    • \n
  • August 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Legnica: By a series of brilliant maneuvers, Frederick the Great manages to defeat the Austrian army of Marshal Laudon before it can unite with that of Marshal Daun.
    • \n
  • September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War: Jeffery Amherst captures Montreal.[5]
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The town (later city) of Mayag\xc3\xbcez, Puerto Rico, is founded.
    • \n
  • October 5 The wedding of Princess Isabella of Parma and Joseph II, Holy Roman Emperor, at Hofburg Palace\'s Redoute Hall - (Redoutensaele) at the former imperial palace in Vienna.[6]
    • \n
  • October 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War: Russian troops enter Berlin.
    • \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Kloster-Kamp: Ferdinand of Brunswick is beaten back from the Rhine by a French army.
    • \n
  • October 25 \xe2\x80\x93 George II of Great Britain dies; his grandson George III ascends to the throne. He reigns until January 1820.
    • \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Seven Years\' War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Torgau: In another extremely hard battle, Frederick defeats Daun\'s Austrians, who withdraw across the Elbe.
    • \n
