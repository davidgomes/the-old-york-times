1811 (MDCCCXI) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1811th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 811th year of the 2nd millennium, the 11th year of the 19th century, and the 2nd year of the 1810s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1811 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 8 \xe2\x80\x93 An unsuccessful slave revolt is led by Charles Deslondes in St. Charles and St. James, Louisiana.
    • \n
  • January 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Casas Revolt begins in San Antonio, Texas.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 British Regency: George, Prince of Wales becomes Prince Regent because of the perceived insanity of his father, King George III of the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: Battle of the Gebora \xe2\x80\x93 An outnumbered French force under \xc3\x89douard Mortier routs and nearly destroys the Spanish near Badajoz in Spain.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Citadel Massacre in Cairo: Egyptian ruler Muhammad Ali kills the last Mamluk leaders.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: Battle of Barrosa, an unsuccessful French attack on a larger Anglo-Portuguese-Spanish force attempting to lift the Siege of C\xc3\xa1diz in Spain.
    • \n
  • March 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Lissa: The British fleet defeats the French.
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Commissioners\' Plan for Manhattan is presented.
    • \n
  • March 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Great Comet discovered by Honor\xc3\xa9 Flaugergues.
    • \n
  • March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Anholt: The British Navy defeats Denmark.
    • \n
  • March 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Henri Christophe is proclaimed King Henri I, turning the northern State of Haiti into the Kingdom of Haiti.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 5\xe2\x80\x936 \xe2\x80\x93 Revolutionary riots occur in Buenos Aires.
    • \n
  • May 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Paraguay declares independence from the Spanish Empire (recognised May 15).
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War Battle of Albuera: Spain, Portugal and Great Britain defeat the French.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Battle of Las Piedras is fought near Las Piedras, Banda Oriental, resulting in the first victory for the independentists of Uruguay.
    • \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Great Podil fire breaks out in Kiev, Ukraine.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Venezuela declares its Independence from the Spanish Empire.
    • \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The Russian Empire removes Anton II, Catholicos-Patriarch of All Georgia, from his office, placing a Russian-appointed bishop at the head of the Georgian church.
    • \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Italian scientist Amedeo Avogadro publishes his memoire about the molecular content of gases.
    • \n
  • July 14 \xe2\x80\x94 David Thompson reaches the mouth of the Columbia River, finding Fort Astoria under construction.
    • \n
  • July 30 \xe2\x80\x94 Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla is executed as a rebel by the Spanish government of New Spain.
    • \n
  • August 3 \xe2\x80\x93 First ascent of Jungfrau, third highest summit in the Bernese Alps,
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Nathan of Breslov leads the first annual Rosh Hashana kibbutz (pilgrimage) of Breslov Hasidim to the grave of Rabbi Nachman of Breslov in Uman, Ukraine.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Inventor John Stevens\' boat, the Juliana, begins operation as the first steam-powered ferry service, between New York City and Hoboken, New Jersey.
    • \n
  • October 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Jos\xc3\xa9 Gervasio Artigas and 16.000 orientales leave the Banda Oriental del Uruguay to go into exile.
    • \n
  • October 26 \xe2\x80\x93 The Argentine Government declares freedom of expression for the press.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 Luddite uprisings begin in northern England and the Midlands.
    • \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Tippecanoe: American troops led by William Henry Harrison defeat the Native American spiritual leader Tenskwatawa, also known as The Prophet (Chief Tecumseh\'s brother).
    • \n
  • November 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Jos\xc3\xa9 Miguel Carrera, Chilean founding father, is sworn in as President of the executive Junta of the government of Chile.
    • \n
  • December 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Reverend Samuel Marsden sends the first commercial shipment of wool from New South Wales to England.
    • \n
  • December 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The New Madrid earthquake in the Mississippi Valley near New Madrid reverses the course of the river for a while. Other earthquakes along the fault occur on January 23, 1812 and February 7, 1812.
    • \n
  • December 21 \xe2\x80\x93 First Constitution of Republic of Venezuela after declares its independence from Spain.
    • \n
