Year 1549 (MDXLIX) was a common year starting on Tuesday (link will display the full calendar) of the Julian calendar. In the Kingdom of England, it was known as "The Year of the Many-Headed Monster", because of the unusually high number of rebellions which occurred in the country.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January \xe2\x80\x93 Burmese\xe2\x80\x93Siamese War (1547\xe2\x80\x9349): King Tabinshwehti of Burma begins his invasion of the Ayutthaya Kingdom. This ends in retreat.
    • \n
  • February 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Burmese\xe2\x80\x93Siamese War: Burmese viceroy Thado Dhamma Yaza I of Prome slays Sri Suriyothai, queen consort of the Ayutthaya Kingdom on her war elephant when she intervenes in battle to protect the life of her husband.
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Salvador da Bahia, Brazil\'s first capital, is founded.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • June 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Book of Common Prayer introduced in English churches;[1] Prayer Book Rebellion against it breaks out in the West Country.[2]
    • \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Kett\'s Rebellion in East Anglia, against land enclosures;[2] rebellion in Oxfordshire against landowners associated with religious changes.[3]
    • \n
  • July 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Francis Xavier arrives in Japan.
    • \n
  • August 8\xe2\x80\x939 \xe2\x80\x93 England and France declare war.[4]
    • \n
  • August 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Sampford Courtenay in England: Prayer Book Rebellion quashed.
    • \n
  • August 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Dussindale in England: Kett\'s Rebellion quashed.[4]
    • \n
  • November 4 \xe2\x80\x93 The Pragmatic Sanction is proclaimed by Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor. The Seventeen Provinces are declared inseparable.
    • \n
  • November 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The lengthy and large Papal conclave, 1549\xe2\x80\x9350 goes into session in the Apostolic Palace in Rome to decide a successor to Pope Paul III.
    • \n
\n

Undated[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Peter Canisius starts the Counter-Reformation in Bavaria.
    • \n
  • The spire of Lincoln Cathedral in England is blown down[5] leaving St. Olaf\'s Church, Tallinn, in Estonia as the World\'s tallest structure.
    • \n
  • Although trade existed between the two beforehand, in this year the Portuguese begins to send regular seasonal maritime trade missions to Ming Dynasty China at Sao Jo\xc3\xa3o Island (also known as Shangchuan Island) near Macau.
    • \n
  • Excerpta antiqua published by Hervagius at Basel.
    • \n
