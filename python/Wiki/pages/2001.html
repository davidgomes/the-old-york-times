January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 11 \xe2\x80\x93 The U.S. Federal Trade Commission approves the merger of America Online and Time Warner to form AOL Time Warner.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 A 7.6 magnitude earthquake hits all of El Salvador, killing at least 800 people and leaving thousands homeless.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Wikipedia is founded.
    • \n
  • January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Impeachment proceedings against Philippine President Joseph Estrada, accused of playing Jueteng, end preeminently and trigger the second EDSA People Power Revolution or People Power II. His Vice-President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo succeeds him as the 14th President of the Republic.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 George W. Bush is sworn into office, succeeding Bill Clinton as President of the United States, over candidate contender Al Gore in the disputed U.S. presidential election, 2000.
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The Tiananmen Square self-immolation incident occurs.
    • \n
  • January 26 \xe2\x80\x93 An earthquake hits Gujarat, India, killing almost 20,000.
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 The Congressional Budget Office of the United States forecasts a $5,600,000,000,000 budget surplus for the next 10 years.[1]

June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • June 1\n
      \n
    • Crown Prince Dipendra of Nepal kills his father, the king, his mother and other members of the royal family with an assault rifle and then shoots himself in the Nepalese royal massacre. Dipendra dies June 4, as King of Nepal. His uncle Gyanendra accedes to the throne.
      • \n
    • A Hamas suicide bomber kills 21, mostly teenagers, in the Dolphinarium disco in Tel Aviv, Israel.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 5\xe2\x80\x939 \xe2\x80\x93 Tropical Storm Allison produces 36 inches (900 mm) of rain in Houston, killing 22, damaging the Texas Medical Center, and causing more than 5 billion American dollars of damage overall.
    • \n
  • June 7 \xe2\x80\x93 George W. Bush signs the Economic Growth and Tax Relief Reconciliation Act of 2001, the first tax cut of a series now known as the Bush tax cuts.
    • \n
  • June 11 \xe2\x80\x93 In Terre Haute, Indiana, Timothy McVeigh is executed for the Oklahoma City bombing.
    • \n
  • June 19 \xe2\x80\x93 A missile hits a soccer field in northern Iraq (Tel Afr County), killing 23 and wounding 11. According to U.S. officials, it was an Iraqi missile that malfunctioned.[3]
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Andrea Yates drowns all 5 of her young children in Houston, as a way to save them from Satan.
    • \n
  • June 21 \xe2\x80\x93 The world\'s longest train is set up by BHP Iron Ore and is recorded going between Newman and Port Hedland in Western Australia (a distance of 275 km, or 170 miles) and the train consists of 682 loaded iron ore wagons and 8 GE AC6000CW locomotives, giving a gross weight of almost 100,000 tonnes and moves 82,262 tonnes of ore; the train is 7.353 km (4.569 mi) long.
    • \n
  • June 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The 8.4 Mw southern Peru earthquake shakes coastal Peru with a maximum Mercalli intensity of VIII (Severe). A destructive tsunami followed, leaving at least 74 people dead, and 2,687 injured.
    • \n
