\n1738 (MDCCXXXVIII) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Sunday (dominical letter A) of the Julian calendar, the 1738th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 738th year of the 2nd millennium, the 38th year of the 18th century, and the 9th year of the 1730s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1738 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June

\n
    \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Court Jew Joseph S\xc3\xbc\xc3\x9f Oppenheimer is executed in W\xc3\xbcrttemberg.
    • \n
  • March/April \xe2\x80\x93 Emperor Iyasu II of Ethiopia is defeated by the Funj at the Battle of the Dindar River.
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Serse, an Italian opera by George Frideric Handel, premieres in London.
    • \n
  • May 24 \xe2\x80\x93 John Wesley experiences a spiritual rebirth at a Moravian Church meeting in Aldersgate in the City of London, essentially launching the Methodist movement; the day is celebrated annually by Methodists as Aldersgate Day. His younger brother Charles had a similar experience three days earlier.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December

\n
    \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Samuel Johnson composes his first solemn prayer (published 1785).
    • \n
  • November 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of Vienna is ratified, ending the War of the Polish Succession. Under the terms of the treaty, Stanis\xc5\x82aw Leszczy\xc5\x84ski receives Lorraine in exchange for renouncing the Polish throne.
    • \n
  • Specific date unknown: Carl Philipp Emanuel Bach - having completed a law degree - was hired as a court musician by Crown Prince Frederick of Prussia, the future Frederick the Great. Bach was to remain in Frederick\'s service until 1768.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown

\n
    \n
  • Russo\xe2\x80\x93Turkish War (1735\xe2\x80\x931739): The Russian army fails to cross the Dniester. They are decimated by plague.
    • \n
  • China\'s Qing government announces that all western businessmen have to use the Cohong in Guangzhou to trade.
    • \n
  • The excavation of Herculaneum, a Roman city buried by Vesuvius in AD 79, begins.
    • \n
  • Pierre Louis Maupertuis publishes Sur la figure de la terre, which confirms Newton\'s view that the earth is an oblate spheroid slightly flattened at the poles.
    • \n
  • Jacques de Vaucanson presents the world\'s first automaton, The Flute Player (1737) to the French Academy of Sciences.
    • \n
  • Black Forest clockmaker Franz Ketterer produces one of the earliest cuckoo clocks.
    • \n
  • Holy Royal Arch is founded.
    • \n
