1966 (MCMLXVI) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar, the 1966th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 966th year of the 2nd millennium, the 66th year of the 20th century, and the 7th year of the 1960s decade.

Events[edit]

January[edit]

\n
Main article: January 1966
\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 In a coup, Colonel Jean-B\xc3\xa9del Bokassa takes over as military ruler of the Central African Republic, ousting President David Dacko.
    • \n
  • January 2 \xe2\x80\x93 A strike of public transportation workers in New York City begins (it would end January 13).
    • \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Acid Test is conducted at the Fillmore, San Jose.
    • \n
  • January 4\n
      \n
    • A military coup occurs in Upper Volta (later Burkina Faso).
      • \n
    • The prime ministers of India and Pakistan meet in Moscow.
      • \n
    • A gas leak fire at the Feyzin oil refinery near Lyon, France, kills 18 and injures 84.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 10\n
      \n
    • Pakistani\xe2\x80\x93Indian peace negotiations end successfully in Tashkent. Indian prime minister Lal Bahadur Shastri dies the next day.
      • \n
    • The French paper L\'Express publishes a story by Georges Figon, who took part in the kidnapping of Mehdi Ben Barka.
      • \n
    • Georgia House of Representatives refuses to seat Julian Bond.
      • \n
    • Home of civil rights activist Vernon Dahmer in Hattiesburg, Mississippi, is firebombed. Dahmer\'s family escapes but he dies the next day from severe burns. (White Knights of the Ku Klux Klan Imperial Wizard Samuel Bowers will be unsuccessfully tried for this murder on four occasions, and then convicted in 1998.)
      • \n
    • Commonwealth Prime Ministers\' Conference convenes in Lagos, Nigeria.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 11\n
      \n
    • A conference on Rhodesia begins in Lagos, Nigeria.
      • \n
    • The first SR-71 Blackbird spy plane goes into service at Beale AFB.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 12 \xe2\x80\x93 United States President Lyndon Johnson states that the United States should stay in South Vietnam until Communist aggression there is ended.
    • \n
  • January 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Robert C. Weaver becomes the first African-American Cabinet member, by being appointed United States Secretary of Housing and Urban Development.
    • \n
  • January 15 \xe2\x80\x93 A bloody military coup is staged in Nigeria, deposing the civilian government.
    • \n
  • January 17\n
      \n
    • The Nigerian coup is overturned by another faction of the military, leaving a military government in power. This is the beginning of a long period of military rule.
      • \n
    • A B-52 bomber collides with a KC-135 Stratotanker over Spain, dropping three 70-kiloton hydrogen bombs near the town of Palomares, and one into the sea, in the 1966 Palomares B-52 crash.
      • \n
    • Carl Brashear, the first African-American United States Navy diver, is involved in an accident during the recovery of a lost H-bomb which results in the amputation of his leg.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 18\n
      \n
    • French police announce that Georges Figon has committed suicide, prior to his arrest for the kidnapping of Mehdi Ben Barka.
      • \n
    • About 8,000 U.S. soldiers land in South Vietnam; U.S. troops now total 190,000.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Indira Gandhi is elected Prime Minister of India; she is sworn in January 24.
    • \n
  • January 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Demonstrations occur against high food prices in Hungary.
    • \n
  • January 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Italian Prime Minister Aldo Moro resigns due to a power struggle in his party.
    • \n
  • January 22\n
      \n
    • The military government of Nigeria announces that ex-prime minister Abubakar Tafawa Balewa was killed during the coup.
      • \n
    • The Chadian Muslim insurgent group FROLINAT is founded in Sudan, starting the Chadian Civil War.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Air India Flight 101 crashes into Mont Blanc, killing all 117 persons on board, including Dr. Homi J. Bhabha, chairman of the Indian Atomic Energy Commission.
    • \n
  • January 26\n
      \n
    • Harold Holt becomes Prime Minister of Australia when Robert Menzies retires.
      • \n
    • Beaumont children disappearance: Three children disappear on their way to Glenelg, South Australia, never to be seen again.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 27\n
      \n
    • The British government promises the U.S. that British troops in Malaysia will stay until more peaceful conditions occur in the region.
      • \n
    • Britain\'s Labour Party unexpectedly retains the parliamentary seat of Hull North in a by-election, with a swing of 4.5% to their candidate from the opposition Conservatives, and a majority up from 1,181 at the 1964 General Election to 5,351.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The first of 608 performances of Sweet Charity opens at the Palace Theatre in New York City.
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Kingdom ceases all trade with Rhodesia.
    • \n

Main article: February 1966
\n
    \n
  • February 1 \xe2\x80\x93 West Germany procures some 2,600 political prisoners from East Germany.
    • \n

    • \n
  • February 4 \xe2\x80\x93 All Nippon Airways Flight 60 plunges into Tokyo Bay; 133 are killed.
    • \n
  • February 6 - The TV series Mister Ed airs its final episode (ran 1961\xe2\x80\x9366).
    • \n
  • February 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Lyndon Johnson of the United States and Nguy\xe1\xbb\x85n Cao K\xe1\xbb\xb3 of South Vietnam convene with other officials in Honolulu, Hawaii to discuss the course of the Vietnam War.[1]
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The National Hockey League announces it will expand to 12 teams for the 1967 season.
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Soviet writers Yuli Daniel and Andrei Sinyavsky are sentenced to five and seven years, respectively, for "anti-Soviet" writings.
    • \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Vietnam War: The T\xc3\xa2y Vinh Massacre occurs.
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Australian dollar is introduced at a rate of 2 dollars per pound, or 10 shillings per dollar.
    • \n
  • February 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The naval minister of the United Kingdom, Christopher Mayhew, resigns.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 While Soviet author and translator Valery Tarsis is abroad, the Soviet Union negates his citizenship.
    • \n
  • February 23 \xe2\x80\x93 An intra-party military coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat in Syria replaces the previous government of Amin al-Hafiz by one led by Salah Jadid.
    • \n
  • February 24 \xe2\x80\x93 A coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat led by the police and military of Ghana raises the National Liberation Council to power while president Kwame Nkrumah is abroad.
    • \n
  • February 26\n
      \n
    • G\xc3\xb2 D\xc3\xa0i massacre: Vietnam War.
      • \n
    • A curfew is declared in Jakarta, Indonesia.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 28\n
      \n
    • British Prime Minister Harold Wilson calls a General Election in the United Kingdom, to be held on 31 March.
      • \n
    • U.S. astronauts Charles Bassett and Elliot See are killed in an aircraft accident in St. Louis, Missouri.
      • \n

March[edit]

\n
Main article: March 1966
\n
    \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 The DKW automobile ceases production in Germany.[2]
    • \n
  • March 1\n
      \n
    • The British Government announces plans for the decimalisation of the pound sterling (hitherto denominated in 20 shillings and 240 pence to the \xc2\xa3), to come into force in February 1971 (Decimal Day).
      • \n
    • Soviet space probe Venera 3 crashes on Venus, becoming the first spacecraft to land on another planet\'s surface.
      • \n
    • The Ba\'ath Party takes power in Syria.
      • \n
    • The Frost Report, which launched the television careers of John Cleese, Ronnie Barker and Ronnie Corbett and also the careers of other writers and performers, is first broadcast on BBC.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Kwame Nkrumah arrives in Guinea and is granted asylum.
    • \n
  • March 4\n
      \n
    • Canadian Pacific Air Lines Flight 402 crashes during a night landing in poor visibility at Tokyo International Airport in Japan, killing 64 of 72 persons on board.
      • \n
    • In an interview with London Evening Standard reporter Maureen Cleave, John Lennon of The Beatles states that they are "more popular than Jesus now".
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 5\n
      \n
    • BOAC Flight 911 crashes in severe clear-air turbulence over Mount Fuji soon after taking off from Tokyo International Airport in Japan, killing all 124 people on board.
      • \n
    • A massive theft of nuclear materials is revealed in Brazil.
      • \n
    • Merci, Ch\xc3\xa9rie by Udo J\xc3\xbcrgens (music by Udo J\xc3\xbcrgens, lyrics by Udo J\xc3\xbcrgens and Thomas H\xc3\xb6rbiger) wins the Eurovision Song Contest 1966 for Austria.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles de Gaulle asks U.S. President Lyndon B. Johnson for negotiations about the state of NATO equipment in France.
    • \n
  • March 8\n
      \n
    • Anti-communist demonstrations occur at the Indonesian Foreign Ministry.
      • \n
    • Vietnam War: The U.S. announces it will substantially increase the number of its troops in Vietnam.
      • \n
    • Nelson\'s Pillar in O\'Connell Street, Dublin, is clandestinely blown up by former Irish Republican Army volunteers marking this year\'s 50th anniversary of the Easter Rising.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Ronnie, one of the Kray twins, shoots George Cornell (an associate of rivals The Richardson Gang) dead at The Blind Beggar pub in Whitechapel, east London, a crime for which he is finally convicted in 1969.
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Crown Princess Beatrix of the Netherlands marries Claus von Amsberg. Some spectators demonstrate against the groom because he is German.
    • \n
  • March 11\n
      \n
    • Transition to the New Order in Indonesia: President Sukarno gives all executive powers to General Suharto by signing the "Supersemar" order.
      • \n
    • French President Charles de Gaulle states that French troops will be taken out of NATO and that all French NATO bases and HQ\'s must be closed within a year.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Bobby Hull of the Chicago Blackhawks sets the National Hockey League single season scoring record against the New York Rangers with his 51st goal.
    • \n
  • March 16 \xe2\x80\x93 NASA spacecraft Gemini 8 (David Scott, Neil Armstrong) conducts the first docking in space, with an Agena target vehicle.
    • \n
  • Paul Van Doren established the Vans shoe company in California.
    • \n
  • March 17\n
      \n
    • More anti-communist demonstrations occur in Indonesia.
      • \n
    • Off the Mediterranean coast of Spain, the United States Navy submersible DSV Alvin finds a missing U.S. hydrogen bomb.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The Texas Western Miners defeat the Kentucky Wildcats with five African-American starters, ushering in desegregation in athletic recruiting.
    • \n
  • March 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Football\'s Jules Rimet Trophy is stolen while on exhibition in London; it is found seven days later by a mongrel dog named "Pickles" and his owner David Corbett, wrapped in newspaper in a south London garden.
    • \n
  • March 22 \xe2\x80\x93 In Washington, D.C., General Motors President James M. Roche appears before a Senate subcommittee, and apologizes to consumer advocate Ralph Nader for the company\'s intimidation and harassment campaign against him.
    • \n
  • March 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Paul VI and Michael Ramsey, the Archbishop of Canterbury, meet in Rome.
    • \n
  • March 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Demonstrations are held across the United States against the Vietnam War.
    • \n
  • March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 In South Vietnam, 20,000 Buddhists march in demonstrations against the policies of the military government.
    • \n
  • March 28\n
      \n
    • Cevdet Sunay becomes the fifth president of Turkey.
      • \n
    • Indira Gandhi visits Washington, D.C.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The 23rd Communist Party Conference is held in the Soviet Union; Leonid Brezhnev demands that U.S. troops leave Vietnam, and announces that Chinese-Soviet relations are not satisfactory.
    • \n
  • March 31\n
      \n
    • The British Labour Party led by Harold Wilson wins the United Kingdom General Election, gaining a 96-seat majority (compared with a single seat majority when the election was called on February 28).[3]
      • \n
    • The Soviet Union launches Luna 10, which later becomes the first space probe to enter orbit around the Moon.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
