2002 (MMII) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar, the 2002nd year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 2nd year of the 3rd millennium, the 2nd year of the 21st century, and the 3rd year of the 2000s decade.

\n

    • \n
  • Year of the Outback in Australia
    • \n
  • National Science Year in the United Kingdom
    • \n
  • Autism Awareness Year in the United Kingdom

Events[edit]

January[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1\n
      \n
    • The Open Skies mutual surveillance treaty, initially signed in 1992, officially enters into force.
      • \n
    • Euro notes and coins are introduced in France, Spain, Germany, Italy, Portugal, Greece, Finland, Luxembourg, Belgium, Austria, Ireland and the Netherlands.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • January 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The UN Security Council unanimously establishes an arms embargo and freezes the assets of Osama bin Laden, al-Qaeda, and the Taliban.
    • \n
  • January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The eruption of Mount Nyiragongo in the Democratic Republic of the Congo displaces an estimated 400,000 people.
    • \n
  • January 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Sierra Leone government - with British assistance, Guinean air support and US logistical support - defeats the Revolutionary United Front, bringing the Sierra Leone Civil War to a conclusion.
    • \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Wall Street Journal reporter Daniel Pearl is kidnapped in Pakistan, accused of being a CIA agent by his captors.
    • \n
  • January 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Lagos armoury explosion: Several explosions at a military dump in Lagos, Nigeria kill more than 1,000.
    • \n
  • January 31 \xe2\x80\x93 A large section of the Antarctic Larsen Ice Shelf begins disintegrating, consuming about 3,250 km (2,020 mi) over 35 days.
