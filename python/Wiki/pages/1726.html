\n1726 (MDCCXXVI) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1726th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 726th year of the 2nd millennium, the 26th year of the 18th century, and the 7th year of the 1720s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1726 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Supreme Privy Council is established in Russia
    • \n
  • April 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Isaac Newton tells William Stukeley the story of how he developed his theory of gravity.
    • \n
  • May 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Voltaire begins exile in England.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Andr\xc3\xa9-Hercule Cardinal de Fleury, recalled from exile by King Louis XV of France, banishes Louis Henri, Duke of Bourbon, and Madame de Prie from court.
    • \n
  • August 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Pirate Nicholas Brown captured near Xtabi, Jamaica
    • \n
  • October 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Jonathan Swift\'s satirical novel Gulliver\'s Travels is first published (anonymously) in London; it sells out within a week.
    • \n
  • November \xe2\x80\x93 Mary Toft allegedly gives birth to 16 rabbits in England; the story is later revealed to be a hoax.
    • \n
  • December 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of Montevideo is founded by the Spaniards.
    • \n
