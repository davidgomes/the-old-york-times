1831 (MDCCCXXXI) was a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Thursday (dominical letter D) of the Julian calendar, the 1831st year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 831st year of the 2nd millennium, the 31st year of the 19th century, and the 2nd year of the 1830s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1831 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 William Lloyd Garrison begins publishing The Liberator, an antislavery newspaper, in Boston, Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • January 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hunchback of Notre-Dame is first published by Victor Hugo.
    • \n
  • February\xe2\x80\x93March \xe2\x80\x93 Revolts in Modena, Parma and the Papal States are put down by Austrian troops.
    • \n
  • February 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Pope Gregory XVI succeeds Pope Pius VIII as the 254th pope.
    • \n
  • February 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Belgian Constitution of 1831 approved by the National Congress
    • \n
  • February 14 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Debre Abbay: Ras Marye of Yejju marches into Tigray and defeats and kills the warlord Sabagadis.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Olszynka Grochowska (Grochow): Polish rebel forces divide a Russian army.
    • \n
  • March 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The French Foreign Legion is founded.
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Bosnian Uprising against the Ottoman Empire begins.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Pedro I abdicates as Emperor of Brazil in favor of his 5-year-old son Pedro II, who will reign for almost 59 years.
    • \n
  • April 18 \xe2\x80\x93 University of Alabama founded.
    • \n
  • April 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles Albert becomes king of Sardinia after the death of King Charles Felix.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ostro\xc5\x82\xc4\x99ka: The Poles fight another indecisive battle.
    • \n
  • May\xe2\x80\x93June \xe2\x80\x93 Merthyr Rising: Coal miners and others riot in Merthyr Tydfil, Wales for improved working conditions.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 British Royal Navy officer James Clark Ross locates the position of the North Magnetic Pole on the Boothia Peninsula.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJuly 21: Coronation of Leopold I of Belgium
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • c.July 15 \xe2\x80\x93 The volcanic island of Graham Island briefly emerges in the Mediterranean.
    • \n
  • July 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Leopold of Saxe-Coburg-Gotha is inaugurated as first King of the Belgians in Brussels.
    • \n
  • August 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The Dutch Ten Days\' Campaign against Belgium is halted by a French army.
    • \n
  • August 7 \xe2\x80\x93 American Baptist minister William Miller preaches his first sermon on the Second Advent of Christ in Dresden, New York, launching the Advent Movement in the United States.
    • \n
  • August 21 \xe2\x80\x93 United States: Nat Turner\'s slave rebellion breaks out in Southampton County, Virginia.
    • \n
  • September 6\xe2\x80\x93September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Warsaw: The Russians take the Polish capital and crush resistance.
    • \n
  • September 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The coronation of King William IV of the United Kingdom who succeeded his brother George IV. He would reign until 1837
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • November 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Slave trading is forbidden in Brazil.
    • \n
  • November 22 \xe2\x80\x93 After a bloody battle with the military causing 600 casualties, rebellious silk workers seize Lyon, France, beginning the First Canut Revolt.
    • \n
  • December 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Charles Darwin embarks on his historic voyage aboard HMS Beagle.
    • \n
  • December 31 \xe2\x80\x93 Gramercy Park is deeded to New York City.
    • \n
