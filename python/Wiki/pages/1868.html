1868 (MDCCCLXVIII) was a leap year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter ED) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Monday (dominical letter GF) of the Julian calendar, the 1868th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 868th year of the 2nd millennium, the 68th year of the 19th century, and the 9th year of the 1860s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1868 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJanuary 3: Emperor Meiji.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • January 2 \xe2\x80\x93 British Expedition to Abyssinia: Robert Napier leads an expedition to free captive British officials and missionaries.[1]
    • \n
  • January 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The 15-year-old Mutsuhito, Emperor Meiji of Japan, declares the "Meiji Restoration", his own restoration to full power, under the influence of supporters from the Ch\xc5\x8dsh\xc5\xab and Satsuma Domains and against the supporters of the Tokugawa shogunate, triggering the Boshin War.[2][3]
    • \n
  • January 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Paraguayan War: Brazilian Army commander Lu\xc3\xads Alves de Lima e Silva, Duke of Caxias enters Asunci\xc3\xb3n, Paraguay\'s capital. Some days later he declares the war is over. Nevertheless, Francisco Solano L\xc3\xb3pez, Paraguay\'s president, prepares guerrillas to fight in the countryside.
    • \n
  • January 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Arkansas constitutional convention meets in Little Rock.
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Penal transportation from Britain to Australia ends with arrival of the convict ship Hougoumont in Western Australia after an 89-day voyage from England. There are 62 Fenians among the transportees.
    • \n
  • January 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Shogun Tokugawa Yoshinobu declares the emperor\'s declaration "illegal" and prepares to attack Kyoto.
    • \n
  • January 27\xe2\x80\x9331 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Toba\xe2\x80\x93Fushimi: forces of the Tokugawa shogunate and the allied pro-Imperial forces of the Ch\xc5\x8dsh\xc5\xab, Satsuma and Tosa Domains clash near Fushimi, Kyoto, ending in a decisive victory for the Imperial forces (although in the January 28 naval Battle of Awa the Shogunate is victorious against Satsuma).
    • \n
  • February \xe2\x80\x93 Foreign ministers meeting in Hy\xc5\x8dgo are persuaded to recognise the restored Emperor Meiji of Japan with promises that harbours will be open in accordance with international treaties.[4]
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 The British War Office sanctions the formation of what becomes the Army Post Office Corps.
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 In New York City the Jolly Corks organization is renamed the Benevolent and Protective Order of Elks (BPOE).
    • \n
  • February 24\n
      \n
    • Impeachment of Andrew Johnson: Three days after his action to dismiss United States Secretary of War Edwin M. Stanton, the United States House of Representatives votes 126 to 47 in favor of a resolution to impeach Andrew Johnson, President of the United States, the first of two Presidents to be impeached by the full House. Johnson is later acquitted by the United States Senate.
      • \n
    • The first parade to have floats takes place at Mardi Gras in New Orleans.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • March \xe2\x80\x93 French geologist Louis Lartet discovers the first identified skeletons of Cro-Magnon, the first early modern humans (early Homo sapiens sapiens), at Abri de Cr\xc3\xb4-Magnon, a rock shelter at Les Eyzies, Dordogne, France.
    • \n
  • March 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Alfred, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha, Duke of Edinburgh, is shot in the back in Sydney, Australia, at a fundraising event for the Sydney Sailors Home by Irishman Henry James O\'Farrell. The prince survives and quickly recovers; O\'Farrell is executed on April 21 despite attempts by the prince to gain clemency for him.
    • \n
  • March 23 \xe2\x80\x93 The University of California is founded in Oakland, California, when the Organic Act is signed into California law.
    • \n
  • March 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Metropolitan Life Insurance Company is formed.
    • \n
  • March 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The Lake Ontario Shore Railroad Company is organized in Oswego, New York.
    • \n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Hampton Normal and Agricultural Institute is established in Hampton, Virginia.
    • \n
  • April 7 \xe2\x80\x93 The Charter Oath, drawn up by his councilors, is promulgated at the enthronement of the Emperor Meiji of Japan, promising deliberative assemblies and an end to feudalism.[5]
    • \n
  • April 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Emperor Tewodros II of Ethiopia massacres at least 197 of his own people at Magdala. These are prisoners incarcerated, for the most part, for very trivial offenses, and are killed for requesting bread and water.
    • \n
  • April 9\xe2\x80\x93April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Magdala: A British-Indian task force under Robert Napier inflicts 700 deaths and a crushing defeat on the army of Emperor Tewodros II; the British and Indians suffer 30 wounded, 2 of whom die subsequently. Tewodros commits suicide and Magdala is captured, ending the British Expedition to Abyssinia.
    • \n
  • April 11\xe2\x80\x93July \xe2\x80\x93 Fall of Edo: the Japanese city is surrendered to the Emperor Meiji. The Shogun Tokugawa Yoshinobu submits to the Emperor.
    • \n
  • April 29 \xe2\x80\x93 General William Tecumseh Sherman brokers the Treaty of Fort Laramie between the federal government of the United States and the Plains Indians.
    • \n
  • May 10\xe2\x80\x9314 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Utsunomiya Castle (Boshin War) in Japan: forces of the Emperor Meiji resist the retreating troops of the Tokugawa shogunate.
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 President Andrew Johnson is acquitted during his impeachment trial, by one vote in the United States Senate.
    • \n
  • May 26 \xe2\x80\x93 Fenian bomber Michael Barrett becomes the last person publicly hanged in the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • May 29 \xe2\x80\x93 The Parliament of the United Kingdom passes the Capital Punishment Amendment Act, thus ending public hanging.
    • \n
  • May 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Memorial Day is observed in the United States for the first time (it was proclaimed on May 5 by General John A. Logan).
    • \n
  • May 31\n
      \n
    • Thomas Spence declares himself president of the Republic of Manitobah; he soon alienates the locals.
      • \n
    • The first popular bicycle race is held at Parc de Saint-Cloud, Paris.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 2 \xe2\x80\x93 The first Trades Union Congress is held in Manchester, England.
    • \n
  • June \xe2\x80\x93 Titokowaru\'s War breaks out in the South Taranaki District of New Zealand\'s North Island between the Ng\xc4\x81ti Ruanui M\xc4\x81ori tribe and the New Zealand Government.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Fort Fred Steele is established to protect what is at this time the western terminus of the Union Pacific Railway, near the modern-day Sinclair, Wyoming.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nJuly 25: Wyoming Territory.
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • July 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The cable-operated West Side and Yonkers Patent Railway in Manhattan becomes the first elevated railway in the United States.
    • \n
  • July 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ueno: Imperial Japanese troops defeat the Sh\xc5\x8dgitai (forces remaining loyal to the Shogun).
    • \n
  • July 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Preacher William Booth establishes the Christian Mission, predecessor of The Salvation Army, in the East End of London.
    • \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution is ratified.
    • \n
  • July 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The Navajo people begin their long march home.
    • \n
  • July 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Wyoming becomes a United States territory.
    • \n
  • July 25 \xe2\x80\x93 the Allies in an amphibious operation capture the fortress of Humait\xc3\xa1 in the Paraguayan War.
    • \n
  • July 27 \xe2\x80\x93 The United States Expatriation Act ("An Act concerning the Rights of American Citizens in foreign States") is adopted.[6]
    • \n
  • July 28 \xe2\x80\x93 The Fourteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution is adopted, including the Citizenship Clause and the Equal Protection Clause, legally, if not actually, guaranteeing African Americans full citizenship and equal protection and all persons in the United States due process of law.
    • \n
  • August 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Arica earthquake in southern Peru (at this time) with an estimated magnitude between 8.5 and 9.0 kills around 25,000 with the resultant tsunami recorded on the far side of the Pacific Ocean.[7][8]
    • \n
  • August 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The element later named as helium is first detected in the spectrum of the Sun\'s chromosphere by French astronomer Jules Janssen during a total eclipse in Guntur, British India, but is assumed to be sodium.[9]
    • \n
  • August 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Abergele rail disaster in Wales: An Irish Mail passenger train collides with 4 cargo trucks loaded with paraffin: 33 are killed (the first major train disaster in Britain).
    • \n
  • August 22 \xe2\x80\x93 The Yangzhou riot in China targets a station of the China Inland Mission, and nearly leads to war between Britain and China.
    • \n
  • September \xe2\x80\x93 Glorious Revolution: Queen Isabella II of Spain is effectively deposed and sent into exile; she formally abdicates on June 25, 1870.
    • \n
  • September 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Emperor Meiji of Japan announces that the name of the city of Edo is to be changed to Tokyo.
    • \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Titokowaru\'s War: M\xc4\x81ori leader Titokowaru defeats a New Zealand military force at Te Ngutu o Te Manu, North Island.
    • \n
  • September 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The University of the South holds its first convocation in Sewanee, Tennessee.
    • \n
  • September 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Grito de Lares: Rebels (some 400\xe2\x80\x93600 led by Ram\xc3\xb3n Emeterio Betances) in the town of Lares declare Puerto Rico independent; the local militia easily defeats them a week later.
    • \n
  • September 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Croatian\xe2\x80\x93Hungarian Settlement (Croatian: Hrvatsko-ugarska nagodba, Hungarian: Horv\xc3\xa1t\xe2\x80\x93magyar kiegyez\xc3\xa9s, German: Kroatisch-Ungarischer Ausgleich) is concluded, governing Croatia\'s political status in the Hungarian-ruled part of Austria-Hungary until 1918.[10]
    • \n

    • \n

    • \n

    • \n
  • November 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Time zone: New Zealand officially adopts a standard time to be observed nationally.

    • \n
  • November 27 \xe2\x80\x93 American Indian Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Washita River: In the early morning, United States Army Lieutenant Colonel George Armstrong Custer leads an attack on a band of Cheyenne living on reservation land with Chief Black Kettle, killing 103 Cheyenne.

    • \n
  • December 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Paraguayan War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Ytoror\xc3\xb3 or Ytoror\xc3\xb3: Field-Marshal Lu\xc3\xads Alves de Lima e Silva, Duke of Caxias leads 13,000 Brazilian troops against a Paraguayan fortified position of 5,000 troops.
    • \n
  • December 9 \xe2\x80\x93 The world\'s first traffic signal lights are installed at the junction of Great George Street and Bridge Street in the London Borough of Westminster.[12]
    • \n
  • December 25 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. President Andrew Johnson grants unconditional pardon to all Civil War rebels.
