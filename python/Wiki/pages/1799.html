\n1799 (MDCCXCIX) was a common year starting on Tuesday (dominical letter F) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1799th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 799th year of the 2nd millennium, the 99th year of the 18th century, and the 10th and last year of the 1790s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1799 is 11 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 English Prime Minister William Pitt the Younger introduces an income tax of two shillings to the pound to raise funds for Great Britain\'s war effort in the Napoleonic Wars.
    • \n
  • February 28 \xe2\x80\x93 Action of 28 February 1799 (French Revolutionary Wars) British Royal Navy frigate HMS Sybille defeats the French frigate Forte off the mouth of the Hooghly River in the Bay of Bengal, but both captains are killed.
    • \n
  • March 1 \xe2\x80\x93 Federalist James Ross becomes President pro tempore of the United States Senate.
    • \n
  • March 3 \xe2\x80\x93 The Russo-Ottoman siege of Corfu ends with the surrender of the French garrison.
    • \n
  • March 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon captures Jaffa in Palestine and his troops proceed to kill more than 2,000 Albanian captives.
    • \n
  • March 29 \xe2\x80\x93 New York passes a law aimed at gradually abolishing slavery in the state.
    • \n
  • May 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Seringapatam: Tipu Sultan is defeated and killed by the British.
    • \n
  • May 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Acre ends after two months: Napoleon\'s attempt to widen his Middle Eastern campaign into Syria is frustrated by Ottoman forces, and he withdraws to Egypt.
    • \n
  • May 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Winterthur: Habsburg forces secure control of north-east Switzerland from the French Army of the Danube.
    • \n
  • June 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Action of 18 June 1799 (French Revolutionary Wars): a French frigate squadron under Rear-admiral Perr\xc3\xa9e is captured by the British fleet under Lord Keith off Toulon.
    • \n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
\n
\n
\n
\nThe Rosetta Stone
\n
\n
\n
    \n
  • July 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Ranjit Singh\'s men take their positions outside Lahore.
    • \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Ranjit Singh captures Lahore from the Bhangi Misl, a key step in establishing the Sikh Empire and becoming Maharaja of the Punjab.
    • \n
  • July 15 \xe2\x80\x93 In the Egyptian port city of Rosetta, French Captain Pierre Bouchard finds the Rosetta Stone.
    • \n
  • July 25 \xe2\x80\x93 At Aboukir in Egypt, Napoleon defeats 10,000 Ottoman Mamluk troops under Mustafa Pasha.
    • \n
  • August 27 \xe2\x80\x93 War of the Second Coalition: Anglo-Russian invasion of Holland \xe2\x80\x93 Britain and Russia send an expedition to the Batavian Republic.
    • \n
  • August 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-Russian invasion of Holland: Vlieter Incident: A squadron of the Batavian Republic\'s navy, commanded by Rear-Admiral Samuel Story, surrenders to the British Royal Navy under Sir Ralph Abercromby and Admiral Sir Charles Mitchell near Wieringen without joining action.
    • \n
  • October 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-Russian invasion of Holland: Battle of Castricum \xe2\x80\x93 Franco-Dutch forces defeat the Russo-British expedition force.
    • \n
  • October 9 \xe2\x80\x93 HMS Lutine (a famous treasure wreck) is sunk in the West Frisian Islands.
    • \n
  • October 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Jeanne Genevi\xc3\xa8ve Labrosse becomes the first woman to jump from a balloon with a parachute, from an altitude of 900 meters.
    • \n
  • October 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Action of 16 October 1799: A Spanish treasure convoy worth more than \xc2\xa36,000,000,000,000 is captured by the British Royal Navy off Vigo.
    • \n
  • October 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Anglo-Russian invasion of Holland: Anglo-Russian expedition forces surrender in North Holland.
    • \n
  • November 9 (Coup of 18 Brumaire) \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon overthrows the French Directory in a coup d\'\xc3\xa9tat.
    • \n
  • November 10 (19 Brumaire) \xe2\x80\x93 A remnant of the Council of Ancients in France abolishes the Constitution of the Year III, and ordains the French Consulate with Napoleon as First Consul with the Constitution of the Year VIII.
    • \n
  • December 14 \xe2\x80\x93 George Washington, the first President of the United States, dies at Mount Vernon, Virginia.
    • \n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • The Place Royale in Paris is renamed Place des Vosges when the Department of Vosges becomes the first to pay new Revolutionary taxes.
    • \n
  • Eli Whitney, holding a 1798 United States government contract for the manufacture of muskets, is introduced by Oliver Wolcott, Jr. to the concept of interchangeable parts, an origin of the American system of manufacturing.[1]
    • \n
  • The small town of Tignish, Prince Edward Island, Canada is founded.
    • \n
  • 12-year-old Conrad John Reed finds what he describes as a "heavy yellow rock" along Little Meadow Creek in Cabarrus County, North Carolina and makes it a doorstop in his home. Conrad\'s father John Reed learns that the rock is actually gold in 1802, initiating the first gold rush in the United States.
    • \n
  • The assassination of the 14th Tu\'i Kanokupolu, Tuku\xca\xbbaho, plunges Tonga into half a century of civil war.
    • \n
  • The Nawab (provincial governor) of Oudh in northern India sends to George III of England the Padshah Nama, an official history of the reign of Shah Jahan.
    • \n
  • William Cockerill begins building cotton-spinning equipment in Belgium.
    • \n
  • Dutch government takes over Dutch East India Company.
    • \n
