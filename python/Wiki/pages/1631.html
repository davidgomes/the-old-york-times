1631 (MDCXXXI) was a common year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter E) of the Gregorian calendar and a common year starting on Saturday (dominical letter B) of the Julian calendar, the 1631st year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 631st year of the 2nd millennium, the 31st year of the 17th century, and the 2nd year of the 1630s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1631 is 10 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

\n

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: Sweden and France sign the Treaty of B\xc3\xa4rwalde, a military alliance in which France provides funds for the Swedish army invading northern Germany.
    • \n
  • February 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Roger Williams emigrates to Boston.
    • \n
  • February 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Gustav Adolf Secondary School is founded in Tallinn, Estonia by Swedish king Gustavus II Adolphus (Gustav Adolf).
    • \n
  • April 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden defeats an imperial garrison at the city of Frankfurt an der Oder.
    • \n
  • May 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: After a two-month siege, an Imperial army under the command of Tilly storms the German city of Magdeburg and brutally sacks it, massacring over 20,000 inhabitants. Shocked by the massacre, many protestant states in the Holy Roman Empire decide to ally with Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden and support his ongoing invasion.
    • \n
  • May 18 \xe2\x80\x93 In Dorchester, Massachusetts, John Winthrop takes the oath of office and becomes the first Governor of Massachusetts.
    • \n
  • May 30\n
      \n
    • La Gazette, the first French newspaper, was founded.
      • \n
    • Thirty Years\' War: Bavaria and France sign the Treaty of Fontainebleau, forming a secret alliance. The secret alliance does not last long, however.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • June 19 \xe2\x80\x93 War of Mantuan Succession: The Treaty of Cherasco is signed, ending the War of Mantuan Succession.
    • \n
  • June 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Algerian pirates sack Baltimore, County Cork in Ireland.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 16 \xe2\x80\x93 The city of W\xc3\xbcrzburg is taken by Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden, putting an end to the W\xc3\xbcrzburg witch trials, but not before an estimated 900 people from the city and its environs had been burned at the stake for witchcraft.
    • \n
  • July 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: Tilly defeats Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden at the Battle of Werben, but not decisively.
    • \n
  • End of August \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: Running out of supply, Tilly is forced to send his army into the Electorate of Saxony in order to secure supplies, as well as to force a reaction from John George, Elector of Saxony and Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden.
    • \n
  • September 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: As a result of Tilly\'s invasion, John George, Elector of Saxony, who had until now stayed neutral, allies with Gustavus Adolphus of Sweden in order to drive the Imperial army out of Saxony.
    • \n
  • September 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Eighty Years\' War: A Spanish fleet under the command of admiral Antonio de Oquendo defeats a Dutch fleet off the coast of Brazil in the Battle of Albrolhos.
    • \n
  • September 12 and 13 \xe2\x80\x93 Eighty Years\' War: A Spanish fleet carrying an invasion force is intercepted and almost completely destroyed by a Dutch fleet in the Battle of the Slaak.
    • \n
  • September 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: In the Battle of Breitenfeld, Tilly\'s imperial army is decisively defeated by Gustavus II Adolphus of Sweden, shattering the imperial army of the Holy Roman Empire and marking the first significant victory for the Protestants in the war.
    • \n
  • October 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: A Saxon army takes over Prague.
    • \n
  • December 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Thirty Years\' War: Gustavus II Adolphus of Sweden takes the city of Mainz without any resistance.
    • \n
