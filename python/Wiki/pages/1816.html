1816 (MDCCCXVI) was a leap year starting on Monday (dominical letter GF) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Saturday (dominical letter BA) of the Julian calendar, the 1816th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 816th year of the 2nd millennium, the 16th year of the 19th century, and the 7th year of the 1810s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1816 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929. This year was known as the \'Year Without a Summer\' because of low temperatures in the Northern Hemisphere, the result of the Mount Tambora volcanic eruption in Indonesia in 1815. The sulfur from this eruption reflected the sun\'s rays and caused global cooling.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Tsar Alexander I of Russia signs an order for the expulsion of the Jesuits from St. Petersburg and Moscow.[1]
    • \n
  • January 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Sir Humphry Davy\'s Davy lamp is first tested underground as a coal mining safety lamp at Hebburn Colliery in north east England.[2]
    • \n
  • January 17 \xe2\x80\x93 Fire nearly destroys the city of St. John\'s, Newfoundland.
    • \n
  • February 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The Governorate of Estonia of the Russian Empire emancipates its peasants from serfdom.
    • \n
  • February 10 \xe2\x80\x93 Friedrich Karl Ludwig, Duke of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Beck, dies and is succeeded by Friedrich Wilhelm, his son and founder of the House of Gl\xc3\xbccksburg.
    • \n
  • February 20 \xe2\x80\x93 Gioachino Rossini\'s opera buffa The Barber of Seville premi\xc3\xa8res at the Teatro Argentina in Rome.
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 10 \xe2\x80\x93 The Second Bank of the United States obtains its charter.
    • \n
  • April 11 \xe2\x80\x93 In Philadelphia, the African Methodist Episcopal Church is established by Richard Allen and other African-American Methodists, the first such denomination completely independent of White churches.
    • \n
  • May 2 \xe2\x80\x93 Leopold of Saxe-Coburg (later King of the Belgians) marries Charlotte Augusta, but she dies the next year.
    • \n
  • June 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Seven Oaks: The Hudson\'s Bay Company is defeated by the North West Company, near Winnipeg, Canada.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July \xe2\x80\x93 Lord Byron, Mary Wollstonecraft Godwin, Percy Bysshe Shelley and John Polidori, gathered at the Villa Diodati by Lake Geneva in a rainy Switzerland, tell each other tales. This gives rise to two classic Gothic narratives, Mary Shelley\'s Frankenstein and Polidori\'s The Vampyre.
    • \n
  • July 9 \xe2\x80\x93 Argentina declares independence from Spain.
    • \n
  • July 17 \xe2\x80\x93 The French passenger ship Medusa runs aground off the coast of Senegal, with 140 lives lost in the botched rescue that takes weeks, leading to a scandal in the French government.
    • \n
  • August 14 \xe2\x80\x93 The United Kingdom formally annexes the Tristan da Cunha archipelago in the southern Atlantic Ocean, ruling it from the Cape Colony.
    • \n
  • August 24 \xe2\x80\x93 The Treaty of St. Louis is signed in St. Louis, Missouri.
    • \n
  • August 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Bombardment of Algiers: Various European Allies ships force the Dey of Algiers to free Christian slaves.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October 21 \xe2\x80\x93 Penang Free School is founded by Rev. Sparke Hutchings on the island of Penang (in modern-day Malaysia).
    • \n
  • November 6 \xe2\x80\x93 James Monroe defeats Rufus King in the United States presidential election.
    • \n
  • November 19 \xe2\x80\x93 University of Warsaw established.
    • \n
  • December 11 \xe2\x80\x93 Indiana is admitted as the 19th U.S. state.
    • \n
  • December 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Merger of the thrones of Sicily and Naples into the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies under King Ferdinand I.
    • \n
  • December 21 \xe2\x80\x93 American Colonization Society established to support the emigration of free African Americans to Africa.
    • \n
\n

Date unknown[edit]

\n
    \n
  • Shaka starts to rule the Zulu Kingdom at about this date.
    • \n
  • Banjul, capital of the Gambia, is founded as a trading post named Bathurst.
    • \n
  • Ren\xc3\xa9 Laennec invents the stethoscope.
    • \n
  • E. Remington and Sons (the famous firearm and later typewriter manufacturing company) is founded in the United States.
    • \n
  • Robert Stirling patents his Stirling engine, at this time known as Stirling\'s air engine.
    • \n
  • A rail capable of supporting a heavy locomotive is developed.
    • \n
