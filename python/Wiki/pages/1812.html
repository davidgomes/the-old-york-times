1812 (MDCCCXII) was a leap year starting on Wednesday (dominical letter ED) of the Gregorian calendar and a leap year starting on Monday (dominical letter GF) of the Julian calendar, the 1812th year of the Common Era (CE) and Anno Domini (AD) designations, the 812th year of the 2nd millennium, the 12th year of the 19th century, and the 3rd year of the 1810s decade. Note that the Julian day for 1812 is 12 calendar days difference, which continued to be used from 1582 until the complete conversion of the Gregorian calendar was entirely done in 1929.

Events[edit]

\n

January\xe2\x80\x93March[edit]

\n
    \n
  • January 1 \xe2\x80\x93 The Allgemeines b\xc3\xbcrgerliches Gesetzbuch (the Austrian civil code) enters into force in the Austrian Empire.
    • \n
  • February 7\n
      \n
    • The last New Madrid earthquake strikes New Madrid, Missouri, with an estimated moment magnitude of over 8.
      • \n
    • Novelist Charles Dickens is born in Portsmouth, England.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
  • February 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon authorizes the usage of Mesures usuelles, the basis of the metric system.
    • \n
  • February 13 \xe2\x80\x93 First Chilean newspaper Aurora de Chile is dealing with political philosophy and standing in favor of the new national government.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 English poet Lord Byron gives his first address as a member of the House of Lords, in defense of Luddite violence against industrialism in his home county of Nottinghamshire.
    • \n
  • February 27 \xe2\x80\x93 Argentine War of Independence: Manuel Belgrano raises the Flag of Argentina in the city of Rosario for the first time.
    • \n
  • March 5 \xe2\x80\x93 Prussia and France sign the Treaty of Paris
    • \n
  • March 15 \xe2\x80\x93 Luddites attack the wool-processing factory of Frank Vickerman in West Yorkshire.
    • \n
  • March 19 \xe2\x80\x93 The C\xc3\xa1diz Cortes, in exile, create the first modern Spanish constitution.
    • \n
  • March 16\xe2\x80\x93April 6 \xe2\x80\x93 Siege of Badajoz (Peninsular War): The Anglo-Portuguese Army, under the Earl of Wellington, besieges Badajoz in Spain and forces surrender of the French garrison.
    • \n
  • March 26\n
      \n
    • In the United States, the Boston Gazette prints a political cartoon coining the term "Gerrymander" after former Massachusetts Governor Elbridge Gerry\'s approval (on February 11) of legislation creating oddly shaped electoral districts designed to help incumbents win re-election.
      • \n
    • The 1812 Caracas earthquake destroys Caracas in Venezuela.
      • \n
    \n
    • \n
\n

April\xe2\x80\x93June[edit]

\n
    \n
  • April 4 \xe2\x80\x93 U.S. President James Madison enacts a 90-day embargo on trade with the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • April 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Louisiana is admitted as the 18th U.S. state.
    • \n
  • May 11 \xe2\x80\x93 John Bellingham assassinates British Prime Minister Spencer Perceval in the lobby of the British House of Commons.
    • \n
  • May 16 \xe2\x80\x93 Russian Field Marshal Mikhail Kutuzov signs the Treaty of Bucharest, ending the Russo-Turkish War (1806\xe2\x80\x9312) and annexing Bessarabia to Imperial Russia.
    • \n
  • May 25 \xe2\x80\x93 Felling mine disaster: A mine explosion at the Felling colliery near Jarrow, England leaves 96 dead.
    • \n
  • June 1 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812: U.S. President James Madison asks the U.S. Congress to declare war on the United Kingdom.
    • \n
  • June 4 \xe2\x80\x93 Following Louisiana\'s admittance as a U.S. state, the territory by that name is renamed the Missouri Territory.
    • \n
  • June 16 \xe2\x80\x93 New York State charters City Bank of New York, which later became Citibank.
    • \n
  • June 18 \xe2\x80\x93 The War of 1812 between the United States, Canada and the United Kingdom begins.
    • \n
  • June 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon\'s Grande Arm\xc3\xa9e crosses the Neman River and invades Russia.
    • \n
\n

July\xe2\x80\x93September[edit]

\n
    \n
  • July 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Americans invade Canada at Windsor, Ontario.
    • \n
  • July 18 \xe2\x80\x93 Russia\'s Patriotic War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Klyastitsy: Kulnev defeats Oudinot but sustains a mortal wound.
    • \n
  • July 22 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Salamanca: British forces led by Lord Wellington defeat French troops near Salamanca in Spain.
    • \n
  • August 5 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812: Tecumseh\'s Indian force ambushes Thomas Van Horne\'s 200 Americans at Brownstone Creek, causing them to flee and retreat.
    • \n
  • August 12 \xe2\x80\x93 Peninsular War: The combined English and Portuguese army under the command of Wellington enters Madrid following the Battle of Salamanca.
    • \n
  • August 15 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812: Battle of Fort Dearborn \xe2\x80\x93 Potawatomi warriors overrun the United States fort in [Illinois Territory].
    • \n
  • August 16 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812: American General William Hull surrenders Fort Detroit without a fight to the British Army.
    • \n
  • August 19 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812: The USS Constitution defeats the British frigate Guerriere off the coast of Nova Scotia. The British shot is said to have bounced off the Constitution\'s sides, earning her the nickname "Old Ironsides".
    • \n
  • September 7 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 French invasion of Russia \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Borodino: The bloodiest battle of the Napoleonic Wars ends in a tactical victory for Napoleon. There are at least 70,000 casualties, with a minimum of 6,562 dead from the French Grande Arm\xc3\xa9e alone.
    • \n
  • September 14 \xe2\x80\x93 French invasion of Russia: Napoleon\'s troops enter Moscow, which is deliberately set on fire by Muscovites on orders of Fyodor Rostopchin.
    • \n
\n

October\xe2\x80\x93December[edit]

\n
    \n
  • October \xe2\x80\x93 The capital of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, United States is permanently moved from Lancaster to Harrisburg.
    • \n
  • October 9 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812: American naval forces under Lieutenant Jesse Duncan Elliott capture two British warships, HMS Detroit and HMS Caledonia.
    • \n
  • October 13 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812 \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Queenston Heights: As part of the Niagara campaign in Ontario, Canada, United States forces under General Stephen Van Rensselaer are repulsed from invading Canada by British and native troops led by Sir Isaac Brock (who dies during the battle).
    • \n
  • October 18\xe2\x80\x9320 \xe2\x80\x93 Second Battle of Polotsk \xe2\x80\x93 Russians attack and defeat a Franco-Bavarian force in Belarus.
    • \n
  • October 19 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleon begins his retreat from Moscow.
    • \n
  • October 23 \xe2\x80\x93 Malet coup of 1812: General Claude Fran\xc3\xa7ois de Malet attempts unsuccessfully to overthrow the Napoleonic r\xc3\xa9gime in Paris.
    • \n
  • October 24 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Maloyaroslavets: An inconclusive encounter between the French vanguard and a Russian force leads Napoleon to decide to retreat along the same line as his advance, with disastrous results.
    • \n
  • November 3 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Vyazma: The rearguard of Napoleon\'s retreating army is defeated.
    • \n
  • November 5 \xe2\x80\x93 James Madison defeats DeWitt Clinton in the U.S. presidential election.
    • \n
  • November 10 \xe2\x80\x93 A general election in the United Kingdom sees victory for the Tory Party under Robert Jenkinson, 2nd Earl of Liverpool.
    • \n
\n
\n
    \n
  • November 15\xe2\x80\x9318 \xe2\x80\x93 Napoleonic Wars \xe2\x80\x93 Battle of Krasnoi: Napoleon\'s retreating army is again defeated in a series of skirmishes.
    • \n
  • December 8 \xe2\x80\x93 The 1812 Wrightwood earthquake affects Southern California.
    • \n
  • December 20 \xe2\x80\x93 The first volume of Grimms\' Fairy Tales is published in Germany.
    • \n
  • December 29 \xe2\x80\x93 War of 1812: The USS Constitution defeats the British frigate HMS Java off the coast of Brazil.
    • \n
  • December 30 \xe2\x80\x93 Convention of Tauroggen is signed.
    • \n
